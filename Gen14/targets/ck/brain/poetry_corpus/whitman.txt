SongOfMyself6:1	A child said What is the grass? fetching it to me with full hands;
SongOfMyself6:2	How could I answer the child? I do not know what it is any more than he.
SongOfMyself6:3	I guess it must be the flag of my disposition, out of hopeful green stuff woven.
SongOfMyself6:4	Or I guess it is the handkerchief of the Lord,
SongOfMyself6:5	A scented gift and remembrancer designedly dropt,
SongOfMyself6:6	Bearing the owner's name someway in the corners, that we may see and remark, and say Whose?

SongOfMyself52:1	I too am not a bit tamed, I too am untranslatable,
SongOfMyself52:2	I sound my barbaric yawp over the roofs of the world.
SongOfMyself52:3	The spotted hawk swoops by and accuses me, he complains of my gab and my loitering.

SongOfMyself52:5	If you want me again look for me under your boot-soles.
