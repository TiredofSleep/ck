Yeats 1889-1919
Innisfree:1	I will arise and go now, and go to Innisfree,
Innisfree:2	And a small cabin build there, of clay and wattles made;
Innisfree:3	Nine bean-rows will I have there, a hive for the honey-bee,
Innisfree:4	And live alone in the bee-loud glade.
Innisfree:5	And I shall have some peace there, for peace comes dropping slow,
Innisfree:6	Dropping from the veils of the morning to where the cricket sings;
SecondComing:1	Turning and turning in the widening gyre
SecondComing:2	The falcon cannot hear the falconer;
SecondComing:3	Things fall apart; the centre cannot hold;
SecondComing:4	Mere anarchy is loosed upon the world,
SecondComing:5	The blood-dimmed tide is loosed, and everywhere
SecondComing:6	The ceremony of innocence is drowned;
WhenYouAreOld:1	When you are old and grey and full of sleep,
WhenYouAreOld:2	And nodding by the fire, take down this book,
WhenYouAreOld:3	And slowly read, and dream of the soft look
WhenYouAreOld:4	Your eyes had once, and of their shadows deep;
HeWishes:1	Had I the heavens' embroidered cloths,
HeWishes:2	Enwrought with golden and silver light,
HeWishes:3	The blue and the dim and the dark cloths
HeWishes:4	Of night and light and the half-light,
HeWishes:5	I would spread the cloths under your feet:
HeWishes:6	But I, being poor, have only my dreams;
HeWishes:7	I have spread my dreams under your feet;
HeWishes:8	Tread softly because you tread on my dreams.
