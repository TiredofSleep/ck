Yasna 28:1	With outstretched hands in petition for that help, O Mazda, I will pray for the works of the holy spirit, O thou the Right, whereby I may possibly bring joy to the soul of the kine.
Yasna 28:2	I who would serve you, O Mazda Ahura, and Vohu Mano, do ye give through Asha the blessings of both worlds, the bodily and that of the mind.
Yasna 28:11	And so do thou, O Ahura, teach me from Thine own mouth what is to come, that I may declare it to the world by Thine inspiration.
Yasna 30:3	And these two spirits united created the first the realities of life and the not-life, and how at last shall be the worst existence for the followers of falsehood, but for the holy thinker the best mental state.
Yasna 31:11	When Thou, Mazda, in the beginning, didst create the soul and the conscience, the intellect and the bodily form, and didst grant to it the power of action, of speech, and of guidance, Thou didst will that everyone should freely profess his faith.
Yasna 33:7	Come ye to me, O ye best ones! Come hither, O Mazda; and Asha, by the power of the song, by the song of the offering and of the praise, that ye may also support our prayer manifestly here before all the assembly!
Yasna 43:1	Blessings of weal upon him! May Mazda Ahura, ruling at His will, grant blessings of weal to him! Strength and vigour I would beseech for that one to maintain the holy Right.
Yasna 44:7	This do I ask Thee, O Lord; tell me of the truth! Whose religion is the best of those which exist, and which one, going on with prosperity, shall make holy by the truth what is verily the highest?
Yasna 60:5	May the desire of the holy come to pass — light, life, and gladness! May the will of the holy come about — and may every one come to that which is best.
