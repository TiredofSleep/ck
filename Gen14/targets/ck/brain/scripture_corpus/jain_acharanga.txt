Acharanga Jacobi1884
Acharanga 1:1	O long-lived (Jambu)! I (Sudharman) have heard the following Discourse from the Venerable (Mahavira): Here many do not remember whether they have descended in an eastern direction, or in a southern, or in a western, or in a northern direction, or in the direction from above, or in the direction from below.
Acharanga 1:2	Some know that their soul is born again and again, that it arrives in this or that direction, whatever direction that may be. He believes in soul, believes in the world, believes in reward, believes in action.
Acharanga 1:3	He who looks upon all beings as his own self, and acts in a friendly manner with all beings: such a person attains liberation.
Acharanga 1:5	Knowing the cause of pain in the world, and how it is brought about, knowing the means of the destruction of pain, the sage walks the path that leads to its destruction.
Acharanga 2:1	He who knows the doctrine of action, knows that of suffering; he who knows the doctrine of suffering, knows the doctrine of action.
Acharanga 3:1	The seeker of truth should renounce the desire for honour, fame, and reputation; should not be elated by gain, nor depressed by loss.
Acharanga 4:1	All breathing, existing, living, sentient creatures should not be slain, nor treated with violence, nor abused, nor tormented, nor driven away. This is the pure, unchangeable, eternal law which the clever ones, who understand the world, have declared.
Acharanga 5:1	No one is permitted to act so as to cause pain to the soul of another, for souls are equal in their nature; therefore, the inflicting of pain on others is forbidden.
Acharanga 6:1	A wise man should not be angry; if angered, he should not give utterance to it; he should not even think evil thoughts. The greatest religion is to remain calm in adversity.
