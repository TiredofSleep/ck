JapjiSahib Macauliffe1909
Japji 1:1	There is but one God whose name is True, the Creator, devoid of fear and enmity, immortal, unborn, self-existent, great and bountiful.
Japji 1:2	The True One was in the beginning; the True One was in the primal age. The True One is now also, O Nanak; the True One also shall be.
Japji 1:3	By thinking I cannot obtain a conception of Him, even though I think hundreds of thousands of times.
Japji 1:4	Even though I be silent and keep my attention firmly fixed on Him, I cannot preserve silence.
Japji 1:5	The hunger of the hungry for God subsideth not though they obtain the load of the worlds.
Japji 1:6	If man should have thousands and hundreds of thousands of devices, even one would not avail him in God's court.
Japji 2:1	By God's order bodies are produced; His order cannot be told.
Japji 2:2	By His order souls are infused into them; by His order greatness is obtained.
Japji 2:3	By His order men are high or low; by His order they obtain pre-ordained pain or pleasure.
Japji 27:1	What is that gate, what is that mansion where Thou sittest and watchest over all things?
Japji 27:2	How many and how endless are Thy musical instruments and Thy minstrels!
Japji 38:1	Continence is the workshop, patience the goldsmith, understanding the anvil, divine knowledge the tools.
Japji 38:2	The fear of God is the bellows, austerity the fire, the love of God the crucible in which the immortal water is melted.
Japji 38:3	In the true mint the word is coined; this is the work of those upon whom He looketh with favour. Nanak, the kind One by a glance maketh them happy.
