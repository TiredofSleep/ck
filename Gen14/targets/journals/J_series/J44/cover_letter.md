## Summary

We report a numerical match: three closed-form rational expressions in two integer primitives — HARMONY = 7 and the substrate size |Z/10| = 10 — hit the Planck 2018 dark-sector parameters Ω_b, Ω_c, Ω_Λ within 1σ each, while the closure Ω_b + Ω_c + Ω_Λ = 1 holds exactly as a rational identity. A search across 784 small-integer (H, N) pairs and seven closure offsets shows that (H, N, a) = (7, 10, +1) is the unique simultaneous match **within the chosen formula family** {Ω_b = H²/N³, Ω_Λ = (2H³+a)/N³, |a| ≤ 3} (R1 framing: explicit formula-family disclaimer; the within-family uniqueness is forced given the family choice, the family choice itself is structural). Three Hubble-independent ratio tests confirm the match at 0.4-1.1% precision (R1 recalibrated to use Planck 2018 Ω_c = 0.2627±0.0020, replacing the R0 Ω_DM = 0.26447±0.00264). The dark-energy scale Λ ≈ 1.74 meV is **conditional on** the open structural relation ρ_DE,0 = 3Λ⁴ (R1: this was Theorem 6.1 in R0 and is downgraded to a Conjecture; the "?=" in R0 is replaced by an explicit conditional statement, since a Theorem with "?" is not a Theorem). We frame the broader pattern as a falsifiable substrate-rational representation conjecture (Conjecture 7.1, R1: bounded admissible family) and report a 260,000-tuple baseline scan against eight fundamental constants whose differential behaviour confirms the family is non-trivially discriminating. **Look-elsewhere correction (R1, §3.5)**: among positive integer triples (a, b, c) with a + b + c = 1000, six fall in all three Planck 1σ envelopes; under uniform sampling the base-rate probability is 6/498,501 ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻⁵.
