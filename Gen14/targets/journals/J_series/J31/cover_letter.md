## Summary

We compute, exactly in rationals, the Wedderburn $D_4$-isotypic decomposition of the lens-pair commutator $[T, B] := TB - BT \in M_{10}(\mathbb{Z})$ for the canonical commutative composition tables $T = \mathrm{TSML\_SYM}$ and $B = \mathrm{BHML}$ on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ (a finite-magma pair in the same intellectual neighborhood as the commutative non-associative quasigroups studied by Drápal & Wanless, *J. Combin. Theory A* **184**, 105510 (2021)).

The dihedral group $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3\rangle \subset S_{10}$, generated by the transposition $P_{56} = (5\,6)$ and the order-2 element $\sigma^3 = (1\,5)(2\,6)(4\,7)$ of the σ-permutation cycle on the units of $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$, acts on $M_{10}(\mathbb{Z})$ by conjugation. The unique Wedderburn isotypic decomposition has component norm-squareds (exact rationals)
$$
\bigl(\|\pi_V\|^2\bigr) \;=\; \Bigl(\,\tfrac{3{,}075{,}027}{2},\;\tfrac{9}{2},\;288{,}164,\;0,\;19{,}608\,\Bigr) \;\;\text{summing to}\;\; 1{,}845{,}290,
$$
i.e. percentage shares $\approx 83.32\%, 0.0002\%, 15.62\%, 0\%, 1.06\%$ for $V \in (\mathrm{triv}, \mathrm{sign}_1, \mathrm{sign}_2, \mathrm{sign}_3, \mathrm{std})$.

Two of the five isotypic components carry essentially zero weight: $\mathrm{sign}_3$ vanishes *exactly*, and $\mathrm{sign}_1$ has relative share $9/3{,}690{,}580 = 2.44 \times 10^{-6}$. We characterize these as bilinear-cancellation identities peculiar to the canonical pair (Proposition 5.1) and conjecture (Conjecture 7.2) that they extend to every member of the TIG family per the 5 conjoint membership criteria of `Atlas/META_PLAN_2026-05-06/FAMILY_STRUCTURE_v1.md`.

The trivial isotypic (Path A) is the 16-dimensional doubly-invariant subalgebra $\mathfrak{g}_0 \subset \mathfrak{so}(10)$. By Killing-form classification (eigenvalue spectrum $(-4)^{15} \oplus (0)^1$), $\mathfrak{g}_0 \cong \mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$ (Theorem 3.2). The $\mathrm{sign}_2$ isotypic (Path B) lies entirely in the symmetric-traceless $\mathbf{54}$ irrep of $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ along an explicit 9-vector with $\|v\|^2 = 13/4$ exactly (Theorem 4.1). The 2-dimensional $\mathrm{std}$ isotypic carries the small ($\sim$1%) interaction term coupling Path A and Path B.

## Note on prior version

An earlier version of this paper (April 2026, internally circulated as "Two Roads to Pati-Salam") advertised a synthesis claim that Path A and Path B both lead to the same Pati-Salam gauge content. A 2026-04-27 deep audit retracted the synthesis claim (Path A's stabilizer is SO(8), not SO(6)$\times$SO(4); Path B is $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$, not the full Pati-Salam algebra). The May-2026 corrected $D_4$-isotypic decomposition (the present paper's central theorem) *constructively resolves* the apparent tension: Path A and Path B are the two leading isotypic components of a *single* canonical Wedderburn decomposition, not competing reductions. Their "tension" is structurally the 1% std-isotypic coupling. The retraction is integrated into §0.3 as the framing of the present paper. The mathematical identifications (the 16-dim $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$ subalgebra and the 9-vector with $\|v\|^2 = 13/4$) all stand intact.
