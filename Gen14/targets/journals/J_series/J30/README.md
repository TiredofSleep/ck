## §1 -- Manuscript

**Local path:** `manuscript/manuscript.md`

The J30 paper is **Joint Lie Closure of a Pair of Z/10Z Magmas: an so(10) Identification** (formerly "WP103 -- An so(10) Identification from the Coupled Coherence Tables"; retitled per SAVE_PLAN §6 to promote "joint closure" as the central object). Abstract: For the canonical pair (TSML_SYM, BHML) of 10×10 commutative non-associative magmas on Z/10Z (each 12.8% non-associative; jointly-closed 4-core {0, 7, 8, 9}), the joint Lie subalgebra g := ⟨G_TSML ∪ G_BHML⟩_Lie ⊂ so(V) generated by the antisymmetrizations of TSML's flow indices and BHML's non-zero antisymmetrizations is isomorphic to so(10, R) = D_5. The substantive computation is dim g = 45 (Theorem 4.1); the four supporting diagnostics (Jacobi; Killing-form negative-definite; one-dim invariant-form space; Cartan rank 5) are *consistency checks* following from D1 plus the Cartan classification, not independent tests of the so(10) identification. Lemma 4.5 (the simplicity test) uses the full 91,125-equation enumeration via `verify_simplicity_rank.py` (no sampling). The TSML-only subalgebra g_TSML ⊂ g realizes the standard inclusion so(8) ⊂ so(10) (companion paper [SandersGishSO8, J29]). Corollary 5.3 records the new SFM_Q6 finding: adding CL_STD to the joint closure preserves the 8-shell sub-magma chain — the chain is a three-substrate fixed point.
