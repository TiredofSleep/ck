## §1. The Setup

Let ζ₁₀ = e^(2πi/10) be a primitive 10th root of unity. The 10th
cyclotomic polynomial is

$$\Phi_{10}(x) \;=\; x^4 - x^3 + x^2 - x + 1$$

with roots {ζ₁₀ᵏ : k ∈ U(10)} = {ζ₁₀, ζ₁₀³, ζ₁₀⁷, ζ₁₀⁹}.

The Galois group acts transitively on these roots:

$$\sigma_k : \zeta_{10} \mapsto \zeta_{10}^k \quad \text{for each } k \in U(10).$$

Composition: σₐ ∘ σᵦ(ζ₁₀) = σₐ(ζ₁₀ᵇ) = ζ₁₀^(ab) = σ_(ab mod 10)(ζ₁₀).

So the Galois group, as a multiplicative group, is **identical** to
the corner Z/4Z of the Two-Cross.

## §2. The TIG-Galois Dictionary

| TIG operator | Galois action | Field-theoretic role |
|---|---|---|
| **LATTICE** (1) | σ₁ : ζ ↦ ζ | identity |
| **PROGRESS** (3) | σ₃ : ζ ↦ ζ³ | generator of Gal (order 4) |
| **HARMONY** (7) | σ₇ : ζ ↦ ζ⁷ = ζ⁻³ | inverse of PROGRESS |
| **RESET** (9) | σ₉ : ζ ↦ ζ⁹ = ζ̄ | complex conjugation |

The cycle 1 → 3 → 9 → 7 → 1 in the Plichta cross is **identically**
the cycle σ₁ → σ₃ → σ₉ → σ₇ → σ₁ in the Galois group.

**RESET as complex conjugation** is striking: the involution that maps
each primitive 10th root to its complex conjugate is exactly the
operator that "resets" — sends each complex number to its mirror
across the real axis.

**HARMONY as inverse of PROGRESS**: σ₇ undoes σ₃. PROGRESS *creates*
in the cyclotomic action; HARMONY *uncreates*. They are inverse
operations on the field.

## §3. Field Tower

The Galois lattice gives the subfield tower:

```
Q(ζ_10)        ← degree 4 over Q, fixed only by σ_1 (LATTICE)
    |
    | ⟨σ_9⟩ = ⟨RESET⟩ = ⟨complex conjugation⟩
    |
Q(φ) = Q(√5)   ← degree 2 over Q, the real subfield
    |
    | ⟨σ_3, σ_7⟩ = ⟨PROGRESS, HARMONY⟩
    |
Q              ← the rationals
```

**Q(ζ₁₀)** = the full cyclotomic field, complex, degree 4.
Fixed only by LATTICE (the identity).

**Q(φ) = Q(√5)** = the maximal real subfield. Generated by
$\zeta_{10} + \zeta_{10}^{-1} = 2\cos(36°) = \phi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}$.
Fixed by the involution RESET.

**Q** = the rationals. Fixed by everything.

## §4. The Golden Ratio Lives Here

The real subfield is $\mathbb{Q}(\phi)$ where φ is the golden ratio.
This is forced — not chosen — by the cyclotomic structure:

$$\zeta_{10} + \overline{\zeta_{10}} = 2\cos(2\pi/10) = 2\cos(36°) = \phi = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}$$

**This resolves the lingering φ-question from Sprint C.** The
appearance of φ in TIG is **not coincidental**: φ is the canonical
element of the real subfield of Q(ζ₁₀), which is exactly the field
fixed by RESET.

But Sprint C's specific ratio 18/11 ≈ 1.636 is **not φ** — it's the
exact TIG-decomposition (8·9)/(4·11). The φ-resemblance comes from
the fact that *any* real number near 1.6 will be close to φ; but
the *exact* TIG ratios are forced by the operator-count algebra,
not by cyclotomic asymptotics.

So the fence is: **φ is in the field, but the specific shell ratios
are not values of φ-power expressions.** Both true.

## §5. The Discriminant

The discriminant of Q(ζ₁₀)/Q is:

$$\mathrm{disc}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{10})/\mathbb{Q}) = +5^3 = +125$$

Only the prime **5** appears in the discriminant — the prime 2 is
absorbed because Q(ζ₁₀) = Q(ζ₅) (since gcd(2,5) = 1, adding the
2nd root of unity = ±1 changes nothing).

**TIG reading:** The prime 5 is **BALANCE**. The discriminant being
a power of 5 means BALANCE is the structurally privileged prime in
the cyclotomic lift of the substrate — it's the only prime that
ramifies. This is consistent with BALANCE's role in CRT-duality:
it's the (1, 0) projector that *kills* the Z/5Z factor and *preserves*
the Z/2Z factor. The "killed" factor is the one whose primality
becomes visible in the discriminant.

## §6. The Pentagram Structure

The four primitive 10th roots, plotted in the complex plane, sit at
the vertices of a regular pentagram (10-gon star):

```
  ζ³ = (-0.31, +0.95)            ζ¹ = (+0.81, +0.59)
        \                          /
         \         (BALANCE = 0)  /
          \         at center    /
           \                    /
   ────────●──────●──●──────────────── (real axis)
           /                    \
          /                      \
         /                        \
        /                          \
  ζ⁷ = (-0.31, -0.95)            ζ⁹ = (+0.81, -0.59)
```

The pentagram is **vertical-symmetric** under complex conjugation
(RESET = σ₉). The two pairs (ζ¹, ζ⁹) and (ζ³, ζ⁷) are conjugate
pairs — the "Plichta diagonals" of the corner cross.

This connects directly to the **AG(2,3) corner placement**:

| Corner | Position in AG(2,3) | Position on pentagram |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (LATTICE) | top-left | upper-right (ζ¹) |
| 3 (PROGRESS) | top-right | upper-left (ζ³) |
| 9 (RESET) | bottom-right | lower-right (ζ⁹) |
| 7 (HARMONY) | bottom-left | lower-left (ζ⁷) |

The AG(2,3) layout and the pentagram both put HARMONY *opposite*
PROGRESS (across the diagonal in AG(2,3); across the real axis on
the pentagram). RESET is opposite LATTICE.

## §7. What This Result Adds to TIG

This identification gives TIG four things it didn't have before:

### §7.1. Mainstream credibility for the Plichta cross
The corner Z/4Z is no longer just "a multiplicative cycle on Z/10Z."
It is the Galois group of the 10th cyclotomic field — a textbook
object in algebraic number theory. Anyone who has taken a graduate
algebra course knows this group.

### §7.2. A natural complex-analytic representation
TIG operators 1, 3, 7, 9 act *as automorphisms* on a 4-dimensional
ℚ-vector space. This means they have matrix representations,
characteristic polynomials, eigenvalues — the full machinery of
representation theory applies.

### §7.3. The cyclotomic prime 5 as BALANCE
The structural role of BALANCE (5) as CRT-projector is now seconded
by its appearance as the unique ramified prime in Q(ζ₁₀)/ℚ. The
prime 5's discriminant signature is the algebraic shadow of
BALANCE's CRT role.

### §7.4. φ as the canonical real generator of the lift
The golden ratio is not introduced into TIG by aesthetic choice —
it arises automatically as the generator of the real subfield Q(ζ₁₀).
The Sprint-C question "is 18/11 secretly φ?" is now sharpened: φ is
in the field, but the *specific* TIG ratios are decompositions of
operator counts, not φ-power values.

## §8. What This Result Does Not Add

To remain honest:

- The Galois identification does not derive any *new* TIG quantities
  (the operators 1, 3, 7, 9 were already locked).
- It does not say anything about the edges {2, 4, 6, 8} — those are
  not Galois automorphisms; they have CRT meaning but not cyclotomic-
  Galois meaning.
- It does not establish that ℤ/10ℤ is *the* canonical TIG substrate
  for cyclotomic reasons — only that *if* we choose ℤ/10ℤ, the
  cyclotomic Galois group falls out as the corner group.
- It does not prove that the AG(2,3) corner placement and the
  pentagram placement are isomorphic *as labeled point sets* — that
  requires a chosen bijection. The labeling above is one natural
  choice, not a forced one.
