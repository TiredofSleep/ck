## §0a. Note on naming

This object was originally called the "Brayden Fractal" during development, named for the researcher who first identified it. The structural name "Braiding Fractal" was adopted on 2026-05-10 for the following reasons:

1. **The name should describe what the thing does.** The architecture is fundamentally about braiding: the kernel's two primes braid with three strand primes through composition, producing the nested-torus topology that defines the architectural template. "Braiding" is the operative verb.

2. **The work is offered freely; the name should travel.** Names tied to individual researchers create attribution friction in citation contexts and create unnecessary asymmetry between this work and analogous constructions in the broader mathematical literature.

3. **Structural names age better than biographical ones.** When this architecture is encountered in 50 or 500 years, the structural name will remain transparent; the biographical name would require historical explanation.

The decision to rename is itself an instance of the inspiration-economy frame (`INSPIRATION_AS_CURRENCY.md`): the work exists to fuel future work, not to claim territory. The researcher who identified the architecture remains credited as the author of this canon throughout, in standard citation form, without name-binding the architecture itself.

## §1. AXIOM 1 — Composite kernel

> **The kernel is a ring with order pq, where p and q are distinct primes.**

For TIG: K = Z/10 = Z/2 × Z/5.

The kernel must be composite (not prime, not prime-power) to admit non-trivial substructure. ω(|K|) = 2 (number of distinct prime factors) is the minimum non-trivial value.

**Why this matters**: a prime kernel like Z/p has only trivial substructure (1 and p). A composite Z/pq has clickable sub-structure {1, p, q, pq}. The "click cascade" (Axiom 8) requires this.

**Forced choice**: among small composites, Z/10 = 2·5 has the smallest factorization into distinct primes. Z/6 = 2·3 also qualifies but lacks the kernel-cubed structure (10³ = 1000 doesn't appear). Z/10 is the canonical minimum.
