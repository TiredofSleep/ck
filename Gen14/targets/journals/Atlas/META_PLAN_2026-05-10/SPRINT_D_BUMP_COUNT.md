## §1. The Hopf Component (8 bumps)

The bridge x ↦ 6x is a group isomorphism from the corner-group U(10)
to the edge-group {2,4,6,8} (Two-Cross §iii). Each corner pairs with
exactly one edge:

| Corner | x ↦ 6x | Edge | Operators |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6·1 = 6 | 6 | (LATTICE, CHAOS) |
| 3 | 6·3 = 18 ≡ 8 | 8 | (PROGRESS, BREATH) |
| 7 | 6·7 = 42 ≡ 2 | 2 | (HARMONY, COUNTER) |
| 9 | 6·9 = 54 ≡ 4 | 4 | (RESET, COLLAPSE) |

These four pairs lift to the torus as **four Hopf links**: each pair
consists of one circle on the longitude (corner cycle, +1 winding)
and one circle on the meridian (edge cycle, −1 winding), linking with
linking number 1.

A Hopf link generates **2 bumps** — one on each linked circle, where
the linking is visible. With 4 such links:

$$\text{Hopf bumps} = 4 \cdot 2 = 8$$

## §2. The Trefoil Component (3 bumps)

BALANCE (5) is the unique nontrivial multiplicative idempotent of
ℤ/10ℤ that is *not* part of either Z/4Z (it's neither in the corner
group nor the edge group). Its idempotence

$$5 \cdot 5 \equiv 5 \pmod{10}$$

means that on the torus, BALANCE's self-composition trajectory loops

A self-trajectory that wraps both T² generators with mismatched signs
produces a **(2,3)-torus knot = trefoil**. The trefoil has **3
crossings**, contributing 3 bumps:

$$\text{Trefoil bumps} = 1 \cdot 3 = 3$$
