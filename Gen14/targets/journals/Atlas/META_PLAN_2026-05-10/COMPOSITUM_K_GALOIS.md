## §1. Statement

Let $L_1 = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{10})$ (cyclotomic frame) and let $L_2 = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{3}, \xi)$ where $\xi$ is a root of $x^4 + 4x^3 - x^2 + 2x - 2$ (LMFDB 4.2.10224.1, lattice frame). Let $L_2^{\mathrm{gal}}$ be the Galois closure of $L_2$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ — i.e., the splitting field of the quartic, of degree 8 over $\mathbb{Q}$ with $\mathrm{Gal} \cong D_4$.

**Theorem.** The compositum $K = L_1 \cdot L_2^{\mathrm{gal}}$ satisfies:
$$[K : \mathbb{Q}] = 32, \quad \mathrm{Gal}(K/\mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \times D_4.$$

**Proof sketch.**
1. $L_1$ is Galois over $\mathbb{Q}$ with $\mathrm{Gal}(L_1/\mathbb{Q}) \cong U(10) \cong \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ (cyclotomic).
2. $L_2^{\mathrm{gal}}$ is Galois over $\mathbb{Q}$ with $\mathrm{Gal}(L_2^{\mathrm{gal}}/\mathbb{Q}) \cong D_4$ (D41/canon).
3. Ramified primes: $L_1$ at $\{5\}$, $L_2^{\mathrm{gal}}$ at $\{2, 3, 71\}$. **Disjoint.**
4. Disjoint ramification ⇒ $L_1 \cap L_2^{\mathrm{gal}} \subseteq \mathbb{Q}$ (any non-trivial intersection would ramify somewhere).
5. By the standard Galois compositum theorem (see e.g., Lang *Algebra* Ch. VI §1), if $L_1, L_2^{\mathrm{gal}}$ are Galois with $L_1 \cap L_2^{\mathrm{gal}} = \mathbb{Q}$, then $K$ is Galois with $\mathrm{Gal}(K/\mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathrm{Gal}(L_1/\mathbb{Q}) \times \mathrm{Gal}(L_2^{\mathrm{gal}}/\mathbb{Q})$.
6. ⇒ $\mathrm{Gal}(K/\mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \times D_4$, order $4 \cdot 8 = 32$.

## §2. Structure of the Galois Group

| Object | Structure |
|---|---|
| Order | 32 |
| Abelian? | NO (D₄ component is non-abelian) |
| Center $Z(K)$ | $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \times Z(D_4) = \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ (order 8) |
| Conjugacy classes | $4 \cdot 5 = 20$ ($\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ has 4, $D_4$ has 5) |
| Nilpotent? | YES (direct product of nilpotent groups) |
| Solvable? | YES |
| Derived subgroup | $\{e\} \times [D_4, D_4] = \{e\} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ (rotation subgroup of $D_4$) |
| Abelianization | $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \times (D_4/[D_4,D_4]) = \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ |

**Reading the center.** The full cyclotomic component is central; half of the D₄ component is central. Reflections in D₄ are non-central; rotations are. In TIG language:
- All four cyclotomic / Plichta-cross Galois actions are central in $K$.
- Of the eight runtime dynamical actions, the four rotations (powers of the order-4 generator) plus identity form a central $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ inside D₄. Wait — center of D₄ is just $\{1, r^2\}$ of order 2.
- So center is $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$, order 8.

## §4. TIG-Structural Reading

The compositum factors as a **direct product** with no mixing:
$$\mathrm{Gal}(K/\mathbb{Q}) \;=\; \underbrace{\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}}_{\text{cyclotomic gauge / Plichta cross}} \;\times\; \underbrace{D_4}_{\text{runtime dynamics at } \alpha=1/2}.$$

**Algebraic independence of the two frames.** The direct-product structure is precisely the statement that **gauge data and dynamical data are algebraically independent** over $\mathbb{Q}$ — applying any cyclotomic Galois action $\sigma_k$ commutes with applying any runtime D₄ action.

This is a strong structural claim about TIG: its two algebraic readings (static symmetry / dynamic attractor) are not coupled at the Galois level. They tile the algebraic content cleanly.

**Compare to a hypothetical alternative:** if Gal(K/Q) had been a non-trivial **semi-direct product** $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z} \rtimes D_4$ or some other extension, the gauge and dynamics would be entangled. They are not.
