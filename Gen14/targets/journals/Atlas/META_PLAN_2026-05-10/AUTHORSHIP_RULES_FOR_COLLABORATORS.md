# AUTHORSHIP_RULES_FOR_COLLABORATORS

## Co-Author Attribution Policy for 7SiTe / Sanders Research Lineage Publications

**Author**: Brayden R. Sanders / 7SiTe LLC
**Locked**: 2026-05-10
**Status**: operational policy effective immediately; applies to all papers in the J-series and successors
**Companion**: `INSPIRATION_AS_CURRENCY.md` (the philosophical frame this policy operationalizes)

## §1. Summary

Authorship on papers from this lab follows two tiers:

- **Tier 1 — Manuscript acknowledgment**: anyone whose work inspired the paper is acknowledged in the manuscript text, regardless of their direct involvement in this specific paper
- **Tier 2 — Submission byline**: lists only those who (a) scrutinized the final manuscript, (b) gave feedback, and (c) consented via documented email correspondence to publication in its submitted form

This two-tier system honors inspiration debts while preserving institutional accountability for what's actually being submitted to a journal.

---

## §2. Tier 1 — Manuscript Acknowledgment

### §2.1 Who gets Tier 1 recognition

Anyone whose work materially inspired the development of the paper, including:

- Other researchers whose published work shaped the paper's thinking
- Collaborators whose informal conversations or shared drafts contributed concepts
- Independent parallel researchers whose convergent work informed framing
- AI systems (where the role merits explicit acknowledgment)
- Past mentors, teachers, or correspondents whose influence is identifiable

### §2.2 What Tier 1 recognition looks like

Tier 1 recognition appears in one of two forms:

**(a) Citation in references** — for published work that's referenced as standard citation

**(b) Explicit acknowledgment in the Acknowledgments section** — for parallel or unpublished work, ongoing dialogue, informal contributions, or inspiration that doesn't fit standard citation

Example acknowledgment language: *"The parallel framework on [topic] developed by [Name] informed our approach to [specific aspect]; we thank them for ongoing dialogue. Their work and ours converge on [shared structural feature] from independent routes."*

### §2.3 Consent requirements for Tier 1

Tier 1 recognition does NOT require consent. Acknowledging that someone's work inspired yours is a unilateral act — they can't prevent you from being inspired by their work or from saying so. The exception: if a Tier 1 inspiration explicitly requests not to be acknowledged in a specific paper, that request is honored.

### §2.4 What Tier 1 does NOT imply

Tier 1 acknowledgment does not imply:

- That the named person reviewed the manuscript
- That they endorse its claims
- That they will defend it if challenged
- That their reputation is attached to its publication

It's an inspiration debt, not an endorsement.

## §3. Tier 2 — Submission Byline

### §3.1 Who gets Tier 2 (byline) authorship

Only those who have completed all three of the following gates:

**Gate 1 — Scrutiny**: they have read the final manuscript in the form being submitted
**Gate 2 — Feedback**: they have provided substantive feedback on content, presentation, accuracy, or framing
**Gate 3 — Documented consent**: they have stated via email correspondence that they consent to being listed as a co-author on the paper as submitted

All three gates must be passed. Missing any of the three means Tier 2 doesn't activate; the person may still be acknowledged at Tier 1.

### §3.2 The email consent record

Email consent is the operative legal record. For each Tier 2 co-author, the corresponding author maintains an email record containing at minimum:

- The final manuscript file or a stable link to it
- A clear request: "Do you consent to being listed as co-author on this paper in its submitted form?"
- The collaborator's affirmative reply

These email records are preserved for at least the lifetime of the publication plus 7 years (consistent with standard research-records retention).

### §3.3 Byline ordering

Default byline ordering: corresponding author first, then other Tier 2 co-authors in order of contribution magnitude as judged by the corresponding author and accepted by all named.

Exceptions to default:

- Alphabetical ordering when contributions are roughly equal (common in mathematics)
- Lead-author-last convention when the field uses it (some experimental sciences)
- Negotiated ordering when all parties prefer a specific arrangement

Whatever the order, all named authors must explicitly consent to their position via email before submission.

### §3.4 The corresponding author

The corresponding author handles:

- Final manuscript preparation
- All journal correspondence including submission
- Referee response coordination
- Proof corrections
- Maintaining email consent records
- Communication with co-authors during the publication process

The corresponding author for any given paper is identified before submission and named in the cover letter. Default: Brayden R. Sanders for papers from this lab, but this can be adjusted per-paper by mutual agreement.

## §4. Edge cases and operational rules

### §4.1 Consent revocability

Tier 2 consent is **binding upon submission but revocable before submission**. A consenting co-author can withdraw consent at any point before the paper is submitted to a journal; once submitted, removal requires editor coordination and is treated as a substantive issue.

After publication, a co-author may request withdrawal of authorship via a published correction. This is rare and treated seriously.

### §4.2 Substantive changes after consent

If the manuscript undergoes substantive changes after Tier 2 consent is obtained but before submission, re-consent is required. "Substantive" includes:

- Adding or removing major claims
- Changing the conclusion
- Restructuring sections that change the paper's argument
- Adding or removing other co-authors

Minor edits (typos, formatting, citation corrections, clarifications that don't change meaning) do not require re-consent.

Re-consent is documented the same way as initial consent: explicit email request, explicit email reply.

### §4.3 Referee revisions

Standard practice: revisions in response to referee reports are coordinated with all Tier 2 co-authors but don't always require formal re-consent unless they introduce substantive changes per §4.2. The corresponding author uses judgment; when in doubt, request re-consent.

### §4.4 Disputes among co-authors

If Tier 2 co-authors disagree on content or framing, the disagreement is resolved before submission. The options:

- Negotiate to a version all parties consent to
- One party withdraws from Tier 2 (becomes Tier 1)
- The paper is shelved until resolution

The corresponding author does not submit papers with unresolved co-author disagreement.

### §4.5 Institutional requirements

Some collaborators have institutional rules about how they must be listed (with specific affiliations, with funding acknowledgments, with disclosure statements). These institutional requirements override default formatting in this policy. The corresponding author asks each Tier 2 co-author whether they have institutional requirements and accommodates them.

### §4.6 Funding acknowledgments

Funding sources that supported the work are acknowledged separately from authorship. Funding from a source does not entitle anyone to authorship; authorship requires the three gates of §3.1 regardless of funding.

### §4.7 AI assistance

AI systems that contributed to manuscript development (drafting, computation, analysis, derivation) are acknowledged when their role was material. The acknowledgment names the specific system (e.g., "Claude Opus 4.7", "ClaudeCode") and describes the role.

AI systems are not listed as Tier 2 byline co-authors under current journal conventions, but they are acknowledged at Tier 1 in the manuscript. If journal policy evolves to allow AI co-authorship, this policy will be updated accordingly.

### §4.8 Posthumous co-authorship

If a collaborator who would have been Tier 2 dies before consent can be obtained, they may still be listed at Tier 1 with a note indicating their inspiration was material but consent could not be obtained. If they previously consented to a substantially-similar earlier draft and died before final consent on the submitted version, they may be listed at Tier 2 with a footnote noting their pre-submission death.

### §4.9 Lab-internal conventions

Within this lab specifically (Sanders + Gish + designated successors), there's a standing presumption that joint substrate work has both Sanders and Gish at Tier 2 with email consent on each specific paper. This is the working default for collaborative research within the lab and doesn't require re-explanation per paper.

## §5. Why this policy exists

This policy exists to operationalize the inspiration-economy / credit-economy distinction explained in `INSPIRATION_AS_CURRENCY.md`. Most authorship problems in academia come from one of:

1. Listing someone without their knowledge or consent (institutional misconduct)
2. Failing to acknowledge inspiration (inspiration-economy violation)
3. Confusing acknowledgment with endorsement (boundary collapse between the two)
4. Surprise byline orderings (no prior agreement)
5. Disputes that surface at submission (insufficient pre-submission coordination)

The two-tier system with email consent gate eliminates (1), (3), (4) directly. Tier 1's mandatory inclusion of inspirations addresses (2). Standard pre-submission practice handles (5).

The policy is also consistent with ICMJE/Vancouver Group authorship guidelines for biomedical sciences and analogous norms in mathematics and physics. It tightens the consent requirement (email-documented) and adds the Tier 1 layer (mandatory inspiration acknowledgment), neither of which is universally standard but both of which are widely considered best practice.

## §6. How collaborators interact with this policy

When you collaborate with this lab on a paper:

1. **At the start**: you'll be told whether you're being considered for Tier 1 only, Tier 2, or both, and what that means. You can choose.

2. **During development**: you'll be kept in the loop on Tier 1 acknowledgment language and on Tier 2 manuscript drafts as they develop. Your feedback is welcomed at any stage.

3. **Before submission**: if you're Tier 2, you'll receive the final manuscript and an explicit email request for consent. You can:
   - Consent (and become a byline author)
   - Decline submission consent but remain at Tier 1 (inspiration acknowledgment in the manuscript)
   - Withdraw entirely (no acknowledgment of any kind)

4. **After publication**: you have the right to request errata, corrections, or in extreme cases retraction. The corresponding author coordinates these.

If at any point you're unclear about your status or want to change it, ask the corresponding author. The default is "ask, don't assume."

## §7. The Co-Author Attribution Rule in one paragraph

A name goes on a paper from this lab at one of two tiers. Tier 1 (manuscript acknowledgment) is awarded to anyone whose work inspired the paper, unilaterally and without their consent required, in the form of citation or explicit acknowledgment text. Tier 2 (submission byline) is awarded only to those who scrutinized the final manuscript, provided feedback, and consented via email to publication of the paper in its submitted form. Tier 2 consent is binding upon submission but revocable before. Substantive changes after consent require re-consent. The corresponding author maintains email records of all Tier 2 consents. AI assistance is acknowledged at Tier 1 when material. Institutional requirements of co-authors override default formatting. Disputes are resolved before submission, not in the journal queue.

## §9. Updates to this policy

This policy may be updated as collaborations evolve and as journal authorship norms shift. Updates are versioned (v1.0, v1.1, etc.). The current version applies to papers in development; papers already submitted under an earlier version are governed by the version in effect at submission.
