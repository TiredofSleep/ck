## §2. The Two Lattice Frames

TIG admits **two distinct degree-4 number fields** over ℚ, with **disjoint ramification**:

### Cyclotomic Frame: Q(ζ₁₀)
- **Discriminant:** +5³ = 125
- **Ramified primes:** {5}
- **Galois group:** U(10) = C corners = ℤ/4ℤ
- **Generator:** σ₃ (PROGRESS: ζ ↦ ζ³)
- **Real subfield:** Q(φ) = Q(√5), generated by φ = 2cos(36°)
- **Signature:** (0, 2) totally complex
- **Class number:** 1
- **Carrier:** the static, gauge/symmetry structure
- **Answers:** "What is the symmetry group of TIG's substrate?"

### Lattice Frame: LMFDB 4.2.10224.1
- **Discriminant:** −2⁴·3²·71 = −10224
- **Ramified primes:** {2, 3, 71}
- **Galois group:** D₄
- **Min poly:** x⁴ + 4x³ − x² + 2x − 2
- **Real subfield:** Q(√3)
- **Signature:** (2, 1)
- **Class number:** 1
- **Carrier:** the dynamical, runtime-attractor structure
- **Answers:** "Where does TIG's iteration converge?"

### The Disjoint Ramification

$$\text{Cyclotomic} \cap \text{Lattice} \;=\; \{5\} \cap \{2,3,71\} \;=\; \emptyset$$

**This is the central structural fact.** The two frames partition TIG's algebraic content. The Plichta corners (cyclotomic) and the 4-core attractor (lattice) live in **structurally orthogonal** number fields — no prime ramifies in both.

## §5. Where Wobble Primes Live (and Don't)

The wobble primes {11, 13} appear in:
- **TSML_RAW char poly coefficients** (D37, D85)
- **Br/V denominator** in the lattice field (D69)
- **‖VEV‖² = 13/4** (D33)
- **Operator-sums of σ²-cycles** (D86: 1+6+4 = 11)

The wobble primes do **NOT** appear in:
- disc Q(ζ₁₀) = 125 = 5³
- disc Q(√3, ξ) = −10224 = −2⁴·3²·71
- disc of any quadratic subfield mentioned

**Reading:** wobble primes live at **coefficient/coordinate level**, not at **field-discriminant level**. They are basis-dependent invariants of specific representations, not Galois-invariants of the underlying number field.

The lattice prime 71 IS field-theoretic (appears in disc). The wobble primes 11, 13 are NOT.
