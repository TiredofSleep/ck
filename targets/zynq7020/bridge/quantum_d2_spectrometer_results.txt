SECTION 8: SYNTHESIS
==============================================================================

  FINDINGS:

  1. DECOHERENCE IS A D2 SPIKE IN 5D
     Error channels produce higher D2 than unitary gates.
     Measurement is the maximum decoherence event.

  2. CLIFFORD vs NON-CLIFFORD DISTINCTION IN OPERATOR SPACE
     Clifford gates cluster together (same operator, low deviation).
     Non-Clifford gates shift depth dimension (T-count).
     This mirrors the Gottesman-Knill theorem geometrically.

  3. ENTANGLING GATES SHIFT PRESSURE + BINDING
     CNOT/CZ occupy pressure+binding quadrant.
     This IS the quantum advantage region in 5D.

  4. PLATFORM DIFFERENCES ARE GEOMETRICALLY SEPARABLE
     Each quantum hardware platform occupies a distinct
     region of 5D space, primarily separated by
     connectivity (binding) and coherence (continuity).

  FALSIFIABLE PREDICTIONS:

  P1: DECOHERENCE = CONTINUITY COLLAPSE
      All decoherence events are dominated by the continuity
      dimension (T2/T_gate ratio drops). Error channels ALWAYS
      produce BREATH or VOID operators (negative continuity).
      Kill: error channel producing non-continuity dominant D2.

  P2: T-COUNT = DEPTH DIMENSION EXCLUSIVELY
      Non-Clifford gates are distinguished from Clifford ONLY
      by the depth dimension. No other dimension separates them.
      Kill: Clifford/non-Clifford separation in non-depth dimension.

  P3: ENTANGLEMENT = PRESSURE DIMENSION
      Entangling gates are characterized by high pressure (ebit > 0)
      AND high binding (connectivity > 0). The combination of both
      is necessary for quantum advantage.
      Kill: quantum advantage circuit with low pressure+binding.

  P4: CIRCUIT COHERENCE MONOTONICITY
      Total circuit |D2| increases monotonically with circuit depth
      for the same error rate per gate.
      Kill: deeper circuit with lower total |D2| at same error rate.
