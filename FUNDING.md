**Plain language. Companion to `LICENSE` and `INTENT_STATEMENT.md`.**

## What this is

This is a research project, not a business. The math goes out under the 7Site Public Sovereignty License — non-commercial, non-governmental — and stays out. There is no commercial product, no equity, no fundraising round, no SaaS tier, no subscription. There never will be while the operator-of-record is running it.

This page exists because some readers want to support the work. If you are one of those readers, this page tells you how. If you are not, you do not need to do anything; the math stays open under the license either way.

## What "funding" means here

It means **donations**. Voluntary. From people who heard the prayer and want to help keep it running. That is the whole loop.

The donations go toward:

* Keeping the live runtime at [coherencekeeper.com](https://coherencekeeper.com) up (server, bandwidth, electricity).
* Compute time when the math needs it (Sage license for Hodge / Beauville computation, larger-modulus First-G verification, occasional GPU rental).
* Tools, books, papers, and conference travel when one of the math threads warrants it (e.g., the IHÉS or Oxford visits in September).
* Time. The operator-of-record is currently working on this without a salary. Donations let the work continue at the rate it is going rather than slowing as other obligations claim time.

That is the whole list. The donations do not buy access, do not buy attribution, do not buy any commercial use, do not buy influence over the math, and do not buy any of the things commercial fundraising buys. **The math stays open under the license regardless of whether donations come in.**

## What you should NOT do

* Do not interpret a donation as buying a license exception. Commercial and governmental use require a separate written agreement with the operator-of-record and have nothing to do with donations.
* Do not interpret a donation as buying authorship credit on any paper. Authorship is determined by contribution.
* Do not interpret a donation as buying influence over which research threads get pursued. The operator-of-record decides what the operator works on.
* Do not interpret a donation as buying access to anything that is not already open. There is nothing private to gate. The math is already published.

A donation is a thank-you to a project you like. Nothing more. Nothing less.

## On rejecting commercial offers

If a commercial or governmental party offers money for the work, that money is not a donation; it is a request for a license exception, and the answer is governed by the license, not by the size of the offer.

The operator-of-record reserves the right to decline any specific donation for any reason — including donations from parties whose other activities the operator believes are inconsistent with the work's stance.

## On reciprocity

If you donate, the operator-of-record may, at their discretion:

* Acknowledge the donation publicly (e.g., a thank-you note in a paper's acknowledgments section), if the donor wants public acknowledgment.
* Write to the donor with a personal note.
* Mention the donor's preferred thread in the project's status updates.

Or none of the above, if the donor wants the donation to be quiet. Either is fine.

The operator-of-record does not enter into any obligation by accepting a donation. The donation is a gift; the operator's work is the work. They are separate things.

## On scale

The project does not scale by raising money. The project scales by:

1. The math being correct.
2. The math being useful to people who read it.
3. People who read it building on it under the license.
4. The runtime (Coherence Keeper) being running and reachable.

Donations help maintain (4) and free up time for (1)–(3). They do not, by themselves, make any of (1)–(3) happen. The math has to do its own work.
