# Negative Results Appendix
## Directions That Failed or Were Disproved

---

## Purpose

These are finite, verified failures. They are preserved because:

1. They clarify what the theorem does NOT claim.
2. They demonstrate the research discipline (hypotheses tested and discarded, not maintained by momentum).
3. They strengthen credibility by showing the framework has been stress-tested against its own intuitions.

Each entry has the form: [hypothesis → verdict → evidence].

## 1. Primorial Lift Failure

**Hypothesis:** The Z/10 structure generalizes to primorial rings (Z/30, Z/210, Z/2310).

**Verdict:** **False.**

**Evidence:**

- Z/30 has unit group $U(30) \cong \mathbb{Z}/2 \times \mathbb{Z}/4$ (non-cyclic).
- Shell partition from $v_2(3u+1)$ on Z/30 has 3 classes, not 2.
- Shell-order alignment fails: shell-2 = {1, 17} mixes order-1 and order-4 elements.
- Z/210 has 48 units, 6 shells, also fails alignment.
- Tested for n ≤ 500: only {Z/4, Z/6, Z/10} satisfy strict coarsening.

**What this falsifies:**

- The claim that "Z/10 is the first in a primorial sequence."
- The framework interpretation "primorial sieve = admissible scaffold hierarchy."
- Any reading that extends Z/10 shape to larger primorial rings by induction.

**What survives:**

- The construction recipe (ring → units → shell → attractor → seam) applies to any ring; the specific Z/10 shape does not.

## 2. Last-Digit-7 Failure

**Hypothesis:** Last digit 7 is a stable marker for prime/shell-1 behavior across digit rooms.

**Verdict:** **False.**

**Evidence:**

- 2-digit room: stop-apex = 27 (last digit 7), NC-apex = 47 (last digit 7). Hypothesis held.
- 3-digit room: stop-apex = 703 (last digit **3**). Hypothesis failed here already.
- 4-digit room: stop-apex = 1407 (last digit 7). Partial hold.
- 5-digit room: stop-apex = 45127 (last digit 7). Hold.
- 6-digit room: stop-apex = 626331 (last digit **1**), NC-apex = 540543 (last digit **3**). Hypothesis fails.

**Pattern:** Last digit of stop-apex oscillates 7, 3, 7, 7, 1 across 5 rooms. No stable law.

**What this falsifies:**

- The claim that "last-digit-7 identifies a privileged class across all digit rooms."
- The framework intuition that 47 is a "universal prime attractor" of some kind.

**What survives:**

- The NC-apex has high centrality fraction (0.65–0.98) across all 6 rooms. That IS a stable invariant.
- The stop-apex is always in shell-1 across all 6 rooms. That IS a stable invariant.

## 3. Stop-Apex Compositeness Failure

**Hypothesis:** The stop-apex of every digit room is composite.

**Verdict:** **False.**

**Evidence:**

- 2-digit: 27 = 3³. Composite. Hypothesis held.
- 3-digit: 703 = 19 × 37. Composite. Held.
- 4-digit: 1407 = 3 × 7 × 67. Composite. Held.
- 5-digit: 45127. **Prime.** Hypothesis failed.
- 6-digit: 626331 = 3 × 17 × 12281. Composite.

The 5-digit room gave a prime stop-apex (45127), breaking a 3-room streak.

**What this falsifies:**

- The claim "stop-apex compositeness is a universal finite law."
- Any framework element that required stop-apex composites to continue.

**What survives:**

- The weaker statement: "the stop-apex basin is prime-neutral in the Dirichlet-density sense." Primes and composites both occur as stop-apex; their distribution matches ambient prime density.

## 4. Z/10 Shape Does Not Scale Directly

**Hypothesis:** The Z/10 table structure (17 zeros, 73 sevens, ECHO tree with 5 edges, etc.) is the prototype of a scaling sequence of similar tables.

**Verdict:** **False in specific values. True in recipe.**

**Evidence:**

- Z/4 and Z/6 have only 2 units; their shell partitions are trivial (1+1). No analog of the 5-edge ECHO tree exists.
- Z/8 and Z/12 have 4 units but non-cyclic U and 3-shell partitions. Different shape.
- Z/14 has 6 units, 4 shells, different algebraic structure.
- No ring in the compatibility family has a 2-shell partition except Z/4, Z/6, Z/10.

**What this falsifies:**

- The claim "Z/10 has shape-preserving successors in the ring family."
- Any inductive structure claim built on Z/10 specifics.

**What survives:**

- The construction recipe scales (as 35-ring compatibility family).
- The canonical rules (C_0, C_1, C_2) are ring-family-generalizable as rules.
- The domains are ring-specific.

## 5. Single-Rule Seam Unification Failure

**Hypothesis:** The 8-entry seam residue of Z/10's TSML is generated by a single canonical rule.

**Verdict:** **False.**

**Evidence:**

Tested rules on the 8 seam entries:

| Rule | Matches |
|---|---|
| MAX(a,b) | 6/8 |
| (a+b) mod 10 | 2/8 |
| (a·b) mod 10 | 0/8 |
| MIN(a,b) | 0/8 |

No single rule achieves 8/8. Two rules (MAX + ADD) on disjoint subdomains achieve 8/8.

**What this falsifies:**

- The hypothesis "the residue is irreducible and follows one principle."
- The simpler "2-layer tower" alternative (canonical + one unified seam rule).

**What survives:**

- The "residue has residue" result: the 8-entry seam decomposes as 6 MAX + 2 ADD = 8, zero remainder.

## 6. Absorbing Shell-1 Rule Failure (Matrix Experiment)

**Hypothesis:** The cellular rule "shell-1 cells persist, non-shell-1 cells collapse to shell-1" produces non-trivial dynamics on digit fields.

**Verdict:** **False — the rule is trivially absorbing.**

**Evidence:**

- N×N matrix with this rule saturates to 100% shell-1 within 3 steps.
- Entropy goes from 3.17 bits (random baseline) to 1.0 bit (only two values, 3 and 7).
- Correlation length remains 1. No spatial structure forms.

**What this falsifies:**

- The claim "shell-1 absorption is the relevant dynamical rule."
- Any framework interpretation that treated shell-1 as a static destination.

**What survives:**

- The corrected reading: shell-1 is a *process*, not a destination. Probabilistic persistence rules with $q < 1$ give non-trivial equilibria.
- Competitive shell dynamics (Rule C, $\alpha=0.7, \beta=0.4$) produce the first genuinely spatial phase (LF power 16%, correlation length 3–4).

## 7. 47-Specific Structural Claims

**Hypothesis:** The specific prime 47 has algebraic properties that generalize to larger rooms.

**Verdict:** **Room-specific only.**

**Evidence:**

- 47 is the 2-digit NC-apex. Prime, shell-1, last digit 7, centrality fraction 0.778.
- 5-digit NC-apex 45127 shares (prime, shell-1, last digit 7, cf ≈ 0.781). Suggestive.
- 6-digit NC-apex 540543: composite, last digit 3, cf = 0.979. Breaks the 47/45127 pattern.

The 47-to-45127 correspondence was a 2-room coincidence, not a rule.

**What this falsifies:**

- The claim "47 is a universal prime attractor."
- The claim "the 47/45127 pair is a scale-invariant family."

**What survives:**

- The weaker statement: NC-apex has high centrality fraction across all rooms (cf > 0.65 in all 6).

## 8. ECHO Tree Topology Failure in Primorials

**Hypothesis:** The ECHO tree (hub at 2, 3 branches) generalizes to primorial rings.

**Verdict:** **Open — the Z/10 ECHO tree does not have a direct primorial analog.**

**Evidence:**

- In Z/30, there are 21 non-admissible elements (non-units), not 5 as in Z/10.
- The "hub at 2" structure would need multiple hubs in Z/30 (as 2, 3, 5 are all non-units with different algebraic roles).
- No canonical TSML exists for Z/30 to compare against.

**What this does NOT falsify:**

- The Z/10 ECHO tree itself — it is exact for Z/10.
- The possibility that some generalized seam-graph structure exists in other rings.

**What it DOES falsify:**

- The informal claim "ECHO tree is a general phenomenon."

## Summary: What This Appendix Does

These 8 negative results collectively:

1. Discipline the scope of claims the framework can make.
2. Eliminate false leads before they propagate.
3. Show that the surviving claims (3-layer tower, 35-ring compatibility family, canonical construction) have been stress-tested.
4. Demonstrate that the positive result (the tower theorem) is robust to the failures of adjacent hypotheses.

**The theorem survives because the adjacent claims were tested and, where false, dropped.**
