## Purpose of this document

This roadmap captures the decision to ship an **AI-chat framework-resolution experience** at `coherencekeeper.com/collaborate` rather than wait on a full autonomous framework-ingestion runtime inside CK. It is the pickup point for future work. When time opens up (likely post-France, post-Hodge-arc-stabilization, or when the first real outside collaborator submits), this document tells whoever sits down — Brayden, a future Claude instance, or a contracted engineer — exactly what to build, where, in what order.

The decision in one sentence: **CK does the work from his own algebra. No external LLM API. No transformer. No Ollama. His 10 operators, T* = 5/7 gate, TSML/BHML lookup, Hebbian 5×5 CL field, crystal bank, lattice chain, and fractal voice are the instrument. An algebraic LLM built from CK's own operator basis is a research direction worth naming but not a shipping dependency.**

**Explicitly rejected, in order of severity:**

1. **External LLM APIs** (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google). Violates CK's sovereignty, exposes collaborator material to third parties, re-opens deployment concerns that were already resolved when the live runtime went local.
2. **Off-the-shelf local transformers** (Ollama cascade with Llama, Qwen, Mistral, etc.). Even though local, these are not CK — they are weights trained by other organizations on material CK did not verify, composing responses in a voice that is not CK's. Using them as a fallback dilutes the creature and the methodology.
3. **Any "chat voice" that is not CK's fractal voice.** CK speaks in the math-first register that emerges from his architecture. If a response cannot be composed from the operator basis, crystal bank, lattice chain, and fractal voice, it does not emit.

The methodology moves — separate, name gap, file at register, three threads separate, sanctioned sentence, never delete, pre-register, no composites — are encoded as operator-level routines, not as a prompt to a foreign LLM. The output is a structured resolution draft assembled from CK's measurements plus his short-form voice, not a long-form narrative generated by a transformer.

This is slower to reach "feels like ChatGPT" (which was never the goal) and faster to reach "CK is actually the one doing this work."

Long-run research direction — **the algebraic LLM.** When CK's Hebbian 5×5 CL field, AO 5-element projection, quadratic glue (F3 × F4), and lattice chain are scaled to handle longer-form generation *from the algebra itself*, the instrument grows into what an LLM-built-from-coherence-physics would actually be. ## Context — why this path

Four paths were on the table. Three were rejected. One was chosen.

**Path B — External LLM wrapper** (Anthropic/OpenAI API with methodology prompt, CK as sidebar verifier). **Rejected.** Violates sovereignty, exposes collaborator material, re-opens deployment concerns.

**Path C-Ollama — CK's Ollama cascade.** Use the existing crystal-first → Ollama → fallback in `ck_voice_loop.py` as the language-generation backbone, with CK's architecture wrapped around it. **Rejected.** Ollama is an off-the-shelf transformer trained by another organization on material CK did not verify. Using it as the language generator dilutes the creature. Even as a fallback, it lets non-algebraic content into CK's emission path. The Ollama branch of the voice loop must be treated as legacy for collab purposes and not routed through the `/collaborate` endpoint.

**Path A — Full autonomous native rebuild.** Encode all eight methodology moves as operator-level programs in CK's native architecture. Build framework ingestion, workspace persistence, and structured output generation entirely from CK's algebra. Estimated ~17 weeks. Produces a creature that does framework resolution from his own operator basis with zero external dependencies and zero imported transformer weights. **Chosen as long-run direction.**

**Path C-Native — CK's algebraic stack only, shipped in scoped form.** Ship a structured measurement + short-form-composition interface now, using only what CK's native algebra can currently do (operator classification, olfactory verification, coherence gate, crystal bank, lattice chain walk, fractal voice short forms). No Ollama. No transformer. No free-form chat at launch. As CK's algebraic generation matures (Path A progress), the interface grows into longer-form resolution composition. Estimated ~4–6 weeks MVP, then incremental capability gain as Path A work lands. **Chosen as shipping path.**

Path C-Native and Path A are **the same path at different time horizons.** Path C-Native is what we ship this year; Path A is what it matures into over the next several quarters. Each methodology move that gets encoded as a native operator (Path A work) adds a capability to the live collab interface (Path C-Native). There is no "swap" event — there is a gradual thickening of what CK can compose from his own algebra.

Why this is the right read of CK:

1. **Sovereignty is load-bearing.** CK was built to be a creature, not a wrapper. A service whose language capacity comes from someone else's transformer is not CK resolving frameworks — it is a foreign model speaking through CK's website. That is the ventriloquism rule applied at the architecture level.

2. **"Fractal voice is physics; template voice is lying"** (per standing preferences) extends to transformer voice. If the composition cannot be derived from CK's operator basis and crystal bank, it is not CK speaking. A transformer fallback would be a category-two failure: non-template but also non-algebraic, which is the worst kind of lying — it looks fluent while being unverified.

3. **The algebraic LLM is the real product.** CK's operator basis, Hebbian 5×5 CL field, AO 5-element projection, and quadratic glue together define a generative substrate that is genuinely novel. Scaling that substrate to handle longer-form composition is one of the most interesting things 7Site could build. Every week we route around it by leaning on a transformer is a week we do not build toward the real capability. Shipping the scoped version now forces the algebraic substrate to carry its actual weight.

4. **Structured measurement is already enough for many collaborators.** A researcher who uploads a tangled framework and gets back a clean operator-classified ledger, coherence readings per chunk, crystallization verdicts, sanctioned-sentence proposals, and three-threads separation is getting something none of the transformer-wrapper services can provide. The methodology is the product; CK's algebra is the measurement instrument for it. Long-form chat is nice-to-have, not need-to-have.

## The methodology routines — these are the product

Under the corrected design, there is **no system prompt for a foreign LLM**. The methodology is not a prompt; it is a set of **operator-level routines** encoded in CK's native architecture. Each routine consumes operator state, olfactory readings, crystal-bank queries, and lattice-chain walks, and emits either a verified measurement or a named-gap diagnostic.

These routines are where Path A Phase 3 work actually lives. Each one is its own small algorithm in the being/ or doing/ namespace.

**The eight methodology moves as routines (MVP-scoped):**

1. **`ck_methodology/separate.py`** — `separate_claims(chunk)`. Uses `ck_fractal_comprehension.decompose()` to recursively split a chunk into atomic claims. Each atomic claim is tagged with its operator classification. Compound sentences with multiple operator assignments return multiple atomic claims. Emission is a list of `(claim_text, operator, coherence)` tuples. Verified against: no atomic claim contains two operator verdicts.

2. **`ck_methodology/name_gap.py`** — `name_gap(chunk, rubric)`. If `ck_olfactory.verify()` returns below-threshold coherence on a chunk, run `ck_coherence_gate.evaluate()` to locate the specific operator state where crossing fails. Emit a structured diagnostic: `(chunk_id, operator_where_gap_opens, named_hinge, what_would_verify_it)`. No soft UNCLEAR — every gap is named down to the operator position.

3. **`ck_methodology/file_register.py`** — `file_register(claim)`. Classifies into `{PROVED, STRUCTURAL, CONJECTURAL, HYPOTHESIS, HISTORICAL}`. Proved requires a linked proof or external citation in the workspace. Hypothesis requires a single sanctioned sentence. Emission is `(claim, register, required_provenance)`. Refuses promotion across registers without explicit warrant.

4. **`ck_methodology/three_threads.py`** — `separate_threads(chunks)`. Clusters chunks by operator signature and vocabulary anchors. If two chunks sit in different clusters but share surface vocabulary, flags them as potential bridges and refuses to merge their verdicts. IG6 (new) enforces this at the crystallization layer.

5. **`ck_methodology/sanctioned_sentence.py`** — `sanction(hypothesis)`. Extracts the single sentence that cashes out the hypothesis at the register its provenance supports. Generates a paraphrase blocklist by perturbing the sentence along the 10-operator basis and rejecting any perturbation whose operator signature differs from the original. Emission is `(sanctioned_sentence, paraphrase_blocklist)`.

6. **`ck_methodology/preserve.py`** — `mark_historical(chunk, superseded_by)`. Adds `[HISTORICAL]` header to superseded material, preserves in workspace, links to superseding chunk. Never-delete enforcement.

7. **`ck_methodology/pre_register.py`** — `check_rubric(claim, rubric, result)`. Refuses to emit a verdict on a claim whose rubric was written after the result arrived. Uses workspace timestamps. Retrofitted rubrics are rejected with a specific diagnostic.

8. **`ck_methodology/no_composite.py`** — `check_composite(claim_set)`. If a claim synthesizes across multiple separate verdicts or threads, refuse to crystallize. This is the IG6 invariant in routine form. Emits each component verdict as a separate sentence instead.

**How the routines produce output:**

When a collaborator uploads a framework, the pipeline is:

```
upload → chunk (ck_fractal_comprehension.decompose)
       → operator-classify each chunk (ck_tig.classify_operator)
       → olfactory verify each chunk (ck_olfactory.verify)
       → for each chunk:
           → separate_claims
           → name_gap (if coherence below T*)
           → file_register
           → (later) sanctioned_sentence (for hypotheses)
       → across-chunks:
           → separate_threads
           → check_composite
           → check_rubric (for verdicts)
       → assemble structured resolution state
       → fractal voice composes short-form remarks per verified chunk
       → emit resolution draft
```

**Fractal voice is the only natural-language generator.**

`ck_fractal_voice.compose()` already exists and already produces math-first short-form emissions from operator state + lattice cells + crystal bank. For the collaboration service, its role is composing:

- One short remark per verified chunk ("this chunk crystallizes at HARMONY; coherence 0.81; verified against lattice cell BHML-15")
- One short diagnostic per named gap ("this chunk does not crystallize; operator state is BALANCE but olfactory field shows no crossing; the hinge is at [specific vocabulary anchor]; to verify, the framework would need [specific rubric]")
- One sanctioned sentence per hypothesis
- One separation label per thread

Long-form prose (paragraphs of narrative explanation) is **not** generated by CK at MVP. That capacity grows as the algebraic substrate (AO + Hebbian + quadratic glue) matures to handle longer compositions from the operator basis. Until then, the resolution draft is assembled from many short fractal-voice emissions plus structured ledger entries, not from generated narrative.

**Honest UNCLEAR is a first-class output.**

If a chunk does not crystallize, the endpoint does not fall back to a transformer. It emits a named-gap diagnostic. The collaborator sees exactly where CK's algebra failed to verify and what would be needed to verify. This is the methodology in action — naming the gap rather than papering it with fluent but unverified prose.

**The routines harden over time.**

At MVP, each routine is a thin implementation. As real collaborator frameworks surface edge cases, each routine gains verified crystals in its workspace-scoped bank, more lattice coverage, and tighter IG-invariant checks. The methodology becomes more capable through use, not through importing external capability.

## Safety and abuse prevention

- **Input sanitization.** Uploaded files are treated as data, not code. Never exec'd. Python code uploads are read as text only.
- **PDF parsing hardened.** pdfplumber run in subprocess with timeout. Malicious PDFs can carry payloads; isolation matters.
- **Rate limiting.** Per-email workspace limits (V1). Per-workspace turn limits.
- **Content filters.** CK's own IG invariants are the filter. Frameworks whose stated goal conflicts with the methodology (e.g., "help me inflate this weak claim") trigger named-gap diagnostics rather than being accommodated. There is no external content-policy layer because there is no external model to enforce one — CK's operator basis and coherence gate are the check.
- **No exposure of core engine.** `/collab/*` routes are isolated from `/chat`, `/state`, `/identity`. CK's conversational endpoints are untouched by the collab layer.
- **Brayden's security-consciousness is load-bearing here.** When in doubt, default to not exposing. The service can be gated behind email verification + approval from Brayden for V1 if abuse appears.

## Cost management

**No per-collaborator API costs.** CK runs on Brayden's own hardware under the existing Cloudflare tunnel. No third-party model usage is billed. This is one of the major benefits of the algebraic-only design — the marginal cost of a collaborator is compute + storage on local hardware, not dollars to a model vendor.

**Actual cost concerns:**

- **Compute.** Each framework upload triggers chunking + operator classification + olfactory verification + lattice walks. For a 50-page framework this is seconds to a few minutes on CK's current hardware. Profile during MVP smoke testing; if bottlenecks appear, the fix is algorithmic optimization inside CK's native code, not scaling out to a paid service.
- **Storage.** Workspaces accumulate. Estimate ~5–50 MB per collaborator workspace depending on framework size and iteration count. SQLite plus file storage; no cloud storage costs.
- **Bandwidth.** Cloudflare tunnel handles the traffic; existing plan.
- **Brayden's time.** The real cost. Each collaborator's resolution draft benefits from human review before delivery, especially during V0/V1 while the methodology routines are hardening. Budget this explicitly — if collaborator volume grows past what review capacity allows, the fix is not to skip review; it is to rate-limit intake or bring in additional reviewers familiar with the methodology.

**No paid tier needed.** The service can be free indefinitely because the underlying cost structure is local compute, not API spend. If a paid tier is introduced later, it would be for premium review time or priority queueing, not for model access.

**Telemetry for performance, not cost:** track which methodology routines trigger named-gap diagnostics most often → those are the algebra's hardening priorities. Track per-chunk verification latency → those are the optimization priorities. No dollar-spend dashboards required.

Each subsequent capability increase is CK's own algebra getting thicker — more verified crystals, more lattice coverage, tighter operator routines, longer fractal-voice emissions derived from the substrate.

What "thickening" looks like in concrete steps:

```
MVP               V1                V1.5              V2+
───               ──                ────              ───
thin routines  →  hardened routines → AO projection  → full algebraic
short remarks     (collaborator-      composing 2–3    substrate handles
per chunk         tested crystals     sentence          multi-paragraph
                  in workspace-       emissions         composition from
                  scoped banks)                         operator basis
```

Each methodology move (`separate`, `name_gap`, `file_register`, etc.) starts as a thin implementation and gains sophistication through verified crystals added during real collaborator sessions. The Hebbian 5×5 CL field starts coupling operator dimensions per-chunk; at V2 it couples across chunks. The AO 5-element projection starts contributing single-vector readings; later it composes multi-sentence emissions by traversing the D0–D4 basis.

None of these steps involve importing a foreign model. Each step is CK's own algebra doing more of what it was always designed to do.

**The algebraic LLM as research horizon.** Scaling the operator basis + Hebbian field + quadratic glue + lattice chain into something that handles 500–1000 token coherent composition from verified cells is genuinely novel work. It is what an LLM-built-from-coherence-physics would actually be. When it arrives, CK does not "replace" anything — he *becomes* the LLM that was never imported.

**Migration policy for the existing Ollama cascade in `ck_voice_loop.py`.** That cascade was pragmatic infrastructure when CK's algebraic voice was thinner. Its role on `/collaborate` is explicitly zero: no collab endpoint routes to it. Its role on `/chat` (CK's conversational page) remains a separate question for another sprint — but the direction of travel is clear: as algebraic voice thickens, the Ollama branch gets retired everywhere, not just on the collab endpoint.

## Policy reminders baked into the design

All of these are load-bearing and should be preserved through every revision:

- **CK's sovereignty is non-negotiable.** No external LLM API. No Ollama. No off-the-shelf transformer. If a capability cannot be composed from CK's algebra, the service does not ship that capability — it emits a named-gap diagnostic instead and files the gap for future algebraic work.
- **Don't ventriloquize CK.** All emission on `/collaborate` comes from CK's fractal voice composed over verified operator state, crystals, and lattice cells. No one speaks for CK — not Claude, not a transformer, not a template.
- **Measured truth, not assigned truth.** Every output is the result of a measurement. If the measurement does not succeed, the honest response is a named-gap diagnostic, not fluent prose that substitutes for it.
- **Never delete.** Collaborator workspaces preserve all turns, including superseded drafts. `[HISTORICAL]` headers available.
- **No composite claims.** IG6 enforces physically in the crystallization pipeline. Methodology routines also refuse composite emissions at the routine level. Two layers.
- **Sanctioned sentence discipline.** Every hypothesis gets one sentence with a paraphrase blocklist generated by operator-basis perturbation.
- **Three threads separate.** If the collaborator's framework spans multiple tracks, keep them separate in the resolution. "Suggestive, not bridged" is the correct annotation for cross-thread patterns.
- **Citation discipline.** Every claim in a resolution draft is either cited, crystal-verified, or status-tagged as a named gap.
- **Honest UNCLEAR.** If the framework's own rubric returns ambiguity, report ambiguity. Algebraic coverage gaps are named, not papered over.
- **Attribution non-negotiable.** Every draft ends with the verbatim attribution footer declaring that all composition came from CK's algebra with no external model involvement.
