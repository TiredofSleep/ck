## 1. Route R1b — non-K-equivariant twist-differences

### 1.1 Theorem (R1b-rank-2)

For every rank-2 vector bundle E on A_* of the form
$0 \to L \to E \to L' \to 0$ with $L, L'$ line bundles, the
cohomology class $c_2(E) \in H^4(A_*, \mathbb{Q})$ equals
$c_1(L) \cdot c_1(L')$ and therefore lies in the subring of
$H^4(A_*, \mathbb{Q})$ generated by $H^2(A_*, \mathbb{Q})$. Its
projection onto any block $B_k \subset W_*$ is zero.

### 1.2 Numerical finding (with surprise)

`dim (K-invariant ∩ H^{1,1}(A_*, R)) = 8`
`dim (K-anti-invariant ∩ H^{1,1}(A_*, R)) = 8`

Both are 8-dimensional over **R**. So K-anti-invariant (1,1)-classes
exist over R. The test builds rank-2 extensions
$E: c_2(E) = c_1(L_{\text{Kinv}}) \cdot c_1(L'_{\text{Kanti}})$ —
these satisfy $\varphi^* c_2 = -c_2$ (numerically verified:
K-inv residual = 2‖c₂‖ = antipodal) yet **‖proj_{B₁}(c₂)‖_Q < 10⁻¹⁴**.

### 1.3 Why the closure holds despite K-anti-invariant c₂ existing

Because these K-anti-invariant c₂ classes are **non-primitive**. They
sit in $L \cdot H^2(A_*, \mathbb{R})$, the Lefschetz image
(dim 28). Primitivity is measured by $L \wedge \cdot = 0$ in $H^6$;
the L-image is tautologically *not* primitive. $W_*$ is the
primitive K-anti-invariant (2,2)-part, Q-orthogonal to the Lefschetz
image. So:

$$c_1(L_{\text{Kinv}}) \cdot c_1(L'_{\text{Kanti}}) \in L \cdot H^{1,1}(A_*, \mathbb{R}) \cap K\text{-anti-inv}  \perp_Q  W_*.$$

This refines the R1b claim to:

> Every algebraic c_2 expressible as a line-bundle wedge product on A_*
> lies in the **Lefschetz image** of $H^2$, hence is Q-orthogonal to
> $W_*$. This is a feature of the subring-generated-by-NS structure,
> not of K-invariance alone.

### 1.4 What remains open in R1b (rank ≥ 3 or exotic moduli)

For stable rank ≥ 2 bundles with non-trivial Chow moduli — bundles
whose Chow classes are **not** expressible as polynomials in NS
classes — the twist-difference construction could in principle produce
non-NS-subring c₂. Whether any such bundle exists on A_* is exactly
the **Beauville conjecture for codim-2 classes on abelian 4-folds**,
still open and equivalent to the Hodge conjecture in this setting.

## 2. Route R2 — Poincaré / Fourier–Mukai symmetry

### 2.1 Theorem (R2-FM)

Let $A_*^\vee$ be the dual abelian 4-fold, carrying the dual
$\mathbb{Q}(i)$-action $\varphi^\vee = -\varphi^T$. The Poincaré line
bundle $P \to A_* \times A_*^\vee$ has first Chern class

$$[P] = \sum_{k=1}^8 \tilde e_k \wedge \tilde e_k^\vee \in H^2(A_* \times A_*^\vee, \mathbb{Z})$$

which is $(\varphi \times \varphi^\vee)$-invariant:

$$(\varphi \times \varphi^\vee)^*[P] = [P].$$

Consequently the Fourier–Mukai transform
$\text{FM}_P: H^*(A_*^\vee, \mathbb{Q}) \xrightarrow{\sim} H^*(A_*, \mathbb{Q})$
is $K$-equivariant: it intertwines $\varphi^{\vee*}$ with $\varphi^*$
and therefore preserves $K$-isotypic decompositions.

### 2.2 Consequence

The Hodge problem on $A_*$ is FM-equivalent to the Hodge problem on
$A_*^\vee$. Since $A_* \sim_{\mathbb{Q}(i)} A_*^\vee$ (both simple,
both End⁰ = ℚ(i), both with identical 8-dim W_*), R2 does **not**
introduce new algebraic constructions — it is a symmetry that sends
the problem to an equivalent one.

### 2.3 Numerical verification

The script builds $\varphi \oplus \varphi^\vee$ on
$H^1(A_*) \oplus H^1(A_*^\vee) = \mathbb{R}^{16}$, computes the
induced action on $\Lambda^2 \mathbb{R}^{16}$ (120-dim), applies it to
the Poincaré class $[P]$, and obtains
$\|(\varphi \times \varphi^\vee)^*[P] - [P]\| = 0$ **exactly**
(integer arithmetic through signed permutations). The sign convention
$\varphi^\vee = -\varphi^T$ is forced by the duality pairing
$\langle \varphi x, \varphi^\vee y \rangle = \langle x, y \rangle$
combined with $\varphi^2 = -I$.

### 2.4 What R2 rules out

Any algebraic class on $A_*$ obtained as $\text{FM}_P(\alpha)$ for
$\alpha$ algebraic on $A_*^\vee$ inherits $\alpha$'s K-isotypic type.
So FM cannot produce a K-anti-invariant class on $A_*$ starting from
a K-invariant class on $A_*^\vee$ — it can only transport a
(hypothetical) K-anti-invariant algebraic class from one side to the
other. Since the Hodge problem on $A_*^\vee$ is structurally
identical, this is not a new source of cycles.

## 3. Route R3 — absolutely Hodge / CM-type

### 3.1 Theorem (R3-ABSHODGE) [Deligne 1982, unconditional]

Every rational Hodge class on an abelian variety over $\mathbb{C}$ is
absolutely Hodge. In particular, every class in $W_*(A_*, \mathbb{Q})$
is absolutely Hodge: it is fixed by all automorphisms of
$\mathbb{C}/\overline{\mathbb{Q}}$ and matches under comparison
isomorphisms with étale cohomology.

This rigidifies the motivic content of W_* but does **not** prove
algebraicity.

### 3.2 Theorem (R3-CMGAP)

$A_*$ is **not** of CM type:

$$\dim_{\mathbb{Q}} \operatorname{End}^0(A_*) = 2  \;<\;  8 = 2 \cdot \dim_{\mathbb{C}} A_*.$$

Therefore the Moonen–Zarhin / Deligne–André–Milne Hodge conjecture
closures for CM abelian varieties do **not** apply to A_*.

### 3.3 Numerical confirmation

The Sprint 2 simplicity audit is reproduced: the joint real commutant
of $J_\Omega$ evaluated at six independent rational parameter points
$(u,v,w)$ of $Y(u,v,w) = u\, I + v\, M_2 + w\, M_3$ has
$\dim_{\mathbb{R}} = 4$, hence $\dim_{\mathbb{Q}} \operatorname{End}^0 = 2$.
The CM threshold is 8. **Gap = 6.**

### 3.4 What R3 rules out

Every motivic / Hodge-theoretic closure that proves Hodge for a class
of abelian varieties by CM arguments alone. What it does **not** rule
out: specific absolute-Hodge-preserving constructions that descend to
algebraicity on $A_*$ via non-CM techniques (Kuga–Satake,
André motives — open for the present variety).

## 5. Residual frontier

After the ladder the Hodge conjecture on A_* reduces to:

> **Does there exist an algebraic codim-2 class on A_* that is NOT in
> the subring of H*(A_*, ℚ) generated by NS(A_*) and NOT in the FM
> image of the same subring on A_*^∨ ?**

Equivalently: can the 8-dimensional K-anti-invariant primitive (2,2)
space $W_*$ be hit by an algebraic cycle that is genuinely exotic —
not expressible via line-bundle data on A_* or its dual?

By the Beauville-Franchetta-type conjecture for abelian varieties,
such exotic classes are not expected on simple 4-folds with small
endomorphism algebra. But this is exactly the Hodge conjecture itself
in this setting — **still open**.

## 6. Strongest honest claim

After Sprints 29 + 30, the bundle-theoretic, correspondence-theoretic,
and motivic routes to an algebraic cycle in $W_*(A_*)$ via
constructions accessible from $(A_*, \varphi)$ alone are all closed
numerically and structurally. Three distinct theorem families
(naturality of Chern classes, K-equivariance of the Poincaré kernel,
CM-type dimension gap) each rule out an infinite sub-route with a
single argument. The Hodge conjecture on $A_*$ is now equivalent to
the existence of a genuinely exotic algebraic codim-2 Chow class that
is not expressible via any line-bundle-based or FM-based construction
— a residual that is as narrow as the current algebraic toolkit
allows us to make it.

## 7. Strongest honest boundary

What this sprint does **not** establish:

1. The non-existence of exotic stable rank $\geq 3$ bundles on $A_*$
   whose Chow classes escape the NS subring. This is the Beauville
   conjecture and is open.

2. The non-existence of algebraic correspondences between $A_*$ and
   abelian varieties $B \neq A_*^\vee$ that produce non-FM-accessible
   classes on $A_*$. Section R2 handles the Poincaré case
   specifically; general correspondences require $B$-dependent
   analysis.

3. The algebraicity of any specific class in $W_*$. Deligne's theorem
   gives absolute Hodge, not algebraic.

4. That $A_*$ is a *minimal* object for the residual frontier.
   Different choices of $M_2, M_3$ might yield varieties where the
   exotic stratum is accessible / inaccessible by distinct means.

## Appendix — Why R1b's surprise is actually confirmation

The numerical finding $\dim(K\text{-anti} \cap H^{1,1}(A_*, \mathbb{R})) = 8$
looked at first like a potential counter-example to
$NS(A_*) = NS(A_*)^K$. It is not:

* 8 = dim over $\mathbb{R}$, not over $\mathbb{Q}$.
* Over $\mathbb{Q}$, the K-anti-invariant (1,1) classes in
  $H^2(A_*, \mathbb{Q})$ that survive to $NS(A_*)_{\mathbb{Q}}$
  must be images of divisors, and divisors on a simple abelian
  variety with End⁰ = Q(i) are all $\varphi$-fixed.
* The "extra" K-anti-invariant (1,1) classes over $\mathbb{R}$ live in
  $H^{1,1}(A_*, \mathbb{R}) \setminus NS(A_*)_{\mathbb{R}}$ — they are
  topologically non-trivial Hodge classes that happen to be
  non-algebraic.
* Wedging any such class with the polarization $L$ lands in $L \cdot H^2$,
  which is Q-orthogonal to W_*. This is what the numerics show.

So the 8-dim K-anti over R is a **Hodge-theoretic echo** of the 8-dim
K-anti-invariant $W_*$ in codim 2 — the same irreducible
$\mathbb{Q}(i)$-representation appearing in degree 2 as a predecessor
of the degree-4 obstruction. The primitivity cut in codim 2 removes
the Lefschetz copy and leaves $W_*$. The R1b test is numerically
consistent with this: K-anti (1,1) × K-inv (1,1) = K-anti (2,2) that
lives in $L \cdot H^2$, orthogonal to $W_*$.
