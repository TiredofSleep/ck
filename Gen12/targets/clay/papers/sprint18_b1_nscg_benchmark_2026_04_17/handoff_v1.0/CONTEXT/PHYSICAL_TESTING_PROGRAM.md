# Physical Testing Program
## TSML/BHML as a Finite Instrument — Not a Theory

---

## Headline Discipline

**TSML/BHML is a finite instrument for encoding, classifying, and comparing transition systems.** Whether it captures physics is an empirical question, answered by testing the instrument against measured data and against alternative instruments.

The instrument framing:

- A **specific encoding** (Φ: measurement space → {0,...,9})
- A **specific pair of operators** (TSML and BHML on Z/10Z)
- A **specific decomposition** (3-layer tower for TSML)
- A **specific comparison algorithm** (does (T, B) predict observed transitions better than a random kernel of the same rank?)

Everything physical-facing is framed as:

- Encoding → prediction → comparison → falsification.

No ontology in the testing path. Interpretation lives at Stage 4, behind three stages of empirical validation.

---

## Level A — Purely Mathematical Use

What TSML/BHML can already do as a finite instrument, without any physical claim:

**A1. State classification.** Given a finite state alphabet of size 10, every state has:
- Admissibility class (in U(R) or not)
- Shell class (via v₂(3u+1))
- Role (attractor, absorber, identity, seam member)

**A2. Transition compression.** Given transition data on the 10-state carrier, one can:
- Fit the canonical construction C₀ and measure recovery fraction.
- Identify the seam residue (mismatches with C₀).
- Attempt tower decomposition of the residue.
- Report compressed description length.

**A3. Basin/seam detection.** The instrument distinguishes:
- Transitions that collapse to an attractor (basin behavior).
- Transitions that preserve or enrich structure (seam behavior).
- Transitions that are invertible (transport behavior — if BHML-analogue fits).

**A4. Paired comparison.** On the same carrier, one can ask:
- Does a collapse operator (TSML-like) fit the data?
- Does a transport operator (BHML-like) fit the data?
- Do both fit on complementary substructures?
- Does the pair (T, B) carry more predictive information than either alone?

**A5. Baseline comparison.** Compare TSML/BHML fits against:
- Random kernels of the same carrier size.
- Permutation-based null hypotheses.
- Pure multiplicative or additive structures.

Level A is already productive with zero physical input.

## Level B — Physical Analog Models (Toy Systems)

What toy systems could be encoded and tested against TSML/BHML without claiming physical ontology:

- **1D cellular automata** with local rules.
- **Logistic map** and other discrete dynamical systems.
- **Ising/Potts lattices** with local spin configurations.
- **Random Boolean networks** (Kauffman NK models).
- **Reaction-diffusion** with few species on lattice.
- **Symbolic dynamics** from deterministic chaotic systems.
- **Error-correcting codes** with finite state.
- **Finite-state controllers** with known attractors.

The role of toy systems: **ground truth is known.** If the instrument detects basin/seam structure in a toy system that matches independently-known structure, that is empirical evidence the instrument works.

## Level C — Actual Physical Tests

Real measured data, symbolically encoded and tested against TSML/BHML as instruments.

Constraint: for every real-data application, the encoding Φ must be stated explicitly before fitting. No post-hoc encoding tuning.

Candidate real data sources (illustrative only):

- **Neuronal firing patterns** symbolically encoded by local activity state.
- **Climate time-series** quantile-binned.
- **Turbulent flow snapshots** motif-encoded.
- **EEG signal segments** binned by spectral power deciles.
- **Financial return sequences** decile-binned.

In each case: TSML/BHML is used as a classifier and compressor, not as a theory. Predictions are of the form "this data partitions into these basin/seam classes with these statistics," not "this data is generated by TSML."

## Minimum Physical Bridge: Φ

The bridge from measurement space to TSML/BHML carrier must satisfy:

1. **Well-defined for all observations.** No ambiguous encoding.
2. **Stated before model fitting.** No data-dependent tuning.
3. **Preserves finite structure needed for the theorem (10-fold partition).**
4. **Reproducible** — different analysts applying Φ get the same symbolic stream.

### Candidate Φ Definitions

**Φ₁: Equiprobable decile binning.**

$$\Phi_1(x) = \lfloor 10 \cdot F(x) \rfloor$$

where F is the empirical CDF of the measurement. Produces 10 approximately equiprobable classes. Universal for any real-valued measurement.

*Strength:* assumption-free, applicable everywhere. *Weakness:* no structural information in the encoding; purely statistical.

**Φ₂: Threshold cascade.**

$$\Phi_2(x) = \text{index of bin containing } x$$

where bin boundaries are chosen from domain knowledge (e.g., physical thresholds, energy levels). 10 non-overlapping bins.

*Strength:* encodes domain structure. *Weakness:* requires domain input.

**Φ₃: Symbolic dynamics (Bandt-Pompe style).**

For a time series $(x_t)$, take windows of length $w$ and encode each window by the rank permutation of its values. For $w = 4$, there are $4! = 24$ permutations; for 10 classes, group into 10 natural subsets (e.g., by monotonicity structure).

*Strength:* captures temporal structure. *Weakness:* requires window length choice; reduces 24 permutations to 10 requires a principled grouping.

**Φ₄: Motif encoding.**

For a spatial field on a 2D lattice, take 3×3 local patches. Classify each patch by local energy or pattern class. 10 motif classes.

*Strength:* captures spatial structure. *Weakness:* many possible classifications; choose one explicitly.

**Φ₅: k-means (k=10) on feature vectors.**

Extract features from data, cluster into k=10 classes.

*Strength:* data-driven. *Weakness:* k-means is non-unique; must be done with fixed seeds and reported.

### Which Φ to Use

For each experiment, choose one Φ before fitting. Compare results across multiple Φ choices to test robustness. If the instrument's findings depend heavily on Φ, that is itself a negative result.

## Ranked Testable Signatures

From weakest to strongest, each with an explicit statistical test.

### Signature S1: All 10 classes non-empty

**Claim:** Under Φ, all 10 symbolic states appear in the measured data with non-negligible frequency.

**Test:** minimum class occupancy > some threshold (e.g., 1% of samples).

**Strength:** Very weak. Almost any continuous distribution mapped through Φ₁ (decile binning) will produce this.

**Falsifiability:** Weak. Failure just means Φ is inappropriate for the data.

### Signature S2: Absorbing class exists

**Claim:** There is a class $h \in \{0, ..., 9\}$ such that $\geq 50\%$ of observed transitions end in $h$ regardless of starting state.

**Test:** compute the empirical transition matrix; check if any column has $\geq 50\%$ marginal weight.

**Strength:** Moderate. Real collapse-like dynamics (diffusion to equilibrium, noise convergence) exhibit this; random kernels rarely do.

**Falsifiability:** Clear — compare against random kernel null, report p-value.

### Signature S3: HARMONY-like absorption rate matches TSML (~73%)

**Claim:** The dominant class under the collapse operator holds approximately 73% of entries in a TSML-analogue fit.

**Test:** fit the canonical construction to the data; measure match fraction; compare against 73%.

**Strength:** Specific quantitative match. If matches, strong signal.

**Falsifiability:** Explicit numerical test. Expected range: 60-85% for basin systems; <50% for mixing systems.

### Signature S4: Seam forms a tree (or controlled graph)

**Claim:** The residue after the canonical fit forms a small connected graph with low maximum degree (ideally a tree).

**Test:** identify residue pairs; build graph; measure structural properties (tree-like? bounded degree? cycle count?).

**Strength:** Moderate-to-strong. Random residue would be dense; a tree-shaped residue is distinctive.

**Falsifiability:** Compare residue graph topology against random-residue null distribution.

### Signature S5: Paired T + B beats T alone

**Claim:** Fitting both a collapse operator and a transport operator to the data gives lower prediction error than fitting either alone.

**Test:** cross-validated prediction error for three models: T-only, B-only, (T, B) pair.

**Strength:** Strong. A measurable improvement from using both operators is empirically decisive.

**Falsifiability:** Standard model-comparison framework (likelihood ratio, information criteria).

### Signature S6: Three-layer tower decomposition fits

**Claim:** The observed TSML-analogue decomposes cleanly into a canonical backbone + MAX seam + ADD seam (or analogs), with empty final residue.

**Test:** attempt the three-layer decomposition explicitly; measure residue after each layer.

**Strength:** Strongest. Specific to the canonical framework; unlikely to arise from generic statistical processes.

**Falsifiability:** Very high — either the tower terminates with zero residue, or it doesn't.

### Ranking Summary

| Signature | Strength | Typical Source |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Weak | Any continuous distribution + Φ |
| S2 | Moderate | Any collapse dynamics |
| S3 | Specific | Basin systems with particular attractor weight |
| S4 | Structural | Systems with structured exception set |
| S5 | Strong | Coupled basin+transport systems |
| S6 | Strongest | Systems whose algebraic structure matches the tower |

The useful targets are S4, S5, S6. S1-S3 are preconditions; without them, the stronger signatures are meaningless.

## Five Toy Experiments

Each experiment has:
- **System**: what it is
- **Measurement**: what's observed
- **Encoding Φ**: how to map to {0,...,9}
- **Success criterion**: what would validate the instrument
- **Falsification criterion**: what would invalidate it

### Experiment 1: Rule 110 Cellular Automaton

**System:** 1D binary CA, rule 110, with random initial state, periodic boundary.

**Measurement:** 3-cell sliding windows of cell states (8 patterns) plus 2 boundary-adjacent classes = 10 symbolic classes.

**Encoding Φ:** Each 3-cell pattern maps to a specific digit 0-9.

**What to test:**
- Does the transition from pattern at time t to pattern at time t+1 exhibit S2 (absorbing class)? Rule 110 has known "gliders" — these should show up as seam structure.
- Does fitting the canonical construction recover any structure?

**Success:** The instrument detects glider propagation as seam pairs and background pattern as basin.

**Falsification:** Fit is indistinguishable from random kernel; no structure detected.

### Experiment 2: Logistic Map Symbolic Dynamics

**System:** $x_{n+1} = r x_n (1 - x_n)$ with $r = 3.7$ (chaotic regime).

**Measurement:** long orbit of $x_n$ values.

**Encoding Φ:** decile binning of observed values (Φ₁).

**What to test:**
- Does the empirical transition matrix show basin/seam structure?
- Does the known Markov partition of the logistic map correspond to the TSML basin structure?

**Success:** S3 or S4 — the instrument produces a structure matching Markov-partition analysis.

**Falsification:** Transitions are spread approximately uniformly, no clear basin.

### Experiment 3: 2D Ising Lattice

**System:** $L \times L$ Ising lattice with Glauber dynamics at temperatures spanning the critical point.

**Measurement:** snapshots of the lattice.

**Encoding Φ:** 3×3 patch energy (nine sites, so 10 distinct energy levels for aligned/anti-aligned configurations).

**What to test:**
- Does the basin structure differ above and below $T_c$?
- Does paired (T, B) capture the phase transition better than T alone (S5)?

**Success:** Qualitative change in instrument output across $T_c$; paired fit outperforms single.

**Falsification:** No change across $T_c$; paired fit same as single.

### Experiment 4: Kauffman NK Random Boolean Networks

**System:** $N$-node Boolean networks with $K$-input random functions.

**Measurement:** state trajectories under synchronous update.

**Encoding Φ:** aggregate local states into 10 categories (e.g., by count of active neighbors in a sliding window).

**What to test:**
- Does the basin structure detected by the instrument match the known attractor landscape of NK networks?
- Does the basin count scale with $K$ as predicted by theory?

**Success:** Instrument-detected basins correlate with known attractor basins.

**Falsification:** No correlation.

### Experiment 5: Lorenz System Symbolic Dynamics

**System:** Lorenz equations with standard parameters in chaotic regime.

**Measurement:** time series of the $x$ component, sampled discretely.

**Encoding Φ:** combination of decile binning and local monotonicity signal (5 decile bands × 2 monotonicity states = 10 classes).

**What to test:**
- Does the instrument detect the two lobes of the Lorenz attractor as distinct basins?
- Does BHML-analogue capture the transitions between lobes?

**Success:** Two-basin structure detected, with seam identifying lobe-crossing events.

**Falsification:** Instrument finds no structure or a structure unrelated to known Lorenz geometry.

## Staged Falsification Ladder

### Stage 0 — Pure Finite Consistency

Already complete:
- Theorem: 3-layer tower recovers Z/10Z TSML exactly.
- Canonical construction defined and tested.
- Ring-family compatibility classified (35 members).

No physical input; no physical claim.

### Stage 1 — Synthetic Toy Systems

Next steps:
- Implement Φ for each toy system (Experiments 1–5).
- Fit TSML-analogue and BHML-analogue using the instrument framework.
- Report: match fractions, seam structures, comparison against random-kernel baselines.

Deliverable: "In toy systems 1–5, the instrument detects [X] with significance [Y] compared to random null."

### Stage 2 — Controlled Lattice / Automaton Analogs

After Stage 1 positives:
- Scale up to larger systems.
- Test robustness across parameter ranges.
- Test across multiple Φ choices.
- Identify what properties of the system map to basin vs seam vs transport.

Deliverable: "The instrument is useful for classifying dynamical regimes in [class of systems]."

### Stage 3 — Real Measured Symbolic Data

After Stage 2 success:
- Apply to real-world measured data with pre-specified Φ.
- Test against domain-specific null hypotheses.
- Compare against existing domain classifiers.

Deliverable: "In [specific dataset], the instrument detects structure not detected by [standard method] at [significance level]."

### Stage 4 — Deeper Physical Interpretation

Only after Stage 3 is established.

If, and only if:
- The instrument produces non-trivial, domain-relevant classifications in real data.
- The classifications are not reducible to standard methods.
- The paired basin-transport structure carries predictive power not available in simpler models.

Then: discuss possible ontological interpretation.

Until then: the instrument is a tool, not a theory.

## Instrument First, Ontology Later

The standard temptation is to say "TSML/BHML captures the structure of [reality/physics/consciousness]." This is the move to avoid until the empirical validation is done.

The correct move is:

- "TSML/BHML is a finite instrument with specific structural properties (3-layer tower, basin+transport pair)."
- "Applied to [specific system] via [specific Φ], it produces [specific classification/prediction]."
- "This classification is compared against [specific null hypothesis / alternative instrument]."
- "The result is [specific p-value / improvement / null finding]."
- "The implications for understanding [system] are [specific and limited]."

Ontological language ("this IS the structure of...") is reserved for after the empirical work accumulates strong evidence.

**The stronger claim is the narrower one.** A specific, validated finite instrument is more valuable than a vague theory of everything.
