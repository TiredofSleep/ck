## §2 The protocol

### 2.1 Sample types

Test across four biological systems plus one cell-free control:

1. **Mammalian neurons** (rat hippocampal cultures or human iPSC-derived neurons)
2. **Paramecia** (single-cell organisms with cilia/flagella that contain microtubules)
3. **Plant cells** (Arabidopsis suspension cultures, where microtubules control cell-wall growth)
4. **Yeast** (Saccharomyces cerevisiae spindle pole microtubules during mitosis)
5. **Cell-free preparations** (purified tubulin polymerized into microtubules in vitro at 25°C, 37°C)

### 2.2 Measurement

For each sample:

1. Apply terahertz pump-probe spectroscopy at 1-100 THz range
2. Identify the dominant resonance band (typically at 8-10 GHz for cell-free, may shift in vivo)
3. Compute the **quality factor** $Q_c = \omega_0 / \Delta\omega$ for the dominant resonance
4. Normalize to the "structural maximum Q" (maximum theoretical Q for a tubulin dipole array)

The **coherence quality** $Q_c$ is the normalized measurement, with $Q_c \in [0, 1]$:
- $Q_c \to 1$: pure quantum coherence (no dissipation)
- $Q_c \to 0$: classical regime (full thermal decoherence)

### 2.3 Statistical analysis

Compare measured $Q_c$ across sample types:

- **Null hypothesis**: $Q_c$ varies systematically with biology (e.g., $Q_c$(mammalian) ≠ $Q_c$(paramecium))
- **Alternative (TIG prediction)**: $Q_c \to T^* = 5/7 \approx 0.714$ across all sample types, with biological variance ≪ 0.05

The framework predicts a **biology-invariant attractor at $T^*$**.

### 2.4 Falsification criteria

The framework is falsified if:

1. Measured $Q_c$ values across the 5 sample types fall outside $0.714 \pm 0.05$ in $\ge 2$ samples
2. Systematic trend with biology is observed (e.g., $Q_c$ correlates with cell complexity)
3. The measured $Q_c$ converges to a different universal value (e.g., 0.6 or 0.8)

The framework is **strongly supported** if all 5 samples give $Q_c$ in $0.714 \pm 0.02$.
