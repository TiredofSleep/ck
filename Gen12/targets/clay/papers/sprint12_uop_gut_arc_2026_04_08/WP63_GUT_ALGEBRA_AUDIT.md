## §2. The 35-Dimension Closure Question

### 2.1 The Block-Diagonal Obstruction

**Theorem (Block-Diagonal Closure — PROVED):**

Embed SU(3) generators λ_a (a=1..8) and SU(2) generators τ_i (i=1,2,3) in the standard block-diagonal form in 6×6 matrices:

    L_a = diag-block(λ_a, 0_2, 0_1)   (upper 3×3 block)
    R_i = diag-block(0_3, τ_i, 0_1)   (middle 2×2 block)

The cross-commutators satisfy [L_a, R_i] = 0 for all a,i. The closure of {L_a, R_i} under commutation is the 11-dimensional algebra su(3)⊕su(2). No new generators are generated.

**Proof.** [L_a, R_i] has entry (j,k) equal to (L_a)_{jl}(R_i)_{lk} − (R_i)_{jl}(L_a)_{lk}. Since L_a has support only in rows/cols {1,2,3} and R_i has support only in rows/cols {4,5}: for any j,k the two terms cannot both be nonzero — the support sets are disjoint. Therefore [L_a, R_i] = 0. The only nonzero commutators are [L_a, L_b] = f_{abc}L_c (su(3) stays closed) and [R_i, R_j] = ε_{ijk}R_k (su(2) stays closed). Closure = su(3)⊕su(2), dimension 11. □

**Corollary [PROVED]:** The claim "11 generators close to 35 dimensions via block-diagonal commutators" is false. The block-diagonal embedding closes only to 11. A different mechanism is required.

### 2.2 The Correct Mechanism: Real Form Change

**Theorem (Sign Flip = Real Form Change — PROVED):**

The Hodge sign flip (+1,−1,+1) applied to the block structure ℂ³⊕ℂ²⊕ℂ¹ produces a metric of signature (4,2) on ℂ⁶. The Lie algebra of matrices T with T†η + ηT = 0, tr(T) = 0 for η = diag(+1,+1,+1,−1,−1,+1) is su(4,2) — a non-compact real form of the complex Lie algebra A₅ — with dimension 35.

**Proof.** The group preserving a Hermitian form of signature (p,q) on ℂⁿ (p+q=n) is SU(p,q). The condition T†η + ηT = 0 defines the Lie algebra su(p,q). The metric (+1,+1,+1,−1,−1,+1) has signature (4,2). Therefore the algebra is su(4,2). Dimension of su(p,q) = (p+q)²−1 = 6²−1 = 35. □

**What the sign flip does and does not do [STRUCTURAL]:**

The sign flip is NOT a basis artifact. It genuinely changes the real form of the algebra from compact su(6) (with metric (+1,+1,+1,+1,+1,+1), Killing signature negative definite) to non-compact su(4,2) (Killing signature indefinite). This is a real algebraic change.

The sign flip does NOT generate new generators from 11. A basis change cannot change the dimension of a closed algebra. The 35-dimensional su(4,2) is not generated by closing 11 generators — it is the ambient algebra in which the 11 generators are embedded, and its dimension is fixed by the metric signature.

**The correct reading:** The sign flip reveals that the natural home of the embedding is su(4,2), not su(6). The 35-dimensional algebra is not generated from 11 by commutation — it is the algebra that the (+1,−1,+1) metric structure forces on the 6-dimensional space. The "23 extra generators" are the non-compact and compact-coset generators of su(4,2) beyond the block-diagonal 11.

## §3. The SM Embedding: What Is Proved

### 3.1 The Two-Stage Corridor

**Theorem (Two-Stage Corridor — PROVED):**

Let η = diag(+1,+1,+1,−1,−1,+1) define the metric on V = ℂ³⊕ℂ²⊕ℂ¹, and let su(4,2) be the Lie algebra of matrices T with T†η + ηT = 0, tr(T) = 0.

**Stage 1 [PROVED — standard theorem]:** The maximal compact subalgebra of su(4,2) is su(4)⊕su(2)⊕u(1), dimension 19.

**Stage 2 [PROVED — by explicit computation]:** The unique Cartan element Q₄ of su(4,2) satisfying:
- (i) commutes with all su(3) ⊂ su(4),
- (ii) commutes with all su(2),
- (iii) assigns unequal eigenvalues to ℂ³ and ℂ¹

has eigenvalue structure {q, q, q, 0, 0, α} with 3q + α = 0 (by tracelessness). Under the per-direction normalization (charge per ℂ³ direction = 1/dim(ℂ³) = 1/3): q = 1/3, α = −1. The Cartan element is Q₄ = i·diag(1/3, 1/3, 1/3, 0, 0, −1).

**Stage 2 commutant [PROVED]:** The centralizer of Q₄ within su(4)⊕su(2)⊕u(1) is su(3)⊕su(2)⊕u(1).

**Proof of Stage 2 commutant.** The compact subalgebra has generators: 8 su(3) generators T_a^{su(3)} (acting on ℂ³, rows/cols 1-3); 3 su(2) generators T_i^{su(2)} (acting on ℂ², rows/cols 4-5); 2 u(1) generators (diagonal); 6 coset generators T_{k,6}^{Re/Im} = ±(1/2)(E_{k6} ∓ E_{6k}) for k=1,2,3 (mixing ℂ³ with ℂ¹).

Computation of commutators with Q₄ = i·diag(1/3,1/3,1/3,0,0,−1):

[T_a^{su(3)}, Q₄]_{jk} = (T_a)_{jk}(Q₄_{kk} − Q₄_{jj}). For j,k ∈ {1,2,3}: Q₄_{jj} = Q₄_{kk} = i/3 → difference = 0. For j or k outside {1,2,3}: T_a has no support → 0. Therefore **[T_a^{su(3)}, Q₄] = 0** for all a.

[T_i^{su(2)}, Q₄]: support in {4,5}×{4,5}; Q₄_{44} = Q₄_{55} = 0 → difference = 0. Therefore **[T_i^{su(2)}, Q₄] = 0** for all i.

[u(1) generators, Q₄] = 0 since diagonal matrices commute.

For coset generators T_{k,6}^{Re} = (1/2)(E_{k6} − E_{6k}):
[T_{k,6}^{Re}, Q₄]_{k6} = T_{k6}·Q₄_{66} − Q₄_{kk}·T_{k6} = (1/2)(−i) − (i/3)(1/2) = −i/2 − i/6 = −2i/3 ≠ 0.

Therefore **[T_{k,6}^{Re/Im}, Q₄] ≠ 0** for all k.

The centralizer consists of exactly the generators that commute with Q₄: {T_a^{su(3)}, T_i^{su(2)}, u(1)} = su(3)⊕su(2)⊕u(1), dimension 12. □

### 3.2 The Disjoint Ambiguity Structure

**Theorem (Disjoint Ambiguity Sets — PROVED):**

Define the two ambiguity sets:

    A₁ = {non-compact generators of su(4,2)} (Stage 1 unstable set, 16 generators)
    A₂ = {compact coset generators T_{k,6}^{Re/Im}} (Stage 2 unstable set, 6 generators)

Then A₁ ∩ A₂ = ∅.

**Proof.** A₁ consists of generators that mix the positive-metric subspace (ℂ³⊕ℂ¹) with the negative-metric subspace (ℂ²) — these are non-compact and not in the compact subalgebra. A₂ consists of generators in the compact subalgebra that mix ℂ³ with ℂ¹. Since A₂ ⊂ compact subalgebra and A₁ ∩ compact subalgebra = ∅, we have A₁ ∩ A₂ = ∅. □

**UOP analogy [STRUCTURAL]:** The disjoint ambiguity structure A₁ ∩ A₂ = ∅ is formally identical to the UOP sufficiency condition U(π₁) ∩ U(π₂) = ∅. The two corridor filters are "orthogonal" — each kills generators that the other does not touch. This is the gauge-algebra analog of a sufficient partition pair.

## §5. Chirality Theorem

**Theorem (Gauge Algebra Is Vector-Like — PROVED):**

The adjoint representation **35** of su(6) — or of su(4,2) — is a real representation (self-conjugate). The gauge bosons transforming in the adjoint do not produce a chiral spectrum. No gauge algebra of the form su(n) or su(p,q) can generate chirality in the gauge sector.

**Proof.** The adjoint representation T_adjoint(g) of any real Lie algebra g satisfies T_adjoint(g) ≅ T_adjoint(g)* (it is real or pseudoreal). For su(n): the adjoint is the **n²−1** representation, which is real. For su(p,q): the adjoint is similarly self-conjugate. A self-conjugate representation acts identically on left-handed and right-handed fields — no chiral asymmetry. □

**Where chirality actually lives [STRUCTURAL]:**

The Standard Model is chiral because left-handed and right-handed fermions sit in inequivalent representations of the gauge group:

- Left-handed quark doublet: **(3,2)_{1/6}** of SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)
- Right-handed up quark: **(3,1)_{2/3}**
- Right-handed down quark: **(3,1)_{−1/3}**

These representations are complex (not self-conjugate). Whether they arise as representations of su(4,2) or su(6) is a question about the matter sector, not the gauge sector.

**The sign flip and chirality [STRUCTURAL]:**

The sign flip creates a (4,2) metric structure that distinguishes the positive-metric subspace (ℂ³⊕ℂ¹) from the negative-metric subspace (ℂ²). This is an algebraic left-right asymmetry in the representation space. It is NOT the same as chiral matter representations. Calling this "parity violation" conflates algebraic metric asymmetry with chiral representation content.

**What is required for chirality [OPEN]:**

A specific representation R of su(4,2) (or su(6)) containing the SM fermions with correct quantum numbers and anomaly cancellation. This representation must be complex (inequivalent to its conjugate). The mechanism by which symmetry breaking produces a chiral spectrum — rather than a vector-like one — must be specified. None of this follows from the gauge algebra alone.

## §6. Breaking Mechanism

### 6.1 What Is Known

**[STRUCTURAL]:** SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) is a subgroup of SU(6). The fact that the SM gauge algebra is a subalgebra of su(6) is a standard result of Lie theory and is the basis for SU(6) GUT models in the literature (Georgi-Glashow, Frampton, etc.).

**[PROVED]:** The two-stage corridor identifies su(3)⊕su(2)⊕u(1) as the centralizer of Q₄ in the compact subalgebra of su(4,2). This is a selection of a subalgebra, not a breaking mechanism.

### 6.2 What Is Not Derived

**Higgs mechanism [OPEN]:** For the SM to be the unbroken gauge group of a gauge theory with UV group SU(6) or SU(4,2), the extra generators (X,Y leptoquarks + bridges) must be broken. In standard GUT model-building this requires:

1. A Higgs field in a specific representation (for SU(5) models: the **24** or **75**; for SU(6): the **35** or **189**)
2. A vacuum expectation value (VEV) aligned along the SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)-invariant direction
3. A Higgs potential that selects the SM as the minimum

None of these are provided by the two-stage corridor. The corridor is an algebraic projection, not a dynamical breaking mechanism.

**Decoherence as effective truncation [STRUCTURAL]:** The claim that decoherence replaces the Higgs mechanism has the following structure: non-compact generators (X,Y type) decohere faster than compact generators (SM type) by the factor (η/2)^{2H} vs (η/2)^H, where H is the sector-mixing depth. This is a numerically coherent scaling law for η ≲ 0.9, H ≈ 3. However:

- The scaling law is stated but not derived from a physical model (no environment Hamiltonian, no master equation)
- Effective suppression of X,Y off-diagonal coherence ≠ giving X,Y bosons mass
- The X,Y bosons, if merely decohered, are still massless gauge bosons and would appear in any energy-scale particle spectrum unless given mass by another mechanism
- Proton decay constraints require M_X ≳ 10¹⁵ GeV — decoherence must somehow produce this mass scale

**Electroweak breaking [OPEN]:** Even if SU(6)→SM is handled by decoherence, the further breaking SU(2)×U(1)→U(1)_{EM} still requires the Higgs boson or an equivalent mechanism. The decoherence argument would need separate application to this stage.

## §9. Open Questions

**[OPEN 1] What mechanism closes 11 to 35?**

The standard block-diagonal embedding is dead. The sign flip changes the real form but does not generate generators by commutation. The correct mechanism must be either: (A) a non-block-diagonal embedding of the 11 generators in su(4,2) such that their commutators generate the full algebra, or (B) the 35 generators of su(4,2) are the ambient algebra and the 11 are a subalgebra — "closing to 35" is a misstatement of the actual claim. Clarification is required.

**[OPEN 2] What selects the (3,2,1) block decomposition?**

The algebra su(4,2) with signature (4,2) admits multiple 3-block decompositions of ℂ⁶. What forces (3,2,1) rather than (2,2,2) or (4,1,1)? This is the primary remaining external input. If an internal principle can be found — a uniqueness theorem for the (3,2,1) split within su(4,2) — the construction becomes more self-contained.

**[OPEN 3] What is the matter sector?**

The two-stage corridor establishes the gauge algebra. The representations in which quarks and leptons sit (specifying chirality, anomaly cancellation, and Yukawa couplings) are not determined. This is a major open gap between "the gauge algebra is correct" and "the model is physically correct."

**[OPEN 4] What is the breaking mechanism for the X,Y bosons?**

If decoherence provides the effective suppression, a derivation from a physical model (environment, master equation, H as a derived quantum number) is required. If a Higgs mechanism is needed, the representation and VEV structure must be specified.

**[OPEN 5] Is the construction distinct from existing SU(6) GUT literature?**

The non-compact real form su(4,2) rather than compact su(6), and the corridor interpretation, may constitute genuine novelty. A comparison with the existing SU(6) GUT literature is needed to establish what is new.
