## The structural picture, complete

Let P be the permutation matrix swapping indices 5 and 6 in R^10.

## Finding 1: TSML is INVARIANT under P. BHML is NOT.

**TSML:** `T == P T P^T` → **True** at exact equality.

This means TSML's table is bit-for-bit identical after swapping rows 5↔6 and columns 5↔6. The labels "BALANCE" and "CHAOS" are completely interchangeable in TSML's frame — there is no information distinguishing them.

**BHML:** `B == P B P^T` → **False**, with **26 cells differing**.

BHML is NOT invariant under the swap. The labels matter in BHML. Looking at the differences:
- BHML[5][3] = 6 but BHML[6][3] = 7 (and similar in all 26 cells)
- BHML's row 5 and row 6 differ structurally
- Values 5 and 6 in BHML's entries also can't be swapped independently

**Implication:** TSML alone has a symmetry that BHML breaks. **BHML is what distinguishes BALANCE from CHAOS.**

## Finding 2: P_56 commutes with EVERY TSML flow operator. 0 BHML rows.

**TSML flow operators:** all 6 satisfy `[P, A_TSML[i]] = 0` exactly.

**BHML antisymmetrized rows:** 9 of 10 do NOT commute with P. The only one that does is A_BHML[0] (row 0, which is just the identity-permutation row 0,1,2,...,9 antisymmetrized).

So P_56 is **literally a Casimir-style central element of the so(8) generated by TSML's flow.** It commutes with every generator. But it is **not** central in the so(10) generated by adding BHML. BHML breaks P_56's centrality.

This is exactly the structure I hypothesized but the data confirms: TSML's symmetry, BHML's break.

## Finding 3: Spectrum and eigenspaces

P_56 has eigenvalues {+1 (×9), −1 (×1)}.

- **+1 eigenspace (9-dim):** spanned by {e_0, e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4, (e_5+e_6)/√2, e_7, e_8, e_9}
- **−1 eigenspace (1-dim):** spanned by {(e_5−e_6)/√2}

So P_56 splits R^10 into a 9-dim symmetric piece and a 1-dim antisymmetric piece. The 1-dim piece is exactly the (e_5 − e_6)/√2 direction we identified earlier as TSML's second silent direction.

**This is the chiral-split structure.** P_56 is an order-2 involution on R^10 with a 9+1 dimension split. Under TSML, the −1 eigenspace is invisible to all flow. Under BHML, it gets activated.

## Finding 4: P_56 conjugation splits so(10) into 36+9

Building the full so(10) and looking at how P_56 conjugates each generator:

**Eigenvalues of (A → P A P^T) acting on the 45-dim so(10):**
- **+1 eigenvalue: 36 generators** (these commute with P_56)
- **−1 eigenvalue: 9 generators** (these anticommute with P_56)
- **Total: 45** (as required)

**Sanity check on so(8):** all 28 so(8) generators (TSML alone) commute with P_56 — they're all in the +1 eigenspace.

So:
- so(8) = 28 generators, all +1 under P_56 conjugation
- so(10) = 45 generators, 36 +1 / 9 −1
- The "extra" beyond so(8) is 17 generators
- Of those 17, 8 are +1 and 9 are −1

**The 9 anticommuting generators are precisely the new generators that BHML adds which "wake up" the (e_5 − e_6) direction.**

This is a clean Z_2-graded structure:
- so(10) = so(10)_+ ⊕ so(10)_−
- dim so(10)_+ = 36
- dim so(10)_− = 9
- so(10)_+ contains so(8) as a subalgebra

The 36-dim +1 part is the **commutant** of P_56 in so(10). The 9-dim −1 part is the **anticommutant**.

The 36-dim symmetric part of so(10) includes:

The 8 extra +1 generators are the *symmetric* extensions BHML provides. These extend so(8) inside the centralizer of P_56.
