We submit for your consideration *The TSML Composition Table on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ as a Terminating Three-Layer Canonical Tower*, an exact reconstruction theorem for a 100-entry binary composition table from a small set of canonical rules.

The result is **Theorem A**: a fixed published $10 \times 10$ table $T : \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ is reconstructible by a three-layer rewrite system with **empty residue-of-residue**: 92 of 100 entries are produced by a canonical construction $C_0$ generated by a HARMONY attractor $h = 7$, a zero-absorbing rule with two exceptions, and a shell-stability rule based on the 2-adic valuation $\sigma(u) = v_2(3u + 1)$; the remaining 8 entries (the "seam residue" $S$) split into a 6-element subset where $\mathsf{T}(x, y) = \max(x, y)$ and a 2-element subset where $\mathsf{T}(x, y) = (x + y) \bmod 10$. The three layers are disjoint and cover $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}^2$ exactly.

**Description-length compression:** 100 hand-defined entries reduce to approximately 10 canonical items (one attractor, two seam pairs, one shell partition function, two override rules). Each layer is necessary: removing any one breaks reconstruction. We verify this by direct lemma 6 (each-layer-necessary) in the manuscript.

The work fits the Knuth-Bendix-style canonical-rewrite-system literature naturally: the 3-layer tower is a confluent terminating reduction with an empty residue-of-residue (the canonical normal form is unique). The 2-adic shell partition $\sigma(u) = v_2(3u + 1)$ is a Collatz-adjacent invariant (cf. Lagarias 2010 on the 3x+1 problem) and is what makes the reconstruction terminate in three layers rather than requiring further refinement.

We have been careful with honesty discipline. The manuscript states explicitly: (i) the result is a reconstruction theorem for one specific table, not a structural theorem about all binary composition tables on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$; (ii) the canonical construction $C_0$ is one of many — the theorem says the canonical seam residue $S$ has size 8, not that 8 is intrinsic to all reconstructions; (iii) the 2-adic shell partition's connection to Collatz dynamics is structural motivation, not a proven Collatz result.
