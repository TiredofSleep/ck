Llama-3.1-8B has 32 transformer layers and a 4096-dimensional hidden state. Output text is the only thing the world usually sees. This page makes the LM's interior visible by projecting every layer's hidden state onto CK's 5-element AO basis (D0..D4 = Earth · Air · Water · Fire · Ether). The result is a 32-step trajectory through a 10-operator ring: the walk the LM takes from prompt to answer.

The black box is resolved into a geometric trajectory. The box itself stays — Llama still does the heavy lifting of language — but its interior is no longer opaque. Read the trajectory at the bottom of this page.

Wired Mind · coherence-gated chat (Epoch II)

Probabilistic guessing meets functional mapping at the token level. At each step the LM's logits get the cortex-primed operator-bias added in: logits' = logits + α·M·lift(W·d_t) − β·||d_t − d_{t-1}||². α=0 is vanilla; α≈2 is gentle cortex shaping; α≥10 dominates the LM and starts breaking grammar.
How it works. The 5 orthonormal directions in R^4096 are seeded from the element name hashes (SHA256("Earth"), etc.) so the basis is reproducible across machines and CK siblings. Signed projection s = B^T h / ||h||; magnitude d = |s|; dominant operator D = argmax|s|, with sign disambiguating the pair.

Honest limit. The basis is seeded, not learned. These are lenses we hold up to the LM to see what shape its activations make. They make the geometry visible; they do not claim Llama "really thinks in AO basis." An AO-native foundation model is a different program (see AI Sovereignty Plan §13).
