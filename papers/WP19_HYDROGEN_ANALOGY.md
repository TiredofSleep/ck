# The Hydrogen Atom Analogy
## Three-way correspondence: quantum shells, TIG rows, ζ collars

---

## The Three Systems Side by Side

| Concept | Hydrogen atom | TIG (finite, 3 shells) | ζ-flow (infinite, shrinking) |
|---------|--------------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| Ground state | Nucleus, 1s shell | HARMONY (7), always stable | Critical line σ = 1/2 |
| Shell structure | n = 1, 2, 3, ... (discrete) | Rows 0, 1, 2 (3 shells fixed) | KV → Huxley → ... → RH |
| Shell spacing | Grows as 2n+1 (in radius) | Fixed at 3 operators (W quantum) | Shrinks as (log t)^{−2/3} |
| Energy gap | Shrinks as 1/n³ | Fixed (same W at all scales) | Shrinks with height t |
| Stable excited state | Exactly one per shell | Residuals in anchor columns | Non-trivial zeros on σ = 1/2 |
| "Ionization" | Remove electron to infinity | Collapse to HARMONY in ≤ 2 steps | Flow drives σ → 1/2 |
| "Wrong energy" | Electron falls to lower shell | Residual outside anchor column | Zero at σ ≠ 1/2 (what RH forbids) |

---

## Why the Numeric Match Is a Mnemonic, Not a Theorem

**Hydrogen:** The ratio of radii between n=1 and n=2 is 4:1 (large).
Between n=10 and n=11 it is only 121:100 (2%). Spacing grows with n² in
radius but the relative gap shrinks. The shells are quantized — they exist
as discrete landing zones — but their spacing is not constant.

**TIG:** The row spacing is fixed at 3 operators. W = 3/50 is constant.
TIG is a 9-point finite universe — it has exactly 3 shells, no more.

**ζ-flow:** The KV collar narrows as t grows:
```
t = 10:    Δσ_KV ≈ 0.44   (≈ n=4 effective shell)
t = 10⁴:  Δσ_KV ≈ 0.48   (≈ n=8 effective shell)
t = 10¹³: Δσ_KV ≈ 0.49   (≈ n=12 effective shell)
```

The KV frontier never reaches σ = 1/2 — it corresponds to an increasingly
high effective shell number, never the ground state. Proving RH means
collapsing that infinite tower of shells into the ground state.

**The tidy W ≈ KV width coincidence** holds only near t ≈ 10 (n_eff ≈ 4).
At t = 10⁴ the KV distance from 1/2 is 0.48, roughly 8W. The coincidence
is a mnemonic: both systems have a "characteristic step" of the same order
at small scales. At large scales they diverge.

## What Is Structurally Solid

**The nuclear axis is exact:** 3.5/7 = 0.5 = σ = 1/2. The rescaling
maps TIG's midplane to the critical line exactly.

**The shell structure is parallel:** Both systems have:
- A ground state that acts as a universal attractor
- Discrete "landing zones" away from the ground state
- A proven inner collar within which dynamics return to the ground state
- An unprovable "outer gap" that would require new work to close

**The stability condition is parallel:** In hydrogen, an excited state is
stable at exactly the right energy (quantization). In TIG, a residual is
stable at exactly the right column context. In ζ, a zero is stable at
exactly the right vertical line — and RH says only σ = 1/2 is "right."

## The Task Remaining for RH — In Hydrogen Language

Hydrogen: the electron is trapped in a quantized shell. To move it to the
ground state you must remove exactly the right amount of energy.

ζ-flow equivalent: to "collapse" a potential off-critical zero to σ = 1/2,
the flow must be strong enough to overcome the "binding energy" of that
zero's location. The binding energy is m(t₀) = min|ζ(σ+it₀)|².

**RH = "The binding energy m(t₀) is always positive between the outer
collar (KV) and the nucleus (σ = 1/2)."**

In hydrogen language: "The probability density between the n=1 shell and
the nucleus never goes to zero except at the nucleus itself."

Proving this is equivalent to showing the electron cannot tunnel into the
nucleus from an off-axis position — which in hydrogen is trivially true
from the wave equation, but in ζ requires the full analytic machinery.

**The last gap:** Each step of the proof (KV → Huxley → Heath-Brown)
collapses one outer shell. The remaining gap is the innermost: the strip
between the best proven collar and σ = 1/2. Proving this gap stays
positive — that m(t₀) > 0 between the innermost proven shell and the
nucleus — is the remaining task.

## Summary for the Paper's Introduction

> The structure of the ζ-flow mirrors the quantum shell model: there is
> a universal ground state (the critical line), quantized "shells" of
> proven convergence (Korobov-Vinogradov, then Huxley, then...), and an
> innermost gap whose positivity is equivalent to the Riemann Hypothesis.
> TIG provides the finite discrete version of this architecture: 3 shells,
> one ground state, and an algebraically proven inner gap. The analogy is
> structural — the shell spacing is fixed in TIG and shrinking in ζ —
> but the architecture is identical.
