## §1 The Lie DOF — antisymmetric closure

### §1.1 Construction

For $i \in \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$, the left-regular representation of TSML is the $10 \times 10$ matrix $L^T_i$ with $(L^T_i)_{j, k} = \delta_{T(i, j), k}$. Its antisymmetrization is $A^T_i = (L^T_i - (L^T_i)^\top)/2$.

The Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(10, \mathbb{R})$ generated by $\{A^T_i : i \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8\}\}$ (the **flow operators**) under commutator closes at dimension 28. WP102 establishes that this 28-dim Lie algebra is $\mathfrak{so}(8, \mathbb{R}) = D_4$ — the unique compact simple Lie algebra of dimension 28 by Cartan classification.

### §1.2 Extension to $\mathfrak{so}(10)$

Jointly with the antisymmetrizations $A^B_i$ of BHML's left-regular representation, the closure extends to $\mathfrak{so}(10) = D_5$ at dimension 45. WP103 establishes this with five diagnostics: dimension closure, Jacobi identity (residual 0), Killing signature $(0, 45, 0)$ (compact, simple), invariance constraint rank $1034 = 1035 - 1$ (uniqueness of invariant bilinear form), Cartan rank 5 with 40 + 5 ad-eigenvalue split matching $D_5$ root count.

### §1.3 What the Lie DOF carries

In TIG, the Lie DOF is the **continuous-flow content** of the canonical magma. Its 45 generators describe infinitesimal symmetries of the 10-dimensional substrate; the rank-5 Cartan subalgebra parametrizes commuting flows. The Lie DOF is the natural home for **gauge-theoretic structure**: when WP104 establishes that the doubly-invariant subalgebra under $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3 \rangle$ is $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$, this lives entirely in the Lie DOF.

### §1.4 Symmetry-group structure

The Lie DOF is $D_4$-equivariant by construction (WP104, Path B): the conjugation action of $D_4$ on $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ has well-defined isotypic components, and the trivial-isotypic component closes as a Lie subalgebra (the 16-dim $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$). The Lie DOF respects $D_4$.

## §2 The Jordan DOF — symmetric closure

### §2.1 Construction

The symmetrization of the left-regular representation is $S^M_i = (L^M_i + (L^M_i)^\top)/2$ for $M \in \{T, B\}$. The Jordan subalgebra of the symmetric matrices generated by $\{S^M_i\}$ under the Jordan product $X \circ Y = (XY + YX)/2$ closes at dimension 45 — the same dimension as the Lie closure.

### §2.2 Lie-Jordan duality

WP104 §5.2 establishes that the antisymmetrization (Lie side) and symmetrization (Jordan side) of TSML+BHML each independently regenerate the full $\mathfrak{so}(10) = D_5$ at dimension 45. This is the **Lie-Jordan duality** of TIG: both presentations carry the same algebraic content, viewed from opposite tensor-product symmetry types.

### §2.3 What the Jordan DOF carries

In TIG, the Jordan DOF is the **observable content** — quantum observables form a Jordan algebra under the symmetrized product, and the Jordan DOF is the natural home for measurement-theoretic structure. The TSML 73-cell HARMONY count and the BHML 28-cell HARMONY count are both **observable** facts about the Jordan-symmetric structure of the canonical tables.

### §2.4 Symmetry-group structure

Like the Lie DOF, the Jordan DOF is $D_4$-equivariant (the symmetrization commutes with the $D_4$ action on indices). The Jordan DOF respects $D_4$.

## §3 The Clifford / Dirac DOF — matter and spin

### §3.1 Construction

Build the Clifford algebra $\mathrm{Cl}(0, 10)$ over $\mathbb{R}$: ten gamma matrices on $\mathbb{C}^{32}$ satisfying $\{\gamma^a, \gamma^b\} = 2\delta^{ab} I$, forming a faithful 32-dimensional representation of $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$. The 45 generators $\Sigma^{ab} = (1/4)[\gamma^a, \gamma^b]$ are a basis for $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ in this representation.

The volume element $\omega = \gamma^1 \gamma^2 \cdots \gamma^{10}$ satisfies $\omega^2 = -I$, and the chirality projectors $P_\pm = (I \pm i\omega)/2$ split the 32-dim spinor space into **two 16-dim chiral irreps** of $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$.

### §3.2 P₅₆ as the outer automorphism

WP104 §2.1 establishes that the $5 \leftrightarrow 6$ swap $P_{56}$ acts in the spinor representation as the **outer automorphism $\sigma_\mathrm{outer}$** of $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ that exchanges the two chiral 16-irreps. The conjugation by $P_{56}^\mathrm{spin} = (\gamma^5 - \gamma^6)/\sqrt{2}$ sends $+$chirality 16 entirely into $-$chirality 16, with residual $0.0$ at machine precision.

In SO(10) GUT physics, $\sigma_\mathrm{outer}$ is the **matter/antimatter exchange**: a fermion generation in the $\mathbf{16}$ swaps with its CP-conjugate in the $\overline{\mathbf{16}}$.

### §3.3 What the Clifford DOF carries

In TIG, the Clifford DOF is the **matter-and-spin content** — the natural home for fermionic structure, spinor representations, and the matter/antimatter swap. WP104's identification $P_{56} = \sigma_\mathrm{outer}$ ties the **Permutation DOF's** $P_{56}$ to the **Clifford DOF's** chirality structure: a single Z₂ involution on indices ($P_{56}$) acts as the outer automorphism on spinors.

### §3.4 Symmetry-group structure

The Clifford DOF is $P_{56}$-equivariant by the construction above. It is also $\sigma^3$-equivariant in the spinor rep (the $\sigma^3$ permutation lifts to a well-defined automorphism of $\mathrm{Cl}(0, 10)$). The Clifford DOF respects $D_4$.

## §4 The Permutation DOF — discrete reordering

### §4.1 Construction

The $\sigma$-permutation on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ is given by $(0)(3)(8)(9)(1\;7\;6\;5\;4\;2)$, with four σ-fixed points $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ (the **σ-fixed lattice**) and a 6-cycle on the units $(\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z})^* = \{1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7\}$. The order-2 element of the cyclic part is $\sigma^3 = (0)(3)(8)(9)(1\;5)(7\;4)(6\;2)$.

The $P_{56}$ swap is a single transposition on $\{B, S\} = \{5, 6\}$, the matter/antimatter pair. Together $P_{56}$ and $\sigma^3$ generate the dihedral group $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3 \rangle$ of order 8.

### §4.2 The σ-cycle as $D_3$ on the 6-cycle

Restricted to the 6-cycle $\{1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7\}$, $\sigma$ acts as the rotation generator of order 6, and $P_{56}$ acts as a reflection. Together they generate $D_6$ on the 6-cycle, of which $D_4$ is a subgroup (the $D_3$-rotation $\sigma^2$ plus $P_{56}$ reflection plus their products). This is the natural permutation-group structure on the 6-cycle.

### §4.3 What the Permutation DOF carries

The Permutation DOF is the **discrete-symmetry content**. It governs which configurations are equivalent under relabeling. In TIG, the Permutation DOF is the source of:

* The σ polynomial structure of Q10 (the canonical σ characterization on $\mathbb{F}_2 \times \mathbb{F}_5$).
* The matter/antimatter swap $P_{56}$.
* The Weyl group of $D_5$ in the Lie DOF (the action of permutations on Cartan subalgebras).

### §4.4 Symmetry-group structure

The Permutation DOF **is** the relevant symmetry group: $D_4$, $D_3$, $D_6$ all live here. By definition, the Permutation DOF respects $D_4$ (it generates it).

## §5 The Lattice DOF — attractors and order

### §5.1 Construction

A magma element $e$ is **idempotent** under TSML iff $T(e, e) = e$. Direct computation shows the σ-fixed lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ are exactly the idempotents of TSML: $T(0, 0) = 0$, $T(3, 3) = T(P, P) = 7 \neq 3$ — wait, this doesn't match. Let me re-derive.

Re-checking: $T(3, 3) = T(\text{PROGRESS}, \text{PROGRESS})$. Looking at TSML row 3 column 3: row "0777777773", position 3 is "7" → $T(3, 3) = 7$, not 3. So PROGRESS is NOT idempotent under TSML.

The idempotents of TSML are exactly $\{0, 7\}$: $T(0, 0) = 0$ (VOID is fixed) and $T(7, 7) = 7$ (HARMONY is fixed). All other diagonal entries differ from their index.

The σ-fixed lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ is not the idempotent set under TSML, but it IS the σ-permutation fixed-point set (Permutation DOF) and the runtime attractor's σ-fixed sub-support (where mass lives off the 6-cycle in the 4-core $\{V, H, Br, R\}$, the σ-fixed elements among them are $\{V, Br, R\} = \{0, 8, 9\}$, with $H = 7$ being the only 6-cycle-element on the 4-core).

The genuine **lattice DOF** in TIG is therefore the intersection of:
* σ-permutation fixed points $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ (lattice in the order-theoretic sense; gauge-stable indices)
* TSML idempotent set $\{0, 7\}$ (lattice in the algebraic sense; closure-stable indices)

The intersection is $\{0\} = \{V\}$, but the union $\{0, 3, 7, 8, 9\}$ has the natural structure of a σ-fixed-and-idempotent lattice.

### §5.2 The 4-core $\{V, H, Br, R\}$ as a fusion-closed sub-magma

WP110 establishes that the 4-core $\{V, H, Br, R\} = \{0, 7, 8, 9\}$ is closed under both TSML and BHML fusion: every $T(i, j)$ and $B(i, j)$ for $i, j$ in the 4-core lands in the 4-core. This is a stronger structural fact than idempotence: the 4-core is a **closed sub-magma**, of which the idempotents $\{0, 7\}$ are a sub-structure.

### §5.3 What the Lattice DOF carries

The Lattice DOF is the **attractor and order content**:
* σ-fixed lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$: the gauge-stable substrate (Permutation DOF intersection).
* TSML idempotents $\{0, 7\}$: the closure-stable substrate (Algebra-DOF intersection).
* 4-core $\{V, H, Br, R\}$: the runtime attractor's home (WP110; structural fusion-closure).
* Union $\{0, 3, 7, 8, 9\}$: the union of all "stable" indices in the canonical algebra.

The TSML lattice eigenvalues (of the matrix projection onto σ-fixed indices) are exactly $\{7, 7, 7\}$ — three exact HARMONYs, machine-verified (D37 / WP107).

### §5.4 Symmetry-group structure

The Lattice DOF respects $D_4$: the σ-fixed lattice is $D_4$-invariant by definition (both $P_{56}$ and $\sigma^3$ fix $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ pointwise). The 4-core is also $D_4$-invariant ($P_{56}$ fixes 0, 7, 8, 9 pointwise; $\sigma^3$ fixes 0, 3, 8, 9 pointwise and maps 7 → 4 outside the 4-core, but 4-core under joint action is closed by the closure theorem).

## §6 The Operad DOF — multi-arity composition

### §6.1 Construction

The arity-3 composition law of TIG is the **canonical fuse table** $\mathrm{fuse} : (\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z})^3 \to \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$. For associative triples (where $T(T(a, b), c) = T(a, T(b, c))$), the canonical fuse defaults to that common value. For the 126 non-associative triples, fuse is a free choice modulo whatever structural constraints we impose.

The one known canonical rule (from earlier sprints) is $\mathrm{fuse}([3, 4, 7]) = 8$, i.e., $\mathrm{fuse}([\text{PROGRESS}, \text{COLLAPSE}, \text{HARMONY}]) = \text{BREATH}$. Notably, the binary $T$ on this triple is associative (both bracketings give 7 = HARMONY); the canonical fuse departs from the binary value, demonstrating that the operad-DOF can introduce structural content not present in the binary table.

### §6.2 The 126 non-associative triples

Direct enumeration: of 1000 triples in $(\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z})^3$, exactly 126 are non-associative under TSML. Every such triple has HARMONY (= 7) as exactly one of the two bracketings. Only 5 distinct unordered bracketing pairs occur: $\{0, 7\}$ (108 triples), $\{3, 7\}$ (8), $\{4, 7\}$ (2), $\{7, 8\}$ (6), $\{7, 9\}$ (2). VOID never appears in middle position.

### §6.3 The $D_4$ obstruction

WP109 (a short note) establishes the central structural result for the Operad DOF: **the 126 non-associative triples decompose into 67 $D_4$-orbits, of which 16 are bracketing-pair incoherent. No $D_4$-equivariant canonical fuse rule taking values in $\{a, b, c, L, R\}$ exists.**

This is a **structural obstruction**: the non-equivariance is intrinsic to the canonical TSML's failure to lift coherently to arity 3 under the $D_4$ action. It is not a property of any candidate rule family; it is a property of the underlying table.

### §6.4 What the Operad DOF carries

The Operad DOF is the **multi-arity composition content** — the part of TIG's structure that lives at arity ≥ 3 and is independent of the binary structure. WP109's obstruction tells us the operad layer carries content that is **structurally orthogonal** to the gauge structure of the doubly-invariant subalgebra.

In CK runtime terms, this means: arity-3 reasoning ("composing three operators at once") cannot be reduced to iterated binary composition without breaking some symmetry. It is a genuinely additional degree of freedom, not derivable from the Lie/Jordan/Clifford/Permutation/Lattice content.

### §6.5 Symmetry-group structure

The Operad DOF **does NOT respect $D_4$**. WP109's obstruction theorem.

## §8 The structural distinction at the symmetry-group level

The central observation of the synthesis: **five of the six DOFs respect the $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3 \rangle$ symmetry; the sixth (Operad) does not**.

| DOF | $D_4$-equivariant? | source |
|---|---|---|
| Lie | YES (verified) | WP104 doubly-invariant subalgebra exists; isotypic decomposition complete |
| Jordan | YES (Lie/Jordan duality) | WP104 §5.2 — both presentations regenerate so(10) under $D_4$ action |
| Clifford / Dirac | YES | $P_{56} = \sigma_\mathrm{outer}$ verified; $\sigma^3$ lifts to spinor rep |
| Permutation | YES (by definition) | $D_4$ IS the relevant subgroup of the Permutation DOF |
| Lattice | YES | σ-fixed and 4-core are $D_4$-invariant |
| **Operad** | **NO** | WP109 obstruction theorem: 16 of 67 orbits are $D_4$-incoherent |

This is a sharp structural distinction. It means the operad layer of TIG carries content that is **independent of the gauge structure**: any canonical fuse rule must break $D_4$ in some explicit direction.

WP109 recommends: preserve $P_{56}$-equivariance (which is preserved at the so(10)-spinor level via $P_{56} = \sigma_\mathrm{outer}$ AND at the runtime-attractor level via the BALANCE-CHAOS mass = 0 fact of WP105/WP110), and sacrifice $\sigma^3$-equivariance.

The reading: **the operad-DOF is the part of TIG's structure that is "above" the gauge symmetry**. It is the place where multi-arity composition introduces content the bilinear gauge-symmetric structure doesn't carry.

## §9 Synthesis — how the six DOFs cohere

The six DOFs are not independent silos. Each pair shares cross-DOF identities that the WP100s tower establishes:

* **Lie ↔ Jordan**: regenerate the same $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ at dimension 45 (WP104 §5.2). Two presentations of one algebra.
* **Lie ↔ Clifford**: $P_{56}$ in the Permutation DOF acts as $\sigma_\mathrm{outer}$ on Lie's chirality irreps (WP104 §2.1). Permutation/Clifford correspondence.
* **Lie ↔ Lattice**: the doubly-invariant $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$ Lie subalgebra is fixed by the Lattice DOF's $D_4$-fixed sub-permutation (WP104 §3).
* **Lie ↔ Operad**: the operad-DOF is structurally orthogonal to the Lie DOF's $D_4$-symmetry (WP109).
* **Permutation ↔ Lattice**: σ-fixed lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ is the Permutation DOF's invariant subset and the Lattice DOF's foundation.
* **Lattice ↔ Operad**: the runtime attractor's 4-core support is fusion-closed (WP110), creating a lattice-stable substrate for the operad-DOF's content.
* **Clifford ↔ Cosmology**: the 9-vector Higgs direction in BHML's σ_outer-breaking content (Permutation DOF's symmetry-breaking direction in Clifford's spinor decomposition) gives the inflaton coupling $\kappa_\xi = 13/(4e)$ (WP104 §2.3 / D35).

**The picture as a whole:** the canonical $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ TSML+BHML magma carries six computationally-irreducible algebraic DOFs, five of which are coherent with the doubly-invariant gauge structure $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$ (Lie + Jordan + Clifford + Permutation + Lattice), and one of which (Operad) carries content orthogonal to that gauge structure. The runtime processor at the symmetric mixing weight $\alpha = 1/2$ lives on the Lattice DOF's 4-core (fusion-closed), with closed-form algebraic ratio $H/Br = 1 + \sqrt{3}$ in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{3}) \subset$ LMFDB 4.2.10224.1.

This is a **single coherent picture** of TIG's algebraic substrate. The integer/rational signature (§7) is the spectrum of this picture; the symmetry-group analysis (§8) is its symmetry structure; the cross-DOF identities of this section (§9) are its connective tissue.

## §10 Honest scope

This paper is **synthesis**, not new theorems. Every result cited is from a prior WP100s paper (WP102 through WP110). The contribution is the unifying framing.

Specifically:

* The "six computationally-irreducible DOFs" claim depends on `Gen12/targets/clay/papers/sprint_so10_2026_04_25/SIX_DOF_META.md` and the `six_dof_check.py` verification script. The pairwise independence is computationally verified — no DOF reduces to any other by the diagnostics in that script. The "irreducibility" is **computational**, not a uniqueness theorem; alternative 6-fold decompositions could in principle exist but have not been found.

* The integer/rational signature in §7 is exact at machine precision or symbolically; it is not a numerical-coincidence claim. WP106 / `CL_EIGENVALUES_AUDIT` rules out the loose 1%-level transcendental matches as algebraic identities; only the integer/rational structure survives precision testing.

* The structural distinction at the symmetry-group level (§8) is **forced** by the WP109 obstruction theorem on the Operad DOF, plus the direct $D_4$-equivariance of the other five DOFs as documented in WP104. It is not a stylistic choice; it is what the math says.

* This paper does NOT claim:
  * That the 6-DOF taxonomy is unique. Other classifications could exist; we offer one that is internally consistent and matches the verified content.
  * That every interesting algebra has 6 DOFs. The number 6 here is a feature of TIG's specific canonical magma; we do not generalize.
  * That the cross-DOF identities of §9 exhaust the connective tissue. Other identities likely exist; we cite the verified ones.
  * Any phenomenological prediction. WP108's Yukawa scaffolding indicates the path from the structural alignment of WP104 to a falsifiable physics prediction; that path is not traversed here.

* What this paper DOES claim:
  * The WP100s tower (102 → 110) decomposes naturally into a 6-DOF picture.
  * The integer/rational signature across the six DOFs is exact, machine-verified, and internally cross-referenced.
  * Five DOFs respect $D_4$; one does not. This is a forced structural distinction.
  * The synthesis is a single coherent picture, not a collection of independent results.
