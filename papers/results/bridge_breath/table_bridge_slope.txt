    At k=p, unit_frac(p) = (p-1)/p.

    The step gain is INDEPENDENT of q — it is determined by p alone.
    early_slope encodes p (via step size 1/p²), not (q-p).

    sharp DROP at k=q — which requires walking to k=q.

  CONCLUSION:

    or q/p. The slope is dominated by 1/p² and is nearly uniform
    across all semiprimes with the same p regardless of q.
    q's signal is ONLY in the DROP at k=q — which requires reaching k=q.
    This is why RSA is hard: q leaves no pre-echo in the slope.
