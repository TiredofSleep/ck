## The Three Strongest Recurrences

### Role 7: Attractor — clearest recurrence across all three levels

**Table level (COMPUTED):** HAR=7 carries all stationary mass; TSML is at the 100th percentile in HAR_mass across the 3000-sample family. The attractor role is the decisive selector.

**Family level (COMPUTED):** HAR_mass is the selector that distinguishes TSML from all other family members. Gap is not the selector — 92% of the family exceeds TSML's gap. The "winner" in the family is the HAR_mass-maximal table.

**Size level (COMPUTED):** At n=2 and n=3, HAR_mass = 1.0 trivially for all tables — the attractor role is degenerate. At n=4, HAR_mass first varies (0.33–0.56) and competition begins. The attractor role becomes non-trivial exactly at the emergence threshold.

**The recurrence:** The attractor is what carries mass, wins selection, and resists deformation — at every level. The attractor role does not change meaning; it scales.

---

### Role 8: Breath — deformation and scaling rhythm

**Table level (COMPUTED):** Mix_λ deformation traces a specific profile:
- λ=0.0: γ=0.750, HAR_mass=0.829
- λ=0.4: γ=0.850, HAR_mass=0.800
- λ=0.6: γ=0.627, HAR_mass=0.726
- λ=1.0: γ=0.458, HAR_mass=0.305

The table breathes — gap rises then falls, HAR_mass decays monotonically.

**Family level (COMPUTED):** gap_stability = min(γ across all λ) = 0.458. This is the family-level "breath minimum" — the tightest point in the deformation cycle. All family members breath; TSML's breath minimum is the highest in the family (100th percentile in gap_stability).

**Size level (STRUCTURAL):** n-scaling from 4→9 shows monotone growth of selector dimensionality — a different kind of breath, across size rather than λ.

**The recurrence:** Breath is the scaling rhythm — whether along λ, along N, or along n. The same role (periodic rise and fall of structure) appears at each level.

### Role 3: Harmony — orbit pairs and coupled motifs

**Table level (EXACT + COMPUTED):** The {3,9} two-cycle: F(3,9)=3 and F(9,3)=3. States 3 and 9 beat coherently against each other before collapsing to HAR=7. Orbit burst exponent +1.49 in the near-critical regime.

**Family level (COMPUTED):** HAR_mass ⊥ BHML_residual (r=−0.003): the two key selectors are harmonically coupled at zero — nearly perfectly independent, and TSML sits at the conjunction of both. The "harmonic pair" at the family level is not two states beating coherently, but two selector axes aligned at the rare conjunction.

**Size level (COMPUTED):** The orbit/transit distinction first appears at n=4. Below n=4, G is too small for an orbit zone to form — harmony is not yet possible.

**The recurrence:** Harmony is the coupling between things that could be independent but are not — whether between two states in an orbit, two selector axes in conjunction, or two levels of structure at a size threshold.

## Roles With Partial or Weak Evidence

**Role 4 (Structure):** The forced-cell backbone (24/81 cells) is exact at the table level. The constraint set I1–I13 forcing 75/81 cells is computed. The free/forced ratio changing with n is structural. This role recurs, but the evidence is tighter at table level than at size level.

**Role 6 (Control/gate):** The one-way gate C→G is exact at the table level. The gap_stability threshold is computed at the family level. The n=4 threshold is computed. This role recurs concretely.

**Role 9 (Fruit):** TSML as the rare conjunction is computed (325/10,000). The five archetypes are computed. The size-level claim that n=9 produces TSML-like richness is structural — not yet computed across n=5,6,7,8.

**Roles 0, 1, 2, 5:** Present as structural analogies. Role 2 (TSML/BHML duality) is exact at the table level. Roles 0 and 5 have no clean computation mapping to them across levels. Role 1 (arithmetic hook) is exact at the table level but weak at family and size levels.

## What Is Not Being Claimed

- That all ten roles are forced by the data
- That the recurrence is exact rather than structural
- That TIG "causes" the family structure at higher levels
- That this is anything other than a pattern worth testing

The question is whether the same role-structure recurs. The answer, for roles 3, 6, 7, 8, 9, is: yes, with computed evidence at two or more levels. For roles 0, 1, 2, 5: the concept recurs but the evidence is structural or conjectural.

## The Most Obvious Recurrence Right Now

**Role 7 (Attractor)** is the clearest. HAR dominance at the table level, HAR_mass extremality at the family level, and the n=4 emergence threshold at the size level — three different expressions of the same structural fact: the dominant support carrier defines the level, and competition for that role only begins when the algebra is large enough to support it.

The second-clearest is **Role 6 (Control/gate)**. The one-way gate at the table level, the corridor threshold structure at the family level, and the n=4 degenerate/non-degenerate boundary at the size level are all different expressions of the same idea: some things are blocked, and the gate is what enforces it.
