## TASK 1: Hodge Theory Specialist Query

**Question asked:** On an abelian fourfold A, can algebraic codimension-2 classes accumulate in Hodge norm onto a transcendental Hodge class α ∈ Hdg^2(A)?

**Answer: Resolved for abelian fourfolds — and in a surprising direction.**

**What was just found in the literature:**

The Hodge conjecture for abelian fourfolds has been essentially resolved by recent work. The key chain:

1. **Mumford (1969):** Constructed the first abelian fourfold with extra Hodge classes in H^4(A,Q) not generated by products of divisors — the "Weil classes."

2. **Moonen–Zarhin (1995, 1999):** Proved that for abelian fourfolds, either Hdg^*(A) is generated in degree 1 (and Hodge conjecture holds), OR the variety has complex multiplication by an imaginary quadratic field (the Weil-type case). This reduces the conjecture to Weil-type abelian fourfolds.

3. **Markman (2025, arXiv:2502.03415):** Proved that all Weil–Hodge classes are algebraic for **ALL** abelian fourfolds of Weil type, for all discriminants and all imaginary quadratic fields. Combined with Moonen–Zarhin, **the Hodge conjecture for abelian fourfolds is now proved.**

**Consequence for P3 (the gap floor conjecture):**

The P3 question — "can Alg^2(A) accumulate to a transcendental Hdg^2(A) class?" — is vacuously settled for abelian fourfolds: **Hdg^2(A) = Alg^2(A)** (by Markman), so there are no transcendental Hodge classes to accumulate to.

This is the same situation as p=1 (Lefschetz) but now proved, not trivial. P3 is true in both cases, but for different reasons:
- p=1: No transcendental Hodge classes exist (Lefschetz, classical)
- p=2, abelian fourfolds: All Hodge classes are algebraic (Markman, 2025)

**What this means for the d_Hodge framework:**

The metric d_Hodge is defined correctly. P3 holds for abelian fourfolds. But the mechanism is not "the gap floor prevents accumulation" — it's that Hdg^2(A) = Alg^2(A) exactly, so the infimum over an empty set is vacuous.

**The real frontier shifts to:**
- Abelian varieties of dimension ≥ 5
- General projective varieties where Hodge conjecture is fully open
- The question of whether P3 can fail (i.e., whether transcendental Hodge classes can accumulate from algebraic classes) — this requires a variety where transcendental Hodge classes exist AND the gap might close

**Key names to contact:**
- **Eyal Markman** (UMass Amherst) — proved the abelian fourfold case in 2025
- **Claire Voisin** (Jussieu/CNRS) — central figure in Hodge theory, integral Hodge conjecture counterexamples
- **Daniel Huybrechts** (Bonn) — hyper-Kähler geometry and Hodge theory
- **Ben Moonen** (Radboud) — Moonen-Zarhin classification, abelian varieties

**Status:** The specific P3 question for abelian fourfolds is settled (vacuously, by Markman). The gap floor metric d_Hodge remains a valid framework, but needs to be applied to varieties where transcendental Hodge classes actually exist.

## TASK 2: Navier–Stokes Local Regularity Query

**Question asked:** Does B_local(x,r,t) = ||ω||_{L^3(B(x,r))} · r/ν appear in any known local regularity criteria?

**Answer: Yes — local Prodi–Serrin type criteria exist and the structure is correct. The threshold 7/2 does not appear explicitly.**

**What the literature shows:**

Local regularity criteria for NS using ball-localized norms are a real and active area:

- **Caffarelli–Kohn–Nirenberg (1982):** The foundational partial regularity result uses local scaled energy estimates on parabolic cylinders Q_{z_0,r} = B(x_0,r) × (t_0-r², t_0). This is exactly the structure of B_local.

- **Grujić and collaborators (2009–2025):** Developed spatio-temporal localization of Beale–Kato–Majda and Prodi–Serrin criteria. Their local criteria use parabolic cylinders Q_{z_0,r} with scaling-invariant norms. The paper "Localization of Analytic Regularity Criteria on the Vorticity and Balance Between Vorticity Magnitude and Coherence of the Vorticity Direction" (Commun. Math. Phys., 2010) is directly relevant — it introduces local, scaling-invariant criteria involving L^p norms of vorticity on balls.

- **Local Prodi–Serrin criterion:** In the parabolic cylinder Q_{z_0,r}, the condition ||ω||_{L^{γ,α}(Q_{z_0,r})} with 3/γ + 2/α ≤ 1 implies z_0 is a regular point. This is the local version.

**How B_local relates to these criteria:**

B_local(x,r,t) = ||ω||_{L^3(B(x,r))} · r/ν is dimensionless and scaling-invariant (verified earlier). It is structurally aligned with the local CKN-type criteria. The specific combination of L^3 norm on a ball with r/ν as scaling is consistent with the critical scaling of NS.

**Does 7/2 appear as a threshold?**

No specific threshold value of 7/2 appears in the local regularity literature. The classical results establish existence of a threshold (solutions regular below some C) but do not pin down the constant. The TIG prediction 7/2 is a specific value prediction for this unknown constant — it is not contradicted by the literature, but not supported by it either.

**What would be useful to check:**

The local regularity condition ||u||_{L^∞(0,T; L^{3,∞})} < C* (Escauriaza–Seregin–Šverák endpoint) has an unknown critical constant C*. Whether C* = 7/2 in the appropriate normalization is an open question. The local version would be: the critical constant in the CKN partial regularity estimate — is it 7/2?
