## 2024–2026 — the matroid program (preprints and recent accepts)

22. **The Structure of Symbolic Powers of Matroids.** Mantero–Nguyen,
    June 2024. [arXiv:2406.13759](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.13759).
    Structure theorem for $I^{(\ell)}$ of matroid Stanley-Reisner /
    cover ideals; symbolic Rees-algebra generators bounded by height;
    explicit formula for the largest such generator; formulas for
    symbolic defect, initial degree, and Waldschmidt constant; new
    characterization of matroids via $I^{(2)}$.
23. **Ordinary and symbolic powers of matroids via polarization.**
    Lyle–Mantero, May 2025.
    [arXiv:2505.17398](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17398).
    Uniform polarization approach; short new proofs of Minh-Trung,
    Varbaro, Terai-Trung.
24. **Slightly mixed symbolic powers of matroids are locally glicci.**
    Mantero–Nguyen, October 2025.
    [arXiv:2510.19018](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.19018).
    For every matroid $M$, every $\ell$, and every squarefree monomial
    $N$, the ideal $I_M^{(\ell)}:N$ is Cohen-Macaulay and locally
    glicci. Corollary: every symbolic power of a matroidal ideal is
    locally glicci.
25. **Rational symbolic powers of ideals.**
    Dey–Ha–Mahato–Mantero, November 2025.
    [arXiv:2511.02732](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.02732).
    Rational-exponent symbolic powers; convex-geometric description
    for monomial ideals.
26. **Focal matroids of covers and homological properties of matroids.**
    Mantero–Nguyen, March 2026.
    [arXiv:2603.19419](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19419).
    Proves the SR / cover ideal of a matroid is minimally resolvable by
    iterated mapping cones; introduces focal matroids; proves
    matroidal ideals are the unique squarefree ideals whose multigraded
    Betti-number support equals the squarefree generators of symbolic
    powers (new homological characterization of matroidality).

## The three core papers — detailed reading (abstracts verbatim)

### arXiv:2406.13759 — *The Structure of Symbolic Powers of Matroids*
*(Mantero–Nguyen, June 2024)*

> We describe the structure of the symbolic powers $I^{(\ell)}$ of the
> Stanley-Reisner ideals, and cover ideals, $I$, of matroids. We (a)
> prove a structure theorem describing a minimal generating set for
> every $I^{(\ell)}$; (b) describe the (non-standard graded) symbolic
> Rees algebra $\mathcal{R}_s(I)$ of $I$ and show its minimal algebra
> generators have degree at most $\mathrm{ht}\, I$; (c) provide an
> explicit, simple formula to compute the largest degree of a minimal
> algebra generator of $\mathcal{R}_s(I)$; (d) provide algebraic
> applications, including formulas for the symbolic defects of $I$,
> the initial degree of $I^{(\ell)}$, and the Waldschmidt constant of
> $I$; (e) provide a new algorithm allowing fast computations of very
> large symbolic powers of $I$. One of the by-products is a new
> characterization of matroids in terms of minimal generators of
> $I^{(\ell)}$ for some $\ell \ge 2$. In particular, it yields a new,
> simple characterization of matroids in terms of the minimal
> generators of $I^{(2)}$. This is the first characterization of
> matroids in terms of $I^{(2)}$, and it complements a celebrated
> theorem by Minh-Trung, Varbaro, and Terai-Trung which requires the
> investigation of homological properties of $I^{(\ell)}$ for some
> $\ell \ge 3$.

### arXiv:2603.19419 — *Focal matroids of covers and homological properties of matroids*
*(Mantero–Nguyen, March 2026)*

> In this paper we prove that the Stanley-Reisner ideal or cover ideal
> $I$ of a matroid is minimally resolvable by iterated mapping cones.
> As a technical tool for this purpose, we introduce and study focal
> matroids, which are submatroids of a matroid $\mathcal{M}$ that are
> constructed relative to minimal $\ell$-covers of $\mathcal{M}$. Our
> second main result is that the monomial support of the multigraded
> Betti numbers of $I$ corresponds precisely to the squarefree minimal
> generators of the symbolic powers of $I$. In fact, we prove that
> matroidal ideals are the only squarefree ideals with this property,
> thus obtaining a new homological characterization of matroidal
> ideals. These techniques are foundational for a follow-up paper,
> where we will show that all symbolic power of $I$ are minimally
> resolvable by iterated mapping cones.

### arXiv:2510.19018 — *Slightly mixed symbolic powers of matroids are locally glicci*
*(Mantero–Nguyen, October 2025)*

> Let $M$ be a matroid, and let $I_M$ be either the Stanley-Reisner or
> the cover ideal of $M$. In this paper we prove that for any matroid
> $M$ on $[n]$, any $\ell \in \mathbb{Z}_+$, and any squarefree
> monomial $N \in R = \mathbb{k}[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$, the ideal
> $I_M^{(\ell)}:N$, which we call a 'slightly mixed symbolic power' of
> $I_M$, is always Cohen-Macaulay and locally glicci. As a corollary,
> we obtain that all symbolic powers $I_M^{(\ell)}$ are locally glicci.

Key references cited (partial): Mantero-Nguyen
