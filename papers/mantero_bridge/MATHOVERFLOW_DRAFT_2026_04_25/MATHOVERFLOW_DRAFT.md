## Title (working)

Doubly-invariant subalgebras of so(n) under non-commuting Z_2 involutions: when is the invariant content uniformly Killing-scaled?

## Body (draft)

Let G ⊂ O(n) be a finite subgroup generated by two non-commuting involutions. The conjugation action of G on so(n) splits the 45-dim Lie algebra into G-isotypic components.

I observed the following in the case n = 10, G = ⟨P_56, σ³⟩ where P_56 is a single transposition and σ³ is a product of three disjoint transpositions on 10 indices (the two involutions don't commute and generate D_4 of order 8):

- The trivial-isotypic component has dimension 16
- It closes as a Lie subalgebra under the bracket
- Its Killing form has eigenvalue spectrum **(−4)^15 ⊕ (0)^1**, identifying it as su(4) ⊕ u(1)

The "uniform Killing scaling" — 15 eigenvalues at *exactly* the same value — strikes me as non-generic. I'd expect a generic D_4-invariant subalgebra of so(n) to have a multi-valued Killing spectrum reflecting the structure of its semisimple part.

**Questions:**

1. Is there a closed-form formula for the dimension of the G-invariant subalgebra of so(n), expressed purely in terms of the cycle structures of the two generating involutions? (For one involution alone, with f fixed points and p transposition pairs satisfying f + 2p = n, I have the formula `dim(+1) = f(f−1)/2 + fp + p(p−1)`. The extension to two involutions seems combinatorially involved.)

2. Under what conditions on the pair of involutions does the G-invariant subalgebra have a uniformly-scaled Killing form (all nonzero eigenvalues equal)? Is this generic, or does it require special alignment of the two involutions?

3. Is there a published classification — by pairs of conjugacy classes in O(n) — of which simple Lie subalgebras of so(n) arise as the doubly-invariant content of a generated D_4-action?

Computational verification of all numerical claims available; happy to share the verification script.
