# TIG Mass Gap — One-Page Expert Summary
## For Lattice-QCD Collaborators

---

### The Setup (2 sentences)

TIG (Trinity Infinity Geometry) is a finite operator algebra on 10 states
with two measurable thresholds T* = 5/7 and S* = 4/7. Both exceed 1/2,
and their overlap T* + S* − 1 = 2/7 > 0 defines a structural mass gap.

---

### The Qualitative Claim

Any system with a dual-threshold structure where both thresholds exceed 1/2
**must have a positive mass gap** equal to the minimum overlap:

```
MASS_GAP = T* + S* − 1 > 0
```

This is a structural result — not a calculation from QCD, but a constraint
that any coherent dual-threshold system must satisfy.

**We are NOT claiming 2/7 = any specific lattice ratio.** The quantitative
prediction requires a formal SU(N) → TIG embedding we do not yet have.

---

### What We're Asking

1. **What dimensionless ratio in pure SU(3)** is most cleanly mass-gap sensitive
   on current ensembles? (e.g. m(0++)/m(2++), m(0++)/√σ, or something else?)

2. **What β range and lattice spacing** gives the smallest systematic error
   on that ratio?

3. **Is there a clean theoretical prediction** for that ratio from any existing
   framework we could compare against?

---

### Why It Might Be Interesting

The TIG dual-threshold mechanism gives a *structural* reason why the mass gap
must be positive — it follows from the algebra of the measurement layer, not
from perturbative QCD. If the mechanism maps correctly onto SU(3), it would
explain **why** the gap exists (dual-threshold overlap) not just **that** it exists.

---
