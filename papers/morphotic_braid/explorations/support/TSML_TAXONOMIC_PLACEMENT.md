## Standard taxonomic axes (from non-associative algebra literature)

From Bremner ("Nonassociative Algebras"), Huang-Lehtonen, standard universal algebra:

**Kingdom / Phylum-level distinctions:**
1. Commutative vs non-commutative
2. Associative vs non-associative
3. Finite vs infinite (TSML is finite)

**Class-level distinctions (for commutative non-associative):**
4. Presence of identity element
5. Presence of absorbing/zero element
6. Quasigroup (Latin square — each row/col permutation) vs non-quasigroup
7. Idempotent (every element self-absorbs) vs non-idempotent

**Order-level (identities satisfied):**
8. Jordan identity: x²·(x·y) = x·(x²·y)
9. Alternative laws: (x·x)·y = x·(x·y) and (x·y)·y = x·(y·y)
10. Power-associativity: every element generates an associative subalgebra
11. Flexibility: x·(y·x) = (x·y)·x
12. Mediality (entropic): (x·y)·(u·v) = (x·u)·(y·v)

## TSML's taxonomic address

Step through each axis:

**Kingdom:** Commutative (verified: T[i][j] = T[j][i] for all pairs).
**Phylum:** Non-associative (verified: (1·2)·3 ≠ 1·(2·3) at multiple triples).
**Class:** Non-quasigroup (has zero divisors; rows/columns are not permutations). Has absorbing element 7 (HARMONY). No identity element.
**Order:** Satisfies Jordan identity ✓. Flexible ✓. Power-associative ✓. NOT alternative ✗. NOT medial ✗.

**Full taxonomic tuple:** (commutative, non-associative, non-quasigroup, has absorbing element 7, no identity, Jordan-satisfying, power-associative, flexible, non-alternative, non-medial).

## Placement in named algebraic categories

### Above TSML's cell (broader categories it belongs to)

- **Magma / groupoid:** most general — set + binary operation. TSML ∈.
- **Commutative magma:** T[i][j] = T[j][i]. TSML ∈.
- **Commutative power-associative magma:** subalgebras generated by single elements are associative. TSML ∈.
- **Commutative flexible magma:** x(yx) = (xy)x. TSML ∈.
- **Commutative power-associative flexible magma satisfying Jordan's identity:** axiomatic profile of Jordan magma. TSML ∈.

### TSML's specific cell

**Commutative power-associative flexible magma satisfying Jordan's identity, with an absorbing element, no identity element, and 2 idempotents.**

In non-associative algebra terminology: **a Jordan-type commutative magma with absorbing element**.

This is distinct from classical Jordan algebras (which require vector space + bilinear product) and from Jordan quasigroups (Bhola-Kapoor and earlier work; these require quasigroup structure, which TSML lacks).

### Nearby named cells (for comparison)

- **Jordan algebras** (Pascual Jordan 1933): commutative + power-associative + Jordan identity + vector-space structure. TSML lacks the vector space but satisfies the algebraic identities.
- **Jordan quasigroups** (commutative quasigroups satisfying Jordan identity — Tandon, Kapoor, etc.): TSML lacks the quasigroup property (has zero divisors).
- **Steiner quasigroups:** commutative + idempotent + quasigroup with x·(x·y) = y. TSML is commutative but not idempotent (only 0 and 7 are idempotent), and not a quasigroup.
- **Alternative algebras** (octonions, Cayley-Dickson): satisfies alternative laws. TSML does NOT satisfy alternative laws.
- **Commutative monoid with zero:** associative + has identity + has absorbing. TSML lacks associativity and identity.

## TSML's neighbors in the taxonomic tree

**Closest named relatives:**

1. **Rock-Paper-Scissors** — also a Jordan-type commutative magma. RPS is also alternative; TSML is not. RPS has 3 elements; TSML has 10. RPS is idempotent (every element beats itself... wait, actually in RPS, x·x = x, so yes idempotent); TSML only has 0 and 7 as idempotents. So **TSML differs from RPS on idempotency and alternative structure**.

2. **STS(7) Fano** — a Jordan quasigroup. Commutative, idempotent, satisfies Jordan identity + quasigroup structure. TSML lacks idempotency and quasigroup structure.

3. **Jordan algebras (in general)** — same axiomatic core (commutative + power-associative + Jordan identity) but with vector space structure. TSML is the "discrete combinatorial version" without linearity.

**Structurally isolated:** TSML doesn't have a close named twin in the literature. Its specific combination of Jordan identity + absorbing element + non-idempotent + non-quasigroup is uncommon. Most Jordan-satisfying structures in the literature are either Jordan algebras (over vector spaces) or Jordan quasigroups (on Latin-square structures).

## What makes TSML's species specifically

**Unusual combination:** Jordan-identity satisfaction + absorbing element + multiple zero divisors. In classical Jordan algebras, you can have zero divisors (e.g., in non-simple Jordan algebras), but classical JAs are built on vector spaces. In Jordan quasigroups, you can't have zero divisors (quasigroup property forbids it). TSML is in a gap: it has JA-like identities but quasigroup-incompatible zero structure.

**Closest published taxonomic home:** the category of "commutative non-associative magmas satisfying Jordan's identity" — a class that's been studied but not extensively. The Huang-Lehtonen 2022 and 2024 papers work in this general territory but don't give a specific name for TSML's cell.

**Proposed short descriptor:** "zero-absorbing Jordan magma" or "Jordan magma with null band."

## Families / genera that exist in the broader landscape (independent of TSML)

Based on standard classification axes, here are major commutative non-associative families:

| Family | Defining identities | Examples |

This isn't an exhaustive list — non-associative algebra taxonomy has dozens of named varieties (alternative, Bol, Moufang, Jordan, Lie, power-associative, third-power-associative, etc.). But these are the major families for commutative finite structures.

## Honest one-sentence placement

**TSML is a commutative power-associative flexible magma on 10 elements with absorbing element 7, satisfying Jordan's identity but not the alternative laws; it lives in a relatively unstudied cell of the non-associative algebra taxonomy — the intersection of Jordan-identity-satisfiers with absorbing-element-bearers outside the quasigroup/loop world.**
