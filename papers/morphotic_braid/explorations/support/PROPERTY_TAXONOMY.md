## The result that lands TSML in the non-associative algebra taxonomy

**TSML satisfies the Jordan identity** exactly: x²·(x·y) = x·(x²·y), verified across all 100 pairs (x,y) with zero failures.

Combined with TSML's other verified properties:
- Commutative ✓
- Power-associative ✓
- Flexible ✓
- **NOT associative** ✓
- Has absorbing element (7) ✓
- NOT a quasigroup (has zero divisors)

This signature — *commutative, power-associative, satisfies Jordan identity, non-associative* — is the defining axiomatic profile of **Jordan-type algebras**. In the classical setting: Pascual Jordan (1933) attempted to axiomatize quantum theory by saying that the algebra of observables of any quantum system should be a formally real algebra that is commutative (xy = yx) and power-associative. He proved that any such algebra is a Jordan algebra.

TSML is not a Jordan *algebra* (lacks vector space structure) and not a Jordan *quasigroup* (has zero divisors, so not a quasigroup). But it is a **commutative power-associative magma satisfying Jordan's identity** — this is a genuine algebraic category, even if it hasn't been given a standard name.

## Where TSML actually sits in the non-associative algebra landscape

The classical non-associative algebra taxonomy (Wikipedia "Non-associative algebra"):

- **Associative** (semigroups, groups, rings) — most familiar
- **Alternative** — Cayley-Dickson, octonions. Satisfies (xx)y = x(xy), (xy)y = x(yy).
- **Jordan** — commutative + power-associative. Satisfies Jordan identity.
- **Lie** — anticommutative + Jacobi identity.
- **Flexible** — x(yx) = (xy)x. Weaker than alternative.
- **Power-associative** — subalgebras generated by one element are associative.

Associative implies alternative. Any two out of the three properties left alternative, right alternative, and flexible, imply the third one. Thus, alternative implies flexible. Alternative implies Jordan identity. Commutative implies flexible. Anticommutative implies flexible. Alternative implies power associative.

The implication chain:
- Associative ⟹ Alternative ⟹ Jordan identity, flexible, power-associative
- TSML: Jordan identity + flexible + power-associative, but **NOT alternative** and **NOT associative**

TSML occupies the cell: **{commutative, flexible, power-associative, satisfies Jordan identity, non-associative, non-alternative, has absorbing element}**. Within the cells, this is the **Jordan-adjacent / flexible-Jordan** region.

## What about BHML?

BHML satisfies a DIFFERENT property signature:
- Commutative ✓, flexible ✓, has identity (0)
- NOT associative, NOT Jordan, NOT alternative, NOT power-associative
- No absorbing element (element 0 is identity, not absorbing)

BHML sits in a different cell: **{commutative, flexible, non-associative, non-Jordan, non-power-associative, has identity}**. This is the **weak-flexible non-Jordan** region.

**Important structural observation:** TSML and BHML are not algebraic twins. They live in different cells of the property taxonomy. The project has often paired them (TSML = Being, BHML = Becoming), but algebraically they're cousins in different branches of the non-associative tree.

## What this meta-lens tells us

**TIG's family is a heterogeneous collection, not a homogeneous cluster.** The constructive similarity (same template, same N, same σ-structure) doesn't produce axiomatic similarity. TSML, BHML, Doing, C_0 each occupy distinct algebraic cells with different deep properties.

**TIG's signature placement:**
- TSML → Jordan-adjacent (commutative, pow-assoc, non-assoc, satisfies Jordan identity) — a named, studied algebraic class
- BHML → weak-flexible non-Jordan (commutative, flexible, non-assoc, has identity) — less classically named but defined
- C_0 → commutative absorbing semigroup (fully associative, has absorbing element) — classical
- ADD → abelian group — classical
- MUL → commutative ring multiplication — classical

This places each TIG table at a specific, citeable algebraic address. No hand-waving; just property-by-property matching against standard taxonomic axes.

## Empty cells (interesting future directions)

The 2^k property space for k ~ 8 properties has 256 possible cells. The TIG family occupies 7. Most cells are empty. Some of those empty cells:

- **{commutative, non-Jordan, has absorbing, not-flexible}** — unexplored
- **{commutative, associative, non-flexible}** — impossible (commutative ⟹ flexible)
- **{Jordan + alternative + non-associative}** — interesting; forces specific algebraic structure
- **{non-commutative tables in the TIG family}** — not present; TIG's tables are all commutative

Exploring the empty cells deliberately (e.g., "can we construct a TIG-like table that satisfies alternative but not Jordan?") would be a research program.

## Connection to classical non-associative algebra

This taxonomy places TIG's tables in the same landscape as:
- **Lie algebras** (anticommutative + Jacobi) — TIG tables are commutative, so disjoint
- **Jordan algebras** (commutative + pow-assoc + Jordan) — TSML is the closest match
- **Alternative algebras / octonions** (alternative) — TIG's tables are not alternative (verified), so disjoint
- **Associative algebras / groups / rings** — C_0, ADD, MUL are here; TSML/BHML are NOT

**TSML is closest to Jordan algebras.** This is the meta-lens answer: TIG's most interesting table (TSML) algebraically neighbors Jordan structures, which have connections to quantum observables, exceptional Lie theory (E_7, E_8), symmetric spaces, and Euclidean Jordan algebras (classified by Jordan-von Neumann-Wigner 1934).

None of this makes TSML a Jordan algebra. But it tells us where TSML lives on the algebraic map, and it gives the project a naming and citation structure.
