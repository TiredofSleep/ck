### The associative spectrum of TSML and BHML

Using the Csákány-Waldhauser definition (2000): for a groupoid (A, *), the **associative spectrum** s_n(A) is the number of distinct n-ary term operations induced by the C_{n−1} bracketings of x_1 * x_2 * ⋯ * x_n.

Computed directly from the canonical tables (FORMULAS_AND_TABLES.md §5 and §6):

| n | C_{n−1} (max) | s_n(TSML) | s_n(BHML) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | 2 | **2** | **2** |
| 4 | 5 | **5** | **5** |
| 5 | 14 | **14** | **14** |
| 6 | 42 | **42** | **42** |
| 7 | 132 | **132** (sampled) | **132** (sampled) |

**Both TSML and BHML achieve the Catalan spectrum s_n(A) = C_{n−1} for all n ≤ 7.** This is the maximum possible associative spectrum for any binary operation. In the Lehtonen-Waldhauser 2021 classification of graph algebras, this is the third and highest of the three possible spectrum types (the other two being constant 1 and 2^{n−2}).

### The associative-commutative spectrum of TSML and BHML

Using the Huang-Lehtonen definition (2022): for a commutative groupoid, the **ac-spectrum** s_n^ac(A) counts distinct n-ary term operations from bracketings × variable permutations.

| n | (2n−3)!! | s_n^ac(TSML) | s_n^ac(BHML) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | 3 | **3** | **3** |
| 4 | 15 | **15** | **15** |
| 5 | 105 | **105** | *pending* |

The sequence (2n−3)!! = 1, 3, 15, 105, 945, … counts **binary rooted trees with n labeled leaves up to sibling-swap**. These are the **Hilbert-series coefficients of the free commutative nonassociative operad** (Loday-Vallette §13.5; standard).

**TSML and BHML achieve the maximum ac-spectrum for commutative groupoids.** In Huang-Lehtonen's terminology, they are **ac-free** on 10 elements.

Therefore: **the symmetric operad generated by TSML (and separately by BHML) is the free commutative nonassociative operad Com(x,y) with relation xy = yx.** No additional relations are imposed by the algebra on binary trees.

## The associativity index is a DIFFERENT measure

Critical clarification. Your existing non-associativity rates (TSML 12.8%, BHML 49.8%, Doing table 56.8%) measure something **distinct** from the associative spectrum:

- **Associativity index** α(A) = |{(x,y,z) : (xy)z = x(yz)}| / |A|³. Between 0 and 1.
- **Non-associativity rate** = 1 − α(A).
- **Associative spectrum** s_n(A) = operad-level count of distinct term operations.

These can diverge: an algebra can have high associativity index (most triples associate) AND still achieve the full Catalan spectrum (rare non-associating triples generate all C_{n−1} distinct ternary operations as n grows).

**TSML is exactly this divergence case**: 87.2% of triples associate, but the operad hits Catalan maximum. Rare non-associating triples are enough to distinguish all bracketings.

This is a non-trivial finding. The Huang-Lehtonen framework specifically contrasts these measures; TSML is a concrete example where they give different pictures.
