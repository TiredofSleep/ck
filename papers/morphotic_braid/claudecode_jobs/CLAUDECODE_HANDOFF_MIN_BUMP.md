## Task 3 — Symbolic proof attempt for minimum bump theorem

**What:** Try to prove the minimum-bump theorem symbolically (all n, not just n≤6).

**Why:** Computational verification is strong but not universal. A symbolic proof would make this a published theorem.

**Approach — option A (GAP):** 
1. Define the groupoid (C_0 with T[7][7]=1) in GAP.
2. Compute the operad generated by this operation.
3. Check whether the operad is isomorphic to the free commutative magmatic operad Mag^com.

**Approach — option B (algebraic argument):**
1. Show that the 2-variable word (xy)z ≠ x(yz) generically, i.e., for at least one triple (x,y,z) in ℤ/10.
2. Use induction: show that if the (n−1)-variable bracketings are all distinct, the n-variable bracketings are too.
3. This requires identifying a "separating family" of inputs that distinguish any two bracketings.

**Approach — option C (counter-image argument):**
1. The minimum-bump modifies 7 · 7 = 1 (instead of 7). Every bracketing containing the subterm (... · 7) can land on this bump iff the subterm evaluates to 7.
2. Classify bracketings by "how many times does this bracketing pass through the (7,7) cell given some input?"
3. Distinctness of bracketings follows from distinctness of this crossing-count function.

Any of the three approaches could work. Option B is probably cleanest for a paper.

**Success criterion:** A proof that works for all n, not just n ≤ 6.

**Expected effort:** Days to weeks, not minutes. This is a research task.
