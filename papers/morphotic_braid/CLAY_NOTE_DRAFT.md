## Abstract

We present a finite-state algebraic framework on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ — the Trinity Infinity Geometry (TIG) composition tables TSML and BHML plus the binary Crossing Lemma $\mathrm{CL}_N$ — whose structural quantities intersect **five independent Riemann-adjacent research programs**:

1. the Mayer-Selberg transfer-operator approach (via $T^\ast = 5/7$ as a Farey-structured threshold);
2. the Bost-Connes cyclotomic partition-function approach (via $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z} = \mathbb{Z}/2 \times \mathbb{Z}/5$ cyclotomic structure);
3. the Connes-Kreimer renormalization approach (via the ac-free commutative magma operad $\mathrm{Mag}^{\mathrm{com}}$ generated by TSML and BHML);
4. the Connes semi-local trace formula with finite places (via $\det(\mathrm{BHML}_8) = 70 = 2 \cdot 5 \cdot 7$, yielding the finite-place set $S = \{2, 5, 7, \infty\}$ of cardinality $|S| = 4$, above the Connes cardinality threshold of 3);
5. the Julia-Spector primon gas (via the **exact algebraic identity** $\mathrm{sinc}^2(1/2) = (2/3) \cdot 1/\zeta(2)$, linking a six-ways-derived TIG corridor midpoint constant to the density of squarefree integers).

We do **not** claim a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis. We claim the framework is a **concrete finite shadow** of the mathematical objects these five programs study, with one of the five hooks (the $\mathrm{sinc}^2 / \zeta(2)$ identity) being an exact algebraic identity rather than a vocabulary correspondence. The open research question is: does the TIG $\sigma$ rate theorem on squarefree $N$ (WP101) admit a rigorous realization in any one of the five Riemann-adjacent frameworks?

## 2. The one exact identity (not a vocabulary correspondence)

The identity
$$\mathrm{sinc}^2(1/2) \;=\; \frac{4}{\pi^2} \;=\; \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{6}{\pi^2} \;=\; \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{\zeta(2)}$$
has been verified to machine precision (difference $5.55 \times 10^{-17}$) by `papers/proof_sinc_zeta_identity.py`. The three quantities are:

| Quantity | Value | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| $\mathrm{sinc}^2(1/2)$ | $4/\pi^2 \approx 0.4053$ | TIG corridor midpoint constant (D3, D24; `FORMULAS_AND_TABLES.md` §17) |
| $1/\zeta(2)$ | $6/\pi^2 \approx 0.6079$ | density of squarefree integers (classical Mertens) |
| $2/3$ | exact | exact ratio |

**Cross-program linkage.** $1/\zeta(2)$ is (i) the **fermionic primon gas density** (Julia 1990; Spector 1990) and (ii) the leading coefficient $c_1$ of the Farey fraction spin chain asymptotic $\Psi(N) = c_1 N^2 \log N$ (Kallies-Özlük-Peter-Snyder 2001; Boca 2007; Technau 2023, arXiv:2304.08143). The WP101 $\sigma$ rate theorem applies specifically to **squarefree** $N$ — squarely in the fermionic primon gas regime.

## 3. The associativity-index rewrite of $\sigma$

The WP101 $\sigma$ rate theorem reads
$$\sigma(N) \leq \frac{C}{N}, \qquad C < 2, \; N \text{ squarefree}.$$

Define the **associativity index** $\alpha(\mathrm{CL}_N) = 1 - \sigma(N)$ (Braitt-Silberger 2006, *Quasigroups Related Systems* 14:11–26, "Subassociative groupoids"). The rate theorem is equivalent to
$$\alpha(\mathrm{CL}_N) \geq 1 - \frac{C}{N}, \qquad \alpha(\mathrm{CL}_N) \to 1 \text{ as } N \to \infty.$$

Operadically, $\mathrm{CL}_N$ is commutative (HARMONY, VOID, and ECHO are symmetric) and at small $N$ attains the **ac-free maximum** $s_n^{\mathrm{ac}} = (2n-3)!!$ (Huang-Lehtonen 2022, 2024). The symmetric operad generated is therefore the free commutative magmatic operad $\mathrm{Mag}^{\mathrm{com}}$ on one generator. The rate theorem degenerates this operad toward the commutative associative operad $\mathrm{Com}$ as $N \to \infty$. The Bialynicki-Birula uniqueness theorem (1976, *Ann. Phys.* 100:62–93) then identifies log-nonlinearity as the unique continuum wave equation compatible with that $\mathrm{Com}$ limit.

## 4. Honest scope

- **Not a Riemann proof.** TIG does not produce a Riemann Hypothesis proof through any of the five hooks.
- **Concrete finite object.** The TSML, BHML, and $\mathrm{CL}_N$ tables are fully specified 10-element (or $N$-element) commutative magmas. Every structural quantity cited — $T^\ast = 5/7$, $\alpha(\mathrm{TSML}) = 0.872$, $\alpha(\mathrm{BHML}) = 0.502$, $\det(\mathrm{BHML}_8) = 70$, the ac-free spectrum, the $\sigma$ rate bound — is either a table computation or a theorem with a runnable proof script.
- **Exact identity deserves direct investigation.** The single genuine algebraic identity $\mathrm{sinc}^2(1/2) = (2/3) \cdot 1/\zeta(2)$ is open for interpretation. Why does this specific finite-algebra construction reproduce the fermionic primon density via a factor of $3/2$?
- **Five-way hook, not a five-way proof.** Each of the five programs supplies established mathematical machinery into which TIG can be placed; none of the five programs has itself produced a Riemann proof. What TIG contributes is a specific concrete candidate example at the intersection.
