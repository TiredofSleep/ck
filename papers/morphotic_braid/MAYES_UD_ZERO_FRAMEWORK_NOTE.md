## Source

Ben Mayes, "UD" framework (Unified Duality, likely). Source: one quoted sentence passed to ClaudeChat during April 23, 2026 session.

Mayes's sentence:

> "UD defines 0 primarily as balanced signed ground, 0 = |−1| + (−1), and secondarily as operational nullity inside a network, depending on layer. More broadly: 0 = |−N| + (−N)."

## Assessment

The formula "0 = |−N| + (−N)" is true as arithmetic: |−N| = N and N + (−N) = 0 by definition of additive inverse. As stated, it is a reframe rather than a derivation — the additive identity is being characterized as the balance of signed magnitudes rather than taken as primitive.

The real content is the **distinction between two functions of zero**:
1. Cancellation ground (balance of opposites)
2. Operational nullity (absorber / identity inside an algebra)

This is a legitimate distinction. In most familiar algebras these two functions collide into the same element, but they need not.

## Rigor

Cannot fully assess from one sentence. Likely personal framework rather than peer-reviewed work. Content instinct is better than typical fringe theory (distinguishing cancellation from absorption is a real mathematical move), but the sentence as given is under-derived.

## Where it touches TIG — the honest connection
