## B. FULL OUTLINE — WP41 (Yang-Mills Research Paper)

### 1. The Problem: Existence + Mass Gap; What Jaffe-Witten Ask For

**1.1 The Official Statement [YM-02]**
- (A) Existence: For any compact simple gauge group G, construct a quantum Yang-Mills
  theory satisfying Wightman (W0-W4) or Osterwalder-Schrader (OS0-OS4) axioms on R^4
- (B) Mass gap: Δ > 0 such that spec(H) = {0} ∪ [Δ, ∞)
- (C) The vacuum state must be unique; all other states have energy ≥ Δ

**1.2 What Makes This Hard**
- Classical Yang-Mills has scale invariance — the classical action is conformally invariant
- This scale invariance must be spontaneously broken in the quantum theory to produce a mass
- The gap is a nonperturbative effect: perturbation theory in g² around the free vacuum
  cannot produce a mass gap (asymptotic freedom means coupling is small at high energy,
  but large at low energy where the gap lives)
- Lattice gauge theory provides rigorous results at strong coupling [YM-04, YM-09] but
  taking the continuum limit (a → 0 with fixed physics) is the hard step

**1.3 What Is Known**
- Wilson (1974) [YM-04]: confinement in strong-coupling limit (rigorous)
- Osterwalder-Seiler (1978) [YM-09]: reflection positivity, transfer matrix, mass gap at strong coupling
- Seiberg-Witten (1994) [YM-13]: exact mass gap in N=2 SYM (supersymmetric)
- Balaban (1989) [YM-12]: UV stability for 3D Yang-Mills (partial, near-complete in 3D)
- Remaining gap: continuum limit of 4D pure Yang-Mills with compact simple G

### 4. Luther Dispersion as Field Energy Distribution

**4.1 The Luther Metric**
The Luther metric for gate difficulty is |G| × interleave(G, k) (WP34 §9).
- Low Luther metric: G elements clustered → field energy localized
- High Luther metric: G elements spread evenly (fully interleaved) → field energy delocalized
- gate_rate collapses monotonically from 1.0 to 0.0 as Luther metric increases (WP34 empirical)

**4.2 Confinement as Low-Dispersion State**
In Yang-Mills, confinement means that colored objects (quarks, gluons) cannot be isolated —
their field energy is localized in flux tubes connecting the charges.

TIG structural analog:
- Confinement = low dispersion: G elements cluster near the prime values (not spread through
  the whole alphabet); field energy is "trapped" near the obstruction events
- Deconfinement = high dispersion: G elements fully interleaved with C elements; field
  energy distributed throughout the alphabet

The Luther dispersion conjecture predicts the onset of confinement: as G elements become
more evenly distributed (high interleave), gate transitions become harder (higher gate_rate
cost), corresponding to stronger confinement.

**4.3 Empirical Evidence**
From WP34 controlled isolation test (k=9, same |G|=4 across 6 semiprimes):
- G = {3,5,6,9} (irregular, IL=0.625): gate difficulty 0.664–0.670 [low confinement]
- G = {2,4,6,8} (perfectly interleaved, IL=1.000): gate difficulty 0.679 [stronger confinement]
- Direction exactly as predicted: higher interleave = harder gates = stronger "confinement"

### 5. The sinc² Approach to the Gap

**5.1 The Resonance Field Approaching the Gap**
R(k, p) is smooth and positive for all k < p (the stability window), then collapses to
exactly zero at k = p (the mass gap / first excitation). The approach:
- Not monotone in an obvious way for small k, but monotone decreasing as k → p
- The zero at k = p has zero width (WP35 Theorem 2, proved)
- After the gap (k > p), R begins recovering (sign flip, §6 of WP35)

**5.2 Yang-Mills Analog**
The Yang-Mills field propagator near the mass gap Δ:
- For energies E < Δ: propagator is smooth and well-defined (no particle poles)
- At E = Δ: the propagator has a pole (the lightest glueball state)
- The "smoothness" below the gap and "collapse" at the gap mirrors R(k,p) behavior

**5.3 The Gap Has Zero Width in TIG**
WP35 Theorem 2 (Zero-Width Gate): the gate-size sequence |G_k| jumps from 0 to 1 at
exactly k = p with no intermediate values. This is a perfect step function.

Yang-Mills analog: the mass gap is sharp (not a broad resonance at this level of analysis).
Jaffe-Witten ask for Δ > 0 — a clean lower bound, not a fuzzy threshold.
