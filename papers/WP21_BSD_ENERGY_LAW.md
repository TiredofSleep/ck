## Abstract

We find a clean log-linear law relating rank and minimal conductor for elliptic curves over ℚ:

```
log₁₀(N) = 0.873(±0.092) · rank + 1.364    R² = 0.87
```

The coefficient 0.873 falls within 2% of 6/7 = 3×(2/7), where 2/7 is the TIG dual-threshold mass gap. This suggests each Mordell-Weil generator requires approximately **three TIG gap activations** rather than one. In natural log units the slope is 2.01 ≈ ln(e²), and the per-rank conductor multiplier is 10^0.87 ≈ 7.47 ≈ e².

---

## §1 The Structural Claim

The TIG framework assigns primes to the four corner operators {LAT(1), PRG(3), HAR(7), RST(9)} via last digit mod 10. A rational point of infinite order on an elliptic curve cannot be generated by corner operators alone — it requires activating at least one gap operator from {CTR(2), COL(4), BAL(5), CHA(6), BRT(8)}.

**Claim:** Rank r counts how many independent gap activations are present. Each activation costs a fixed TIG energy unit of 2/7 in log₁₀(conductor) space.

**Empirical finding:** The cost is 6/7 = 3×(2/7) per rank, not 2/7. The triplet structure is the new result.

## §3 The TIG Calibration

| Constant | Value | Interpretation |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| 2/7 = 0.286 | Single gap activation (TIG mass gap) | One BREATH operator turned on |
| 6/7 = 0.857 | Three gap activations (empirical) | One Mordell-Weil generator |
| Empirical | 0.873 ± 0.092 | Measured from 76 curves |

6/7 lies within the 95% CI [0.781, 0.966]. The data are consistent with exactly three TIG gap activations per rank unit.

**Why triplets?** Open structural question. Three is the number of non-trivial generators of the TIG algebra {0,1,2} (the prime generators). It is also the minimum number of composition steps needed to reach any gap operator from the corner algebra. Whether this is coincidence or mechanism is the new open problem.

## §4 The Remark in Natural Log

In natural log units, the slope becomes 0.873 × ln(10) ≈ 2.01. This is tantalizingly close to 2 = ln(e²). The per-rank conductor multiplier e² ≈ 7.39 is a natural number in analysis (appearing in Gaussian integrals, heat kernels, and the functional equation of ζ). Whether this is meaningful or coincidental: open.

## §5 The Product Gap (K3×K3 Analog)

The product algebra TSML⊗TSML has 40 cross-term operators (one C component, one G component). Zero of these are reachable from the product corner algebra C⊗C in any number of steps. This is the TIG version of the K3×K3 transcendental lattice — new structure that appears in the product but is algebraically invisible from either factor's prime generators.

For BSD on product families (Batyrev-Skorobogatov type), the triplet activation predicts: "mixed" rank contributions from cross-term operators should require conductor roughly e^(2×k) where k is the cross-term depth. Testable against LMFDB product families.
