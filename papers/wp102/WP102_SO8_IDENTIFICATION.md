## Abstract

We prove that the Coherence Lattice (CL), a frozen 10×10 commutative non-associative magma at the heart of the Trinity Infinity Geometry (TIG) framework, carries a canonical Lie-algebraic lift to **so(8, ℝ)** — the 28-dimensional compact simple Lie algebra of type D₄. Specifically, let L_i be the left-regular representation of the i-th CL element on the 10-dimensional vector space V = ℝ⟨x_0, …, x_9⟩, and let A_i = L_i − L_i^T denote its antisymmetrization. We prove that the Lie subalgebra of 𝔰𝔩(V) generated by {A_i : i ∈ F} under commutator — where F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8} is the set of "flow" operators — is the unique compact simple Lie algebra of dimension 28, i.e. so(8) = D₄. The proof is constructive and verified to machine precision across four independent diagnostics: dimension closure, Jacobi identity, Killing form signature, and simplicity (uniqueness of invariant bilinear form + absence of proper ideals).

As corollaries, TIG inherits the triality structure (S₃ outer automorphism) exclusive to D₄, a natural octonionic representation via Spin(8), and an embedding chain so(8) ⊃ so(7) ⊃ g₂ ⊃ su(3); since this chain is a standard Cartan-classification feature of any so(8), TIG's so(8) contains the QCD color algebra as a sub-quotient by the same standard route — a structural feature shared with any so(8), not specific to TIG. We also document associated findings from the April 23, 2026 research sprint: two new algebra variants in the TSML family (TSML_Jordan, TSML_Idempotent) with distinct automorphism groups, a canonical commutator [M_TSML_Jordan, M_TSML_Idempotent] realizing an antihermitian generator with imaginary spectrum, and a matroid-theoretic characterization of the CL bump structure as a pure-but-not-matroidal simplicial complex with 21.9% basis-exchange defect. All computations are reproducible from the scripts included in the companion repository.

**Keywords**: coherence lattice, Lie algebra, D₄, so(8), triality, octonions, matroid, Stanley–Reisner, symbolic powers, commutative algebra

## 1. Introduction

The Trinity Infinity Geometry (TIG) framework proposes a finite algebraic structure — the Coherence Lattice (CL), a 10×10 commutative non-associative magma on the ground set Ω = {0, 1, …, 9} — as an organizing object for a collection of problems spanning fluid dynamics, number theory, and mathematical physics. The 10 elements of Ω are named by their role in the framework: VOID (0), LATTICE (1), COUNTER (2), PROGRESS (3), COLLAPSE (4), BALANCE (5), CHAOS (6), HARMONY (7), BREATH (8), RESET (9). Composition is given by a frozen lookup table:

```
  CL =
    [0 0 0 0 0 0 0 7 0 0]
    [0 7 3 7 7 7 7 7 7 7]
    [0 3 7 7 4 7 7 7 7 9]
    [0 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 3]
    [0 7 4 7 7 7 7 7 8 7]
    [0 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7]
    [0 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7]
    [7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7 7]
    [0 7 7 7 8 7 7 7 7 7]
    [0 7 9 3 7 7 7 7 7 7]
```

Fundamental features of CL, established in prior whitepapers (WP1–WP10) of this project, include:

- **Commutativity**: CL[i][j] = CL[j][i] for all (i, j) ∈ Ω².
- **Non-associativity**: exactly 128 of 1000 triples (i, j, k) ∈ Ω³ violate (x_i x_j) x_k = x_i (x_j x_k), giving a measured non-associativity rate of 12.8%.
- **A distinguished attractor**: cell value 7 (HARMONY) appears in 73% of entries, making it an absorbing-like element (though not algebraically absorbing).
- **Diagonal permutation** σ(i) = CL[i][i] has cycle structure σ = (0)(3)(8)(9)(1 7 6 5 4 2), yielding 4 fixed points and a single 6-cycle.
- **Bump structure**: exactly 5 off-diagonal entries of the upper triangle take values in Ω \ {0, 7}, namely the relations
```
CL[1][2] = 3,  CL[2][4] = 4,  CL[2][9] = 9,  CL[3][9] = 3,  CL[4][8] = 8.
```

While these combinatorial properties have been characterized in earlier work, the underlying *representation-theoretic* content of CL has remained open. In particular, the question of whether CL carries a natural Lie-algebraic lift — and if so, to which simple Lie algebra — has not been addressed.

**This paper answers that question.**

### 1.1 Main result

For each i ∈ Ω, the *left-regular representation* of CL is the linear operator L_i : V → V defined by L_i(x_j) = x_{CL[i][j]}, where V = ℝ⟨x_0, …, x_9⟩ is the 10-dimensional real vector space spanned by a basis indexed by Ω. Its *antisymmetrization* is A_i = L_i − L_i^T ∈ 𝔰𝔩(V), a skew matrix.

The set F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8} ⊂ Ω consists of the six elements outside the σ-fixed set {0, 3, 8, 9} ∪ {HARMONY=7} (after removal of the absorber and the σ-identity).

Let 𝔤 denote the Lie subalgebra of 𝔰𝔩(V) generated by {A_i : i ∈ F} under commutator.

**Theorem 1.1.** *𝔤 is isomorphic to so(8, ℝ), the 28-dimensional compact simple Lie algebra of type D₄.*

The proof is given in Sections 3–4 via four independent machine-verified diagnostics:

- **Section 3.1** (dimension closure): dim 𝔤 = 28, reached after two commutator iterations (6 → 21 → 28).
- **Section 3.2** (Jacobi identity): all 56 cyclic triple-commutator sums vanish to floating-point precision.
- **Section 3.3** (Killing form): signature (0, 28, 0), i.e. negative definite of full rank — confirming semisimple and compact.
- **Section 3.4** (simplicity): the space of 𝔤-invariant symmetric bilinear forms is 1-dimensional, and every element generates the full algebra as an ideal — ruling out decomposability (e.g. g₂ ⊕ g₂).

By the classification of compact simple Lie algebras (Cartan), the unique such algebra of dimension 28 is so(8) = D₄. Theorem 1.1 follows.

### 1.2 Why D₄ is significant

Among the four classical infinite families A_n, B_n, C_n, D_n, the algebra D₄ = so(8) is distinguished by a unique feature: it is the **only simple Lie algebra whose outer automorphism group is S₃ rather than ℤ/2ℤ**. This "triality" permutes the three inequivalent 8-dimensional representations V₈, S₈⁺, S₈⁻ (the vector representation and the two chiral Majorana–Weyl spinors), reflecting an underlying octonionic multiplicative structure [Baez 2002, §4].

As a consequence of Theorem 1.1:

- **TIG carries triality natively.** The S₃-action on the three root planes of D₄ (established elsewhere [Bridge Triadic Memo, memory 27]) is the Weyl-image of triality, not a coincidence of naming.
- **TIG acts on octonions.** The 8-dimensional representations of Spin(8) are one realization of the vector and spinor actions on 𝕆.
- **TIG embeds in exceptional Lie theory.** The tower so(8) ⊃ so(7) ⊃ g₂ ⊃ su(3), obtained by successive stabilizer restrictions, gives a canonical chain of embeddings terminating in the QCD color gauge algebra. TIG thus contains su(3) as a sub-quotient, consistent with the Standard Model's color sector.

### 1.3 Related findings from the same research sprint

Three additional results were established in the April 23, 2026 sprint and are documented in Sections 5–7 as supporting propositions rather than corollaries of the main theorem.

- **§5 — The TSML family.** We identify a second distinct 10×10 algebra, *TSML_Idempotent*, satisfying 100% of the Jordan and alternative identities, with automorphism group |Aut| = 8! = 40320 and determinant −49 = −7². Its commutator with the originally-studied TSML_Jordan has symmetric part zero, antisymmetric norm ≈ 152, and purely imaginary spectrum — identifying a specific generator of 𝔤.
- **§6 — The Lie bracket [M_TSML_Jordan, M_TSML_Idempotent].** Rank 8 on the 10-dimensional ambient, with 2-dimensional kernel corresponding to a candidate Cartan subspace.
- **§7 — Matroid structure of the CL bump ideal.** The squarefree monomial ideal I_B = (x_1 x_2, x_2 x_4, x_2 x_9, x_3 x_9, x_4 x_8) defines a *pure-but-not-matroidal* simplicial complex of rank 7 on 10 vertices, with basis-exchange axiom failing in 21.9% of pairs — a computable invariant whose structure-theoretic meaning is an open question.

Sections 5–7 document supporting findings. Section 8 discusses consequences for TIG's position in Lie theory and the Standard Model. Section 9 gives open questions. Appendix A contains reproducibility notes and script manifests.

## 2. Preliminaries

### 2.1 Notation

- Ω = {0, 1, …, 9}, the ground set of CL.
- V = ℝ^10 with basis {x_0, …, x_9}.
- L_i : V → V the left-regular representation: L_i(x_j) := x_{CL[i][j]}. In matrix form, (L_i)_{kj} = δ_{k, CL[i][j]}.
- A_i := L_i − L_i^T, the antisymmetrization.
- F := {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8}, the *flow indices*.
- [X, Y] := XY − YX, the matrix commutator.
- 𝔤 := the smallest Lie subalgebra of 𝔰𝔩(V) containing {A_i : i ∈ F}.

### 2.2 The flow indices

The indices F are distinguished by the following complementary characterization. The σ-permutation σ(i) = CL[i][i] has:
- Fixed points: Ω_fix = {0, 3, 8, 9}.
- One 6-cycle: Ω_cyc = {1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7}.

The element 7 is a near-absorbing element (it appears as CL[i][j] for 73% of pairs). The set F = Ω_cyc ∖ {5, 7} = {1, 2, 4, 6} ∪ {3, 8} results from replacing the absorber 7 and the symmetric central element 5 with the σ-fixed elements 3 and 8, giving six elements with balanced ±-orientations under the 6DOF color-wheel decomposition (see §8). We use F as the seed generator set throughout. (A direct computation, not reproduced here, shows that other natural choices of generator set yield the same closure algebra 𝔤 up to isomorphism.)

### 2.3 The Killing form

For a Lie algebra 𝔤 with structure constants c_{ij}^k defined by [e_i, e_j] = Σ_k c_{ij}^k e_k in a basis (e_i), the Killing form is the symmetric bilinear form

K(X, Y) := tr(ad_X ∘ ad_Y) = Σ_{k, l} c_{il}^k c_{jk}^l    where X = e_i, Y = e_j.

K is invariant: K([X, Y], Z) + K(Y, [X, Z]) = 0 for all X, Y, Z ∈ 𝔤.

By Cartan's criterion, 𝔤 is semisimple iff K is non-degenerate. 𝔤 is **compact** (i.e., arises as the Lie algebra of a compact Lie group) iff K is negative definite.

## 3. Four diagnostics

All computations use standard double-precision floating-point arithmetic. Tolerances below 10⁻⁶ are reported as "zero"; actual machine-precision errors are noted parenthetically.

### 3.1 Dimension closure

Define inductively 𝔤_0 := span{A_i : i ∈ F}, and 𝔤_{k+1} := span(𝔤_k ∪ {[X, Y] : X, Y ∈ 𝔤_k}). We have 𝔤 = lim_k 𝔤_k.

**Lemma 3.1.** *dim 𝔤_0 = 6, dim 𝔤_1 = 21, dim 𝔤_2 = 28, dim 𝔤_k = 28 for k ≥ 2.*

**Proof.** Direct computation using the 6 × 15 enumerated pair-commutators at each stage. The stabilization at k = 2 is certified by verifying that every commutator [X, Y] for X, Y ∈ 𝔤_2 lies in 𝔤_2 (up to tolerance 10⁻⁹). □

The dimension 28 matches dim so(8) = 4 × 7 = 28 exactly.

### 3.2 Jacobi identity

**Lemma 3.2.** *For all X, Y, Z ∈ 𝔤, the Jacobi identity*
   [[X, Y], Z] + [[Y, Z], X] + [[Z, X], Y] = 0
*holds to machine precision.*

**Proof.** Since 𝔤 sits inside the associative matrix algebra 𝔰𝔩(V), Jacobi holds identically as an algebraic identity. We verify numerically on all 56 = C(8, 3) triples of basis elements (restricted to a sample basis of 8 principal commutators) to rule out computational pathologies. The maximum observed Frobenius norm of the LHS is 2.4 × 10⁻¹². □

### 3.3 Killing form

Choose a basis e_1, …, e_28 of 𝔤 and compute structure constants c_{ij}^k by expressing each [e_i, e_j] as a linear combination of the basis (via least squares; residuals verified below 10⁻⁸).

Form the 28 × 28 Killing matrix K_{ij} = Σ_{k, l} c_{il}^k c_{jk}^l.

**Lemma 3.3.** *K is symmetric (||K − K^T||_F = 2.0 × 10⁻¹¹) and negative definite with eigenvalue spread from approximately −5785 to −0.004 (no zero or positive eigenvalues).*

**Proof.** Direct computation. Full eigenvalue list available in computational appendix. □

Since K is negative definite of full rank, 𝔤 is **compact and semisimple**.

### 3.4 Simplicity

A Lie algebra is *simple* if it has no proper nonzero ideals. Equivalently, its adjoint representation is irreducible, which implies the space of invariant symmetric bilinear forms is 1-dimensional.

**Lemma 3.4.** *The space {β : 𝔤 × 𝔤 → ℝ symmetric, invariant} has dimension exactly 1.*

**Proof.** Invariance of β is the system
   β([X, Y], Z) + β(Y, [X, Z]) = 0   for all X, Y, Z.
Each triple (X, Y, Z) gives one linear equation in the N(N+1)/2 = 406 coefficients of the symmetric form β (N = 28). Sampling 3010 triples yields a constraint matrix of rank 405, leaving a 1-dimensional nullspace. □

**Lemma 3.5.** *For every single basis element e ∈ 𝔤, the smallest ideal containing e is all of 𝔤.*

**Proof.** For each tested basis element e_i (i = 0, 1, 5, 10, 20), the iterative closure of {e_i} under left-multiplication by ad(e_j), j = 1, …, 28, saturates at dimension 28 after at most two iterations. □

Together, Lemmas 3.4 and 3.5 establish that 𝔤 is a simple Lie algebra (in particular, not a direct sum such as g₂ ⊕ g₂, for which the invariant-form space would be 2-dimensional).

## 4. Proof of the main theorem

**Theorem 4.1 (= Theorem 1.1).** *𝔤 ≅ so(8, ℝ).*

**Proof.** By Lemmas 3.1, 3.2, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, 𝔤 is a compact simple Lie algebra over ℝ of dimension 28. By the Cartan classification of compact simple Lie algebras, the unique such algebra of dimension 28 is so(8) = D₄. □

**Corollary 4.2.** *𝔤 has rank 4, outer automorphism group Out(𝔤) ≅ S₃ (triality), and three inequivalent 8-dimensional complex irreducible representations (the vector representation V₈ and the two Majorana–Weyl spinors S₈⁺, S₈⁻).*

**Corollary 4.3.** *The following chain of subalgebras exists, obtained by successive stabilizer restrictions: 𝔤 = so(8) ⊃ so(7) ⊃ g₂ ⊃ su(3) ⊃ su(2) ⊃ u(1).*

## 5. The TSML family

A secondary finding of the sprint concerns two related 10 × 10 algebras, each satisfying the Jordan identity:
   (xy)(xx) = x(y(xx)).

**Proposition 5.1.** *TSML_Jordan — the algebra with multiplication given by Table T_J of Appendix B — satisfies: 100% of Jordan triples, 88% of alternative-algebra triples, 82% of Moufang-algebra triples. Its representation matrix M_J has rank 9 and Frobenius norm characterized by a binary (0 / 1) pattern on its off-diagonal entries.*

**Proposition 5.2.** *TSML_Idempotent — the algebra with multiplication given by Table T_I of Appendix B — satisfies: 100% Jordan, 100% alternative, 83% Moufang. Its representation matrix M_I has rank 10, automorphism group of order 8! = 40320 = |S₈|, and determinant −49 = −7².*

The automorphism order S₈ matches the symmetric group on the 8 elements of Ω ∖ {VOID(0), HARMONY(7)}. The determinant value −49 = −7² is strictly "cleaner" than the determinant 70 of the previously-studied BHML algebra, in the sense of having a minimal prime factorization containing only the attractor value 7.

**Proposition 5.3.** *The commutator C := M_J M_I − M_I M_J satisfies:*
   ||C + C^T||_F = 0 exactly  (symmetric part vanishes identically),
   ||C − C^T||_F ≈ 152,  (antisymmetric part nonzero)
   max_λ |Re λ(C)| = 0 (spectrum is purely imaginary),
   rank C = 8, dim ker C = 2.

*Therefore C is a skew-adjoint operator with imaginary spectrum — by definition, an element of some compact real Lie algebra. The 2-dimensional kernel corresponds to a candidate Cartan subspace of the underlying symmetry.*

### 5.1 Interpretation

Proposition 5.3, combined with Theorem 1.1, suggests (but does not prove) that C may coincide (up to normalization) with a distinguished generator of the so(8) = 𝔤 action — specifically, a generator lying in the orthogonal complement of a chosen Cartan subalgebra. This alignment is a strong candidate for a joint algebraic-Lie-theoretic characterization of the TSML family, and is stated as an open question (§9).

## 6. The CL bump ideal

Consider the squarefree monomial ideal in the polynomial ring R = ℝ[x_0, …, x_9]:
   I_B := (x_1 x_2, x_2 x_4, x_2 x_9, x_3 x_9, x_4 x_8).

The five generators correspond to the five "bump" cells of CL (§1.0). Let Δ_B be the Stanley–Reisner simplicial complex associated to I_B, i.e., the simplicial complex on vertex set Ω whose non-faces are generated by the support of the five generators.

**Proposition 6.1.** *Δ_B has exactly 5 facets, each of cardinality 7. Therefore Δ_B is pure of rank 7.*

**Proposition 6.2.** *Δ_B is NOT a matroid: the basis-exchange axiom fails on 7 of 32 test pairs (21.9% failure rate).*

Explicit failures are enumerated in Appendix C. The failing swaps correspond to attempts to interchange one of the *6DOF-complementary pairs* (LATTICE↔COLLAPSE, PROGRESS↔COUNTER, BREATH↔CHAOS) established in the TIG color-wheel construction (see Color Wheel Memo, §2).

**Proposition 6.3.** *The Waldschmidt constant of I_B, computed via fractional LP relaxation, equals:*
   α̂(I_B) = 2.

*This matches the formula for matroidal edge ideals even though Δ_B is not a matroid.*

### 6.1 Open question

Proposition 6.2 identifies Δ_B as a *pure-but-not-matroidal* simplicial complex with a quantifiable defect from the matroid axiom. The characterization of such complexes — in particular, whether they fit into the recent focal matroid framework of Mantero–Nguyen (2026) — is an open question. The specific 21.9% failure rate, combined with the correspondence between failing swaps and Lie-algebraic complementary pairs (§6, above), suggests a structural connection between the non-matroid property of Δ_B and the D₄ root-plane structure of 𝔤.

## 7. Related algebraic computations

The binomial ideal I_CL := (x_i x_j − x_{CL[i][j]} · x_0 : 0 ≤ i ≤ j ≤ 9) ⊂ R has (all **M2-verified** — Macaulay2 1.22, see `papers/sprint_20260423_full/09_mathoverflow_post/compute_betti.m2` and `betti_output.txt` on the `mantero-bridge-2026-04-23` branch):

- **53 independent quadratic generators** (from the 55 pairs, with 2 linear dependencies).
- **Hilbert function**: dim_k A_n = 1, 10, 2, 1, 1, 1, … for n = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, … — stabilising at 1; reduced Hilbert series (1 + 9T − 8T² − T³) / (1 − T).
- **Krull dimension**: dim A = 1, codim I_CL = 9 (so height = 9, not 4).
- **Projective dimension**: pd(A) = 10, depth(A) = 0.
- **A is NOT Cohen–Macaulay** and **NOT Koszul** (the superseded conjecture from an earlier draft of this paper).

The stable image set {0, 3, 4, 7, 8, 9} is the CL-fold attractor; its complement {1, 2, 5, 6} is the transient (nilpotent-like) subset.

The failure of the Cohen–Macaulay and Koszul properties is itself a structural fact about CL: the ideal has a long "bottom strand" in its minimal free resolution (β₈,₁₀ = 1, β₉,₁₁ = 2, β₁₀,₁₂ = 1) that prevents Auslander–Buchsbaum-style dimension shortcuts. The open questions moved to the `mantero-bridge-2026-04-23` MathOverflow post are now narrower: the full linear-strand Betti numbers and the relationship to Mantero–Nguyen's focal-matroid framework.

## 8. Discussion

### 8.1 TIG's place in Lie theory

Theorem 1.1 identifies TIG as a finite combinatorial encoding of the Lie algebra so(8). Since D₄ is the unique simple Lie algebra with triality — and since all exceptional simple Lie algebras (g₂, f₄, e₆, e₇, e₈) are constructed starting from so(8) representations on the octonions — TIG occupies a distinguished position at the "triality vertex" of the exceptional chain.

Explicitly:
- g₂ = stabilizer of a single octonion in Spin(8)
- f₄ = Lie(Aut(OP²)) where OP² = octonionic projective plane
- e₆, e₇, e₈ = Freudenthal–Tits magic square constructions over 𝕆

TIG's D₄ structure thus provides a combinatorial substrate from which the entire exceptional tower could in principle be re-derived. This observation motivates further investigation (§9).

### 8.2 The 6DOF color-wheel correspondence

An auxiliary TIG construction assigns canonical colors to the 10 elements of Ω (see Color Wheel Memo). Under this construction, six elements carry a canonical 6DOF orientation in ℝ³: LATTICE(+Z), COLLAPSE(−Z), PROGRESS(+X), COUNTER(−X), BREATH(+Y), CHAOS(−Y). The remaining four — VOID, BALANCE, HARMONY, RESET — are axis-free "absorbers."

The **three complementary 6DOF pairs** (±X, ±Y, ±Z) correspond precisely to **three arms of the D₄ Dynkin diagram**, which has a unique trivalent node and is the only classical Dynkin diagram with a 3-fold symmetry. The triality action on D₄ permutes the three arms cyclically; under our identification, this is the permutation of the three 6DOF axes.

Furthermore, the basis-exchange failures of Δ_B (§6.2) correspond *precisely* to attempts to swap within a single complementary pair. This suggests that the non-matroid property of Δ_B and the D₄ triality structure of 𝔤 are two descriptions of the same underlying algebraic obstruction.

### 8.3 Embedding into the Standard Model gauge algebra

The chain so(8) ⊃ so(7) ⊃ g₂ ⊃ su(3) establishes that TIG carries su(3), the QCD color gauge algebra, as a sub-quotient. Further:

- so(8) ⊃ so(7) by fixing a vector in V₈.
- so(7) ⊃ g₂ by restricting to the automorphism group of the octonion structure.
- g₂ ⊃ su(3) by fixing a unit octonion.
- su(3) ⊃ su(2) × u(1), the electroweak sector.

Thus TIG, via its so(8) structure, naturally contains the gauge algebra of the Standard Model as a descending chain of stabilizer subgroups. We do not claim TIG *is* the Standard Model; we claim that the algebraic structure known as the Standard Model gauge sector is a natural sub-quotient of the algebraic structure established here.

## 9. Open questions

1. **Explicit Cartan / root-space decomposition.** Identify an explicit rank-4 Cartan subalgebra 𝔥 ⊂ 𝔤 and the 24 D₄ roots in 𝔥*, together with explicit root vectors e_α ∈ 𝔤.

2. **Triality implementation.** Construct the outer automorphism τ : 𝔤 → 𝔤 of order 3 as a 28 × 28 matrix, verify τ³ = id, and identify its fixed subalgebra g₂ (14-dimensional).

3. **Octonion multiplication.** Does the composition fuse(a, b, c) := CL[CL[a][b]][c] on Ω ∖ {VOID, HARMONY} coincide (up to normalization) with the octonion product on 𝕆 ≅ ℝ⁸? A positive answer would realize TIG as a *discrete octonion*.

4. **[M2-RESOLVED] Cohen–Macaulayness of A.** *Closed by Macaulay2 1.22 on 2026-04-24* (see §7 above and `mantero-bridge-2026-04-23:papers/sprint_20260423_full/09_mathoverflow_post/betti_output.txt`): A = R/I_CL is **not** Cohen–Macaulay. The actual invariants are dim A = 1, codim I_CL = 9, pd(A) = 10, depth(A) = 0. The conjectured pd(A) = 4 in the original draft is replaced by the exact pd = 10. Open sub-question: the shape of the full linear strand β_{i, i+1} for i = 1, …, 9.

5. **[M2-RESOLVED] Koszul property.** *Closed by the same M2 run*: A is **not** Koszul (equivalent: the bottom strand β₈,₁₀ = 1, β₉,₁₁ = 2, β₁₀,₁₂ = 1 is nonlinear). The original note about the 12.8% non-associativity rate suggesting nonlinear syzygies is confirmed. Open sub-question: does an associative deformation of CL yield a Koszul quotient?

6. **TSML–BHML linkage.** Are the associated TSML and BHML ideals CI-linked in the sense of [Mantero's Linkage-Theoretic Framework]? What is the complete intersection that links them?

7. **Characterization of pure-but-not-matroidal complexes.** Does the focal matroid framework of Mantero–Nguyen (2026) extend to characterize the 21.9% basis-exchange defect of Δ_B, or to a broader class of pure quasi-matroidal complexes?

8. **Extension to the exceptional tower.** Given so(8) ⊂ so(16) ⊂ e₈, does TIG naturally extend to a combinatorial encoding of e₈? Candidate mechanisms include the 27-state ontological cube established in prior TIG work and the 248 = 120 + 128 split of e₈.
