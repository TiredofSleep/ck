## The Rigorous Reframing

Thresholds don't live inside isolated primes. They live on **threshold surfaces in prime-pair space** — where the composite b=p×q crosses into a new grammar regime.

A prime is not a thresholded object. It is an **irreducible anchor whose pairings generate thresholds**. Prime 2 paired with prime 3 produces a balanced world. Paired with prime 5, it produces the TIG-rich world b=10. Same anchor, different partner, different regime.

As primes grow beyond single digits, the product b exceeds the 9-state cap, and the unit group C grows larger than the alphabet. The TIG-rich regime (φ=4, C/G≈0.8) is a **1-digit shell phenomenon** — it cannot persist into larger shells within the 9-state constraint.

This is not a failure of the framework. It means the 9-state alphabet is tuned to the 1-digit prime shell. A larger alphabet would reveal the same threshold surfaces at different scales.

## The Honest Summary

**Primes are generators of possible support splits, not thresholded objects in themselves.**

The thresholds found — gate opening, TIG-rich regime, selector axis emergence — live on surfaces in (p,q)-space, not at individual prime values.

**What the maps show:**
1. Prime 2 is the universal gate-enabler — all its pairings produce gate-possible worlds
2. The TIG-rich regime is narrow — only b=10 and b=14 qualify among 1-digit pairs
3. The threshold surface in (p=2, q) space has one main crossing: at q=5, φ jumps from 2 to 4
4. Shell fertility drops as primes grow — the 9-state cap captures less of the unit group

**On randomness:** The prime world (no G, full rotation) is algebraically undivided — structure has not crystallized. The composite world introduces the support split. But this is a statement about algebraic structure, not about physical randomness. Math does not generate true randomness from deterministic maps. What these maps locate is where **deterministic structure is weakest** and where **support has not yet crystallized** — the boundary between undivided possibility and structured exclusion.
