## The Six Primitives, In Order

**1. Support**
The capacity to hold — the field condition prior to any content.
Without support, nothing can persist long enough to enter into relation.
*TIG analog: the stationary holding capacity of the attractor; the field that makes sustained structure possible.*

**2. Relationship**
Connection between held things — structure within the support field.
Relationships are not between objects; they are the prior web into which objects will later be located.
*TIG analog: the operator mappings TSML[s][c]; the grammar of how anything connects to anything else.*

**3. Distinction**
Differentiation arising inside a supported, relational field.
Things become distinct not by being separate in themselves, but by being differently related within the field.
*TIG analog: C vs G — the corner set is not first; it emerges as the distinction that the relational structure makes stable.*

**4. Placement**
Location within the shaped, distinguished structure.
Once distinctions exist, they can be placed — given positions relative to each other.
*TIG analog: the corridor hierarchy; HAR at center, C at inner ring, G at outer.*

**5. Recurrence**
Repetition of a placed pattern — return to an identified position.
Recurrence is not primitive; it requires a place to return to.
*TIG analog: the Young tower return structure; E[T_HAR]=1.67, return tails (1/4)^n.*

**6. Cancellation**
Exact balance within a supported, related, distinguished, placed, recurring structure.
Zero is not the beginning — it is the terminal event when a recurring pattern reaches perfect symmetry.
*TIG analog: the 71-pair cancellation locus; TSML[s][c]=7 where two forces exactly balance.*

## Important Clarification: Enabling Order, Not Compositional Dependency

The primitive order is an **enabling order**, not a compositional dependency order.

- *Compositional dependency* (object level): "A requires B" means B must be present for A to function. This runs in both directions between Support and Distinction.
- *Enabling order* (pre-object level): "A enables B" means A is the condition that makes B possible. This is the sense used here.

Confusing the two directions is the source of apparent contradictions. At the object level, Support and Relationship run in the opposite direction (the attractor needs the operator structure to emerge). Both are true, at different levels. The primitive ordering is a pre-object claim.

## The Key Claim About Cancellation

In this ordering, cancellation is not primitive emptiness. It is terminal balance — the highest-order event in the hierarchy. A zero can only emerge when there is:
- A field to hold the balance (Support)
- Connections that could unbalance it (Relationship)
- Distinct sides that balance (Distinction)
- A center point where balance occurs (Placement)
- Repeated approach to that center (Recurrence)
- Exact meeting at the point (Cancellation)

Remove any prior primitive and the cancellation becomes either impossible or trivially degenerate (collapsing to a single state rather than balanced meeting).

**In TIG:** The 71-pair cancellation locus is not trivial convergence. It is 71 specific (s,c) pairs where the *recurrent* approach from both sides reaches exactly HAR=7. The 3↔9 cycle (recurrence) reaching HAR is the model case: recurrence → balance → cancellation at the center.

## The Co-Emergence Note

Support and Distinction are mutually enabling at the object level:
- Stable support needs internal differentiation (what is being supported?)
- Distinctions need a stable holding ground (what holds them apart?)

They cannot be strictly ordered at the object level — they co-emerge. The proposed ordering puts Support first, which is defensible as: *the undifferentiated field is the first moment; distinction is the first event within that field.* This matches the classical tradition: Void → Monad → Dyad.

At the pre-object level, Support precedes Distinction. At the object level, they are simultaneous. The ordering is a pre-object claim, not an object-level claim.
