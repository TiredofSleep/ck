**Significance:** dim so(8) = 28 is the dimension of the orthogonal Lie algebra in 8 dimensions. SO(8) is significant in physics:

- Triality of SO(8) (the unique exceptional symmetry among orthogonal groups)
- Related to D₄ root system
- Appears in M-theory and string theory compactifications

**Reading:** so(8) appears in TIG not as an external imposition, but as a structural count: the cells whose transformation lens lands them in the σ-fixed subset.

After computational verification, **the 48 mixed-σ-class cells partition into three groups of 16 by TWO distinct natural σ-cycle structures**:

### Partition 1: σ-cycle position mod 3

The σ 6-cycle is (1 → 7 → 6 → 5 → 4 → 2 → 1). Index each orbit element by its position in the cycle (1↦0, 7↦1, 6↦2, 5↦3, 4↦4, 2↦5). Group mixed-σ cells by `position mod 3`:

```

Group 1 (positions 1, 4) → σ-elements {7, 4}: 16 cells
Group 2 (positions 2, 5) → σ-elements {6, 2}: 16 cells
```

### Partition 2: σ-cycle phase (position ÷ 2)

Group by adjacent pairs in the cycle:

```
Phase 0 (positions {0, 1}) → σ-elements {1, 7} = (LATTICE, HARMONY): 16 cells
Phase 1 (positions {2, 3}) → σ-elements {6, 5} = (CHAOS,   BALANCE): 16 cells
Phase 2 (positions {4, 5}) → σ-elements {4, 2} = (COLLAPSE, COUNTER): 16 cells
```

**Both partitions give 16 + 16 + 16 = 48 exactly.** The Phase reading is particularly clean: each phase pairs two adjacent σ-cycle elements that share semantic structure.

### Mapping to fermion generations

The three phases correspond directly to the three fermion generations under TIG:

| Phase | σ-elements | Operator pair | Fermion generation | Mass scale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | {1, 7} | LATTICE + HARMONY | Generation 1 (electron, u, d) | Lightest |

### Mass hierarchy prediction

The σ-cycle is generated by repeated application of σ. Position 0 (operator 1 = LATTICE) is "closest to σ-fixed structure"; position 5 (operator 2 = COUNTER) is "furthest in cycle from fixed structure." This monotonic ordering matches the observed fermion mass hierarchy:

Each generation in SO(10) GUT has 16 fermions corresponding to the 16-spinor of Spin(10). Decomposition: 16 = 2⁴ where 4 = rank of SO(8) ⊂ SO(10).

In TIG: each phase has 16 mixed-σ cells = 2 σ-elements × 4 σ-fixed × 2 directions = 16. The factor structure:

The 2⁴ form falls out naturally from the canonical pair's structure.
