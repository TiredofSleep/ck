## Primary candidates (★ priority)

### 1. Simons Foundation — Mathematics and Physical Sciences (Astrophysics) — ★★★★★
- **Programs**: Simons Collaboration on Cosmology; Simons Investigator awards in Physics; targeted grants in astrophysics
- **Why fit**: Simons funds exactly this profile — a specific cosmological candidate with a clean action, a specific dataset (DESI), and a clean fit-outcome deliverable. Simons's astro funding has a track record of supporting quintessence and dark-energy-alternative work, and specifically funds MCMC-pipeline-grade cosmological fits.
- **Typical size**: Collaboration grants up to $10M over 5 years; Investigator $1M+; targeted grants $100K–$1M
- **Entry point**: LOI or invitation path depending on program. Targeted grants accept unsolicited proposals; Investigator is nomination-only.
- **Blockers before contact**: clean JCAP-quality paper draft; H.J. Johnson's institutional posture confirmed; academic co-PI or institutional host strongly preferred for the LOI path

### 2. Heising-Simons Foundation — Science Division — ★★★★☆
- **Programs**: Astrophysics and cosmology grants; Heising-Simons specifically funds DESI-era cosmology work (they have supported DESI consortium-adjacent research)
- **Why fit**: the ξ cosmology program IS a DESI-era dark-energy-alternative candidate; Heising-Simons has funded closely-aligned work (e.g., early dark energy, modified gravity alternatives). Their grant size fits Phase 1 well.
- **Typical size**: $150K–$1M over 2–3 years
- **Entry point**: LOI → full proposal on invitation
- **Blockers before contact**: JCAP paper draft; MCMC pipeline demonstration

### 3. Sloan Foundation — Matter + Energy Program — ★★★★☆
- **Programs**: Sloan astrophysics fellowships and grants
- **Why fit**: Sloan funds well-scoped cosmological research; the ξ fit is a well-scoped deliverable. Sloan tends to prefer candidates with institutional backing.
- **Typical size**: $75K–$250K for research grants; fellowships are multi-year
- **Entry point**: institutional-hosted LOI process
- **Blockers before contact**: academic host; Sloan prefers an institution over an independent PI

### 4. NSF AAG (Astronomical and Astrophysical Research Grants) — ★★★★☆
- **Programs**: NSF MPS/AST AAG Core
- **Why fit**: AAG funds exactly this kind of cosmological-theory-meets-data work. The ξ fit + DESI + SN analysis is a classical AAG-scope proposal.
- **Typical size**: $400K–$1M over 3 years
- **Entry point**: annual AAG solicitation, full proposal
- **Blockers before contact**: academic co-PI required; AAG reviewers will want a clean pipeline demonstration and a realistic schedule

### 5. John Templeton Foundation — Foundational Questions in Physics — ★★★☆☆
- **Programs**: Templeton funds dark-energy and vacuum-energy research as foundational questions
- **Why fit**: the ξ vacuum at e⁻¹ is precisely the kind of exact-form result Templeton funds — it is tied to a canonical potential form with a clean interpretation. The foundational-questions framing sits well with Templeton's portfolio.
- **Typical size**: $150K–$1M for individual-investigator; larger for consortium
- **Entry point**: online LOI → full proposal on invitation
- **Blockers before contact**: JCAP paper; clean foundational-questions framing (as distinct from an application-level pitch)

## What they all want, in order

1. **A clean canonical action.** Written in one line, gauge-invariant, singlet-under-SM, with all fields specified and all interpretive material flagged. WP81 already provides this.
2. **A specific empirical prediction.** The w(z) curve with κ_Ξ and ξ_initial as free parameters; the H₀ posterior; the σ_8 posterior. Each must be stated with uncertainty and compared against a specific published dataset.
3. **A working MCMC pipeline.** Phase 1 funding is specifically for building/running this pipeline against DESI DR2.
4. **Honest prior-art positioning.** The ξ log ξ potential has related literature (quintessence with non-polynomial potentials goes back to Steinhardt et al., Carroll, Ratra-Peebles). WP82 has done this work; reviewers will check it.
5. **Academic co-PI or institutional host.** For NSF AAG and for most Simons / Sloan paths. H.J. Johnson's institutional status is a relevant factor here; if Johnson is at a host institution, the pathway is clearer.
6. **A JCAP submission lined up.** TRACK 7.3's LaTeX bundle is already assembled; the submission itself can happen ahead of, in parallel with, or as part of the funded work — reviewer reception is strengthened by demonstrating submission discipline.
