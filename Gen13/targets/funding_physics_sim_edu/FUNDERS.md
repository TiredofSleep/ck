## Primary candidates (★ priority)

### 1. NSF EHR — Division of Undergraduate Education (DUE) + DRK-12 + ECR — ★★★★★
- **Programs**:
  - **Improving Undergraduate STEM Education (IUSE)**: Engaged Student Learning track supports pedagogical innovation + classroom-tested materials for undergraduate STEM. ~$300K–$600K typical.
  - **DRK-12 (Discovery Research K-12)**: supports novel curriculum-and-assessment materials for grades K-12, with advanced-secondary physics as a natural target. ~$450K–$3M.
  - **ECR (EHR Core Research)**: supports research on STEM learning at all levels including simulations + learning-environments research. ~$500K–$2M over 3 years.
- **Why fit**: NSF EHR funds exactly this profile — "a new interactive simulator with a pedagogical productionization plan + classroom-tested curriculum module + learning-outcomes evaluation." IUSE-ESL fits Phase 1 + Phase 2 together. The IUSE proposal format explicitly anticipates projects of this shape.
- **Typical size**: IUSE-ESL $300K–$600K / 2–3 years; DRK-12 up to $3M; ECR up to $2M
- **Entry point**: annual IUSE solicitation (typically Jan deadline); DRK-12 annual; ECR annual
- **Blockers before contact**: academic PI required (this is a hard NSF rule for IUSE/DRK-12/ECR), PER co-investigator identified, institutional host, IRB plan

### 2. NSF PHY — Education & Interdisciplinary Research in Physics — ★★★★☆
- **Programs**: NSF Division of Physics periodically supports physics-education research under its interdisciplinary / education umbrella; specific calls for interactive-simulator + learning-outcomes work appear in the NSF DOE/PHY joint calls
- **Why fit**: the simulator's content is physics-grade (not generic STEM); NSF PHY's education track is sometimes a better-scoped home than the broader NSF EHR tracks
- **Typical size**: $200K–$500K / 2–3 years
- **Entry point**: annual PHY core solicitation (coordinate with program officer)
- **Blockers before contact**: academic PI; institutional host; PER collaborator; preliminary learning-outcomes data or at minimum a pre-registered evaluation plan

### 3. Simons Foundation — Science Education subprogram — ★★★★☆
- **Programs**: Simons Foundation Math + Physical Sciences has an education subportfolio including Math for America (teacher fellowships), early-career investigator support for education-oriented projects, and targeted grants in physics-and-math-education innovation.
- **Why fit**: Simons values rigor + classroom-relevance + measurable impact — well-matched to the branch's "classroom pilot + learning-outcomes publication" framing
- **Typical size**: $150K–$1M over 2–3 years
- **Entry point**: Simons Foundation Education contact; institutional LOI
- **Blockers before contact**: institutional host strongly preferred; PER collaboration; classroom partner

## What they all want, in order

1. **A working prototype** — the Crystal-Lattice-Matrix-MYTHDRIFT simulator is already written (1,157 LOC). This is already satisfied at a prototype level; Phase 1 upgrades it to classroom-grade.
2. **A PER (physics-education-research) collaborator** — an academic researcher in physics education whose job it is to design the learning-outcomes study. Without a PER collaborator, an IUSE / DRK-12 / PER-journal pitch will not survive review.
3. **An institutional academic host** — for IRB purposes (classroom pilot with students requires IRB approval) and for NSF-PI eligibility. Brayden is an independent PI; the pitch needs a co-PI with academic affiliation who will host the study.
4. **A classroom partner** — at least one institution (undergraduate or advanced-secondary) willing to run the one-week module with pre/post assessment. Phase 1's outcome must include a signed classroom-partner agreement, even if Phase 2 is what runs the pilot.
5. **A measurable learning-outcome goal** — what specific concept or skill will the simulator improve learning on? "Students will demonstrate improved understanding of [specific learning objective X]" at a measurable delta in pre/post assessment.
6. **Open-source release + accessibility** — permissive license (MIT / Apache-2.0 for code, CC-BY for curriculum + guide), WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility audit, content readable on low-bandwidth connections.
7. **A dissemination plan** — how does the simulator reach other classrooms after the pilot? Phase 3 maps this out (AAPT workshops, PhysPort library, teacher-PD partnerships).
