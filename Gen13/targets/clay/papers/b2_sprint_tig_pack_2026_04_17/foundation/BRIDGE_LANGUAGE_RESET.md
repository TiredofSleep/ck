# Bridge Language Reset
## Approved Phrases and Banned Phrases

---

## Purpose

Language choices carry epistemic weight. Words like "subsystem," "completion," "equivalence," "physics" assert containment or identity that has not been earned. Words like "structural overlap," "candidate invariant," "local chart" preserve investigation while making narrower claims.

This document freezes the approved vocabulary for discussing relations between TSML/BHML and any external framework (including STD). Drafts that use banned language are to be reworded before publication.

---

## Approved Phrases

**About the relation between frameworks:**

- "Structural overlap between [X] and TSML/BHML."
- "Shared grammar of collapse / coherence."
- "Candidate collapse/coherence rule family."
- "Common treatment of non-injectivity."
- "Both frameworks occupy overlapping conceptual territory."
- "Family resemblance in how [specific aspect] is handled."
- "Potentially transferable invariant, pending test in [setting]."

**About TSML itself:**

- "TSML is a finite local chart on Z/10Z."
- "The theorem spine pertains to Z/10Z only; its scope is explicit."
- "The canonical construction is definable for every ring in the compatibility family."
- "Specific values are ring-specific; rule-shapes are candidate invariants."

**About invariants:**

- "Candidate invariant."
- "Confirmed transport in [specific settings]."
- "Asymptotically recoverable."
- "Holds in the prior-free discovery across [N] datasets."
- "Structurally observed in [K] of [M] tested cases."

**About partial results:**

- "Structurally suggestive, quantitatively unearned."
- "Retained as calculus fact, not as derived bridge."
- "Framework in place, empirical transport not yet exercised."
- "Awaiting [specific test]."

## Banned Phrases

**Containment / identification language:**

- ❌ "[External framework] is a subsystem of TIG."
- ❌ "TIG completes [external framework]."
- ❌ "[External framework] is contained within TSML/BHML."
- ❌ "TIG equivalent of [external framework]."
- ❌ "TSML corresponds to [specific physical quantity]."
- ❌ "[External quantity] emerges from TSML."

**Marketing / overclaim language:**

- ❌ "Perfect fit."
- ❌ "Excellent match."
- ❌ "Exact match" (unless it is a bit-exact verified computation, not a convenient coincidence).
- ❌ "Direct embodiment."
- ❌ "Very strong / Strong" as evidence tags without supporting numbers.
- ❌ "Natural completion."
- ❌ "Derivation" applied to assertions that are not derivations.

**Ontology language (unless narrowly scoped):**

- ❌ "TSML is physics."
- ❌ "TSML is the structure of [reality / consciousness / spacetime]."
- ❌ "TSML captures the geometry of [X]."
- ❌ Any statement of the form "[TSML property] = [physical constant]" without explicit derivation and independent verification.

**Retracted specific claims:**

- ❌ "Spectral gap γ ≈ 3/4" (on TSML).
- ❌ "Dominant eigenvalue ≈ 9" (on TSML).
- ❌ "TSML's spectral properties match STD's OSR scaling."
- ❌ Any soft reintroduction of these as approximate or "conceptual" — they are retracted, not approximate.

---

## Phrases Requiring Careful Scoping

**"Bridge":**
- ❌ "Bridge" unqualified, as in "the TSML-STD bridge."
- ✓ "Candidate bridge, pending quantitative verification."
- ✓ "Structural bridge at the conceptual level, quantitative bridge not yet earned."

**"Maps to":**
- ❌ "TSML maps to STD's Master Sampler."
- ✓ "TSML may correspond to STD's Master Sampler under the mapping [X], to be tested."

**"Observer / consciousness / readout":**
- ❌ "CK's 50 Hz loop IS the observer knot."
- ✓ "CK's 50 Hz loop is a concrete nested feedback implementation that has structural similarity to STD's observer-knot concept; whether they are the same kind of system is open."

**"Transport":**
- Reserved for verified movement of invariants across settings.
- ✓ "The default-plus-seam architecture transports across 23 tested carriers."
- ❌ "The invariants transport to physics" (unless a specific physical derivation is in hand).

---

## When Bridge Language May Return

Banned language becomes approved only after:

1. The relevant quantitative claim is independently audited (Moves 2, 3, 4 in the STD program).
2. A specific STD equation is cited, with a direct derivation to a TSML object.
3. At least one pre-registered prediction from the proposed bridge passes independent verification on new data.

Even then, preferred language remains "bridge" or "correspondence" — not "subsystem" or "completion."

## Enforcement Rule

Before any memo, README section, or sprint goes to a collaborator (human or AI):

1. Search the draft for each banned phrase.
2. Reword any occurrences using the approved templates.
3. Verify every evidence tag ("observed," "confirmed," "strong") has specific numerical or citation backing.
4. If a claim cannot be supported under the approved vocabulary, remove it — do not re-phrase to preserve it.

This is the same discipline that made Sprints 18–27 produce real findings. Extending it to all bridge-relevant writing is the current task.
