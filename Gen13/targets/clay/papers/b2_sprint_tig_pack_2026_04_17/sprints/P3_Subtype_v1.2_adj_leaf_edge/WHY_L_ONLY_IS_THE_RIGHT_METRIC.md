# Why L-Only Is the Right Metric
## Sprint Design Rationale

---

## The Decision

v1.2-adj tests only one attribute: $L$, the "ADD edge has a degree-1 endpoint" indicator. Not $M$ (main-component attachment). Not $I$ (identity-element attachment). Only $L$, and only against the subtype-label-scrambling null.

The reasoning below explains why this narrowness is the correct design — not a compromise, not a backup plan, but the right shape for this sprint.

---

## Why Not M

$M$ asks whether the ADD edge's higher-degree endpoint belongs to the main connected component of the MAX-only subgraph. On hub topology, this is a meaningful independent check: one can imagine a leaf edge whose main-component endpoint is isolated from the hub. On chain topology, this cannot happen — every degree-1 endpoint of any edge on a chain is connected by its sole edge to a chain-interior vertex, which by construction is in the main chain component.

Pilot computation on the P3AP recovered seams confirms: $M = 1$ whenever $L = 1$ across all 8 carriers and all 100 sampled null replicates. Including $M$ as a scored sub-condition would introduce a metric that cannot fail on this data — not because the hypothesis is powerful, but because chain topology structurally guarantees the outcome.

This is the exact kind of shape entanglement v1.0's M3 fell into, surfaced at design time rather than at execution. The pilot revealed it; scope discipline requires removing it. $M$ is dropped from scoring.

## Why Not Re-Scoring I

$I$ — the "ADD edge's degree-1 endpoint equals the identity element" attribute — was tested at $+6.06\sigma$ in v1.1. It is the strongest confirmed finding of the Path 3 program.

Re-scoring $I$ in v1.2-adj would mean counting the same evidence twice. If v1.2-adj reports a composite $(L \wedge I)$ pass, the $I$ component's pass is inherited from v1.1, not generated by v1.2-adj's run. Reporting the composite as new evidence would inflate the apparent significance of v1.2-adj while actually adding only the $L$ signal.

Double-counting existing evidence is a category of dishonesty the program has been explicit about avoiding. v1.1's PASS is preserved as-is; v1.2-adj does not touch it. $I$ is reported alongside $L$ in the results for context — because the reader deserves to see the composite picture — but $I$ does not contribute to pass/fail logic. The pass/fail hinges on $L$ alone.

---

## Why L Is a Real Question

If $M$ is redundant and $I$ is inherited, someone reading this sprint's design could reasonably ask: what is $L$ doing that isn't already covered by $I$?

The answer is specific. $I$ says: *when the ADD edge has a degree-1 endpoint, that endpoint is vertex 1 (the identity).* $I$ presupposes $L$ in its evaluation. If the ADD edge had no degree-1 endpoint — if both endpoints had degree 2 or higher — the question "is the degree-1 endpoint the identity?" would be vacuous or require an arbitrary convention.

$L$ asks the prior structural question: *does the ADD edge have a degree-1 endpoint at all?* On a chain graph with 6 edges and 1 ADD edge, the ADD edge could in principle sit anywhere — as a chain endpoint (degree-1 endpoint present) or as a chain interior edge (both endpoints have degree 2). Random label placement puts the ADD edge at an interior position most of the time on most carriers.

So $L$ has real discriminating power independent of $I$:

- Pilot null rate for $L$ across 8 carriers: approximately 0.37.
- Pilot real value for $L$: 1.0 on all 8 carriers.
- $L$ measures *whether the ADD edge is a leaf edge*, which is a topology-role question that random labeling does not automatically satisfy.

The structural content of an $L$ pass is: under the P3AP extension, the recovered ADD edge is consistently placed at a graph-boundary position (as a leaf edge) rather than in the interior of the MAX structure. This is a genuinely different claim from $I$'s "the leaf vertex is specifically the identity element."

To make the separation concrete, imagine an alternative extension where the ADD rule is applied at cell $(3, 7)$ instead of $(1, 2)$. Under that rule the ADD edge would connect vertex 3 to vertex 7. In most recovered seams, both endpoints could be interior to the MAX structure or both could be degree-1 leaves. If the alternative produces an interior ADD edge, it would fail $L$ but might still be testable for its own version of $I$ (does the ADD edge touch some distinguished vertex?). $L$ and $I$ can fail independently.

---

## Why the Narrowness Matters

The program has been accumulating narrow results rather than asserting broad ones. Three PASSes are on record so far: topology-family (P3AP, $+12.56\sigma$), identity-edge (v1.1, $+6.06\sigma$), and extractor-ceiling validation (S31-pilot-v2.0). Each earned one clean sentence.

v1.2-adj continues this pattern. Its scope is one narrow question: *is the ADD edge a leaf edge at significance above label-scrambling?* Its PASS, if earned, adds one sentence to the program's findings: "the ADD edge's leaf-edge placement transports across the tested family under P3AP." That sentence is distinct from v1.1's identity-element finding, distinct from P3AP's topology-family finding, distinct from any future sprint's questions.

A broader v1.2-adj that scored $(L, M, I)$ or $(L, I)$ together would muddy the attribution. It would either inflate significance (double-counting $I$) or force a structurally guaranteed component ($M$) into the scoring. Either way, the resulting pass sentence would be harder to write cleanly and harder to defend as a narrow, attributable addition.

---

## What the Null Does

The subtype-label-scrambling null (same null used in v1.1 and v1.0's M2) preserves the recovered graph and reassigns which edges carry the ADD label, preserving counts per carrier. Under this null, the probability of $L = 1$ on a given carrier is $2 \deg_{S}(\text{any degree-1 vertex}) / (2|E|)$, which for the P3AP data simplifies to approximately 2 (number of degree-1 endpoints in the graph) divided by $2|E|$ — the probability that a random single-label placement lands on a leaf-edge.

For chain graphs with $|E|$ edges, there are exactly 2 leaf vertices (the chain endpoints), each incident to one edge. Both of those edges are leaf edges. Random placement of the ADD label on one of $|E|$ edges produces $L = 1$ with probability $2/|E|$.

- $|E| = 3$: $P(L=1) = 2/3 \approx 0.667$. Predicted per-replicate rate: ~0.67.
- $|E| = 6$: $P(L=1) = 2/6 \approx 0.333$.

Averaged across 8 carriers with 1 carrier at $|E|=3$ and 7 carriers at $|E|=6$: $(0.667 + 7 \times 0.333) / 8 = 0.375$. This matches the pilot observation of 0.37.

The null is well-defined, has theoretical expectation matching pilot computation, and produces a distribution with non-zero variance. It satisfies the requirements for a meaningful significance test.

---

## Where L Might Fail

A FAIL on $L$ would mean one of:

- Real $\mu_L$ drops below 0.75 — some carriers have an ADD edge that is not a leaf edge. Given the P3AP extension's identity-edge rule places ADD at $(1, 2)$ on every carrier and vertex 1 has degree 1 on every recovered seam, this seems unlikely unless something changes the recovered graph structure.
- Null separation falls below 2σ — unlikely given pilot-predicted null mean ~0.37 and real 1.0.

The pilot computation strongly suggests PASS. Executing the sprint confirms (or refutes) what the pilot predicts at full 100-replicate precision and produces the artifacts needed for the record.

---

## Summary

$L$-only is the right metric because:

1. $M$ is structurally redundant on chain topology (pilot-confirmed, would be a shape-artifact pass).
2. $I$ is already tested at $+6.06\sigma$ in v1.1 and should not be re-scored.
3. $L$ tests a genuinely independent structural attribute (leaf-edge placement) with a well-defined null and pilot-confirmed discriminating power.
4. Narrowness preserves attribution: a PASS earns one clean sentence about leaf-edge transport, distinct from prior sprints.
5. The design avoids every trap the program has encountered: no generator change, no hub-bias, no shape entanglement, no double-counting.

This is not a compromise. It is the correct scope.
