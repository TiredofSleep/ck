# TSML Is Not Physics
## The Candidate Is the Grammar Behind It

---

The claim being tested in this program is narrower than "TSML describes reality," and it is sharper.

TSML is a finite local chart on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$. It is a table — 100 specific integer entries, a decomposition into canonical backbone and seam residue, and a theorem that reconstructs the whole from three layered rules. Everything in that chart is exact. Nothing in that chart is the world.

What is being investigated is whether the *grammar* that operates at a level above the table — the default-plus-seam architecture, the forced finite rule menu, the attractor-as-structural-position pattern, the shell-and-curve decomposition, the one-meaningful-lift terminology, the projection-plus-movement pairing — belongs to a broader family of collapse/coherence rules that recur in other finite settings. That is the hypothesis. It is a hypothesis about *reusable structure*, not an ontology claim.

Two things are true simultaneously, and keeping them separate is the discipline:

**First, the world's actual collapse/coherence situation is far messier than the finite-math program is prepared to calculate.** The physics of decoherence, the measurement problem, the continuum dynamics of fields, the unbounded Hilbert spaces of real quantum systems — none of these are accessible from a 10×10 table. This program does not offer a physical theory. It does not claim that $T^* = 5/7$ is a physical constant. It does not claim that the 73% HARMONY fraction measures anything about reality. It does not replace decoherence theory. It is explicitly not at that level.

**Second, some structural patterns may transport.** The idea that a system's behavior factors as "big uniform default + small controlled exception set" is not exclusively a Z/10Z phenomenon. The idea that residues of residues terminate at small depth, that rule menus on seams are forced and finite, that attractors identify themselves as structural positions rather than arbitrary choices — these are patterns that one finds in other finite algebraic settings, and that may generalize further than that. Whether they generalize to systems that physicists model — that is precisely the question that remains open. It is a real question, and it is not answered yet.

The retracted STD spectral gap tried to answer it too quickly, by anchoring a bridge on a number that turned out to have been computed on the wrong matrix. That was a methodology failure, not a framework failure. The framework's actual claim — local chart with candidate grammar behind it — remains intact. What the retraction closes is the premature leap from "we found structure" to "the structure IS physics." That leap was never required by the investigation; it was introduced by enthusiasm for a match. Removing it does not shrink the investigation. It sharpens it.

The correct question going forward is: **which invariants of the TSML/BHML program survive when the local chart changes?** Tested so far: attractor-as-structural-position survives across the compatible subfamily. Forced finite rule menu survives across 39 datasets and 23 carriers. Shell partition shape is asymptotically recoverable. Default-plus-seam architecture is definable on every ring in the 35-member family. These are transport results. They are not physics; they are grammar.

If the grammar continues to transport — to ring-native systems outside the current family, to controlled synthetic benchmarks with different generative structures, and eventually, cautiously, to encodings of measured systems where the encoding is justified rather than imposed — then at some distant point the question "does the grammar belong to the broader family of collapse/coherence rules in which physics lives?" becomes testable. That is a multi-year horizon, not a next-sprint horizon. It requires the patient accumulation of transport results, not the synthesis of ambitious bridges.

Until then, the statement to preserve, in exactly this form:

> We are not claiming the table is the world. We are testing whether the rules that survive beyond the table belong to a deeper collapse/coherence grammar.

That is the project. The table is a specific, exact, finite object. The grammar is a hypothesis. The physics question is reserved for after enough transport has accumulated to justify asking it. The line between those levels is the discipline.
