# Local Chart vs Transferred Grammar
## Two-Column Split of the TSML/BHML Program

---

## Why This Split Matters

The project's findings live at two levels. Conflating them produces overclaiming; separating them produces disciplined transport. The left column is Z/10Z-specific data — real and exact within its scope, but not generalizable. The right column is candidate transportable grammar — rules and patterns that may apply beyond Z/10Z, with each entry tagged by evidence strength.

Every numerical or structural claim in the TSML/BHML program belongs to exactly one column. Entries do not migrate between columns without new evidence.

## Status Tags for the Right Column

Every right-column entry is tagged by evidence:

**Confirmed in ≥ 2 settings (compatible rings, synthetic benchmarks, or prior-free discovery):**

- Attractor emerges as structural position.
- Ring-specific exceptions at V0.
- Zero-divisor-row absorption pattern.

**Framework in place, transport not yet meaningfully exercised:**
- Curve / deformation consistency.
- Attractor-row absorption generality.

**Conjectured, awaiting test:**
- Branch-rule mapping generality.
- Hub + branch seam topology generality.

**Retracted (do not transport):**
- Any spectral-gap-equals-3/4 claim.
- Any dominant-eigenvalue-equals-9 claim.
- Any universal-physical-constant reading of ring-specific numbers.

## Rule for Using This Split

When writing about the TSML/BHML program:

- If a claim references a specific cell value, a specific count, a specific vertex, or a specific numerical ratio on Z/10Z: **left column**. State scope explicitly: "in Z/10Z" or "for this specific local chart."

- If a claim references a structural pattern, a rule type, a data-recoverable invariant: **right column**. State transport evidence explicitly: "confirmed across N carriers," "asymptotically recoverable," or "conjectured — not yet tested."

- If a claim cannot be cleanly assigned to either column, **do not publish it yet**. Either refine it into one column with its evidence, or acknowledge it as speculative.

## What This Split Prevents

- Treating Z/10's 73% HARMONY fraction as a universal coherence ratio.
- Treating Z/10's specific seam pairs as universal exceptions.
- Treating the retracted 3/4 spectral gap as a recoverable invariant under "softer" phrasing.
- Treating any carrier-specific numerical ratio as "physical."
- Conflating "transportable pattern" with "established physical law."
