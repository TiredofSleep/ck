# What Counts As A Robust V0 Result
## Short Note on Verdict Categories Under Sensitivity

---

## Why "Robust" Is The Right Standard Here

With Source 3 split into S3a and S3b, the sprint produces two scored runs on the same data. The question "what verdict did the sprint earn?" now has to account for whether the verdict depends on the S3 direction or survives it.

**Robust** means the verdict is invariant under the S3 direction choice — the same map wins under both S3a and S3b at cleanness, or neither wins under either. A robust result is a verdict the sprint has earned *independently of the S3 argument-direction question*.

**Sensitive** means the verdict changes with the S3 direction. This is not a failure of the sprint; it is a specific finding — the checkpoint's discriminating content depends partly on the unresolved argument about what attractor-privilege means under the framework's vocabulary.

The distinction matters because a PASS that depends on S3 direction is a different epistemic object than a PASS that survives both directions. The first is a PASS of "rubric plus argument choice"; the second is a PASS of "rubric alone."

## Four Possible Robust Verdict Categories

### Robust PASS-V0-I

Map-V0-I meets the PASS thresholds (winner ≥ +3, loser ≤ +1, gap ≥ 2) under BOTH S3a AND S3b.

Sentence earned: *Under the pair-primitive framework's vocabulary applied to Z/10's V0 region, Map-V0-I (V0_Z = persistent, V0_H = excluded) produces a coherent structural fit at pre-registered cleanness **under both frozen readings of Source 3**. The checkpoint is robust to the attractor-privilege argument direction.*

### Robust PASS-V0-II

Map-V0-II meets the PASS thresholds under BOTH S3a AND S3b.

Sentence earned: symmetric to above with Map-V0-II.

### Robust FAIL

Neither map meets the PASS thresholds under EITHER S3a OR S3b.

Sentence earned: *Under the pair-primitive framework's vocabulary applied to Z/10's V0 region, neither map reaches pre-registered cleanness under either frozen reading of Source 3. The framework's vocabulary does not cash out decisively on V0 boundary behavior, and this non-result is robust to the attractor-privilege argument direction.*

### Robust UNCLEAR

One or both maps score in the ambiguous range (e.g., ≥+3 but gap <2, or both in {+2,+3}) under BOTH S3a AND S3b.

Sentence earned: *Under both frozen readings of Source 3, the checkpoint produces ambiguous aggregate scores. The ambiguity is not due to Source 3 direction sensitivity; it is a property of the rubric's application to V0 under either reading.*

## One Non-Robust Verdict Category

### UNCLEAR by Sensitivity

The verdict changes with the S3 direction. For example:
- PASS-V0-I under S3a, FAIL under S3b.
- PASS-V0-I under S3a, PASS-V0-II under S3b.
- PASS-V0-I under S3a, UNCLEAR under S3b.
- Any other asymmetric outcome.

Sentence earned: *Under the pair-primitive framework's vocabulary applied to Z/10's V0 region, the verdict depends on the frozen reading of Source 3: [specific S3a verdict] under S3a and [specific S3b verdict] under S3b. The checkpoint's discriminating content on V0 is not robust to the attractor-privilege argument direction.*

This is the outcome category that did not exist in the prior PPM sprints. It is not a failure; it is a specific finding about rubric fragility at Source 3.

## What Each Outcome Authorizes Next

### If Robust PASS (either map)

The pair-primitive framework has earned a second independent Z/10 checkpoint. The sentence stands regardless of Source 3 direction. Successors:
- V0 transport to Path 2 carriers (PPM-v3.0-transport, parallel to v2.0's move from v1.0).
- BHML or unit cyclic structure as a third checkpoint on Z/10.

### If Robust FAIL

The framework's scope on Z/10 is the seam, not the ring broadly — and this conclusion is not fragile to rubric choice. Successors:
- Foundation note work on why V0 is outside the framework's scope.
- Possibly a redesigned V0 checkpoint with different rubric sources, but this would be a different sprint under a different version.

### If Robust UNCLEAR

The rubric does not discriminate on V0 under either S3 direction. Successors:
- Diagnosis of the specific ambiguity (similar to v1.1's Reason A / Reason B attribution).
- Possibly a sharper rubric, but as a separate sprint.

### If UNCLEAR by Sensitivity

The framework's V0 reading depends on a vocabulary-level argument the framework has not settled. Successors:
- Foundation note work on the attractor-privilege argument — trying to determine whether one direction is structurally preferred on independent grounds.
- Not another V0 sprint until the argument direction is resolved at foundation level.

This outcome specifies precisely what foundation work the program would need before re-running V0.

## What None of the Outcomes Authorize

Per inherited PPM discipline:
- No composite claim merging V0 verdict with seam-checkpoint verdicts (Rule 19).
- No upgrade of prior PPM findings based on V0 result.
- No extension to rings beyond Z/10 without separate pre-reg.
- No scale examples, physics, ontology, or cross-domain reading.
- No shape-filter sprint authorization.
- No SAH validation or refutation (SAH stays at foundation register regardless).

## The Structural Character of the Robust/Sensitive Distinction

The distinction is not about confidence level (p-values, sigma, etc.). It is about whether the verdict is a property of the data-plus-rubric alone, or whether it additionally depends on a rubric-design choice the framework has not forced.

A "robust PASS" says: the framework's vocabulary and the V0 data produce a coherent reading *no matter which direction Source 3's argument runs*. That is a stronger epistemic claim than a fragile PASS.

An "UNCLEAR by sensitivity" says: the framework's vocabulary and the V0 data produce different readings depending on an argument choice the framework has not settled. That is a specific diagnostic finding — the program now knows *where* to do foundation work to stabilize the rubric.

Both are honest outputs. Neither is a failure.

## One-Sentence Framing

A robust V0 result is one that survives the Source 3 sensitivity branch with the same verdict under both S3a and S3b; an UNCLEAR-by-sensitivity result is one whose verdict depends on the S3 direction; the distinction matters because it separates verdicts the sprint has earned from verdicts that depend on unresolved argument-direction choices at the rubric-design level.
