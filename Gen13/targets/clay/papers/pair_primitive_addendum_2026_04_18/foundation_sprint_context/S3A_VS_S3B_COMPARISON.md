## 2. Where They Agree

Both readings agree on every point except the direction of the role-assignment for V0_H.

### Agreement 1 — V0_H is structurally special
Both readings treat V0_H as the distinguished subset of V0, not as one cell-set among many. S3a calls its specialness "resistance"; S3b calls it "signature." Both agree that V0_H is structurally non-trivial.

### Agreement 2 — V0_Z is the default
Both readings treat V0_Z as V0's default behavior. S3a reads this as "the absorbing nothingness" against which V0_H stands out; S3b reads this as "the non-attractor region" where h's signature is absent.

### Agreement 3 — The 17/2 split matters
Both readings treat the 17/2 cardinality ratio as significant. Under S3a, the ratio supports V0_H being "localized" (small, specific, not-by-default). Under S3b, the ratio is compatible with V0_H being "concentrated at the attractor" (not distributed).

### Agreement 4 — Rule (b) is the governing structure
Both readings accept Rule (b) as the defining constraint on V0. Neither attempts to redescribe Rule (b) or to extract structure from outside the rule.

### Agreement 5 — Sources 1, 2, 4 are algebraically anchored
Both readings agree that Sources 1, 2, and 4 are decided by algebraic facts (majority, override-structure, swap-symmetric cardinality). Neither reading disputes the fixed-source scores of +3/−3.

### Agreement 6 — The framework's vocabulary is the relevant interpretive tool
Both readings work within the framework's declared vocabulary (hold, gap, persistent, excluded, attractor). Neither imports external interpretive machinery.

**Consequence of the agreements:** The disagreement is genuinely local to Source 3. The two readings are not rival full-framework interpretations; they are rival interpretations of one specific structural feature.

## 3. Where They Diverge

### Divergence 1 — Which framework sub-vocabulary dominates

S3a prioritizes the **gap/hold** axis: the boundary region is defined by what it absorbs (gap) vs. what it doesn't absorb (hold). Attractor-privilege at the boundary means "what the attractor preserves," which is hold content.

S3b prioritizes the **persistent/gathering** axis: structure accumulates at the attractor, so wherever the attractor's signature appears we are at the structural backbone. Attractor-privilege at the boundary means "where the backbone reaches," which is persistent content.

Both sub-vocabularies are declared in the framework's foundation notes. The framework has not specified which dominates when they conflict.

### Divergence 2 — What "privilege" means

S3a treats "privilege" as resistance: privileged cells are those the default cannot absorb. Privilege is a property of being an exception to the default.

S3b treats "privilege" as participation: privileged cells are those that participate in the attractor's structural role. Privilege is a property of carrying the attractor's signature.

Both definitions are coherent. They are not the same definition. The framework has not picked one.

### Divergence 3 — What happens at the boundary is either-or

S3a says: at the boundary, the default is nothingness-collapse; the exception is hold-at-boundary (excluded-role).

S3b says: at the boundary, structure can either gather (V0_H, persistent) or be absent (V0_Z, excluded).

Under S3a, V0_H is the minority/exception role. Under S3b, V0_H is the majority/structural role (not in cardinality — in structural weight).

This is a genuine category-level disagreement. The framework's vocabulary does not force either resolution.

### Divergence 4 — What supports each reading from prior sprints

S3a aligns with v1.0, v2.0, and P3-Subtype-v1.1 patterns: "minority subtype at structurally distinguished location → excluded-side." Three sprint PASSes point this direction, but all three are on the *seam*, not on V0.

S3b aligns with `ATTRACTOR_RECONCILIATION.md`'s Role R2, the canonical construction's h-as-default role (R1), and v1.1's discussion of generator-vs-identity structural roles. These are framework-level anchors, not sprint-level results. They are more abstract.

The divergence here is: *does sprint-level pattern volume override framework-level vocabulary commitments, or vice versa?* The framework has not answered this.

### Divergence 5 — Whether the seam-pattern transports to V0

S3a's strongest argument is the seam-pattern transport: the framework has repeatedly been shown to read "minority at distinguished location" as excluded-side on the seam, so V0 should inherit this reading.

S3b's strongest argument against seam-pattern transport: the seam and V0 are structurally different regions. Rule 19 explicitly prohibits composing prior verdicts with current verdicts. Automatically transporting the seam's pattern to V0 would be exactly the kind of unjustified extension the rule forbids.

The framework has not specified whether seam patterns should serve as priors for V0 readings. This is a methodological divergence layered on top of the semantic divergence.

## 4. Why v3.0 Became Sensitivity-Limited

### The sprint was specifically designed to surface this

The original v3.0 draft froze Source 3 in the S3a direction. User review identified that choice as "too load-bearing" and required a sensitivity branch. The revised pre-reg made the choice explicit as a branch axis rather than as a rubric design decision.

This was the right move for a specific reason: **a rubric decision that cannot be defended from the framework's vocabulary without picking a side of an unresolved disagreement is not a legitimate rubric decision**. It is a framework commitment disguised as a rubric.

By splitting Source 3 into S3a and S3b, the sprint exposed that the framework had not actually made the commitment it needed to make for v3.0 to produce a single verdict. The UNCLEAR-by-Sensitivity outcome is not a failure of the sprint — it is the sprint correctly identifying the framework's gap.

### Why this specific hinge rather than a different one

Sources 1, 2, and 4 were algebraically anchored:
- Source 1: majority is a numerical property of the data (17/19 or 2/19).
- Source 2: override-status is a structural property of Rule (b).
- Source 4: exact-cardinality is a counting property of V0_H.

Source 3 has no algebraic anchor. "Attractor privilege" is a vocabulary phrase the framework uses without a declared structural predicate. The phrase's interpretation requires a reading of "privilege" — which the framework has not supplied.

The hinge is specifically about Source 3 because Source 3 is specifically the source where the framework's vocabulary was asked to do work it has not been specified for.

### What "sensitivity-limited" means operationally

The v3.0 outcome is not a FAIL (the rubric does discriminate, under S3a) and not a PASS (the rubric doesn't discriminate under S3b). The sprint's discriminating content is conditional on a framework commitment that the framework has not made. This is the precise meaning of "sensitivity-limited."

If the framework had explicit vocabulary for attractor-privilege direction, v3.0 would have produced a non-sensitivity verdict. It doesn't, so v3.0 produced a sensitivity verdict. That's the correct operational response to the framework's current state.

## 5. Reading The Divergences As A Program-Level Finding

The divergences above identify a **specific gap in the framework**: attractor-privilege at boundary regions has not been given directional vocabulary. This is not a vague shortcoming — it is a named, located gap.

The gap has three features worth recording:

**Feature 1 — It is local to one argument type.** The gap is not about the framework's general coherence; it is about one specific interpretive move (attractor-privilege at boundary) that the framework's vocabulary has not fully specified.

**Feature 2 — It is symmetric.** Both directions are equally supportable from existing materials. The framework has not privileged one direction through foundation-note language, through prior sprint verdicts (which are about the seam, not about V0), or through algebraic anchor.

**Feature 3 — It is testable once resolved.** If the framework made a directional commitment (through foundation-note work), a v3.0.1 sprint could re-run V0 with the committed direction frozen and produce a non-sensitivity verdict. The hinge is resolvable in principle; it is unresolved only because the foundation work to resolve it has not been done.

These features together mean the hinge is a **specific, named, future-resolvable gap** — not a fuzzy weakness. That is a better kind of open question than the program had before v3.0.

## 7. One-Paragraph Summary

S3a and S3b agree on every aspect of V0 except the direction of role-assignment for V0_H, which is decided by which framework sub-vocabulary (gap/hold vs persistent/gathering) is made to dominate at boundary regions — a choice the framework has not made. v3.0 became sensitivity-limited because Source 3 is the only rubric source without an algebraic anchor, and its vocabulary-level argument requires a framework commitment that does not exist. The outcome is a specific, named, future-resolvable gap in the framework's vocabulary, located at "attractor privilege at boundary regions," resolvable in principle by foundation-note work that picks a direction on independent grounds.
