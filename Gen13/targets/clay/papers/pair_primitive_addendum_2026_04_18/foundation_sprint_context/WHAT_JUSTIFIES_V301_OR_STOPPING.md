# What Would Justify A v3.0.1 Rerun, And What Would Justify Stopping At UNCLEAR
## Short Scope Note

---

## Register

Foundation-register. Companion to `ATTRACTOR_PRIVILEGE_FOUNDATION.md` and `S3A_VS_S3B_COMPARISON.md`. Not a sprint. Not a pre-registration.

---

## The Decision The Program Faces

PPM-v3.0 returned UNCLEAR by Sensitivity. The sprint's two possible futures:

- **Path A — Rerun as v3.0.1 with a frozen Source 3 direction.** Requires that the direction be established on independent grounds. The sprint itself would be mechanical — it reuses all of v3.0's infrastructure, removing only the sensitivity branch and committing to a single direction.

- **Path B — Stop the V0 lane at UNCLEAR.** Record that the framework's current vocabulary cannot produce a robust V0 verdict. Redirect future checkpoint work to other Z/10 features (BHML, unit cyclic structure) or to foundation-register work elsewhere.

This note specifies what conditions justify each path. It does not pick the path.

---

## What Would Justify A v3.0.1 Rerun

A rerun is justified **only** if all four conditions are met:

### Condition 1 — A directional commitment is produced on independent grounds

The Source 3 direction must be fixed through foundation-register work that argues for S3a or S3b based on the framework's existing vocabulary, declared principles, or structural commitments — NOT based on:

- What direction would make v3.0 pass.
- What direction would match v1.0/v2.0 patterns on the seam.
- What direction is more "natural" in an unargued sense.
- What direction produces a more useful result.

The argument must be reproducible from the framework's own foundation notes. A reader looking only at `ATTRACTOR_RECONCILIATION.md`, `GAP_AS_BOUNDARY_NOTHINGNESS.md`, `HOLD_GAP_FLOW_FOUNDATION.md`, and any new foundation notes produced for this purpose should be able to reach the same conclusion about the direction.

### Condition 2 — The three open questions have clear answers

From `ATTRACTOR_PRIVILEGE_FOUNDATION.md` §4, three specific questions were identified:

1. Does "attractor privilege at a boundary" have a declared status in the framework's vocabulary?
2. When a subtype is minority AND attractor-carrying AND at a boundary, is there a prioritization rule?
3. Is h's treatment meant to extend uniformly into boundary regions, or does the boundary transition trigger a role-shift?

A v3.0.1 rerun needs all three answered. Partial answers are not enough; if any of the three remains open, Source 3 still has unconstrained degrees of freedom and the sprint would re-fragilize.

### Condition 3 — The directional commitment is pre-registered

The foundation-register work settling Source 3's direction must be committed to the archive before the rerun is drafted. The pre-reg must cite the specific foundation note that settles the direction. This is the same discipline that made v1.0–v2.1 reproducible: the interpretive commitments must exist as documents, not as "the current thinking."

### Condition 4 — The rerun's value exceeds its cost

A v3.0.1 rerun under a resolved direction would most likely produce:
- **PASS-V0-I** if the direction resolves to S3a.
- **FAIL** if the direction resolves to S3b.

Both outcomes are predictable given the v3.0 results. So a v3.0.1 is primarily a **certification sprint**: it earns a non-sensitivity verdict in place of the sensitivity verdict, but it does not discover new structural information.

The cost of the rerun is: one pre-reg, one scoring execution, three result documents, and the implicit commitment to the resolved direction being the program's settled position on attractor privilege.

The rerun is justified if the settled direction is defensible enough that the program wants it on record as the framework's position. It is not justified if the direction is chosen to produce a PASS (in which case the rerun is inflation disguised as certification).

## What Would Justify Stopping At UNCLEAR

Stopping is justified if **any** of these conditions hold:

### Condition 1 — Independent grounds for the Source 3 direction are not available

If foundation-register work on attractor privilege at boundary regions does not produce a clear directional commitment — because the framework's existing vocabulary genuinely does not determine the direction, or because both directions remain equally defensible after extended analysis — then the hinge is fundamentally ambiguous on current grounds. Stopping at UNCLEAR is the honest response.

This is the outcome of a failure mode that is worth surfacing honestly: the framework is incomplete on this point. A rerun under an unjustified commitment would be covering up the incompleteness.

### Condition 2 — Independent grounds exist but argue that the hinge should remain unresolved

Some framework gaps are appropriate as gaps. Attractor privilege at boundary regions might turn out to be intentionally undetermined — e.g., because the framework is meant to apply to different kinds of boundary contexts where the appropriate direction varies. If foundation work concludes that the hinge should be left open as part of the framework's design, stopping at UNCLEAR is also the honest response.

This is a substantive outcome, not a cop-out. It would be captured in a foundation note explaining why the framework treats attractor privilege as context-dependent.

### Condition 3 — Other Z/10 checkpoint work is more valuable than resolving V0

Even if the hinge could be resolved, resolution may not be the best use of program effort. Building a BHML checkpoint or a unit-cyclic-structure checkpoint would give the framework additional independent points of contact on Z/10 without requiring the V0 resolution. If other checkpoint work has higher information yield, stopping at UNCLEAR on V0 and moving to the other work is justified.

The V0 UNCLEAR-by-Sensitivity result stands as a recorded finding; it is not a failure the program needs to clean up before moving on.

### Condition 4 — The program needs to package current state rather than extend

If the program's near-term need is to deliver current state to external audiences (e.g., for the France trip), stopping at UNCLEAR and packaging is more valuable than pursuing resolution. Resolution work can wait. The UNCLEAR-by-Sensitivity verdict is perfectly legitimate to deliver; it is not shameful and does not need to be replaced with a non-sensitivity verdict before packaging.

## The Asymmetry Between The Two Paths

Path A (rerun) requires a new foundation commitment from the framework. If that commitment is made well, the program advances; if it is made under pressure to produce a PASS, the program regresses (because inflation is harder to undo than an open question).

Path B (stop) requires no new commitment. It accepts the current framework state and redirects effort elsewhere. The cost is one open question remaining open; the benefit is no risk of inflation.

**Path B is the safer default.** Path A is justified only under specific positive conditions; Path B is justified whenever any of its conditions holds.

The program should adopt Path B unless and until all four Path A conditions are affirmatively met.

---

## A Specific Test For The Decision

Before moving to Path A, the program should answer this question:

> *If v3.0.1 returned FAIL under the resolved direction, would the program consider that result as informative as a v3.0.1 PASS?*

If the answer is yes — both outcomes would be accepted equally — then the directional commitment was genuinely produced on independent grounds, and the rerun is justified.

If the answer is no — the program is primarily hoping for a PASS and would treat a FAIL as a setback — then the directional commitment is not genuinely independent; it is being chosen to produce a preferred outcome. In that case Path A is inflation-prone and Path B should be chosen.

This test is a way of checking whether the directional commitment passes the pre-registration discipline that all prior PPM sprints have followed.

## One-Sentence Framing

A v3.0.1 rerun is justified only if a directional commitment for Source 3 is produced on independent grounds, three specific open questions from the attractor-privilege foundation note are answered, the commitment is pre-registered, and the rerun's value exceeds its cost; stopping at UNCLEAR is justified whenever independent grounds are not available, the hinge is intentionally open, other checkpoint work has higher yield, or packaging is the near-term priority — with Path B as the safer default.
