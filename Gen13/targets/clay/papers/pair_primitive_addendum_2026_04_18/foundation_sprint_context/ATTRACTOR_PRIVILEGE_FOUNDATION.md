# Attractor Privilege — Foundation Note
## On the Directional Ambiguity at Boundary Regions

---

## Register

Foundation-register work. Not a sprint. No pre-registration, no rubric, no verdict. Same register as `HOLD_GAP_FLOW_FOUNDATION.md` and `THML_BSML_SHAPE_ADMISSIBILITY_NOTE.md`.

Produced in response to PPM-v3.0's UNCLEAR-by-Sensitivity outcome, which identified a specific unresolved hinge: the direction of attractor privilege at boundary regions.

This note does not claim to resolve the hinge. It aims to state the two readings cleanly, locate each in existing evidence, and identify what remains undecidable on current grounds.

## 1. The Hinge, Stated Precisely

When a distinguished attractor $h$ receives a **boundary exception** — i.e., when a region of the carrier that would otherwise collapse to some default reveals a small number of cells that preserve $h$'s value — how should the framework read that privilege?

The two candidate readings:

- **S3a reading — persistent gathering denied at boundary; the exception is "irreducibility."** The boundary region's default is nothingness-collapse (absorption to 0 in the Z/10 case). The exception cells are what resists that collapse. Under the framework's vocabulary, resistance-at-boundary is **hold** content — irreducible distinction surviving what would otherwise absorb it. Hold content → excluded-side role (localized, distinguished, not-by-default).

- **S3b reading — structure gathers at the attractor; the exception is "gathering."** The attractor $h$ is "where structure collapses to" in the broader Z/10 dynamics. Cells that preserve $h$ carry the attractor's structural signature. Wherever the attractor's signature appears, we are at the persistent backbone of whatever process brought us there. Persistent content → persistent-side role.

Both readings are internally consistent with the framework's declared vocabulary. The hinge is whether "attractor exception cells" belong to the hold-role (excluded) or the backbone-role (persistent).

## 2. Mapping Each Reading To Hold/Gap Vocabulary

### S3a maps cleanly to "hold at boundary"

From `GAP_AS_BOUNDARY_NOTHINGNESS.md`:
- **Gap** = boundary nothingness; a region where distinction fails to propagate.
- **Hold** = irreducible distinction; what persists against absorption.

V0 under S3a:
- V0_Z (zero-absorption cells) = gap content. They enact the nothingness-collapse.
- V0_H (HARMONY exceptions) = hold content. They persist against the collapse.

This reading treats the boundary region as a test: *does anything survive?* The answer is *yes, only the attractor*. The attractor's survival IS the hold-signature at this specific boundary.

Mapping is clean. The framework's own vocabulary is being used in its native sense — gap as boundary nothingness, hold as distinction that boundary can't absorb.

### S3b maps cleanly to "structure gathers at attractor"

From `ATTRACTOR_RECONCILIATION.md`:
- $h$'s Role R2 is "structural position" — a distinguished element the construction is built around.
- $h$ is the element HARMONY collapses toward.

V0 under S3b:
- V0_H cells = where the attractor's structural signature appears at the boundary.
- V0_Z cells = where the attractor is absent; the boundary's default (zero) dominates.

Under this reading, V0_H is "structure at the boundary" and V0_Z is "no-structure at the boundary." Persistent-side = where structure is; excluded-side = where structure isn't.

Mapping is also clean. The framework's attractor reconciliation is being used — h as the gathering point of structure.

### The problem: both mappings are clean

Each reading uses the framework's own vocabulary consistently. Neither reading violates a declared principle. Neither reading requires importing outside interpretation.

The framework has two internally-coherent readings of the same data. The V0 cells $\{(0,7), (7,0)\}$ can be read as "hold content at boundary" (excluded) *or* as "attractor signature at boundary" (persistent), with equal vocabulary-level justification.

## 3. What Existing Results Support Each Reading

### Results compatible with S3a

**v1.0 PASS on Z/10 seam with Map B (MAX=persistent, ADD=excluded):**
- ADD (2 cells) = excluded-side, located at leaf, attaches to multiplicative identity.
- The excluded-side is characterized by **being at the boundary of the seam graph** (leaf) and **attaching to a structurally special element** (identity at vertex 1).
- By parallel: V0_H (2 cells at the boundary region, attaching to the distinguished attractor h) would also be excluded-side.
- **Pattern:** minority subtype at a structurally distinguished location → excluded-side.

**v2.0 PASS on 8 Path 2 carriers:**
- Same pattern: ADD (1 edge per carrier) at vertex 1 = excluded-side, multiplicatively-trivializing, at leaf.
- The pattern "minority + structurally distinguished position" → excluded-side is reinforced 8 times.

**P3-Subtype-v1.1 identity-edge at +6.06σ:** establishes that ADD's attachment to identity is a bridge-level transport feature. The finding is specifically about the structurally-distinguished-position-of-minority reading.

**Compatibility summary:** v1.0, v2.0, and P3-Subtype-v1.1 all support the pattern "minority subtype at structurally distinguished location → excluded-side role." V0_H fits this pattern exactly: 2 cells (minority) at h (distinguished attractor). S3a reads V0_H as excluded-side, which extends the pattern to the boundary region.

### Results compatible with S3b

**`ATTRACTOR_RECONCILIATION.md`'s Role R2 for h:**
- h is the element HARMONY collapses toward; cells near h accumulate structural importance.
- The framework treats h as a gathering point for structure. Wherever h appears prominently, we should expect structural backbone.
- V0_H cells literally preserve h's value against V0 absorption. This is h's signature at the boundary.
- **Pattern:** h-presence → structural backbone → persistent-side.

**Canonical construction's default role for h (Role R1):**
- h is the value filling 70-80% of the non-boundary, non-seam table. h is the construction's **default output**.
- V0_H cells produce h, aligning with the construction's default output.
- Under this pattern, V0_H is "default behavior extending into the boundary" → persistent-side (backbone of the construction's default).

**v1.1 FAIL on Z/10 under additive reading:**
- Vertex 1 was identified as the additive generator rather than additive identity, and the identity-element reading of v1.0 was said to have "no clean parallel" under additive reading.
- This suggests the framework's vocabulary treats "generator" and "identity" differently with respect to persistent/excluded roles.
- By analogy: h as attractor is a generator-like role (it produces structure), and under S3b h's appearance at V0_H would also read as generator-like → persistent-side.

**Compatibility summary:** `ATTRACTOR_RECONCILIATION.md`'s treatment of h, the canonical construction's h-as-default pattern, and the v1.1 discussion of generator-vs-identity roles are each compatible with S3b's reading that attractor presence at V0_H puts those cells at the persistent-side.

### The inconvenient symmetry

Each reading has independent existing support. **The supports are not equal in volume** (S3a has three direct sprint PASSes; S3b has framework-level vocabulary and one indirect sprint discussion), but the framework has not established that sprint-volume is the correct weighting criterion for resolving vocabulary disputes.

If sprint-volume were the criterion, S3a would win. But that criterion would need to be pre-registered as a resolution rule, and it has not been. And there's a specific reason to be careful about using it: v1.0 and v2.0 scored the *seam*, which is a different object than V0. The seam's "minority at distinguished position" pattern may not transport to V0 automatically. That is exactly the kind of unjustified transport Rule 19 was written to prevent.

## 4. Where The Hinge Remains Undecidable

The hinge is genuinely undecidable on current grounds because the framework has:

- **No explicit rule about attractor-at-boundary.** `ATTRACTOR_RECONCILIATION.md` discusses h's Role R1 (default output) and Role R2 (structural position) but does not address what happens when h appears at a boundary region that otherwise absorbs structure.

- **No explicit rule about "hold/gap" application to exception cells.** `GAP_AS_BOUNDARY_NOTHINGNESS.md` describes gap as boundary nothingness and hold as irreducible distinction, but does not specify what the framework does when a boundary region contains both gap content (absorption) and hold content (exceptions).

- **No explicit rule about minority vs attractor-carrying status.** When a subtype is both minority (supporting S3a's "excluded = minority-at-distinguished-location") and attractor-carrying (supporting S3b's "persistent = attractor-signature"), the framework has not specified which property dominates.

- **No algebraic anchor.** Sources 1, 2, 4 each have algebraic anchors (majority counts, override structure, cardinality-symmetry). Source 3's argument is purely vocabulary-level. There is no structural fact about Z/10 that picks S3a over S3b or vice versa.

Three specific foundation-register questions would need answers before the hinge could be resolved:

**Question 1:** Does "attractor privilege at a boundary" have a declared status in the framework's vocabulary, or is the phrase a coinage of the v3.0 pre-reg that lacks foundational grounding?

**Question 2:** When a subtype is minority AND attractor-carrying AND at a boundary, does the framework have a prioritization rule among these three properties? (Source 1 uses majority; Source 2 uses override-structure; Source 4 uses exact-cardinality. Source 3 would need its own prioritization, and the framework has not supplied one.)

**Question 3:** Is the framework's treatment of h as attractor (R2) vs default-output (R1) meant to extend uniformly into boundary regions, or does the boundary transition trigger a role-shift for h?

None of these questions has a current answer. Answering them would require foundation-register work separate from this note, on `ATTRACTOR_RECONCILIATION.md` and possibly `GAP_AS_BOUNDARY_NOTHINGNESS.md`.

## 5. Two Honest Paths Forward

### Path A — Resolve the hinge

Extend the framework's vocabulary to answer Questions 1–3 above. This would likely require a foundation note or series of notes arguing for a specific reading on independent grounds (not just "it makes v3.0 pass"). If such grounds exist and the argument is disciplined, a v3.0.1 sprint could re-run V0 with the resolved Source 3 direction frozen.

This path is non-trivial. It requires structural commitments the framework has not made. The commitments should come from independent reasoning about attractor behavior, not from looking at v3.0's outcome and picking the direction that produces a PASS.

### Path B — Certify the hinge as fundamentally ambiguous

Accept that the framework's vocabulary, as currently specified, does not determine the direction of attractor privilege at boundary regions. The v3.0 UNCLEAR-by-Sensitivity result stands as the earned finding. No v3.0.1 is run under the current vocabulary. A different v3.x checkpoint (BHML, unit cyclic structure) can still be pursued on Z/10, but the V0 lane is closed pending a different framework specification.

This path is also non-trivial. It requires acknowledging that the framework is less complete than it appeared. But it has the virtue of being honest: the hinge is a real gap, and the sprint discipline produced the evidence that the gap exists.

## 6. What This Note Earns And Does Not Earn

### Earns

- Two clean readings stated with their own names (S3a, S3b), not as casual variants.
- Each reading's connection to the framework's own vocabulary made explicit.
- Three specific open questions identified whose answers would unblock resolution.
- Two honest paths forward articulated: resolve with discipline, or certify ambiguity.

### Does not earn

- A verdict on which reading is correct.
- A resolution of the hinge.
- A claim that the hinge is unimportant, or that UNCLEAR-by-Sensitivity is equivalent to a PASS in disguise.
- A modification of any prior verdict.
- Authorization for any sprint.
- Extension of the hinge discussion to other frameworks, rings, or domains.

## 7. Relationship to SAH

The Shape-Admissibility Hypothesis (see `shape_admissibility_foundation_2026_04_18/`) proposes that THML/BSML joint flow selects admissible morphologies. The attractor-privilege hinge is orthogonal: it concerns how the framework reads a specific structural feature (boundary exceptions involving the attractor), not how the framework selects morphologies.

A resolution of the attractor-privilege hinge would not directly support or refute SAH. A persistent SAH inquiry would not resolve the hinge. The two foundation-register questions are independent.

## 8. One-Sentence Framing

The framework's vocabulary supports two internally-coherent readings of attractor privilege at boundary regions (S3a: hold-at-boundary → excluded; S3b: structure-gathers → persistent); current results support each reading without equally weighting them; three specific foundation-register questions would need answers to resolve the hinge; until those answers are produced, either disciplined resolution or certified ambiguity is the honest path forward.
