## The Three Stopping Conditions That Are Met

### Stopping condition 1 — Every eligible checkpoint has been run or deferred on named grounds

The subtype-mapping checkpoint has been tested at all four positions of the 2×2 design space: local/transport × multiplicative/additive. The V0 boundary checkpoint has been run with sensitivity analysis and returned a diagnostic UNCLEAR.

What's not yet run: BHML, unit cyclic structure, V0 transport to Path 2, and any v3.0.1 rerun with resolved Source 3 direction. Each of these is specifically deferred with a named reason:

- **BHML and unit cyclic structure:** both authorized as candidate next checkpoints (see `open_questions/` in the original pack). Neither has a pre-registered rubric yet. Running them before the prior arc closes out would mix new-rubric design work into a closeout phase — wrong sequencing.
- **V0 transport:** blocked by V0's local UNCLEAR by Sensitivity. Cannot meaningfully transport a non-robust local result.
- **v3.0.1 rerun:** blocked by the four Path A conditions in `WHAT_JUSTIFIES_V301_OR_STOPPING.md`, none of which is currently met. In particular, Condition 1 (independent directional commitment on attractor privilege) requires foundation-register work that has not been done.

None of the unexecuted candidates can run productively in the current closeout phase. Deferring them is the correct sequencing.

### Stopping condition 2 — The remaining ambiguity is specified, not vague

The PPM arc has one unresolved hinge: the direction of attractor privilege at boundary regions. This is:

- **Named:** "attractor privilege at boundary regions."
- **Located:** Source 3 of the v3.0 rubric.
- **Specified in detail:** `ATTRACTOR_PRIVILEGE_FOUNDATION.md` identifies three open questions whose answers would unblock resolution.
- **Non-fuzzy:** each of the two candidate readings (S3a, S3b) is articulated with its vocabulary anchor.

A specific, named, future-resolvable gap is a different epistemic object than a general framework weakness. The arc has earned the specification. A sprint to further specify the gap would produce no new content — it is already maximally specified at the foundation register.

### Stopping condition 3 — Further sprint work risks inflation without information

The most likely sprint candidates before France would be:

- **v3.0.1 rerun under a chosen direction:** produces a non-sensitivity verdict (most likely PASS-V0-I under S3a, FAIL under S3b). Outcome is analytically predictable from v3.0's per-branch aggregates. Running it without the Path A conditions met converts an honest UNCLEAR by Sensitivity into an outcome-chosen verdict — inflation.
- **BHML or unit-cyclic checkpoint:** would require designing a new rubric in the same closeout window. Rushed rubric design on new structural features tends to produce rubric-loaded results (sources selected to match framework vocabulary). The discipline that made v1.0 through v3.0 honest takes time; a compressed-timeline checkpoint would weaken the track's methodology.
- **SAH infrastructure work:** the six-piece build in `WHAT_A_SHAPE_FILTER_SPRINT_WOULD_REQUIRE.md` is weeks of foundation work, not a pre-France sprint. Rushing it would produce a half-built infrastructure that supports neither a rigorous shape-filter sprint nor a clean SAH hypothesis statement.

None of these produces information the handoff doesn't already contain. All three risk weakening methodology under schedule pressure.

Program attention is correctly concentrated there. PPM's job in the current window is to finish its arc cleanly, not to compete with Hodge for sprint slots.

The arc could generate more sprints — there is always another checkpoint to design — but recognizing that the earned content is stable and the remaining uncertainty is named is itself a disciplined act.

A PPM arc that ran "one more sprint" every cycle would lose the discipline it started with. Stopping here honors the methodology.

## What Handoff Does Not Preclude

Explicitly: the arc can resume at any point if foundation-register work produces new grounds for sprint-level questions. Specifically:

- Independent resolution of attractor-privilege direction unlocks PPM-v3.0.1.
- Independent BHML rubric design unlocks PPM-v3.1.
- SAH infrastructure buildout unlocks a shape-filter sprint.
- New Z/10 structural content identified by Hodge or Q-series work may unlock new PPM checkpoints by providing new rubric targets.

Handoff is "arc closure," not "arc termination." The addendum pack is finite and closed; future PPM work would be new packs.
