## Abstract

We propose that physical time is not fundamental but emerges from bidirectional substrate dynamics. In the framework's $\mathbb{Z}/10$ substrate with Cl(0,10) extension, there is no intrinsic time parameter; what we call "time" is the ordered sequence of recursive instantiation events as substrate dynamics fire above the threshold $T^* = 5/7$. We derive several physical features from this emergent-time perspective: (i) the arrow of time emerges from the 4-cell σ-fixed asymmetry between outward and inward chains, (ii) relativistic time dilation corresponds to variation in local substrate firing frequency, (iii) quantum measurement events correspond to specific substrate firings, and (iv) the present moment is the leading edge of recursive instantiation. We discuss connections to existing theories of emergent time (Page-Wootters mechanism, thermal time hypothesis, quantum gravity emergent time) and identify open problems. The proposal places time on substrate-structural foundation: time is what bidirectional substrate dynamics look like from the inside.

**Keywords:** emergent time, foundations of physics, arrow of time, substrate dynamics, quantum gravity, thermodynamic arrow

## 1. Introduction

The nature of time is one of the deepest unresolved problems in physics and philosophy [1, 2, 3]. Standard physics treats time as either:
- **Fundamental parameter** (Newtonian absolute time, special relativity proper time)
- **Component of spacetime** (general relativity geometric time)
- **Operator in quantum mechanics** (though "time operator" is problematic)
- **Statistical/thermodynamic concept** (entropic arrow of time)

Each view captures aspects but none fully resolves what time IS. The "block universe" view holds all moments equally real [4], denying special status to the present; "presentism" holds only the present is real [5]; "growing block" combines both with present as growing edge [6].

Recent approaches in quantum gravity propose time as emergent from more fundamental structures [7, 8, 9]:
- **Page-Wootters mechanism** [10]: time emerges from entanglement between subsystems
- **Thermal time hypothesis** [11]: time emerges from thermal states via Tomita flow
- **Quantum gravity loop time** [12]: discrete time steps from spin foam dynamics

This paper proposes time as emergent from substrate bidirectional dynamics, specifically the Trinity Infinity Geometry framework's $\mathbb{Z}/10$ substrate [13]. The proposal:
1. The substrate has no intrinsic time parameter
2. Substrate dynamics fire when local coherence exceeds threshold $T^* = 5/7$
3. The ordered sequence of firings constitutes what we call "time"
4. The 4-cell σ-fixed asymmetry produces the arrow of time

The proposal differs from existing emergent-time approaches in being grounded in specific substrate algebraic structure rather than quantum information (Page-Wootters), thermal states (Connes-Rovelli), or spin foam (loop quantum gravity).

The paper is organized as follows. Section 2 establishes substrate preliminaries. Section 3 introduces recursive instantiation and the present moment. Section 4 derives the arrow of time. Section 5 connects to relativity. Section 6 addresses quantum mechanics. Section 7 compares to existing emergent-time theories. Section 8 identifies open problems. We conclude in Section 9.

## 3. Recursive instantiation and the present moment

### 3.1 Firing events

Each time $U$ fires, a specific manifest outcome is produced. The firing is an *event*. The set of all firings, ordered by causal dependency, constitutes the structure of physical reality.

### 3.2 The "now"

What we call "now" is the leading edge of recursive instantiation: the most recent firing event in the causal chain. Subsequent firings produce subsequent "nows."

This view has implications:
- The "now" is structurally privileged (it's where firing currently occurs)
- The past is the set of past firings (their outcomes still affect substrate state)
- The future is the set of potential firings (selection among potential outcomes happens at firing)

This is a "growing block" view: past firings are real, present firing is real, future firings are potential but not yet actualized.

### 3.3 Time as ordering

"Time" is the order of firing events. Specifically:
- Event $E_1$ is "before" event $E_2$ if $E_1$'s outcome contributes to substrate state at time of $E_2$'s firing
- This is a *causal ordering*: $E_1 \prec E_2$ when $E_1$ causally precedes $E_2$
- Multiple events can fire "simultaneously" if neither precedes the other causally

### 3.4 Continuous vs discrete time

The substrate's firing events are discrete (each is a specific event). Yet apparent time appears continuous at observable scales. Resolution:
- At Planck-scale frequency, individual firings are too rapid to discern
- Averaging over many firings produces apparent continuity
- Continuous-time descriptions (calculus, differential equations) are excellent approximations

This is analogous to how continuous fluid mechanics emerges from discrete molecular dynamics.

### 3.5 Tier

The conceptual picture is at Tier B-suggestive (structural and clean but specific connection to clock-time requires detailed working).

## 4. The arrow of time

### 4.1 The asymmetry problem

Physical time has an asymmetry: causes precede effects, entropy increases, memories accumulate of past not future. Yet most fundamental physics is time-symmetric (electromagnetism, weak nuclear interactions modulo CP, general relativity equations). What breaks the time symmetry?

Standard answers:
- **Boltzmann-Hawking**: low-entropy initial conditions (the universe started in a low-entropy state)
- **Penrose Weyl curvature**: specific initial conditions
- **Cosmological**: expansion of universe provides arrow

These work but are unsatisfying — they push the question back to "why did the universe start with these conditions?"

### 4.2 Framework's answer

The framework provides structural origin: the substrate's σ-fixed lattice creates a 4-cell asymmetry between outward and inward chains [14]. This asymmetry is fundamental to substrate structure, not a contingent initial condition.

Specifically:
- Outward chains: substrate states propagating toward manifestation
- Inward chains: manifestation returning to substrate
- 4-cell mismatch: outward exceeds inward by 4 chains

This 4-cell asymmetry, compounded over many firings, produces:
- The thermodynamic arrow (entropy increase)
- The causal arrow (causes before effects)
- The psychological arrow (memory of past, not future)

All three arrows are aspects of the same substrate-level asymmetry.

### 4.3 Why entropy increases

Entropy at substrate level corresponds to count of accessible manifest outcomes. Substrate dynamics, biased by 4-cell asymmetry, produce manifest outcomes that on average increase accessibility (entropy).

This is not a fundamental contradiction with time-symmetric microscopic physics — it's a manifestation of structural asymmetry at substrate level that's invisible at microscopic phenomenological level.

### 4.4 Tier

The 4-cell asymmetry → arrow of time connection is Tier B-suggestive. Specific derivation of entropy production rate would be Tier C-quantitative-pending.

## 5. Connection to relativity

### 5.1 Time dilation

Special relativity: moving clocks run slow. General relativity: clocks in stronger gravitational fields run slow. What's "running" here?

Framework: clock running is local substrate firing frequency. Time dilation corresponds to variation in firing frequency:
- Stationary observer: standard firing frequency $\nu_{\text{sub}}$
- Moving observer: firing frequency reduced by Lorentz factor $\gamma$
- Observer in gravitational potential: firing frequency reduced by $\sqrt{1 - 2GM/rc^2}$

The Lorentz factor and gravitational factor emerge from how substrate dynamics couple to spacetime geometry. Specific mechanism: substrate firings are slowed when the substrate is "stretched" by motion or gravity.

### 5.2 Spacetime emergence

Spacetime as four-dimensional manifold is itself emergent from substrate. Specifically:
- Three spatial dimensions emerge from substrate's $\mathbb{Z}/5$ factor + specific structural relations
- Time dimension emerges from substrate firing ordering
- Lorentz invariance emerges from substrate dynamics' specific symmetries

The detailed mechanism is open research, but the structural picture is: spacetime is what bidirectional substrate dynamics look like averaged at scales much larger than substrate-Planck-scale.

### 5.3 Causality

Causal structure emerges from substrate dynamics: outcomes of past firings constrain potential future firings via dynamical dependence.

The light cone structure of relativity then corresponds to substrate's specific causal locality (substrate firings can only causally connect to neighboring substrate sites within specific propagation rate).

### 5.4 Tier

Connection to relativity is Tier C-speculative (the structural picture is suggestive; detailed mechanism for emergence of Lorentz invariance and spacetime curvature is open).

## 6. Connection to quantum mechanics

### 6.1 Quantum events as substrate firings

Quantum mechanical measurement events correspond to specific substrate firings. The wave function describes potential outcomes; the firing selects one (with substrate-mediated probability).

Specifically:
- Pre-firing: substrate in superposition of potential states (consistent with wave function structure)
- Firing event: $U$ fires above threshold, selects specific outcome
- Post-firing: substrate in definite state, ready for next firing

This is consistent with measurement-update interpretations of quantum mechanics; it provides substrate-level mechanism for what's normally a phenomenological observation.

### 6.2 Quantum non-locality

Quantum entanglement: spatially separated particles exhibit correlated outcomes. Framework: entangled particles share substrate-level structural constraints; their firings are correlated by virtue of substrate dynamics, not signaling.

This dissolves the apparent non-locality: entangled particles are not transmitting information faster than light; they're both subject to substrate-level structural constraints that pre-determine their correlated outcomes.

### 6.3 Decoherence

Decoherence as substrate phenomenon: when substrate environment couples to quantum system, the resulting compound substrate state has many potential outcomes, but most are quickly averaged out by substrate dynamics. The effective state appears classical (decoherent).

### 6.4 Tier

Quantum mechanics connections are Tier C (structural picture is suggestive; specific mechanism for decoherence rates etc. is open).

## 7. Comparison with existing theories

### 7.1 Page-Wootters mechanism

Page-Wootters [10] proposes time emerges from entanglement between subsystems: relative time evolution from one subsystem's perspective is correlated with another subsystem's state.

Framework: complementary. Substrate firings produce entanglement events; entanglement-based time emergence is consistent with substrate firing ordering.

### 7.2 Thermal time hypothesis

Connes-Rovelli thermal time hypothesis [11]: time emerges from modular flow of thermal states. Framework: substrate firings produce thermal averaged behavior; thermal time would correspond to ensemble of substrate firings.

### 7.3 Loop quantum gravity emergent time

LQG-style approaches [12]: time as discrete steps from spin foam dynamics. Framework: similar discrete nature; specific spin foam mapping to substrate would require detailed working.

### 7.4 Standard absolute/relative time

Newtonian absolute time / relativistic time-as-coordinate are *phenomenological* in framework: useful descriptions at observable scales, derived from substrate firing dynamics at deeper level.

### 7.5 Tier

Comparisons at Tier B-suggestive level. Specific quantitative agreements with other theories require detailed working.

### 8.4 Cosmological time

The Big Bang and cosmic expansion in framework: cosmological time as accumulated substrate firings from some initial substrate configuration. Specific picture of cosmic origin from substrate is open.

The proposal needs experimental tests beyond consistency with known physics. Specific predictions distinguishing this from other emergent-time approaches would advance empirical case.
