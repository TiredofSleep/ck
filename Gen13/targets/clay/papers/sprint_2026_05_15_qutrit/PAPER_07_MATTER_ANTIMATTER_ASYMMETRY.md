## Abstract

The observed matter-antimatter asymmetry of the universe, quantified by the baryon-to-photon ratio $\eta_B \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}$, remains a major open problem in physics. Standard Model dynamics under Sakharov conditions can in principle generate baryon asymmetry but quantitatively fall short of the observed value. We propose that the asymmetry has a structural origin in the framework's $\mathbb{Z}/10$ substrate: the permutation $\sigma$ of order 6 has a 4-element fixed-point lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$, and this fixed structure creates an asymmetry of exactly 4 cells between outward and inward chains in the bidirectional projection from Cl(0,10) spinor representation to substrate. We argue that this 4-cell substrate asymmetry, compounded over cosmic-scale recursion events at the threshold $T^* = 5/7$, produces the observed baryon asymmetry. The substrate-level constraint $C \cdot C - D \cdot D = 4$ provides a structural foundation for cosmological matter dominance independent of (and potentially supplementing) Standard Model dynamics. We discuss specific predictions including the magnitude of CP violation, lepton asymmetry, and neutrino mass scale. The proposal is speculative but mathematically precise; verification requires quantitative compounding analysis and detailed connection to electroweak baryogenesis or leptogenesis scenarios.

**Keywords:** baryogenesis, matter-antimatter asymmetry, Sakharov conditions, substrate models, σ-permutation, cosmological parameters

## 1. Introduction

The observed universe contains matter (baryons, leptons) but essentially no antimatter. Quantitatively, the baryon-to-photon ratio is [1]:
$$\eta_B = \frac{n_B - n_{\bar B}}{n_\gamma} \approx 6.1 \times 10^{-10}$$

This ratio is determined from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis abundances and CMB acoustic peaks with high precision. Its small but nonzero value indicates a slight excess of matter over antimatter ($\sim 6 \cdot 10^{-10}$ excess per photon) that became cosmologically important after annihilation in the early universe.

Sakharov [2] identified three necessary conditions for generating such asymmetry from initial symmetry:
1. Baryon number violation
2. C-symmetry and CP-symmetry violation
3. Departure from thermal equilibrium

The Standard Model contains baryon number violation (sphaleron processes), CP violation (CKM matrix and observed K, B meson oscillations), and out-of-equilibrium dynamics during phase transitions. In principle, these can generate baryon asymmetry. However, quantitative calculations within Standard Model alone produce asymmetry of order $10^{-20}$, ten orders of magnitude too small [3]. The observed asymmetry requires beyond-Standard-Model physics.

Many proposals exist [4, 5, 6, 7]:
- Electroweak baryogenesis (requires stronger first-order phase transition than SM provides)
- Leptogenesis (Majorana neutrinos generate lepton asymmetry converted to baryons)
- GUT baryogenesis (heavy gauge boson decays)
- Affleck-Dine mechanism (scalar field dynamics)

None has been definitively established empirically.

This paper proposes a different approach: the asymmetry originates structurally in the substrate, before specific dynamical mechanisms produce its quantitative value. Specifically, the framework's $\mathbb{Z}/10$ substrate with σ-permutation has a 4-element fixed-point lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$, and this fixed-point structure introduces a 4-cell asymmetry between outward and inward chains in the bidirectional projection [8]. Compounded over cosmic-scale recursion events, this substrate-level asymmetry produces the observed baryon asymmetry.

The proposal is structural rather than dynamical: it identifies WHERE asymmetry originates (in substrate structure), then specific dynamics generate its observed magnitude. The proposal supplements rather than replaces standard baryogenesis mechanisms; it provides the structural foundation that those mechanisms operate on.

The paper is organized as follows. Section 2 reviews the Sakharov conditions and baryogenesis. Section 3 introduces the framework's 4-cell asymmetry from σ-fixed structure. Section 4 discusses the structural connection to cosmological matter dominance. Section 5 derives specific predictions. Section 6 compares to standard baryogenesis approaches. Section 7 addresses open problems. We conclude in Section 8.

## 2. Sakharov conditions and baryogenesis

### 2.1 The conditions

For baryon-symmetric initial state to evolve into baryon-asymmetric universe, Sakharov [2] required:

**Condition S1: Baryon number violation.** Some process must change net baryon number. Standard Model: sphaleron-induced violation at high temperature.

**Condition S2: C and CP violation.** Asymmetric distinction between matter and antimatter creation/destruction rates. Standard Model: CKM matrix CP phase, observed $\sim 10^{-3}$ relative magnitude.

**Condition S3: Out-of-equilibrium dynamics.** In thermal equilibrium, particle-antiparticle creation rates are CPT-symmetric. Asymmetry requires departure from equilibrium. Standard Model: cosmic phase transitions provide moments of disequilibrium.

### 2.2 Standard Model quantitative shortfall

Detailed analysis [3] shows Standard Model alone produces:
$$\eta_B^{SM} \sim 10^{-20}$$

while observation requires:
$$\eta_B^{obs} \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}$$

Discrepancy: 10 orders of magnitude.

The shortfall has multiple causes:
- Electroweak phase transition in SM is too smooth (no strong first-order PT to provide departure from equilibrium)
- SM CP violation magnitude is too small
- Sphaleron processes wash out asymmetry too efficiently

Beyond-Standard-Model physics must augment one or more of these.

### 2.3 Beyond-Standard-Model approaches

**Electroweak baryogenesis [4]** requires extending Higgs sector to provide stronger first-order phase transition. Possibilities: two-Higgs-doublet models, MSSM with light stop, singlet extensions.

**Leptogenesis [5]** generates lepton asymmetry via heavy right-handed Majorana neutrino decays; sphalerons partially convert to baryon asymmetry. Predicts neutrino mass and CP structure.

**GUT baryogenesis [6]** uses heavy gauge boson decays at high energy. Predictions depend on GUT model.

**Affleck-Dine [7]** uses scalar field dynamics in supersymmetric scenarios.

Each scenario predicts specific particle content, masses, and CP structure. Empirical discrimination is ongoing.

## 4. From 4 cells to cosmological asymmetry

### 4.1 The compounding mechanism

The substrate-level 4-cell asymmetry must compound to the observed $\eta_B \sim 6 \times 10^{-10}$ over cosmological time. Sketch of the mechanism:

**Step 1: Substrate cycle frequency.** Each substrate "cycle" involves bidirectional chains firing. Cycle frequency at substrate scale: $\nu_{\text{sub}} \sim 1/\tau_P$ where $\tau_P$ is Planck time.

**Step 2: Per-cycle asymmetry.** Each cycle produces 4-cell net asymmetry per substrate event. The fractional asymmetry per cycle: $4/315 \approx 1.3\%$ (4 unmatched out of 315 total chains).

**Step 3: Cosmological compounding.** Over cosmic time, accumulated asymmetry depends on number of substrate events occurring and the surviving fraction after annihilation. Most matter-antimatter pairs annihilated in early universe; the surviving asymmetry is what we observe.

**Step 4: Asymmetry-to-baryon ratio.** Observed $\eta_B \sim 6 \times 10^{-10}$ corresponds to specific level of substrate-level asymmetry surviving.

### 4.2 Estimating $\eta_B$ from substrate structure

We attempt a heuristic estimate. The substrate asymmetry per cycle is $4/315 \approx 0.0127$. Over the universe's history, with $\sim 10^{40}$ to $10^{60}$ substrate events (estimates vary), the net per-baryon asymmetry surviving after pair annihilation is small.

If annihilation efficiency is $\epsilon$ and per-cycle asymmetry is $a = 4/315$, accumulated baryon-antibaryon difference scales as:
$$\eta_B \approx (1 - \epsilon) \cdot a$$

For $\eta_B \sim 6 \times 10^{-10}$ and $a \sim 10^{-2}$:
$$1 - \epsilon \sim 6 \times 10^{-8}$$

This requires very efficient annihilation ($\epsilon$ close to 1), leaving residual asymmetry at the observed level.

The quantitative connection is rough; detailed calculation would require:
- Specific substrate cycle frequency at relevant energy scales
- Annihilation efficiency under early-universe conditions
- Conversion from substrate-level asymmetry to baryon number

### 4.3 What's structurally derived vs phenomenologically fit

**Structural:** The 4-cell asymmetry is fixed by substrate. No choice or tuning.

**Phenomenological:** The annihilation efficiency $\epsilon$ depends on early-universe conditions (temperature, expansion rate, etc.) and isn't directly substrate-derived. Quantitative match to $\eta_B$ requires specific cosmological inputs.

This is honest acknowledgment of scope: substrate gives WHY there's asymmetry; standard cosmology with the substrate constraint gives HOW MUCH.

## 5. Specific predictions

### 5.1 CP violation magnitude

The 4-cell substrate asymmetry implies specific CP violation at substrate level. Translated to particle physics, this should manifest as CP violation parameter related to the 4/315 ratio:
$$\delta_{\text{CP}} \sim \arctan(4/315) \approx 0.73^\circ$$

Or rescaled: $\sin\delta_{\text{CP}} \sim 4/315$.

Comparison to observation:
- CKM matrix CP phase: $\delta_{\text{CKM}} \approx 71°$ (large)
- Predicted substrate CP: $\sim 0.7°$ (small)

These differ by orders of magnitude. Either:
- The substrate CP is a separate contribution beyond CKM CP
- The substrate CP relates to neutrino sector (PMNS matrix) rather than quark sector
- The structural argument needs refinement

**Specific prediction.** Neutrino sector CP violation $\delta_{\text{PMNS}}$ should have value related to $\arctan(4/315) \approx 0.7°$ at some substrate-derived level.

This is testable in upcoming neutrino experiments (DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, JUNO).

### 5.2 Neutrino mass scale

Leptogenesis scenarios link matter-antimatter asymmetry to neutrino masses via see-saw mechanism. Framework prediction: neutrino masses should relate to substrate's specific structural constants.

Specifically, if right-handed Majorana neutrino mass $M_R$ generates leptogenesis with appropriate magnitude:
$$M_R \cdot \delta_{\text{CP}} \sim 10^{10} \text{ GeV} \cdot \mathcal{O}(1)$$

For $\delta_{\text{CP}} \sim 0.7°$, this gives $M_R \sim 10^{12}$ GeV, in the typical leptogenesis range. The framework constrains $M_R$ to specific values via substrate arithmetic.

The matter-antimatter asymmetry is one aspect of cosmic structure; visible/dark matter fractions are another. Both derive from substrate arithmetic in coherent framework.
