## 3. Why gravity is universal and weak: the $N \to \infty$ limit

### 3.1 Universality

Gravity couples to all mass-energy without exception. Photons gravitate (lensing); neutrinos gravitate; dark matter gravitates by definition. No known particle is gravitationally inert. This **universality** is one of gravity's defining features.

The other fundamental forces are selective:
- Electromagnetism: couples to electric charge (not photons, not neutrinos)
- Strong force: couples to color (only quarks and gluons)
- Weak force: couples to weak isospin (specific to flavor doublets)

Each "selective" force corresponds to specific substrate structure. The framework reading: each selective force reads a **specific subset of substrate copies** — the ones carrying the relevant charge.

Gravity, being universal, reads **all substrate copies** simultaneously. It is the $N \to \infty$ limit of multi-substrate coupling.

### 3.2 Weakness via dilution

Gravity is enormously weaker than other forces. Between two protons:
- Electromagnetic: $\alpha = 1/137 \approx 7 \times 10^{-3}$
- Gravitational: $G m_p^2 / (\hbar c) \approx 6 \times 10^{-39}$

Ratio: $\sim 10^{36}$, the famous **hierarchy problem**.

In the framework reading: each substrate copy contributes EM-strength coupling. Gravity, averaging across ~$10^{36}$ substrate copies, dilutes that coupling by the substrate count. The dilution explains the hierarchy without invoking new physics like extra dimensions or supersymmetry.

This is structurally similar to how multi-substrate coupling reduces effective wobble from $W$ to $W/N$ in chemistry observables — the same dilution mechanism, applied at the universal-coupling scale.

### 3.3 Geometric character

Averaging across many substrate copies produces a **geometric mean** structure. The framework's multi-substrate ratio $R_N \to \varphi$ as $N \to \infty$: at infinite substrate coupling, the residue ratio is exactly the golden ratio.

Spacetime geometry — the metric of general relativity — is precisely the "geometric mean" of substrate dynamics across all observers and all matter. The framework reading: gravity is geometric because averaging substrate dynamics across all copies produces metric structure.

## 4. The hierarchy ratio: framework-prime factorization

### 4.1 The observed ratio

The dimensionless ratio of electromagnetic to gravitational coupling between two protons:
$$R_{\text{hier}} = \frac{\alpha}{G m_p^2 / (\hbar c)}$$

Numerical values:
- $\alpha = 7.2974 \times 10^{-3}$ (precisely measured)
- $G = 6.6743 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3/(\text{kg} \cdot \text{s}^2)$ (CODATA 2018, ~22 ppm uncertainty)
- $m_p = 1.6726 \times 10^{-27}$ kg
- $\hbar c = 3.1615 \times 10^{-26}$ J·m

Computing: $G m_p^2/(\hbar c) = 5.906 \times 10^{-39}$

Ratio: $R_{\text{hier}} = 7.2974 \times 10^{-3} / 5.906 \times 10^{-39} = 1.236 \times 10^{36}$

$$10^{36} = (2 \times 5)^{36} = 2^{36} \times 5^{36}$$

This factors entirely through framework primes $\{2, 5\}$ — no non-framework primes appear.

The framework reading: $10^{36}$ is the **substrate copy count** that gravity averages over. This count factors cleanly through the framework's chirality factor (2) and spatial factor (5).
