# What a PASS Would Mean, and What It Would Not Mean
## Scope Discipline Before the Checkpoint Runs

---

## Why This Note Exists Before the Sprint

A PASS on the subtype-mapping checkpoint will be tempting to read broadly. The pair-primitive framework is new to the program's register, it has structural appeal, and a first PASS will arrive with momentum behind it. The foundation note explicitly held the scale examples in a compatibility schema without making any physical claim, and the central question was deliberately narrowed to the ADD/MAX mapping on Z/10's seam. But the gap between "the mapping fits" and "the framework is vindicated" is exactly the gap the program's discipline has been designed to keep clean.

This note fixes the width of the PASS-sentence before the sprint runs. Whatever comes back, the PASS reading stays inside the box this note draws.

---

## What a PASS Would Mean

A PASS means exactly one thing:

> **One of Map A or Map B produces a coherent structural reading across the 8 seam cells and the three inherited Path 3 findings, with a cleanness gap over its alternative that exceeds a pre-registered threshold.**

Unpacking the three load-bearing qualifications:

**"Coherent structural reading."** The winning map must place every seam cell in exactly one of the pair's two readings (persistent side or excluded side) without contradiction. Every cell gets a side. No cell plays both sides. Every Path 3 finding the map implicates must point in the direction the map predicts, not against it.

**"Cleanness gap over its alternative."** If Map A and Map B both produce readings that fit the data at comparable quality, the mapping is not forced by the data. In that case, the framework has not cashed out on the existing measurements — it has just been confirmed as one consistent interpretation alongside its mirror image. A PASS requires one map to fit strictly better than the other by a pre-registered margin, with that margin defined on the cellular fit structure.

**"Exceeds a pre-registered threshold."** Following the program's discipline, the sprint's pre-reg specifies the cleanness-gap threshold before execution. Post-hoc adjustment of the threshold is forbidden. Whatever threshold the pre-reg fixes, the PASS sentence inherits.

The PASS sentence is therefore narrow and conditional. It names a specific mapping, a specific ring, a specific 8-cell seam, a specific set of three inherited findings, and a specific cleanness margin. It does not extend beyond those.

---

## What a PASS Would NOT Mean

The PASS sentence licenses nothing beyond its exact content. In particular, a PASS would not mean:

### It would not mean the pair primitive is correct

A PASS is one point of contact between the framework and measurable data. The program has seen four substantive PASSes in the B2 pack. Each one earned one narrow sentence. None of them individually established the framework they operated under. This checkpoint's PASS, like those, is a single confirmation of consistency at a single test point. Framework correctness would require multiple independent points of contact, tested under different operational lenses, across different objects.

### It would not extend to Z/n for n ≠ 10

The test operates on Z/10's seam. The pair-primitive framework might or might not have the same mapping on other compatibility-family rings. The P3-Subtype-v1.1 and v1.2-adj results carried from 8 Path 2 carriers, but each carrier had exactly one ADD edge and the transport was a specific role attribute. The mapping question is deeper than transport — it concerns which side of the pair the ADD subtype plays. Whether that mapping is stable across the family is a separate question that requires a separate sprint.

### It would not license scale examples

The foundation note's five-feature schema for scale realizations (bounded flow, stabilized identity, seam/threshold, gap-defined curvature, recursive nested holds) was explicitly a compatibility check, not a prediction. A PASS on the subtype mapping does not carry over to the schema. Neutrons, cells, planets, black holes — none of them are authorized by this PASS. Any move toward scale realization requires a separate sprint, a separate pre-reg, and the same discipline.

### It would not upgrade the identity-edge or leaf-edge findings

P3-Subtype-v1.1 earned a specific sentence at +6.06σ: under the P3AP overlay-extension, recovered ADD-subtype edges attach the ring's multiplicative identity element. P3-Subtype-v1.2-adj earned a specific sentence at +3.73σ: under the same extension, ADD edges are consistently leaf edges. Those sentences are the entirety of those sprints' content. A PASS here does not add "and this is because the ADD subtype plays the persistent-side reading of the pair primitive." It does not upgrade. It adds its own separate sentence: the ADD/MAX split on Z/10's 8 seam cells admits a coherent reading under one of the two candidate mappings.

### It would not prove that hold, gap, and flow are the correct primitives

The foundation note's three-document chain reasoned toward the pair as primitive under a specific methodology. The chain's reasoning is not proven by this sprint. A PASS confirms one structural consequence of the framework. The methodology that led to the framework — the rejection of hold-first, gap-first, and flow-first readings — remains a methodological commitment, not a theorem. This sprint does not upgrade methodology to theorem.

### It would not close the count-transport lane

The count-transport lane is closed under the P3AP generator, for reasons documented in the B2 pack's `COUNT_TRANSPORT_CLOSED_UNDER_P3AP.md`. A PASS here does not reopen it. A PASS might add structural reading to the existing closure (explaining why count is not the right question for this framework), but it does not change the closure's status.

### It would not settle the hub-extension question

Hub-extension remains deferred. A PASS here does not authorize hub-extension sprints. The inherited Path 3 findings all operate on P3AP's chain topology. The mapping's fit on chain topology is what the sprint tests. Whether the same mapping holds on a hub-extension would be a separate sprint with a separate extension, a separate validation, and a separate pre-reg.

## What a FAIL Would Mean

Symmetrically, a FAIL means:

> **Neither Map A nor Map B produces a coherent structural reading across the 8 seam cells and three inherited Path 3 findings at the pre-registered cleanness threshold.**

A FAIL tells the framework: it cannot cleanly organize Z/10's seam under the two exhaustive mappings its vocabulary allows. Either the vocabulary is missing readings that would fit the data, or the framework is not the right organization of the program's pieces.

A FAIL does not retract the foundation note's suggestive claims about mutual constitution or the hold-generator analysis. Those were carefully tagged. What a FAIL retracts is the specific structural claim that TSML's ADD/MAX split is the pair's two readings on a finite ring. The framework's broader structure would need to be revisited before any further sprint built on it.

---

## What an UNCLEAR Would Mean

An UNCLEAR arises when both maps fit comparably, or when neither fits at threshold but both fit above some lower floor, or when the four data sources point in inconsistent directions within a single map.

An UNCLEAR is the most diagnostically useful outcome because it pinpoints the specific ambiguity. The sprint's results document will name which data sources agreed with which map and where the disagreements came from. That attribution is how a later sprint — potentially with a refined mapping vocabulary — would know what to fix.

The framework after an UNCLEAR is not confirmed and not refuted. It is a candidate organization with a documented place where it is not decidable from the current data. Further checkpoints would be required before the framework could be built on.

## The Narrow Sentence Each Outcome Earns

| Outcome | Sentence the program adds to the record |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | "Under the pair-primitive framework and a pre-registered cleanness threshold, one specific mapping (A or B) produces a coherent structural reading of Z/10's 8 seam cells and the three inherited Path 3 findings, at a margin exceeding the threshold over the alternative mapping." |
| **FAIL** | "Under the pair-primitive framework's two candidate mappings on Z/10's 8 seam cells, neither mapping produces a coherent structural reading across the inherited findings at the pre-registered cleanness threshold." |
| **UNCLEAR** | "The pair-primitive framework's mapping on Z/10's 8 seam cells is not decidable from the existing data at the pre-registered cleanness threshold. Specific disagreements are documented." |

Each sentence is a narrow, attributable addition to the program's findings. None of them asserts framework correctness, framework refutation, or framework ambiguity beyond the specific test.

---

## The Discipline This Note Fixes

Whatever the sprint returns, the PASS reading cannot widen beyond the table above.

- No physics prose, no matter what comes back.
- No scale-example sprints authorized by this result.
- No upgrade of prior bridge findings.
- No claim about primes beyond the multiplicative context already established.
- No cross-framework synthesis ("the pair primitive and the 2×2 theorem together show…") without a new sprint that pre-registers that synthesis.

The first checkpoint is worth doing because it tests the framework at the right scope. The scope is fixed by this note. Nothing the sprint returns changes the scope.

---

## One-Sentence Statement

A PASS would confirm that the pair-primitive framework's first structural prediction survives contact with existing data at the checkpoint level; it would earn the framework one narrow sentence and no more; it would not upgrade any prior finding, authorize any broader sprint, or license any move toward physics or scale realization without separate, pre-registered work.
