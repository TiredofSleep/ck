# What It Means If the Map Is Stable, and What It Means If It Flips
## Scope Note for PPM-v1.1 Outcomes

---

## Why This Note Exists Before the Sprint

A PASS on the first framework checkpoint came with a scope note (`PASS_MEANING_SCOPE_NOTE.md`) that fixed what the PASS could and could not mean before the sprint ran. The same discipline applies here, with an added dimension: v1.1 has more interesting outcome space than v1.0 did, because the relationship to v1.0 is part of what each outcome earns. This note fixes the reading of each outcome before v1.1 executes, so whatever comes back, the program's response is bounded.

Three outcomes have distinct structural readings. Each gets a precisely-scoped sentence and an explicit list of things the outcome does not license.

---

## Outcome 1 — Stable (Map B under additive reading)

### What this would mean

Map B — MAX = persistent-side, ADD = excluded-side — produces a coherent structural fit on Z/10's seam under *both* the multiplicative operational interpretation of PPM-v1.0 and the additive operational interpretation of PPM-v1.1. The mapping is not operation-structure-dependent on this seam; it is a property of the framework's reading independent of which base operation structure is chosen.

The structural implication: the pair-primitive framework, applied to Z/10's seam, has a preferred subtype-to-pair-side assignment that holds across base operation structures. The ring's seam has a multiplicatively-framed character that survives the switch to additive reading — likely because Z/10's TSML is originally multiplicative and the seam inherits that framing even when we change the reading lens.

### The sentence this outcome earns

> Under both the multiplicative (PPM-v1.0) and additive (PPM-v1.1) operationalizations of the pair-primitive framework, Map B produces a coherent structural fit on Z/10's 8 seam cells, meeting the pre-registered cleanness threshold in both cases. The mapping is operationalization-stable on this seam under the two tested static-structure interpretations.

That sentence is the entirety of what Outcome 1 adds to the record.

### What Outcome 1 does NOT license

- **Does not establish operationalization-stability in general.** Two operationalizations tested; many more exist (flow-based readings, dual-operation readings, operation structures beyond additive and multiplicative). Stability under both static-structure readings does not imply stability under all possible readings.
- **Does not upgrade the framework to "operationalization-independent."** The checkpoints confirm the mapping under specific readings. "Operationalization-independent" would require testing under readings that genuinely differ from the two tested, and confirming stability across them.
- **Does not extend to other rings.** The test is on Z/10 only. Whether Map B is stable across operationalizations on Z/14, Z/22, or any other compatibility-family carrier is a separate question.
- **Does not weaken the v1.0 wording clarification.** The v1.0 clarification said failure would refute only the multiplicative operationalization, not the framework in all readings. A success on both tested operationalizations does not retroactively widen v1.0's PASS. Each sprint earns its own narrow sentence.
- **Does not authorize scale examples, physics, or ontology.** These remain unauthorized regardless.

## Outcome 2 — Flipped (Map A under additive reading)

### What this would mean

Map A — ADD = persistent-side, MAX = excluded-side — wins under the additive operationalization, while Map B won under the multiplicative. The mapping flips when the reading lens switches between the ring's two base operation structures.

The structural implication: the pair-primitive framework, applied to Z/10's seam, has an operation-structure-dependent reading. Whichever subtype aligns with the active operation structure (MAX aligns with multiplicative flow; ADD aligns with additive flow) plays the persistent-side role under that structure. The 2×2 theorem's symmetry is preserved at the framework's pair-assignment level: both operations support a coherent reading, with their native subtype as persistent.

This would be a structurally satisfying outcome because it suggests the pair primitive is not multiplicatively loaded — it reads symmetrically across the ring's base operations, with the subtype-to-pair assignment tracking the active operation's native rule.

### The sentence this outcome earns

> Under the multiplicative operationalization (PPM-v1.0) Map B (MAX = persistent, ADD = excluded) produces a coherent fit; under the additive operationalization (PPM-v1.1) Map A (ADD = persistent, MAX = excluded) produces a coherent fit; both at pre-registered cleanness thresholds. The pair-primitive framework's subtype-to-pair mapping is operationalization-dependent on Z/10's seam, with each base operation structure placing its native subtype in the persistent-side role.

That sentence is the entirety of what Outcome 2 adds to the record.

### What Outcome 2 does NOT license

- **Does not prove framework symmetry in general.** Symmetry is shown across the two base static-structure operationalizations on Z/10's seam. Whether the same symmetry holds under flow-based readings, or on other rings, is untested.
- **Does not merge v1.0 and v1.1 into a composite claim stronger than either sentence above.** The two PASSes are separate findings. A "symmetric pair-primitive framework" claim would require its own pre-registered checkpoint, not a composition of the two sprint verdicts.
- **Does not weaken v1.0's PASS.** Map B remains the correct reading under multiplicative operationalization. Map A becomes the correct reading under additive. Neither invalidates the other.
- **Does not extend to other rings.** Same scope constraint as Outcome 1.
- **Does not authorize scale examples, physics, or ontology.**

### Why Outcome 2 is structurally the most informative

Of the three outcomes, Outcome 2 is the most informative about the framework's nature because it produces two narrow results that together tell a specific story: the framework reads symmetrically across the two base operation structures, with a predictable flip. This is more informative than either Outcome 1 (which tells us only that one mapping dominates) or Outcome 3 (which tells us only that the additive reading is not diagnostic).

This is an observation about informativeness, not about preference. The sprint's verdict follows the rubric-scored result regardless of which outcome would be most interesting.

## Outcome 3 — Indecisive (FAIL or UNCLEAR under additive reading)

### What this would mean

Neither Map A nor Map B reaches the pre-registered cleanness threshold under the additive operationalization. Either:

- Neither map scores ≥ +3 (FAIL), or
- Both maps score comparably without a ≥2 cleanness gap (UNCLEAR).

The structural implication: the additive operationalization does not discriminate between the two candidate mappings on Z/10's seam. The seam's data — 8 cells, 3 inherited Path 3 findings — does not produce a clean additive reading of the pair-primitive framework.

This is not a framework refutation. It is a diagnostic: the framework's vocabulary applied under additive operational interpretation on this specific data does not produce a decisive mapping. Two possible reasons, each with different implications for future sprints:

- **Reason A:** Z/10's seam is multiplicatively loaded. The TSML itself is multiplicatively framed; the seam inherits that framing; additive reading finds the seam's structural signals too weak to score cleanly. Successor sprints would need different data (e.g., a seam structure on a ring with a more additive-loaded TSML) to test the additive operationalization.
- **Reason B:** The v1.1 rubric's reformulation under additive reading is not tight enough. The rubric tried to preserve binary scoring but some sources score 0 (neutral) under additive reading because the structural anchors of v1.0 (multiplicative identity, doubling chain backbone) don't have clean additive analogs. A refined rubric — with additive-structure-specific anchors — might produce decisive results that the current rubric does not.

The results document would diagnose which of these reasons produced the indecisive verdict, based on per-source score patterns.

### The sentence this outcome earns

> Under the additive operationalization (PPM-v1.1) of the pair-primitive framework on Z/10's 8 seam cells, neither Map A nor Map B reaches the pre-registered cleanness threshold. The additive operational interpretation of PPM-v1.1 is not diagnostic on this seam data; the reason (seam's multiplicative loading vs rubric under-specification) is documented in the results.

That sentence is the entirety of what Outcome 3 adds to the record.

### What Outcome 3 does NOT license

- **Does not refute the pair-primitive framework.** Per the v1.0 wording clarification: failure refutes the operationalization, not the framework. Outcome 3 refutes the specific additive operationalization as tested in v1.1, not the framework broadly.
- **Does not refute Map B.** Map B's v1.0 PASS under multiplicative operationalization stands.
- **Does not close the additive operationalization permanently.** A v1.1.1 with a refined additive rubric could be pre-registered and run if the results suggest rubric under-specification as the cause.
- **Does not establish that Z/10's seam is multiplicatively loaded.** That would be a stronger structural claim requiring separate evidence. The results document would note it as a diagnostic possibility, not a confirmed property.
- **Does not authorize scale examples, physics, or ontology.**
