# Why the Whole Interaction Must Be Seen at Once
## Methodological Note

---

## The Failure Mode This Note Guards Against

The program has accumulated pieces — 2×2, TSML, BHML, seam, prime, void, flow. Each has been sharpened in isolation. Each now has a clear internal scope. But progress has stalled at the point where the pieces ought to meet, and the stall has a specific shape: every time the meeting is attempted, one piece gets promoted to the role of "primitive" and the others are reduced to consequences of it.

Three versions of this failure have already appeared in the record:

- **Primitive = hold.** Gap becomes "absence of hold." Flow becomes "sequential holds." Seam becomes "a hold under noise." Everything derives from the single concept and the program becomes an account of holds with accessories. But then the gap is not doing any work — it has been made into a ghost of hold.
- **Primitive = void.** Hold becomes "region where void ceases." Flow becomes "void's complement." Seam becomes "where void meets non-void." Everything derives from absence, and presence becomes a derived concept. But then hold is not doing any work — it has been made into the ghost of void.
- **Primitive = flow.** Hold becomes "a fixed point of flow." Gap becomes "a region flow avoids." Seam becomes "where flow changes regime." Everything derives from dynamics and structure becomes a slow dynamic. But then hold and gap are not doing any work — they have been made into limits of motion.

Each of these is internally consistent. None of them is the program's actual object. The program's actual object is whatever it is that lets all three descriptions be simultaneously true without any of them being primary.

## The Positive Requirement

A unified ground for the program needs to treat hold, gap, and flow as **mutually constitutive**, not as a hierarchy of derived concepts. Specifically:

- A hold cannot be defined without the gap it curves around.
- A gap cannot be defined without the hold it complements.
- Flow cannot be defined without the holds it transports identity through and the gaps it cannot cross.

If any one of these fails — if you can cleanly define hold without mentioning gap, or gap without mentioning hold, or flow independent of both — then you have not found the unified ground. You have just renamed one of the pieces.

The 2×2 is a hint about how this constraint can be satisfied structurally. Z/10Z is additive structure and multiplicative structure and additive flow and multiplicative flow *at once*; no pair of two can be made primary without the other pair becoming a derived artifact, and the flatness obstruction that forces T\* = 5/7 *comes from* the requirement that none of the four collapse into the others. The T\* value is the signature of the refusal-to-collapse.

If a unified hold–gap–flow framework exists at the program level, it should have the same signature: the ground object is the interaction itself, and any attempt to make one aspect prior to the others produces a specific, measurable obstruction.

## Why Isolation Still Helped Up to Now

Isolation was not wrong at earlier stages. It was how each piece got sharpened enough to be testable. TSML could not have been characterized without treating it narrowly. The 2×2 theorem could not have been proven without treating Z/10Z's four structures as separable for the purpose of the proof. The subtype transport sprints (v1.1, v1.2-adj) required isolating individual role attributes to avoid v1.0's bundling failure.

What changes now is the level of the question. The sharp isolated pieces are in hand. The question is no longer "what does TSML do?" but "what is the category of thing that TSML is an instance of?" Answering the second question without the discipline of seeing all the pieces at once will just produce a new rename of one of the existing pieces.

## What This Note Is Not

This is not a claim that the unified framework exists. It might not. The program may live permanently as a set of disciplined narrow findings without a common ground. That would be a coherent outcome and would be documented as such.

This is not a claim that seeing things whole will produce a theorem. Foundation-mapping sprints do not produce theorems. They produce definitions, constraints, and obstructions — the inputs to later theorem work.

This is not a license to generate poetic prose about unity. Every claim in the hold–gap–flow foundation note must survive the same scope discipline that has governed the sprint program: what is exact, what is suggestive, what is a research direction. The foundation note is a research direction, not a result.

## The Operational Rule for the Sprint

While writing the foundation note, a single rule applies:

> Whenever a candidate primitive appears, the first test is not "is it sharp enough?" but "does making it primary reduce one of the other pieces to a consequence?" If yes, reject and keep looking.

The candidate primitives to watch for are: hold, gap, flow, seam, 2×2, collapse, transport, pair, topology, curvature, identity, decomposition.

The unified ground — if it exists — will not be any of these individually. It will be whatever generates the interaction among them such that each one becomes meaningful only when the others are also in play.

## One-Sentence Statement of Purpose

The foundation note exists to find the minimal object whose structure forces hold, gap, and flow into existence simultaneously — not as a theorem, but as the research hypothesis that could organize the program's pieces without requiring any one of them to rule the others.
