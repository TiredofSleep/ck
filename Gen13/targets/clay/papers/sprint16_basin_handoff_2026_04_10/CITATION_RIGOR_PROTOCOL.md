## A. One-Page Rigor Protocol

**What this project is:** A finite arithmetic study of two parallel structures — prime/coprime sieving in decimal rooms and odd-core Collatz dynamics — organized by the operator pair (2,3). The core findings are exact finite results, supported hypotheses, and one spatial cellular experiment.

**The three-tier discipline:**

**Tier 1 — Established background.** Claims that reproduce known results, place the work mathematically, or use standard tools. These need citations to the source literature. Do not re-prove them; cite and apply.

**Tier 2 — Reproducible new finite results.** Exact computed facts with explicit domains, parameters, and observables. These need: (a) exact statement of the computation, (b) explicit room/modulus/parameter, (c) the numerical result, (d) code available for replication. External citations are not required, but computation must be reproducible.

**Tier 3 — Hypotheses and interpretations.** Conjectures supported by finite data but not proved; speculative connections to RH, physics, consciousness, etc. These must be explicitly quarantined from the theorem path. Language: "we conjecture," "the data is consistent with," "no proof exists." Never appear in the statement of a theorem or as support for a Tier 2 claim.

**Cardinal rules:**
- A Tier 3 claim cannot be used to support a Tier 2 claim.
- A Tier 2 result cannot lean on a Tier 1 citation as proof — only as context.
- "Consistent with" and "implies" are different. The difference must be visible in the text.
- Any claim involving physics, consciousness, or RH is automatically Tier 3 until an explicit operator bridge is proved.

## D. Rewrite Guide

The following pairs show current loose language and its referee-safe replacement.

---

**Loose:** "Shell-1 is the deepest pull in the Collatz dynamics."

**Referee-safe:** "Among the shells $\mathcal{S}_s = \{n \text{ odd} : v_2(3n+1) = s\}$, shell $s=1$ is the unique expanding shell: $\Delta_1 = \mathbb{E}[\log_2 T_*(n) - \log_2 n \mid v_2(3n+1)=1] = \log_2 3 - 1 \approx +0.585 > 0$. All shells $s \geq 2$ contract: $\Delta_s = \log_2 3 - s < 0$. This is verified for $s = 1, \ldots, 14$ at modulus $2^{14}$."

---

**Loose:** "47 is special in the 2-digit room."

**Referee-safe:** "Among the 21 primes in $[11, 99]$, the prime $p = 47$ has the maximum normalized centrality score $\text{NC}(p) = (\text{stop}(p)/\max_q \text{stop}(q)) \cdot (\text{bd}(p)/\max_q \text{bd}(q)) = 0.971$, where $\text{stop}(p)$ is the Collatz odd-core stopping time and $\text{bd}(p)$ is the prime's rank distance from the nearest room boundary. This score is room-specific: the NC-leading prime in the 6-digit room is 540543 (composite)."

---

**Loose:** "The basin is prime-neutral."

**Referee-safe:** "Let $\mathcal{B}_n = \{m \in [10^{n-1}+1, 10^n) : \gcd(m,10) = 1, v_2(3m+1) = 1\}$ be the shell-1 coprime basin of the $n$-digit room. For $n = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6$, the prime fraction $|\mathcal{B}_n \cap \mathbb{P}|/|\mathcal{B}_n|$ equals $0.611, 0.411, 0.296, 0.233, 0.163$ respectively, deviating from the Dirichlet-corrected PNT estimate $2.5/\ln(\bar{m}_n)$ (where $\bar{m}_n$ is the midpoint of $R_n$) by less than 5\%. We observe no systematic enrichment or depletion of primes within the shell-1 basin beyond ambient prime density."

---

**Loose:** "The block lift compresses structure."

**Referee-safe:** "Let $\mathcal{L}_k(S)$ denote the block-lift operator mapping a digit sequence $S \in \{1,\ldots,9\}^N$ to $\{1,\ldots,9\}^{N/k}$ by assigning to each non-overlapping block of $k$ digits the shell-1 digit $7$ (if majority shell-1) or shell-2 digit $9$ (otherwise). For the competitive-shell dynamics with $\alpha=0.7$, $\beta=0.4$ at $N=500$ after 300 equilibration steps, the base layer has shell-1 fraction 0.013 and low-frequency spectral power (modes $k=1$–$10$) equal to 8\% of total variance. The lifted layer $\mathcal{L}_5(S)$ has low-frequency power equal to 17\% of total variance. The lift increases spatial coherence. The second lift $\mathcal{L}_5(\mathcal{L}_5(S))$ has shell-1 fraction 0.000 (trivial collapse)."

---

**Loose:** "The (2→3) vs (3→2) duality connects primes and Collatz."

**Referee-safe:** "We observe a structural symmetry in the operator order applied to the prime pair $\{2,3\}$ in the two systems: In the prime sieve, the 2-coprimality filter (admissibility) is applied first, then the mod-3 digit-sum filter (wobble), yielding $4/15$ of the room as prime candidates. In the odd-core Collatz map, the 3-lift $n \mapsto 3n+1$ is applied first, followed by the 2-collapse $n \mapsto n / 2^{v_2(n)}$. Both processes involve the same pair of primes in opposite compositional order. We make no claim that this structural parallel implies a deeper algebraic connection; it is an organizational observation that structures our classification."

---

**Loose:** "The σ framework connects to the Clay problems."

**Referee-safe:** "The Bialynicki-Birula theorem (Ann. Phys. 100, 1976) shows that the logarithmic nonlinearity is the unique nonlinearity preserving separability of composite quantum systems. The known log-improvement results in 3D Navier-Stokes regularity (Kozono-Taniuchi 2000, Comm. Math. Phys. 214) take the form $\int_0^T \|u\|^2_{BMO}/\log(e + \|u\|_{H^2})\,dt < \infty \Rightarrow \text{regularity}$. We note that both results involve logarithmic corrections at a regularity boundary. Whether this reflects a common underlying structure — specifically, whether the NS nonlinearity can be characterized by a 'separability defect' $\sigma$ analogous to the discrete CL algebra — remains a conjecture. No operator bridge is currently proved."

**Loose:** "Rule C creates spatial structure."

**Referee-safe:** "We define a stochastic cellular automaton on $\{1,\ldots,9\}^N$ with competitive-shell dynamics (Definition 3.4). At parameters $\alpha=0.7$, $\beta=0.4$, $N=500$, after 300 update steps from a uniform random initial state, we measure: shell-1 fraction $0.013 \pm 0.004$; exponential correlation length $\ell = 3.2 \pm 0.4$ cells (defined as the lag at which the normalized autocorrelation of the shell-1 indicator function falls below $0.5$); low-frequency spectral power (Fourier modes $k=1$–$10$) comprising $15.9 \pm 1.8\%$ of total variance. The random baseline at the same shell-1 fraction gives low-frequency power $0.4\%$. We conclude the system is in a spatially structured phase distinct from uniform frequency bias."

## E. Paper Structure Recommendation

Based on the current findings, the natural paper structure is:

**Section 1: Background.** 2-adic dynamics, sieve theory, cellular automata. Tier 1 citations. Place the work mathematically.

**Section 2: Static 2→3 / Dynamic 3→2 duality.** Formal definitions of decimal rooms, coprime scaffold, shell grammar. The 4 exact lemmas (shell distribution, conditional contraction, 2-corridor fraction, 4/15 combined fraction). Cite Terras, Apostol.

**Section 3: Basin structure across rooms.** Stop-apex and NC-apex tables for rooms 2–6. The two invariants. Basin prime neutrality. Twin-group analysis. All Tier 2.

**Section 4: Competitive shell dynamics.** The three candidate update rules. Phase table. Rule C characterization as sparse-cluster phase. Block-lift results for Rule C vs frequency-bias rules. All Tier 2.

**Appendix A: Conjectures.** The stop-apex compositeness pattern (5/6 rooms). The central-band shell-1 control hypothesis. Explicitly labeled as conjectures with supporting evidence only.

**Do not include:** RH bridge, Clay problem connections, physics interpretation, 47 as universal object. These belong in a separate speculative note, not in the main paper.
