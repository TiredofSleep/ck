## The answer: NO

The Hilbert tail of R/I_CL is generated by powers of x_0 (VOID). This is concentrated entirely on the index 0 (VOID) variable.

The u(1) center of the D_4-invariant subalgebra is generated by an antisymmetric matrix Z whose nonzero entries live entirely in the **6-cycle subspace {1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7}**. Z has zeros on the σ-fixed indices {0, 3, 8, 9} — including index 0 (VOID).

**The two 1-dim residuals are concentrated on DISJOINT subsets of indices:**
- Hilbert tail: lives on VOID (index 0)
- u(1) center: explicitly avoids VOID

They are **complementary**, not the same.

## What this means

Both pictures (commutative-algebra of R/I_CL, Lie-algebra of so(10)) have 1-dimensional residual structures. But they're different 1-dimensional structures, sitting in different categories.

The temptation to identify them was wrong. Without a functor connecting commutative-algebra picture to Lie-algebra picture, the identification is heuristic, and direct check shows they don't match.
