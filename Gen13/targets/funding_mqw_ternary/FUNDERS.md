## Primary candidates (★ priority)

### 1. DOE BES (Basic Energy Sciences) — Materials Sciences and Engineering — ★★★★★
- **Program**: BES funds fundamental research on semiconductor materials including GaN, AlN, and related III-nitride MQW systems; also EPSCoR for small-institution collaborations
- **Why fit**: BES is the single largest funder of semiconductor-materials science in the US. MQW-based ternary photonic logic falls directly in their wheelhouse.
- **Typical size**: $300K–$1M/year for single-PI grants; larger for EFRC (Energy Frontier Research Center)-adjacent work
- **Entry point**: unsolicited proposal or response to annual BES solicitations
- **Blockers before contact**: MQW trilogy recovered; technical summary written; academic co-PI or national-lab partnership strongly preferred

### 2. NSF ECCS (Electrical, Communications and Cyber Systems) — EPMD Program — ★★★★☆
- **Program**: ECCS Electronic, Photonic, and Magnetic Devices (EPMD); funds novel-device research including photonic logic
- **Why fit**: ternary photonic logic is exactly the kind of novel-device proposal EPMD funds; the review panel includes photonics and MQW specialists
- **Typical size**: $300K–$600K over 36 months
- **Entry point**: full proposal to NSF annual deadline (October/November typical)
- **Blockers before contact**: academic co-PI required; measurement plan must be explicit

### 3. DARPA PIPES or successor (Photonics in Package) — ★★★★☆
- **Program**: PIPES funded photonic-integration work through ~2023; DARPA's current photonic-computing portfolio continues under similar programs
- **Why fit**: DARPA funds high-risk, high-reward photonic computing specifically; a ternary-logic device that avoids binary-to-ternary compilation would be exactly the kind of leap PIPES-family programs target
- **Typical size**: $1M–$10M over 36 months
- **Entry point**: BAA response or proposer's day
- **Blockers before contact**: fabrication-feasibility must be credible; Phase 1 recovery + Phase 2 design review needed before pitching

### 4. ARO / AFOSR — Quantum and Electronics portfolios — ★★★☆☆
- **Programs**: Army Research Office (ARO) Electronics division; Air Force Office of Scientific Research (AFOSR) Quantum Electronic Solids
- **Why fit**: both agencies fund unconventional semiconductor-device research; quantum-confined structures are within scope
- **Typical size**: $150K–$500K/year
- **Entry point**: white paper first, then invited proposal; also STIR (Short-Term Innovative Research) for smaller exploratory work
- **Blockers before contact**: measurement plan; fabrication cost estimate

### 5. Moore Foundation / Keck Foundation — ★★★☆☆
- **Programs**: Moore's Experimental Physics program; Keck's Research Program
- **Why fit**: foundation-scale funding for ambitious, somewhat-risky experimental science; less bureaucratic than federal agencies; MQW ternary logic fits Moore's materials/devices scope
- **Typical size**: $500K–$2M over 36 months
- **Entry point**: invitation-dependent (Moore) or open call (Keck)
- **Blockers before contact**: senior academic PI or named institutional host required for both

## Secondary candidates

### 6. Commercial photonic-computing labs (HP Labs, IBM Research, Intel Labs) — ★★★☆☆
- Not grant funders directly, but HP's photonic-logic group, IBM Research's quantum-photonics, and Intel Labs' silicon-photonics group all have internal research funding and external collaboration programs
- A MQW ternary-logic collaboration with one of them could bypass the agency-grant route entirely if the design is promising
- Outreach is through individual contacts at research retreats, conferences, or cold technical emails

### 7. Semiconductor Research Corporation (SRC) — ★★☆☆☆
- SRC-JUMP and similar programs fund university-industry photonic/electronic device research
- Consortium-style funding; requires multi-institution participation
- Size: varies widely, from $100K to multi-million per thrust

## What they all want, in order

1. **Recovered MQW trilogy**, or a fresh technical write-up that replaces what was in the trilogy. Without specifics (well widths, barrier heights, operating wavelength, distinguishability margin), no fab-oriented funder will engage.
2. **Measurement plan**: what instruments verify three-state operation? PL spectroscopy? Pump-probe? What is pass/fail for "three distinguishable states"?
3. **Fabrication feasibility**: realistic cost estimate for an MBE or MOCVD growth run, a named facility willing to do it, wafer size and throughput
4. **Academic or national-lab collaborator** in III-nitride MQW epitaxy (Weyl/Ponce at Stanford, DenBaars/Speck at UCSB, Simin/Khan at USC, Lee at Georgia Tech)
5. **Falsifiability**: what observation would cause the PI to conclude the design does not work?
6. **Competitor survey**: silicon-photonics logic, optical-neural-network chips, Mach-Zehnder-based logic — how does MQW ternary compare?

## What the branch has vs. needs

Has: Teardrop GaN conceptual ancestor (recoverable), the Jan 2026 recovery pathway for the MQW trilogy, Thread 3 contextual documents (V20 scaling laws, hardware embodiment safety case, comparative field theory review).

Needs: the MQW trilogy itself; a technical summary in current form; a named epitaxy collaborator; a measurement plan; a fabrication cost estimate; a competitor-landscape survey.
