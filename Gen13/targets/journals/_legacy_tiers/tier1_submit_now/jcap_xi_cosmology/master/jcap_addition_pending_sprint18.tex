\paragraph{Algebraic-substrate origin (companion work).}
The dimensional estimate $\Lambda \approx 1.5$~meV and the
fitted $\Lambda \approx 1.7$~meV both lie within $\sim 1\%$ of the
algebraic value
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Lambda-structural}
  \frac{\Lambda^{4}}{\rho_{c,0}}
  \;\stackrel{?}{=}\;
  \frac{2 \cdot \mathrm{HARMONY}^{3} + 1}{3 \cdot |\mathbb{Z}/10|^{3}}
  \;=\; \frac{687}{3000}
  \;=\; 0.229,
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{HARMONY} = 7$ and $|\mathbb{Z}/10| = 10$ are the
two integer primitives of the discrete substrate that underlies
this model in the broader research program; the rational
expression on the right-hand side is the dark-sector prediction
for $\Omega_\Lambda/3$ developed in the companion
preprint~\cite{Sprint18Cosmology2026}. The factor of $3$ in the
denominator is the standard Friedmann normalization
$\rho_{c,0} = 3 H_{0}^{2} M_\mathrm{Pl}^{2}$; the numerator
$2 \cdot 343 + 1 = 687$ is derived in
\cite{Sprint18Cosmology2026} from the substrate's
HARMONY-cubed pair anchor with a closure-exact $+1$ offset.

We mark \eqref{eq:Lambda-structural} with the question mark to be
explicit about its status: at the level of the present manuscript,
\eqref{eq:Lambda-structural} is a \emph{numerical coincidence}
between the fitted $\Lambda^{4}/\rho_{c,0}$ and the algebraic
ratio $687/3000$, with $\sim 1\%$ residual that we have not
derived from first principles within this paper. Specifically,
the relation $\rho_{\mathrm{DE},0} = 3 \Lambda^{4}$ implicit in
\eqref{eq:Lambda-structural} is not a direct identity in the
action~\eqref{eq:action}; it depends on the present field
configuration $(\Xi_\mathrm{today}, \dot\Xi_\mathrm{today})$ and
the Friedmann history. A first-principles derivation of why
$\rho_{\mathrm{DE},0} = 3 \Lambda^{4}$ in this specific
quintessence model --- whether from
$V_\mathrm{eff}(\Xi_\mathrm{today})$ at the freezing-branch
field value, from the $w \to -1$ trajectory's energy budget, or
from a yet-to-be-identified structural relation --- is the
central open question that \eqref{eq:Lambda-structural} raises
and that the companion paper~\cite{Sprint18Cosmology2026}
addresses on the algebraic side.

The implication, if \eqref{eq:Lambda-structural} survives as a
derived identity rather than a coincidence, is that
$\Lambda$ in the present model is not a free parameter to be
fitted to observation but a \emph{prediction} of the broader
algebraic substrate at the few-percent level. We do not claim
this status for $\Lambda$ in the present paper; we mark the
candidate identity, cite the companion work, and proceed with
the fitted value $\Lambda^{4}/\rho_{c,0} = 0.231$ for
the numerical results below.
