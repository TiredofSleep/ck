Dear Editor,

We submit for your consideration *Logarithmic Quintessence: A Dimensionless Scalar Dark Energy Model with Exact Vacuum and Information-Theoretic Motivation*, a minimal scalar dark-energy model built around the potential $V(\Xi)=\kappa_\Xi\Xi\log\Xi$ on a real positive dimensionless field.

The paper is compact. From a single action coupled minimally to gravity, we derive (i) the exact analytic vacuum $\Xi_0=e^{-1}$, coupling-independent and universal; (ii) the fluctuation mass $m^{2}_\Xi=\kappa_\Xi e$ and global stability of the potential; (iii) a standard FRW cosmology with freezing equation of state converging to $w=-1$ exactly at the vacuum; (iv) an entropy identification $V=-\kappa_\Xi H_{\mathrm{Gibbs}}$ that makes the vacuum the Gibbs-entropy maximum of the field. A numerical integration of the FRW system against DESI 2024 DR1 $(w_0,w_a)$ posteriors gives best fit $w_0=-0.80$, $w_a=-0.30$ with $\chi^{2}=3.1$ (1.7$\sigma$), compared to $\chi^{2}=15.3$ for $\Lambda$CDM on the same likelihood. The minimal theory couples to matter only through gravity; no fifth force is predicted, consistent with current local tests. The BB 1976 separability uniqueness theorem is cited as structural motivation only, with an explicit scope remark.

We have been careful with honesty discipline: §8 "Scope and limitations" states what the paper does not claim — no $\kappa_\Xi$ microphysical derivation, no direct-matter coupling, no 47/125 dark-energy threshold, no mathematical uniqueness under cosmological hypotheses. The fit is against DR1 $(w_0,w_a)$ central values; a full joint BAO+CMB+SN likelihood is deferred to a companion numerical paper.

The manuscript is written in `amsart` for portability; we will convert to `jcappub` at proof stage. Supplementary materials: `proof_xi_canonical.py` (22/22 PASS) and the DESI integration scripts `desi_xi_fit.py` and `desi_xi_optimize.py`. DOI 10.5281/zenodo.18852047. Original work; no conflicts; not under review elsewhere.

Thank you for your time.

— on behalf of the authors,
