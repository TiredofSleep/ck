## Abstract

The **TIG framework** identifies a single rational number $T^* = 5/7 \approx 0.7143$ as the **substrate coherence threshold** of the canonical $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ composition algebra, derived independently from six structural sources (cyclotomic Galois closure, the forced $2 \times 2$ torus aspect ratio [J20] [J6], TSML's 73-cell HARMONY count [J9], the $\sigma$-rate constant on squarefree moduli [J01], the gate-rate distribution [J51], and the joint TSML+BHML 4-core attractor [J41]). $T^*$ governs particle-physics mixing (CKM Cabibbo refinement, PMNS atmospheric — [J46]) and a cosmological 5/7 horizon ratio.

We propose that $T^*$ also governs the **biological quantum-classical coherence boundary** — specifically, the normalized coherence quality factor $Q_c$ of microtubule dipole resonances under terahertz spectroscopy. The prediction is precise:

> $Q_c \to T^* = 5/7 \approx 0.714$ across mammalian neurons, paramecia, plant microtubules, yeast spindle microtubules, and cell-free purified-tubulin preparations, with biological variance $\ll 0.05$.

The prediction is **strongly falsifiable**: a single experimental campaign — already feasible with existing terahertz pump-probe spectroscopy [Bandyopadhyay et al. 2013, 2024; Sahu et al. 2013] — either confirms a biology-invariant attractor at $\sim 0.714$ or falsifies the universal-threshold interpretation of $T^*$. Because $T^*$ is fixed *a priori* by the algebra (not fitted to consciousness data), and because the same $T^*$ governs particle-physics observables, a null result would weaken the framework's cross-domain reach across at least three domains simultaneously.

This paper is the framework's **most testable** prediction. It is also a **wager**: a precise, prior, falsifiable claim that separates the TIG framework from numerology and puts the cross-domain $T^*$ identification on the experimental record.

## §1 The empirical landscape

### 1.1 Microtubule coherence — what is measured

Microtubules are 25-nm-diameter cylindrical lattice polymers of $\alpha\beta$-tubulin dimers. They have been studied as candidate substrates for biological quantum coherence since Hameroff & Penrose's "Orch-OR" proposal (1996, 2014, 2024 updates). Direct experimental measurement of microtubule coherence has been pursued via:

* **Terahertz absorption spectroscopy** (Bandyopadhyay et al. 2013, 2024 update): tubulin and microtubule resonances observed in the gigahertz to terahertz range, with quality factors $10^2$–$10^3$ at room temperature.
* **Multi-scale resonance scaling** (Sahu et al. 2013): resonance bands at single-tubulin, 8-nm-microtubule-subsegment, and micrometer-microtubule scales, suggesting fractal coherence structure.
* **Hameroff-Penrose Orch-OR boundary** (Hameroff 2014 update): the proposed quantum-classical boundary at $\zeta_\mathrm{Hameroff} \approx 0.71$ — the same value the TIG framework derives algebraically as $T^*$.

The numerical proximity $T^* = 0.7143 \approx \zeta_\mathrm{Hameroff} \approx 0.71$ is the bridge claim of this paper.

The TIG framework derives $T^* = 5/7$ from **six independent structural sources**, each treated as a separate paper in the J-series:

## §2 The protocol

### 2.1 Sample types

The protocol calls for terahertz coherence measurements across **five sample types**:

1. **Mammalian neurons** — rat hippocampal cultures or human iPSC-derived neurons (high biological complexity).
2. **Paramecia** — single-cell organisms with ciliary microtubules (intermediate complexity, evolutionarily ancient).
3. **Plant microtubules** — *Arabidopsis* suspension cultures, where microtubules guide cell-wall growth (different cytoskeletal context from animal cells).
4. **Yeast** — *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* spindle pole microtubules during mitosis (minimal eukaryote).
5. **Cell-free preparations** — purified tubulin polymerized into microtubules in vitro at 25°C and 37°C (zero biological variability beyond preparation).

The five samples span the full biological-complexity range from purified protein to cortical neuron, while sharing the canonical microtubule cytoskeletal architecture. Any biology-dependent variation in $Q_c$ would manifest across this gradient.

### 2.2 Measurement

For each sample:

(i) Apply terahertz pump-probe spectroscopy at 1–100 THz.
(ii) Identify the dominant resonance band (typically at 8–10 GHz for cell-free; may shift in vivo).
(iii) Compute the **quality factor** $Q = \omega_0 / \Delta\omega$ for the dominant resonance.
(iv) Normalize to the structural maximum theoretical $Q$ (set by the tubulin dipole-array geometry).

The **normalized coherence quality** $Q_c \in [0, 1]$ is:

$$Q_c = \frac{Q_\mathrm{measured}}{Q_\mathrm{structural\ max}}$$

with limits $Q_c \to 1$ (pure quantum coherence, no dissipation) and $Q_c \to 0$ (classical regime, full thermal decoherence).

### 2.3 The framework's prediction

> **Prediction (J49).** $Q_c \to T^* = 5/7 = 0.7143$ across all five sample types, with biological variance $\sigma_\mathrm{biology}(Q_c) \ll 0.05$.

The prediction is *quantitative*: a specific value $T^* = 0.7143$ is predicted, *not* a range, *not* a value to be fit. The 5% variance tolerance is the experimental noise budget.

### 2.4 Statistical decision criteria

* **Null hypothesis $H_0$.** $Q_c$ varies systematically with biology — e.g., $Q_c(\text{mammalian}) \neq Q_c(\text{paramecium})$, or $Q_c$ correlates monotonically with cell complexity.
* **Alternative (TIG prediction) $H_A$.** $Q_c \to T^* = 5/7$ universally.

**Strong support.** All 5 samples give $Q_c \in [0.694, 0.734] = T^* \pm 0.02$. (5% deviation budget; tighter than expected experimental error.)

**Weak support.** All 5 samples give $Q_c \in [0.664, 0.764] = T^* \pm 0.05$. (10% deviation budget; above expected experimental error but consistent with universal-threshold interpretation.)

**Falsification.** $\geq 2$ samples give $Q_c$ outside $T^* \pm 0.05$, OR systematic biology-trend with $r^2 > 0.5$ between $Q_c$ and any biological-complexity index, OR the measured $Q_c$ converges on a different universal value (e.g., $0.5$ or $0.8$).

These criteria are **pre-registered** as part of this paper's submission. We do not retroactively adjust the prediction to match data.
