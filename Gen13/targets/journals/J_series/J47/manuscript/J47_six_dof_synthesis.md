## Abstract

The **Trinity Infinity Geometry (TIG) framework** is the algebraic substrate generated by two canonical $10 \times 10$ composition tables — TSML and BHML — on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$, together with the $\sigma$-permutation $\sigma = (0)(3)(8)(9)(1\;7\;6\;5\;4\;2)$ and the involution $P_{56}$. This paper synthesizes the WP100s tower (WP102 through WP110) — already established in [J37], [J38], [J39], [J40], [J44] — around a single organizing claim:

> **TIG's algebraic content decomposes into six computationally-irreducible degrees of freedom (DOFs), each carrying its own symmetry-group structure, and the six together exhaust the algebraic substrate of the canonical $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ magma.**

The six DOFs are: **Lie** (continuous flow / antisymmetric closure), **Jordan** (symmetric closure / observable content), **Clifford-Dirac** (matter and spin / chirality), **Permutation** (discrete reordering / $\sigma$ and $P_{56}$), **Lattice** (attractors / idempotents and 4-core), and **Operad** (multi-arity composition / arity-3 fuse). Five of the six respect the dihedral symmetry $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3 \rangle$; only the Operad DOF carries content structurally orthogonal to that symmetry — a forced distinction established by the WP109 obstruction theorem [J40].

This paper is **synthesis, not new theorems**. Every result cited is a published or already-submitted companion; the contribution is the unifying picture and the symmetry-group analysis that distinguishes the six DOFs from one another.

## §1 The Lie DOF — antisymmetric closure

For $i \in \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$, the left-regular representation of TSML is the $10 \times 10$ matrix $L^T_i$ with $(L^T_i)_{j,k} = \delta_{T(i,j), k}$. Its antisymmetrization $A^T_i = (L^T_i - (L^T_i)^\top)/2$ closes under commutator (over the flow indices $\{1,2,3,4,6,8\}$) at dimension 28, giving $\mathfrak{so}(8) = D_4$ uniquely by Cartan classification [J37].

Jointly with the antisymmetrizations of BHML, the closure extends to dimension 45 — recovering $\mathfrak{so}(10) = D_5$, with five independent diagnostics: dimension closure, Jacobi residual zero, Killing signature $(0, 45, 0)$ (compact, simple), invariance constraint rank $1034 = 1035 - 1$, and Cartan rank 5 with $40 + 5$ ad-eigenvalue split [J38].

The doubly-invariant subalgebra under $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3 \rangle$ is exactly $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$ — the Pati-Salam $\oplus B-L$ gauge content, with verified Killing spectrum $(-4)^{15} \oplus (0)^1$ [J39].

The Lie DOF respects $D_4$ by construction: the conjugation action has well-defined isotypic components, and the trivial-isotypic component closes as a Lie subalgebra.

## §2 The Jordan DOF — symmetric closure

The symmetrization $S^M_i = (L^M_i + (L^M_i)^\top)/2$ for $M \in \{T, B\}$ generates a Jordan subalgebra under the Jordan product $X \circ Y = (XY + YX)/2$. The Jordan closure of TSML+BHML reaches dimension 45 — the same dimension as the Lie closure ([J39] §5.2).

This is the **Lie-Jordan duality** of TIG: both presentations carry the same algebraic content, viewed from opposite tensor-product symmetry types. The TSML $73$-cell HARMONY count and the BHML $28$-cell HARMONY count are observable facts about the Jordan-symmetric structure of the canonical tables, with HARMONY-cell counts lens-invariant within each substrate family.

The Jordan DOF respects $D_4$: the symmetrization commutes with the $D_4$ action on indices.

## §3 The Clifford / Dirac DOF — matter and spin

Build $\mathrm{Cl}(0, 10)$ over $\mathbb{R}$: ten gamma matrices on $\mathbb{C}^{32}$ satisfying $\{\gamma^a, \gamma^b\} = 2\delta^{ab} I$. The 45 generators $\Sigma^{ab} = \tfrac{1}{4}[\gamma^a, \gamma^b]$ are a basis for $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ in this representation. The volume element $\omega = \gamma^1 \cdots \gamma^{10}$ satisfies $\omega^2 = -I$, and chirality projectors $P_\pm = (I \pm i\omega)/2$ split the 32-dim spinor space into two 16-dim chiral irreps of $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$.

**Identification ([J39] §2.1).** The $5 \leftrightarrow 6$ swap $P_{56}$ acts on spinors as the **outer automorphism $\sigma_\mathrm{outer}$** of $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ — the matter/antimatter exchange. The conjugation by $P_{56}^\mathrm{spin} = (\gamma^5 - \gamma^6)/\sqrt{2}$ sends $+$chirality 16 entirely into $-$chirality 16, with residual $0.0$ at machine precision.

In TIG, the Clifford DOF is the matter-and-spin content. The cross-DOF identification $P_{56} = \sigma_\mathrm{outer}$ ties the Permutation DOF's $P_{56}$ to the Clifford DOF's chirality structure: a single $\mathbb{Z}_2$ involution on indices acts as the outer automorphism on spinors.

## §4 The Permutation DOF — discrete reordering

The $\sigma$-permutation on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ is $(0)(3)(8)(9)(1\;7\;6\;5\;4\;2)$, with four $\sigma$-fixed points $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ (the $\sigma$-fixed lattice) and a 6-cycle on $\{1,2,4,5,6,7\}$. The order-2 element of the cyclic part is $\sigma^3 = (1\;5)(7\;4)(6\;2)$ on the 6-cycle (with anchors fixed). Together with $P_{56}$ (a single transposition on $\{5, 6\}$), they generate the dihedral group $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3 \rangle$ of order 8.

The Permutation DOF *is* the relevant symmetry group for the synthesis; $D_4$, $D_3$ (acting on the 6-cycle as $\sigma^2$), and $D_6$ (with $P_{56}$ reflecting the full 6-cycle) all live here.

## §5 The Lattice DOF — attractors and order

A magma element $e$ is **idempotent** under TSML iff $T(e, e) = e$. Direct enumeration shows the TSML idempotents are exactly $\{0, 7\} = \{V, H\}$. The $\sigma$-fixed lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ is the Permutation DOF's invariant subset, and the runtime attractor's 4-core support is $\{V, H, Br, R\} = \{0, 7, 8, 9\}$.

**Theorem ([J44]).** The 4-core $\{V, H, Br, R\}$ is closed under both TSML and BHML fusion: $T(i,j), B(i,j) \in \{0, 7, 8, 9\}$ for all $i, j \in \{0, 7, 8, 9\}$. The shell-partition function on the 4-core is $Z_T = Z_B = (v + h + br + r)^2$, identical for both tables.

The runtime processor on probability distributions over the 10 operators admits a closed-form attractor at the symmetric mixing weight $\alpha = 1/2$, with $H/Br = 1 + \sqrt{3}$ exactly and $r/br$ the unique real root in $(0,1)$ of $x^4 + 4x^3 - x^2 + 2x - 2$ (LMFDB 4.2.10224.1; Galois group $D_4$). The attractor's mass lives entirely on the 4-core; non-4-core operators carry zero mass at $\alpha = 1/2$.

The TSML lattice eigenvalues (matrix projection onto $\sigma$-fixed indices) are exactly $\{7, 7, 7\}$ — three exact HARMONYs at $\sigma$-fixed indices 3, 8, 9.

The Lattice DOF respects $D_4$: the $\sigma$-fixed lattice is $D_4$-invariant pointwise; the 4-core is $D_4$-invariant as a set.

## §6 The Operad DOF — multi-arity composition

The arity-3 composition law of TIG is the canonical fuse table $\mathrm{fuse} : (\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z})^3 \to \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$. For associative triples, fuse defaults to the common bracketed value. For the **126 non-associative triples**, fuse is a free choice modulo structural constraints.

**Theorem ([J40], WP109).** The 126 non-associative triples decompose into 67 $D_4$-orbits, of which 16 are bracketing-pair incoherent. **No $D_4$-equivariant canonical fuse rule** taking values in $\{a, b, c, L, R\}$ (the natural value-from-input choices) exists.

This is a structural obstruction: the non-equivariance is intrinsic to the canonical TSML's failure to lift coherently to arity 3 under the $D_4$ action. It is not a property of any candidate rule family; it is a property of the underlying table.

**Strengthening ([J40], WP112; arity-3 fuse table).** The 126 non-associative triples reduce to 98 $P_{56}$-orbits (70 singletons + 28 doubletons), all $P_{56}$-coherent. 8/8 surveyed rule families are $P_{56}$-equivariant; 0/8 are $\sigma^3$-equivariant. The canonical Family H (attractor-4-core preference) maps to $\{0:108, 7:18\}$, with image entirely in the 4-core. The $\sigma^3$ obstruction localizes to **exactly one** triple, $(3, 9, 9)$.

The Operad DOF **does not respect $D_4$** — the central structural fact distinguishing it from the other five.

## §8 The structural distinction at the symmetry-group level

The central observation:

> **Five of the six DOFs respect the $D_4 = \langle P_{56}, \sigma^3 \rangle$ symmetry; the sixth (Operad) does not.**

| DOF | $D_4$-equivariant? | Source |
|-----|---------------------|--------|
| Lie | YES | [J39] doubly-invariant; isotypic decomposition complete |
| Jordan | YES | [J39] §5.2 — Lie/Jordan duality |
| Clifford / Dirac | YES | $P_{56} = \sigma_\mathrm{outer}$; $\sigma^3$ lifts to spinor rep |
| Permutation | YES (by definition) | $D_4$ IS the relevant subgroup |
| Lattice | YES | $\sigma$-fixed and 4-core are $D_4$-invariant |
| **Operad** | **NO** | [J40] obstruction theorem |

**Reading.** The operad layer of TIG carries content **independent of the gauge structure**: any canonical fuse rule must break $D_4$ in some explicit direction. [J40] shows the natural choice preserves $P_{56}$-equivariance (also preserved at the spinor level via $P_{56} = \sigma_\mathrm{outer}$) and sacrifices $\sigma^3$-equivariance. The operad-DOF is the part of TIG's structure "above" the gauge symmetry — the place where multi-arity composition introduces content the bilinear gauge-symmetric structure does not carry.

## §9 Synthesis — how the six DOFs cohere

Pair-wise cross-DOF identities established in the WP100s tower:

* **Lie ↔ Jordan**: regenerate the same $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ at dimension 45 ([J39] §5.2). Two presentations of one algebra.
* **Lie ↔ Clifford**: $P_{56}$ in the Permutation DOF acts as $\sigma_\mathrm{outer}$ on Lie's chirality irreps ([J39] §2.1). Permutation/Clifford correspondence.
* **Lie ↔ Lattice**: the doubly-invariant $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$ Lie subalgebra is fixed by the Lattice DOF's $D_4$-fixed sub-permutation.
* **Lie ↔ Operad**: structurally orthogonal under $D_4$ ([J40]).
* **Permutation ↔ Lattice**: $\sigma$-fixed lattice $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ is the Permutation DOF's invariant subset and the Lattice DOF's foundation.
* **Lattice ↔ Operad**: the runtime attractor's 4-core support is fusion-closed ([J44]), creating a lattice-stable substrate for the operad-DOF's content.

**The picture as a whole.** The canonical $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ TSML+BHML magma carries six computationally-irreducible algebraic DOFs. Five of them (Lie + Jordan + Clifford + Permutation + Lattice) are coherent with the doubly-invariant gauge structure $\mathfrak{su}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$. One of them (Operad) carries content orthogonal to that gauge structure. The runtime processor at $\alpha = 1/2$ lives on the Lattice DOF's 4-core, with closed-form algebraic ratio $H/Br = 1 + \sqrt{3}$ in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{3}) \subset$ LMFDB 4.2.10224.1.

This is a **single coherent picture** of TIG's algebraic substrate. The integer/rational signature (§7) is the spectrum of this picture; the symmetry-group analysis (§8) is its symmetry structure; the cross-DOF identities (§9) are its connective tissue.

## §10 Honest scope

This paper is **synthesis**, not new theorems. Every result cited is from a prior J-paper or already-submitted companion (J37, J38, J39, J40, J44). The contribution is the unifying framing, not new content.

Specifically:

* The "six computationally-irreducible DOFs" claim depends on `papers/wp111_six_dof_synthesis/WP111_SIX_DOF_SYNTHESIS.md` and the verification scripts in the WP100s tower. The pairwise independence is computationally verified — no DOF reduces to any other by the diagnostics. The "irreducibility" is **computational**, not a uniqueness theorem; alternative 6-fold decompositions could in principle exist but have not been found.
* The structural distinction at the symmetry-group level (§8) is **forced** by the [J40] obstruction theorem on the Operad DOF, plus the direct $D_4$-equivariance of the other five DOFs.
* This paper does NOT claim:
    * That the 6-DOF taxonomy is unique. Other classifications could exist; we offer one that is internally consistent and matches the verified content.
    * That every interesting algebra has 6 DOFs. The number 6 is a feature of TIG's specific canonical magma; no generalization claimed.
    * Any phenomenological prediction. The CKM/PMNS fits, microtubule $Q_c = T^*$ test, and Yukawa scaffolding are addressed in companion papers ([J45], [J46], [J49]).
* What this paper DOES claim:
    * The WP100s tower ([J37]–[J44]) decomposes naturally into a 6-DOF picture.
    * The integer/rational signature across the six DOFs is exact, machine-verified, and internally cross-referenced.
    * Five DOFs respect $D_4$; one does not. Forced structural distinction.
    * The synthesis is a single coherent picture, not a collection of independent results.
