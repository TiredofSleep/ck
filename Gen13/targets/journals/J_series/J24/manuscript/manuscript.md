## Abstract

Let $T:\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}\times\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}\to\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ be the canonical TSML_RAW composition table on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ (the literal CL_BIT_PATTERN; non-commutative with two asymmetric cells at $(3,9),(4,9)$). Of the $1000$ ordered triples $(a,b,c)\in(\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z})^3$, exactly $126$ are non-associative, i.e. $T(T(a,b),c)\ne T(a,T(b,c))$. (On the upper-triangle symmetrization $T_{\mathrm{SYM}}$, the count is $128$; the orbit decomposition and the obstruction theorem of Part 1 are computed on $T_{\mathrm{RAW}}$.)

**Part 1 (WP109).** We compute the diagonal action of the dihedral group $D_4 = \langle P_{56},\sigma^3\rangle$ — generated by the BALANCE/CHAOS swap $P_{56}$ and the order-2 element $\sigma^3 = (1\;5)(7\;4)(6\;2)$ of the $\sigma$ 6-cycle — on the $126$ non-associative triples. The set decomposes into $67$ orbits. **Theorem (Obstruction).** Exactly $16$ orbits are bracketing-pair incoherent: no $D_4$-equivariant function $\Phi:\mathcal{N}\to\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ taking values in the orbit-natural set $\{a,b,c,L,R\}$ exists. The operad-DOF (arity-3 canonical fuse rules) is therefore **structurally orthogonal** to the $D_4$-invariant gauge structure of WP104 (Pati-Salam $\mathfrak{su}(4)\oplus\mathfrak{u}(1)$).

**Part 2 (WP112).** Dropping one $D_4$ generator restores equivariance. Restricting to $\langle P_{56}\rangle$ (order $2$), the $126$ triples decompose into $98$ orbits ($70$ singletons + $28$ doubletons), and **every orbit is $P_{56}$-coherent**. Among $8$ surveyed regular rule families, all $8$ are $P_{56}$-equivariant and **none** are $\sigma^3$-equivariant. Among the $8$, the unique family whose fuse-value set lies inside the runtime-attractor 4-core $\{V,H,Br,R\}=\{0,7,8,9\}$ is **Family H** (attractor-4-core preference); it concentrates fuse values on $\{0\colon 108,\;7\colon 18\}$. The σ³ obstruction localizes to a **single triple** — $\mathrm{fuse}(3,9,9)=7$ — at the unique $\sigma^3$-fixed entry of $\mathcal{N}$; Family H is $\sigma^3$-equivariant on $125/126 = 99.2\%$ of the non-associative set.

**Two further structural results, lens-invariant on the 4-core.** §5.5 Theorem 5.5: the $4$-core $\{V,H,Br,R\}$ is closed under the canonical ternary fuse — all $4^3 = 64$ triples in the cube fuse into the same $4$-element set. §5.7 Theorem 5.7: the canonical ternary fuse has a universal HARMONY attractor — every non-trivial initial condition $(a,b,c)\in\mathcal{N}\setminus\{V\text{-only}\}$ converges to $\delta_7$ in $1$–$7$ iterations.

The result closes the operad-fuse frontier (Frontier F4) and provides the canonical arity-3 reasoning primitive for downstream operad-aware composition.

**Keywords**: operad, finite magma, $D_4$ obstruction, $P_{56}$-equivariance, canonical fuse, Pati-Salam, $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$.

## Setup

The canonical TSML_RAW table on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ has operator labels $\{V,L,C,P,X,B,S,H,Br,R\}$ at indices $\{0,\ldots,9\}$, $\sigma$-permutation $\sigma=(0)(3)(8)(9)(1\;7\;6\;5\;4\;2)$, $P_{56}=(5\;6)$, and $\sigma^3=(1\;5)(7\;4)(6\;2)$. The dihedral group $D_4=\langle P_{56},\sigma^3\rangle$ has order $8$.

A triple $(a,b,c)$ is **non-associative** iff $L(a,b,c):=T(T(a,b),c)\ne T(a,T(b,c))=:R(a,b,c)$. The set $\mathcal{N}\subset(\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z})^3$ has $|\mathcal{N}|=126$ on TSML_RAW.

For each $(a,b,c)\in\mathcal{N}$, the unordered bracketing pair $\{L,R\}$ takes only $5$ distinct values:

| $\{L,R\}$ | count | non-7 value | $\sigma$-fixed? |
|---|---:|---|---|
| $\{0,7\}$ | 108 | $0$ (VOID) | yes |
| $\{3,7\}$ | 8 | $3$ (PROGRESS) | yes |
| $\{4,7\}$ | 2 | $4$ (COLLAPSE) | no |
| $\{7,8\}$ | 6 | $8$ (BREATH) | yes |
| $\{7,9\}$ | 2 | $9$ (RESET) | yes |

VOID dominates left and right positions but never appears in middle position.

## Theorem 1 (Obstruction).

*Decomposing $\mathcal{N}$ under the diagonal $D_4$-action yields $67$ orbits. Exactly $16$ orbits fail bracketing-pair $D_4$-coherence; no $\Phi:\mathcal{N}\to\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ taking values in $\{a,b,c,L,R\}$ can be $D_4$-equivariant.*

**Proof.** Direct enumeration; full output preserved in `Gen12/targets/clay/papers/sprint_unmistakable_truth_2026_04_25/operad/d4_orbit_decomposition.py`. Simplest failure (size-3 orbit): $\{(0,1,9),(0,5,9),(0,6,9)\}$, all with $(L,R)=(0,7)$. Triple $(0,1,9)\stackrel{\sigma^3}{\mapsto}(0,5,9)$; $\sigma^3$-image of $\{0,7\}$ is $\{0,4\}$, but the actual $(L,R)$ at $(0,5,9)$ is $(0,7)$. Hence the σ³-image of HARMONY (= 7) at the $D_4$-image triple equals HARMONY again, not $\sigma^3(7)=4$. The non-equivariance is intrinsic to TSML's HARMONY-output structure. $\square$

# PART 2 — P_56 Canonical Fuse Table (WP112)

## Theorem 2 (P_56 orbit decomposition).

*Restricting to $\langle P_{56}\rangle$ (order $2$), the $126$ non-associative triples decompose into $98$ orbits: $70$ singletons (i.e. $P_{56}$-fixed triples) and $28$ doubletons. **Every orbit is $P_{56}$-coherent**: a $P_{56}$-equivariant fuse rule taking values in $\{a,b,c,L,R\}$ exists.*

**Proof.** The orbit count follows from the Burnside formula applied to the $P_{56}$-action on $\mathcal{N}$. Coherence is verified by direct check: for each $P_{56}$-doubleton $\{t,P_{56}\cdot t\}$, the bracketing pairs $\{L(t),R(t)\}$ and $\{L(P_{56}\cdot t),R(P_{56}\cdot t)\}$ satisfy $\{P_{56}(L(t)),P_{56}(R(t))\}=\{L(P_{56}\cdot t),R(P_{56}\cdot t)\}$ as multisets. Verification: `papers/wp112_p56_canonical_fuse/verification/p56_canonical_fuse.py`. $\square$

## Theorem 3 (genericity).

*Among 8 surveyed canonical rule families (harmony-pull, anti-harmony, middle-determined, left-bracketing, right-bracketing, σ-fixed-preference, doubly-invariant-preference, attractor-4-core preference), **all 8 are $P_{56}$-equivariant** and **none are $\sigma^3$-equivariant**. The maximal preservable symmetry on the operad-DOF is $\langle P_{56}\rangle$.*

**Proof.** Direct enumeration on each rule family across all $126$ non-associative triples; verification in `rule_families.py`. $\square$

## Theorem 4 (canonical adoption).

*Among the 8 families, exactly one has fuse-value range contained in the 4-core $\{V,H,Br,R\}=\{0,7,8,9\}$: Family H (attractor-4-core preference). Family H produces fuse-value distribution $\{0\colon 108,\;7\colon 18\}$, concentrated on $\{$VOID, HARMONY$\}$.*

**Proof.** Per `rule_families.py` §"survey." Family H selects, for each triple, the bracketing whose value lies inside the 4-core; ties broken toward HARMONY. The output histogram is computed directly. $\square$

## Theorem 5 ($\sigma^3$ obstruction localization).

*Adopting Family H as the canonical fuse rule, the $\sigma^3$-equivariance failure localizes to **exactly one triple**: $\mathrm{fuse}(3,9,9)=7\ne\sigma^3(7)=4$. Family H is $\sigma^3$-equivariant on $125/126=99.2\%$ of $\mathcal{N}$. The single failure occurs at the unique σ³-fixed triple in $\mathcal{N}$ where neither $L$ nor $R$ equals $\sigma^3$-target $4$ inside the 4-core.*

**Proof.** Direct check on each $\sigma^3$-orbit; the only σ³-fixed-triple residual is $(3,9,9)$. $\square$

## Theorem 5.5 (4-core arity-3 closure; lens-invariant).

*All $4^3=64$ triples in the cube $\{V,H,Br,R\}^3$ fuse under Family H into values in $\{V,H,Br,R\}$. The 4-core is closed under the canonical ternary fuse.*

**Proof.** Direct enumeration. The 4-core sub-magma is lens-invariant (TSML_RAW and TSML_SYM agree on $\{V,H,Br,R\}$), so this closure is lens-invariant. $\square$

## Theorem 5.7 (universal HARMONY attractor; lens-invariant).

*Define the ternary iteration $p_{n+1} = \mathrm{fuse}(p_n,p_n,p_n) = (p_n,p_n,p_n)\mapsto \mathrm{Family\ H}(p_n,p_n,p_n)$. Every non-trivial initial probability vector on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ converges to $\delta_7$ in $1$ to $7$ iterations.*

**Proof.** Verified in `papers/wp112_p56_canonical_fuse/verification/p56_canonical_fuse.py` §5. $\square$
