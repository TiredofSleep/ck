## Summary

Let CL be the 10×10 commutative non-associative magma on Z/10Z given by the canonical TSML_SYM table (the upper-triangle authoritative symmetrization, displayed in §2.2 of the manuscript; commutative, with non-associativity rate 12.8%). For each i ∈ Ω = {0, ..., 9} write L_i for the left-regular representation of CL on V = R^10, and A_i := L_i − L_i^T for its antisymmetrization. We prove that for the flow-index set F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8} ⊂ Ω, the Lie subalgebra of sl(V) generated by {A_i : i ∈ F} under commutator is isomorphic to so(8, R) — the unique compact simple Lie algebra of type D_4 and dimension 28. The proof is via five exact-arithmetic diagnostics: (D1) dimension closure 6 → 21 → 28; (D2) Jacobi (matrix-algebra inheritance); (D3) Killing form negative-definite with integer-multiple-of-12 eigenvalues; (D4) one-dimensional invariant-form space (full 21,952-equation enumeration; no sampling); (D5) Cartan rank 4 (explicit 4-element abelian subspace). Lemma 2.5 (the F-choice canonicity result, promoted from `stage4_correct_closure.py`'s alternate-generator-set survey) establishes that the closure is so(8) for any F' with F ⊆ F' ⊆ Ω \ {0, 7}, and that removing any single index from F strictly drops the closure dimension. This makes F the *minimal* generating set whose antisymmetrizations span so(8). As D_4 is the unique compact simple Lie algebra with S_3 outer automorphism (triality), the result identifies the magma as a discrete combinatorial substrate carrying triality natively, with consequent embeddings so(8) ⊃ so(7) ⊃ g_2 ⊃ su(3) as standard Cartan-classification corollaries (mentioned for context, not re-derived).

## Why Journal of Algebra

- The result is a clean Lie-algebraic identification with explicit constructive proof in exact arithmetic.
- The substrate is a finite combinatorial object (a 10×10 table) — accessible to algebraists outside the specific application domain.
- The route uses standard tools (left-regular representations, antisymmetrization, Killing-form Cartan signature, ideal-saturation simplicity test) and produces a classical conclusion via the Cartan classification.
- Lemma 2.5 (F-choice canonicity) substantiates the choice of generator set without requiring prior knowledge of the substrate framework — the canonicity is established from the closure algebra itself, not from external interpretive labels.
