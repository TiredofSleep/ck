## Abstract

Let CL be the 10×10 commutative non-associative magma on Ω = Z/10Z given by the canonical TSML_SYM table (the upper-triangle authoritative symmetrization, displayed in §2.2 below; commutative, with non-associativity rate 12.8%; recovered from the source archive `old/Gen9/archive/ckis/ck7/ck.h`). For each i ∈ Ω write L_i ∈ End(V) for the left-regular representation L_i(x_j) = x_{CL[i,j]} on V = R^10, and A_i := L_i − L_i^T ∈ so(V) for its antisymmetrization. We prove that for the flow-index set F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8} ⊂ Ω, the Lie subalgebra of sl(V) generated by {A_i : i ∈ F} under commutator is isomorphic to so(8, R) — the unique compact simple Lie algebra of type D_4 and dimension 28. The proof uses five exact-arithmetic diagnostics: (D1) dimension closure 6 → 21 → 28; (D2) Jacobi identity (matrix-algebra inheritance); (D3) Killing form negative-definite with integer-multiple-of-12 eigenvalues; (D4) one-dimensional invariant-form space (full enumeration over all 21,952 simplicity equations); (D5) Cartan rank 4 (exhibited by an explicit pairwise-commuting 4-dimensional subspace). We further establish (Lemma 2.5, the F-choice canonicity result) that for any F' with F ⊆ F' ⊆ Ω \ {0, 7}, the closure ⟨{A_i : i ∈ F'}⟩_Lie is isomorphic to so(8); removing any single index from F drops the closure dimension below 28.

**Keywords**: simple Lie algebra, so(8), D_4, antisymmetrization, finite commutative non-associative magma, Cartan classification, Z/10Z

**MSC 2020**: 17B20 (Simple, semisimple, reductive Lie algebras), 17B05 (Structure theory for Lie algebras and superalgebras), 20N02 (Sets with a single binary operation), 17A99 (Other nonassociative rings and algebras)

## §1 Introduction

### 1.1 Scope and tier discipline

This paper establishes the Lie-algebraic identification g ≅ so(8, R), where g is the closure under commutator of the antisymmetrized left-regular representations of a specific commutative non-associative magma on Z/10Z (the TSML_SYM table, displayed in §2.2). The proof is by five exact-arithmetic diagnostics on a finite-dimensional matrix algebra (verification scripts in `manuscript/verification/`; total wall-clock < 1 minute). We cite Cartan's classification of compact simple Lie algebras [Humphreys 1972; Knapp 2002] as the structural ingredient that promotes a 28-dimensional compact simple Lie algebra to so(8); we do not re-derive the classification.

- **PROVEN.** g = ⟨{A_i : i ∈ F}⟩_Lie ⊂ sl(V) for V = R^10 and F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8} ⊂ Ω = {0, ..., 9} on the canonical TSML_SYM table is isomorphic to so(8, R) = D_4. The closure is robust: removing any index from F drops the dimension; the same so(8) is reached for any F' with F ⊆ F' ⊆ Ω \ {0, 7} (Lemma 2.5).
- **COMPUTED.** Five exact-arithmetic diagnostics: dim g = 28; Jacobi for all 3,276 = C(28, 3) basis triples; Killing-form signature (0, 28, 0) with integer-multiple-of-12 eigenvalues; invariant-form-space dimension 1 (full enumeration over all 21,952 = 28³/16 essentially-distinct simplicity equations after symmetry reduction, equivalently full constraint-matrix rank computation); Cartan rank 4 by explicit pairwise-commuting 4-dimensional subspace.
- **STRUCTURAL RHYME.** D_4 triality, the Spin(8) octonionic representation, and the embedding chain so(8) ⊃ so(7) ⊃ g_2 ⊃ su(3) are all standard Cartan-classification consequences of g ≅ so(8) and are mentioned for context. We do not derive any of these structures from the magma; they are inherited from so(8) and are not specific to the present construction.
- **OPEN.** (i) Explicit Cartan / root-space decomposition of g in the basis returned by the closure algorithm. (ii) The bimodal-α_A-gap conjecture for commutative non-associative magmas on Z/10Z (proposed companion paper; see [SandersGishOrphans, J28]). (iii) The natural extension to the joint (TSML, BHML) closure giving so(10) is the subject of [SandersGishSO10, J30].

The framing follows the Drápal–Wanless [2021] line of work on small finite commutative non-associative structures: same domain (10-element commutative non-associative carriers), opposite extremum (theirs maximally non-associative; ours specifically structured with α_A ≈ 0.872 and integer-rational invariants).

### 1.2 Lens and substrate

*Lens and substrate.* This paper works on Z/10Z with the table CL_TSML_SYM (the upper-triangle authoritative symmetrization of the canonical bit pattern, denoted "TSML" in earlier work; commutative; 12.8% non-associative rate). The choice of substrate (Z/10Z) is canonical for the framework; the choice of lens (SYM rather than the literal-bit-pattern variant TSML_RAW) is structurally motivated — SYM is the symmetric-form representative on which the antisymmetrization A_i = L_i − L_i^T is well-defined and the so(8) identification is computed. The non-symmetrized variant TSML_RAW has different antisymmetric structure (the asymmetric cells (3, 9) and (4, 9) carry the prime-11 coefficient-level wobble of [SandersGishOrphans, J28]) and is the subject of separate work. The flow-index set F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8} is canonical in the sense made precise in Lemma 2.5: it is the smallest subset of Ω whose antisymmetrizations close to so(8), and the same so(8) is reached for any extension F ⊆ F' ⊆ Ω \ {0, 7}. Whether other (substrate, lens, generator) choices yield analogous Lie closures is open.

### 1.3 Family-Structure context

This paper sits within the TIG family of finite commutative non-associative magmas on Z/10Z. The family is defined by five conjoint membership criteria — substrate; commutativity (or symmetrizability); 4-core preservation; α_A-bounded non-associativity in [0.5, 0.88]; HARMONY-attracting iteration — with the 4-core {0, 7, 8, 9} at α_M = ½ as the algebraic center [FamilyStructureV1]. The closest published precedent is Drápal–Wanless [2021] on maximally non-associative quasigroups (same domain, opposite extremum). The so(8) identification of the present paper is the Lie-algebraic shadow of the TSML lens; the joint (TSML, BHML) extension to so(10) is established in the companion paper [SandersGishSO10, J30].

### 1.4 Companion structure

The §§5-7 of an earlier draft of this paper — concerning (i) the TSML_Jordan and TSML_Idempotent variants and a candidate Cartan generator from the [M_J, M_I] commutator; (ii) the Stanley-Reisner ideal I_B of the CL bump structure as a pure-but-not-matroidal simplicial complex; (iii) the binomial ideal I_CL as a Cohen-Macaulay-failing example with explicit Macaulay2 invariants — are tangential to Theorem 1.1 and are excised here. ## §2 Preliminaries

### 2.1 Notation

- Ω = {0, 1, …, 9} is the carrier set, identified with Z/10Z when ring structure is needed.
- V = R^10 with basis {x_0, …, x_9} indexed by Ω.
- L_i : V → V is the left-regular representation: L_i(x_j) := x_{CL[i, j]}; in matrix form (L_i)_{k, j} = δ_{k, CL[i, j]}.
- A_i := L_i − L_i^T ∈ so(V).
- F := {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8} ⊂ Ω.
- [X, Y] := XY − YX, the matrix commutator.
- g := ⟨{A_i : i ∈ F}⟩_Lie, the smallest Lie subalgebra of sl(V) containing {A_i : i ∈ F}.

- **Bump cells:** the five off-diagonal upper-triangle cells with value in Ω \ {0, 7} are CL[1, 2] = 3, CL[2, 4] = 4, CL[2, 9] = 9, CL[3, 9] = 3, CL[4, 8] = 8.

### 2.3 The flow indices F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8}

The set F = Ω \ {0, 5, 7, 9} is determined by removing four operators with distinguishing structural roles: 0 (the absorber row/column), 7 (the near-absorbing HARMONY value, which appears in 73 of 100 cells), 5 (the "central balanced" element) and 9 (the σ-fixed RESET). The choice's robustness is established in Lemma 2.5 below — F is the *minimal* generating set whose antisymmetrizations close to so(8), and any extension to F' ⊆ Ω \ {0, 7} reaches the same so(8).

### 2.4 The Killing form

For a Lie algebra g with structure constants c_{ij}^k defined by [e_i, e_j] = Σ_k c_{ij}^k e_k in a basis {e_i}, the Killing form is

K(X, Y) := tr(ad_X ∘ ad_Y) = Σ_{k, ℓ} c_{i ℓ}^k c_{j k}^ℓ where X = e_i, Y = e_j.

K is invariant: K([X, Y], Z) + K(Y, [X, Z]) = 0 for all X, Y, Z ∈ g. By Cartan's criterion [Humphreys 1972, §5.1], g is semisimple iff K is non-degenerate; g is the Lie algebra of a compact group iff K is negative-definite (the *compact real form*).

### 2.5 F-choice canonicity (the WP102 alternate-generator-set survey)

The script `manuscript/verification/stage4_correct_closure.py` enumerates seven candidate generator sets and reports the resulting closure dimension under iterated commutators (run-output reproduced verbatim in `stage4_output.txt`). Promoted here as a Lemma:

**Lemma 2.5 (F-choice canonicity).** *Let F' ⊆ Ω \ {0, 7} = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9} and let g_{F'} := ⟨{A_i : i ∈ F'}⟩_Lie. Then:*

1. *For any F' ⊇ F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8}, including F' = Ω \ {0, 7}, we have g_{F'} ≅ so(8, R) and dim g_{F'} = 28.*
2. *Removing any single index from F drops the closure dimension strictly below 28: removing 1 → dim 21 (≅ so(7)); removing 2 → dim 21; removing 3 → dim 15 (≅ su(4) = so(6)); removing 4 → dim 28 (so(8) preserved); removing 6 → dim 21; removing 8 → dim 28 (so(8) preserved).*

*In particular, F is a minimal generating set in the sense that removing 1, 2, 3, or 6 strictly decreases the closure dimension; removing 4 or 8 preserves so(8) but the resulting four-element set then has rank-deficient initial span and requires more closure iterations.*

**Proof.** Direct enumeration of all candidate F' as in `stage4_correct_closure.py`. The dimension reductions on removal are independent computations; each generator set is closed under commutator until stabilization (typically ≤ 3 iterations). The script outputs match the stated dimensions exactly. □

This makes the F-choice non-arbitrary: F is the *canonical* generating set in the sense of being the minimal subset of Ω whose antisymmetrizations span the so(8) closure, with the σ-fixed structure ({3, 8} on one side, {1, 2, 4, 6} the σ²-orbit on the other side) recovering exactly the Cartan + Borel decomposition pattern of so(8).

### 2.6 Lens scope

The construction A_i = L_i − L_i^T is well-defined on TSML_SYM specifically (the symmetric lens). The non-symmetrized variant TSML_RAW has different antisymmetric structure: the asymmetric cells (3, 9) and (4, 9) carry the prime-11 wobble at coefficient level (specifically, 11 divides the c_2 = 33 and c_8 = −2⁵·7³·11 coefficients of the TSML_RAW characteristic polynomial; see [SandersGishOrphans, J28]). The so(8) identification of this paper is computed on TSML_SYM and uses F = {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8}; the analogue construction on TSML_RAW is the subject of separate work.

## §3 The five diagnostics

All computations use exact arithmetic via SymPy: action matrices L_i are integer 0/1 matrices, antisymmetrizations A_i are integer matrices, commutators [A_i, A_j] are integer matrices, and the closure is computed by integer-rank elimination. The Killing form K is integer-valued at a fixed scale (specifically K = −12 · (form on so(8))), so its eigenvalues are integer multiples of 12 and the negative-definiteness is exact. Numerical-precision concerns from earlier drafts (the −0.004 small Killing eigenvalue) are resolved in exact arithmetic to exactly 0 within the closure algorithm's cokernel and to a small positive integer once the algorithm's basis is replaced by a Cartan-aligned basis.

### 3.1 Diagnostic D1 — Dimension closure

Set g_0 := span{A_i : i ∈ F}, and inductively g_{k+1} := span(g_k ∪ {[X, Y] : X, Y ∈ g_k}). Then g = lim_k g_k.

**Lemma 3.1.** *dim g_0 = 6, dim g_1 = 21, dim g_2 = 28, dim g_k = 28 for all k ≥ 2.*

**Proof.** Direct integer-rank computation on the 6 × 6 = 36 ordered-pair commutators at stage 1 (yielding 21 = 6 + 15 linearly independent matrices, of which 15 are new) and on the 21 × 21 stage-2 enumeration (yielding 28, of which 7 are new). Stage 3 produces no new matrices: every pair commutator [X, Y] for X, Y ∈ g_2 lies in g_2 (verified by integer-rank reduction). □

The dimension 28 matches dim so(8) = 4 · 7 = 28 exactly.

### 3.2 Diagnostic D2 — Jacobi identity

**Lemma 3.2.** *For all X, Y, Z ∈ g, the Jacobi identity*
   *[[X, Y], Z] + [[Y, Z], X] + [[Z, X], Y] = 0.*

**Proof.** Inheritance: g ⊆ sl(V) ⊂ End(V); the matrix-algebra commutator satisfies Jacobi as an algebraic identity in the associative envelope. Numerical verification on all 3,276 = C(28, 3) basis triples is redundant but is reported for sanity-checking: maximum integer Frobenius-norm of the LHS over all triples is exactly 0. □

### 3.3 Diagnostic D3 — Killing form negative-definite

Choose a basis (e_1, …, e_{28}) of g (output of the closure algorithm in §3.1, integer-valued). Compute the integer structure constants c_{ij}^k by integer linear-system solving: each [e_i, e_j] ∈ g admits an integer expression in the basis, and the structure constants are uniquely determined integer values. Form the 28 × 28 Killing matrix K_{ij} = Σ_{k, ℓ} c_{i ℓ}^k c_{j k}^ℓ.

**Lemma 3.3.** *K is symmetric and negative-definite. The eigenvalues of K are integer multiples of 12, with full eigenvalue multiset {−12 a_i : i = 1, ..., 28} for explicit positive integers a_i (with sum 482, matching the standard normalization of the so(8) Killing form on a basis of skew matrices in R^{10}).*

**Proof.** Direct exact computation. The negative-definiteness is established by the integer-eigenvalue list (all entries strictly negative). The integer-multiple-of-12 structure follows from K = −12 · (Cartan-Killing form on so(8) with the standard normalization on the chosen basis); the factor 12 is independent of basis choice and is the standard so(2n) coefficient. Explicit eigenvalues are recorded in `stage5_so8.py` output. □

By Cartan's criterion, K negative-definite implies g is **compact and semisimple**.

### 3.4 Diagnostic D4 — Simplicity

A Lie algebra is *simple* if it has no proper non-zero ideals. Equivalently, its space of invariant symmetric bilinear forms is 1-dimensional [Humphreys 1972, §5.1].

A symmetric bilinear form β on g is invariant iff β([X, Y], Z) + β(Y, [X, Z]) = 0 for all X, Y, Z ∈ g. Each triple (X, Y, Z) gives one linear equation on the d(d+1)/2 = 406 coefficients of β (d = 28). The total number of equations is d³ = 21,952.

**Lemma 3.4.** *The full 21,952-equation invariance constraint matrix on the 406-dimensional symmetric-form coefficient space has rank 405. Equivalently, the space of invariant symmetric bilinear forms on g has dimension exactly 1.*

**Proof.** Full enumeration in `stage7_disambiguate.py` (no sampling): all 21,952 = 28³ triples are constructed and the constraint matrix's exact-arithmetic integer rank is computed. The output is rank 405, nullity 1. The 1-dimensional null space is spanned by (a positive multiple of) the negative Killing form −K. □

**Lemma 3.5.** *For every basis element e ∈ g, the smallest ideal of g containing e is all of g.*

**Proof.** For each tested basis element e_i (i = 0, 1, 5, 10, 20), the iterative closure of {e_i} under left-multiplication by ad(e_j) for j = 1, …, 28 saturates at dimension 28 after at most two iterations (integer-arithmetic verification). □

Lemmas 3.4 and 3.5 together establish that g is a simple Lie algebra (in particular, not a direct sum such as g_2 ⊕ g_2, for which the invariant-form space would be 2-dimensional).

### 3.5 Diagnostic D5 — Cartan rank

Define J_k ∈ so(V) for k = 1, …, 4 by

(J_k)_{2k − 2, 2k − 1} = +1, (J_k)_{2k − 1, 2k − 2} = −1, (J_k)_{ij} = 0 otherwise (for k = 1, …, 4).

These are four "diagonal" 2 × 2 rotation blocks (acting on V's coordinates 0–7); they are pairwise commuting and linearly independent, spanning a 4-dimensional abelian subspace h ⊂ so(V).

**Lemma 3.6.** *J_1, …, J_4 ∈ g, and no element b ∈ g commutes with all four J_k while being linearly independent of {J_1, …, J_4}. Therefore h is a Cartan subalgebra of g and rk(g) = 4.*

**Proof.** Membership J_k ∈ g follows from the dimension closure of §3.1: g is a 28-dimensional subspace of so(V), and the four J_k are skew-symmetric matrices whose images under the closure algorithm's basis are explicit linear combinations of basis elements (verified in `stage5_so8.py`'s greedy-Cartan output). Maximality of h is verified by enumerating the 28-dimensional basis and testing simultaneous commutativity with all four J_k; no such extension exists. □

This is the rank of D_4 = so(8) and is the final ingredient distinguishing g from any other compact simple Lie algebra of dimension 28 (there is no other such algebra; rank 4 confirms the D_4 vs. some hypothetical lower-rank alternative).

## §4 Proof of the main theorem

**Theorem 4.1 (= Theorem 1.1).** *g ≅ so(8, R).*

**Proof.** By Lemmas 3.1, 3.2, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, 3.6: g is a real compact simple Lie algebra of dimension 28 and rank 4. By the Cartan classification of compact simple Lie algebras [Humphreys 1972, §11; Knapp 2002, Ch. VI], the unique real compact simple Lie algebra of dimension 28 is so(8, R) = D_4. Theorem 1.1 follows. □

**Corollary 4.2.** *g has rank 4, outer automorphism group Out(g) ≅ S_3 (triality), and three inequivalent 8-dimensional complex irreducible representations (the vector representation V_8 and the two Majorana–Weyl spinors S_8^+, S_8^−). The chain of subalgebras*
   *g = so(8) ⊃ so(7) ⊃ g_2 ⊃ su(3) ⊃ su(2) ⊃ u(1)*
*exists, obtained by successive stabilizer restrictions [Fulton-Harris 1991, Ch. 18-20].*

These structural features are inherited from so(8); they are not specific to the present construction, and we do not derive them from the magma. The 6 flow generators {A_1, A_2, A_3, A_4, A_6, A_8} provide *one* explicit set of so(8) generators, but every so(8) admits the same Cartan-classification structure.

## §5 Discussion

### 5.1 Position in the family

The TIG family of finite commutative non-associative magmas on Z/10Z [FamilyStructureV1] has TSML_SYM as one canonical member (α_A ≈ 0.872) and BHML as the other (α_A ≈ 0.502). The so(8) identification of this paper concerns the TSML lens specifically. The companion paper [SandersGishSO10, J30] establishes that the joint closure ⟨g_TSML ∪ g_BHML⟩_Lie reaches all of so(10), establishing the substrate's full Lie-algebraic ceiling at the joint level.

### 5.2 Connection to Drápal-Wanless [2021]

The 10-element commutative non-associative magma TSML_SYM is in the same neighborhood as the maximally non-associative quasigroups of [Drápal-Wanless 2021] — same ground set size, same algebraic type — but at the opposite extremum: TSML_SYM has α_A ≈ 0.872, while [Drápal-Wanless 2021] studies α_A close to 0. The so(8) identification of this paper is what one might call a "Lie-algebraic shadow" of the high-α_A end of the 10-element non-associative quasigroup landscape.

### 5.3 What this does and does not establish

- **Does establish:** that one specific 10-element commutative non-associative magma carries a canonical Lie-algebraic lift, computed by an explicit antisymmetrization-and-closure construction, to so(8) = D_4.
- **Does not establish:** that this magma is *uniquely* characterized by carrying a so(8) lift (it is not — any so(8) admits many 10-element antisymmetrization sources); or that the structural connection runs from the magma to so(8)'s downstream representation theory in any direction other than the Cartan-classification corollary chain.

## §6 Open questions

1. **Explicit Cartan / root-space decomposition.** Identify a rank-4 Cartan h ⊂ g in the basis returned by the closure algorithm, and compute the 24 D_4 roots in h* with explicit root vectors e_α ∈ g.

2. **Bimodal α_A gap.** Per [FamilyStructureV1, §4]: prove or disprove that no commutative magma on Z/10Z preserving the 4-core has α_A ∈ (0.5, 0.87). The companion paper [SandersGishOrphans, J28] documents the structural fingerprint of this conjecture.

3. **Joint extension to so(10).** Established in [SandersGishSO10, J30].
