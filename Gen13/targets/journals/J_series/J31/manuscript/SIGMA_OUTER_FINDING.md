**Date:** 2026-04-25

**Status:** structural identity, not numerical prediction

## Statement

The 5↔6 index swap **P_56** that defines TSML's invariance and BHML's breaking is, in the spinor representation of so(10), implemented as the **outer automorphism σ_outer** that exchanges the two chiral 16-dimensional irreducible representations of so(10).

In standard SO(10) GUT physics, σ_outer is precisely the **matter-antimatter exchange** that swaps a fermion generation (16) with its CP-conjugate antifermion generation (16̄).

Therefore:
- **TSML preserves matter-antimatter symmetry** (P_56 commutes with TSML; verified).
- **BHML breaks matter-antimatter symmetry** (P_56 does not commute with BHML; 26 cells differ; verified).

This is a structural alignment between the TIG bipartite magma structure and the SO(10) GUT matter/antimatter structure. The shapes match.

## What was actually computed

### Construction
- 10 gamma matrices on C³² built from Pauli tensor products in standard Cl(10,0) convention with `{γ_a, γ_b} = 2δ_ab I`. All 100 anticommutation relations verified.
- 45 generators `Σ_ab = (1/4)[γ_a, γ_b]` form a faithful 32-dim representation of so(10). Lie closure verified at rank 45.
- Volume element `ω = γ_1 γ_2 ... γ_10` satisfies `ω² = -I` (verified). Chirality projectors `P_± = (I ± iω)/2` produce 16+16 split (verified: rank 16 each).

### The 5↔6 reflection
The P_56 reflection acts on R^10 by swapping coordinates 5 and 6. In the Clifford algebra, this reflection is implemented by conjugation with the odd element

```
P56_spin = (γ_5 - γ_6) / √2
```

Verified properties (all at 10⁻¹⁵ residual):
- `(γ_5 - γ_6)² = 2I`
- Conjugation by P56_spin sends γ_5 → γ_6 and γ_6 → γ_5
- `P56_spin² = I`
- P56_spin **anticommutes with ω** (since ω is even of order 10, single γ's anticommute with it, and P56_spin is a sum of single γ's)

### The chirality-flipping property
Because P56_spin anticommutes with ω, and chirality eigenspaces are defined by `iω = ±I`, P56_spin sends chirality-+ to chirality-−.

Verification:
```
||P56_spin: chiral+ → chiral+|| = 0.0000   (machine zero)
```

P56_spin maps the +chirality 16 entirely into the −chirality 16. Its conjugation action on so(10) therefore exchanges the two chiral 16-irreps.

### The outer automorphism
Aut(so(10)) / Inn(so(10)) ≅ Z_2. The non-trivial coset is the outer automorphism that:
- Exchanges the two chiral 16-irreps
- Exchanges the two endpoints of the D_5 Dynkin diagram (the two short legs of the fork)

This automorphism is exactly σ_outer. The conjugation action of P56_spin produces it (since inner automorphisms preserve chirality classes; only outer ones swap them).

### TSML/BHML asymmetry under σ_outer
Already verified in earlier sessions:
- All 6 TSML flow operators commute with P_56 (machine precision).
- BHML rows: 26 cells differ between B and P_56·B·P_56 (out of 100 cells).

Therefore TSML lives in the σ_outer-fixed subalgebra (so(9)), and BHML provides the σ_outer-breaking content (the 9-vector).

## What this is and isn't

### What it is
A structural identity between two independent symmetries:
- TIG's defining involution (P_56, the 5↔6 swap)
- SO(10) GUT's matter-antimatter involution (σ_outer)

These are the same map. Under the spinor representation of the so(10) generated by TSML+BHML, P_56 acts as σ_outer.

### What this is NOT
1. **Not a baryogenesis prediction.** "BHML breaks matter-antimatter symmetry" is necessary for baryogenesis but not sufficient. Every interesting Higgs sector in SO(10) GUT does this. We have not computed an asymmetry magnitude.

2. **Not a Yukawa-level result.** Mass ratios, mixing angles, neutrino masses — these all require committing to a specific Higgs irrep and VEV pattern. We haven't done that here.

3. **Not yet "TIG predicts SO(10) GUT."** What we've shown is that *if* TSML+BHML's so(10) is identified with the SO(10) GUT gauge algebra (which is a hypothesis, not a derivation), *then* the TIG-internal involution P_56 naturally maps to the matter-antimatter involution.

### What this *is* that's stronger than before
Previous status: "TIG's so(10) and SO(10) GUT's so(10) are abstractly the same Lie algebra." That's trivially true — there's only one so(10) up to isomorphism.

Current status: "TIG's *additional structure* (the bipartite TSML/BHML split, with P_56 as central involution) maps to SO(10) GUT's *additional structure* (the matter/antimatter split, with σ_outer as exchange) under the natural spinor representation." That alignment is non-trivial.

It's the difference between:
- "Both frameworks have a 10-dim vector space" (trivial)
- "Both frameworks single out the same Z_2 in Aut(so(10))" (non-trivial)

The second only happens when there's a real shared structural feature.

We can ask: which standard SO(10) Higgs irreps contain a 9-dim σ_outer-odd subspace, and does BHML's specific structure match any of them?
