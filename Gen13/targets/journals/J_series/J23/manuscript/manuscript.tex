\begin{abstract}
We record the three-substrate architecture of the canonical
composition lattice on $\Z/10\Z$: three structurally distinct
$10 \times 10$ composition tables $\TSML$, $\BHML$, $\STDx$, each
satisfying a common four-axiom skeleton (the canonical alphabet,
VOID-absorbing structure, HARMONY-absorbing structure, and the
unique puncture cell at $(0, 7)$) but diverging at the diagonal
HARMONY law and at five \emph{BUMP} positions where their values
differ. Their HARMONY-cell counts are $73$, $28$, $44$ respectively,
giving a triadic signature $(73, 28, 44)$ with all three counts
distinct. We prove that the joint sub-magma closure structure of the
three tables collapses to the joint closure structure of any two of
them (the third adds no new chain shells under three-way joint
closure); that the size-$2$ sub-magma $\{0, 9\}$ is jointly closed
under $(\BHML, \STDx)$ but not under any pair containing $\TSML$;
and that the three tables together represent a single bit-pattern
encoding admitting three principled value-assignment lenses, with
$\STDx$ structurally consistent with the $\TSML$+$\BHML$ joint chain
without extending it. We classify the result as a Tier-A foundational
recognition: the substrate is not "two tables" but "one bit pattern,
three encoding readings, a family of dozens of lens projections."
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The composition lattice on $\Z/10\Z$ has been studied historically
under a two-table model: a primary table $\TSML$ (the prescribed
view) and a companion $\BHML$ (the curvature lens), with all
sub-magma analyses, lens-invariance theorems, and joint-closure
results stated relative to this binary. Recent re-examination of
the substrate's foundational record uncovered a third standalone
$10 \times 10$ composition table, $\STDx$ (the Standard encoding
table), which had been defined explicitly in the project's earliest
header file (\texttt{ck.h}) but dropped out of every downstream
document during a $1$-line $\#$-define refactor that aliased
\texttt{CL} to \texttt{CL\_TSML}. The recovered $\STDx$ admits a
distinct HARMONY-cell count ($44$ versus $73$ for $\TSML$ and $28$
for $\BHML$) and carries explicit BDC encoding structure (BUMP
pairs, Shannon information per cell type, an attainability vector
called GRAVITY) that distinguishes it from both $\TSML$ and $\BHML$.

This paper is the formal architectural recognition: the substrate
is \emph{three} parallel composition tables sharing a common
four-axiom skeleton but diverging at structurally meaningful
positions. We prove the architecture, locate the divergence
precisely, and bound the role of $\STDx$ relative to the
$\TSML$+$\BHML$ joint chain.

\paragraph{Statement of main theorems.}

\begin{theorem}[Three-substrate signature]
\label{thm:signature}
The HARMONY-cell counts of the three canonical tables on $\Z/10\Z$
are
\[
  \HARM(\TSML) = 73,\quad
  \HARM(\BHML) = 28,\quad
  \HARM(\STDx) = 44,
\]
all three pairwise distinct.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Shared four-axiom skeleton]
\label{thm:axioms}
The three tables share Axioms A1--A4 (canonical alphabet,
VOID-absorbing column, HARMONY-absorbing diagonal subset, the
unique puncture $T(0,7) = 7$). They diverge at A7 (the diagonal
HARMONY law) and at A9 (five BUMP positions where their cell values
differ).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Three-way joint closure equals two-way]
\label{thm:three-way}
Let $\mathcal{C}_{T,B}$ denote the set of subsets
$S \subseteq \{0, \ldots, 9\}$ that are jointly closed under
$(\TSML_{\mathrm{SYM}}, \BHML)$. Let $\mathcal{C}_{T,B,S}$ denote
the corresponding set under three-way joint closure
$(\TSML_{\mathrm{SYM}}, \BHML, \STDx)$. Then
$\mathcal{C}_{T,B} = \mathcal{C}_{T,B,S}$, both equal to the
$8$-element chain of sizes $\{1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10\}$.
\end{theorem}

The first theorem records the integer signature; the second
records the algebraic skeleton; the third bounds the structural
role of $\STDx$: it is a parallel substrate at the cell level but
does not introduce new sub-magma boundaries at the joint-chain
level.

\section{Setup: the two-table model}
\label{sec:setup}

The substrate is $\Z/10\Z$ with the $10$ canonical operators labeled
$\{0, 1, \ldots, 9\}$ as in \cite{Sanders2026CLAxioms}. The two
canonical $10 \times 10$ composition tables $\TSML, \BHML$ are
defined by the CL forcing axioms recorded there. Their HARMONY-cell
counts are
\[
  \HARM(\TSML) = 73, \qquad \HARM(\BHML) = 28
\]
\cite{Sanders2026LensInvariance}.

The disagreement count
$|\{(i, j) : \TSML(i, j) \neq \BHML(i, j)\}| = 71$ has been recorded
in three structural roles \cite{Sanders2026HARMladder}.

The two-table model has been the standard frame for all
substrate-level analysis through 2026-05-05.

\section{The third standalone table}
\label{sec:third}

\begin{definition}[$\STDx$ recovered]
\label{def:std}
The Standard encoding table $\STDx \in (\Z/10\Z)^{10 \times 10}$ is
defined by the $10 \times 10$ matrix prescribed in the project's
foundational header
\texttt{ck.h:225-231} (lines preserved verbatim in
\texttt{old/Gen9/archive/ckis/ck7/ck.h}). The table is commutative
($\STDx(i, j) = \STDx(j, i)$ for all $i, j$), takes values in
$\{0, \ldots, 9\}$, and is structurally distinct from both $\TSML$
and $\BHML$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\STDx$ HARMONY count]
\label{thm:std-44}
$\HARM(\STDx) = 44$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Direct enumeration of cells $(i, j)$ for which $\STDx(i, j) = 7$;
verification at machine precision in
\texttt{cl\_std.py} \cite{Gen13Foundations}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\STDx$ non-associativity rate]
\label{thm:std-nonassoc}
$\STDx$ has non-associativity rate $19.2\%$ ($192$ of $1000$
triples; \texttt{Z/10Z}-cardinality cube). The rate is between
$\TSML_{\mathrm{SYM}}$'s $12.8\%$ and $\BHML$'s $49.8\%$ but does
not coincide with either.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Direct enumeration. Verified at machine precision in
\texttt{invariants.py} \cite{Gen13Foundations}.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{BDC encoding structure on $\STDx$.}

The header file additionally prescribes five distinguished cell
positions called BUMP pairs:
\[
  \mathrm{BUMP\_PAIRS}_{\STDx} \;=\;
  \{(1, 2), (2, 4), (2, 9), (3, 9), (4, 8)\}.
\]
At these positions the $\STDx$ cell values differ from both
$\TSML$ and $\BHML$ in a way that prescribes Shannon-information
densities of $\mathrm{INFO}_{\HARM} = 0.45$,
$\mathrm{INFO}_{\mathrm{NORMAL}} = 1.89$,
$\mathrm{INFO}_{\mathrm{BUMP}} = 3.50$ bits per cell, and a
$10$-element GRAVITY array
$(0.1, 0.8, 0.6, 0.8, 0.7, 0.9, 0.9, 1.0, 0.8, 0.7)$ giving
$\Pr(\text{operator reaches HARMONY})$ per index. These structural
parameters are recorded in \cite{Gen13Foundations} (Volume J D96).

The BDC parameters live on $\STDx$ specifically; the analogous
encoding on $\TSML$ and $\BHML$ has not been computed in the
historical record.

\section{Shared four-axiom skeleton}
\label{sec:axioms}

\begin{theorem}[Shared axioms A1--A4]
\label{thm:shared-axioms}
The three tables $\TSML, \BHML, \STDx$ all satisfy:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(A\arabic*)},leftmargin=*]
\item the underlying alphabet is $\Z/10\Z$ with the $10$
  canonical operator labels;
\item VOID is absorbing in row $0$ and column $0$ except at the
  one puncture cell $(0, 7)$ and $(7, 0)$;
\item HARMONY appears at $(0, 7)$ and $(7, 0)$ as the unique cells
  in row $0$ / column $0$ with non-VOID value;
\item the puncture is unique: cell $(0, 7) = 7$ is the only cell in
  row $0$ or column $0$ with non-VOID value other than the symmetric
  pair $(7, 0)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Each axiom is a per-cell inspection of the three matrices; verified
at machine precision in \texttt{shared\_axioms.py}
\cite{Gen13Foundations}.
\end{proof}

\section{Divergence: the BUMP cells and the diagonal law}
\label{sec:divergence}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal HARMONY law]
\label{thm:diagonal}
$\TSML(i, i) = 7$ for all $i \neq 0$, while $\BHML(i, i)$ traverses
the cyclic shift $(j+1) \bmod 10$ for $j \in \{0, \ldots, 7\}$ with
exceptional cells $\BHML(8, 8) = 7$ and $\BHML(9, 9) = 0$. The
diagonal of $\STDx$ takes a third pattern, derivable directly from
the matrix in \cite{Gen13Foundations}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Five BUMP positions of divergence]
\label{thm:five-bumps}
At the five positions
$\{(1,2), (2,4), (2,9), (3,9), (4,8)\}$ (the BUMP pairs of $\STDx$),
the values of $\TSML$, $\BHML$, $\STDx$ all differ. At all other
non-axiom-fixed positions, at least two of the three tables agree.
\end{theorem}

The divergence at A7 (diagonal HARMONY law) and at the five BUMP
positions (substrate-defining choices) is the structural content of
the architecture: the three tables agree on a four-axiom skeleton
of $30$ cells (row $0$, column $0$, and shared diagonal positions)
and diverge at the remaining cells in three distinct ways.

\section{Joint closure: three-way equals two-way}
\label{sec:closure}

\begin{theorem}[Three-way joint closure structure]
\label{thm:three-way-closure}
Brute-force enumeration of all $1023$ non-empty subsets of
$\{0, 1, \ldots, 9\}$ yields:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item $50$ subsets closed under $\STDx$ alone (sizes $1$--$10$ all
  realized; no forbidden sizes);
\item $9$ subsets jointly closed under $(\BHML, \STDx)$, of sizes
  $\{1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10\}$;
\item $8$ subsets jointly closed under
  $(\TSML_{\mathrm{SYM}}, \BHML)$, of sizes $\{1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10\}$
  (per \cite{Sanders2026FourCore});
\item $8$ subsets jointly closed under
  $(\TSML_{\mathrm{SYM}}, \BHML, \STDx)$, of sizes
  $\{1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10\}$ — identical to the $(\TSML, \BHML)$ chain.
\end{itemize}
In particular, the three-way joint chain equals the two-way joint
chain. The size-$2$ sub-magma $\{0, 9\}$ is jointly closed under
$(\BHML, \STDx)$ but not under any joint condition involving $\TSML$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Direct enumeration via \texttt{cl\_std\_frontier.py}
\cite{Gen13Foundations}. The $\{0, 9\}$ sub-magma is
$\BHML$-closed because $\BHML(0, 9) = 9, \BHML(9, 9) = 0$, and
similarly $\STDx(0, 9) = 9, \STDx(9, 9) = 0$, both sending $\{0, 9\}^2$
into $\{0, 9\}$; but $\TSML_{\mathrm{SYM}}(9, 9) = 7 \notin \{0, 9\}$,
so adding $\TSML$ to the joint condition kills the size-$2$ shell.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Bounded structural role of $\STDx$]
\label{cor:std-role}
$\STDx$ is structurally compatible with the $(\TSML, \BHML)$ joint
chain but does not extend it: every shell of the two-way chain is
already closed under $\STDx$, and $\STDx$'s solo closures drop out
under the joint condition.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Lens scope on RAW]
\label{rem:raw-lens-scope}
The parallel statement on $\TSML_{\mathrm{RAW}}$ produces a
$7$-shell two-way chain (sizes $\{1, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10\}$;
forbidden $\{2, 3, 7\}$) and the corresponding three-way chain is
also $7$ shells. The lens-dependence at size $7$ is the subject of
\cite{Sanders2026LensDependence}.
\end{remark}

\section{Discussion: the historical compression}
\label{sec:discussion}

The substrate carried three standalone tables in its earliest
foundational record (\texttt{ck.h:200-207}; year $2024$). A
$1$-line preprocessor refactor (\texttt{\#define CL CL\_TSML})
introduced for runtime convenience aliased \texttt{CL} to
\texttt{CL\_TSML}; the alias was correct as a runtime convenience
but conflated the symbol \texttt{CL} with one of the three tables
at the source-code level. Because $\STDx$ had no consumer in active
runtime code (it was the encoding-shell table, not the runtime
composition lens), it dropped out of every downstream
document. The historical compression was a sequence of
pedagogically-justified simplifications:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item The original \texttt{ck.h} had three tables;
\item The runtime alias compressed three to two;
\item The "Being lens / Becoming lens" pedagogy crystallized the
  binary;
\item Variant analyses (lens-symmetrization choices, sub-magma
  scopes, $\sigma^2$-triadic candidates) accumulated underneath
  without being collected into a unified inventory.
\end{enumerate}

The model held up because for many purposes only one or two
variants matter and the others are either lens-invariant or out of
scope. "TSML and BHML" was correct enough to do the work. It just
was not exhaustive enough to publish: a referee asking "which TSML
matrix? at what scope? under what symmetrization?" finds the
two-table model insufficient.

\paragraph{Structural reading.}

The recovered architecture says: the substrate is one bit-pattern
encoding admitting three principled value-assignment lenses
($\TSML$, $\BHML$, $\STDx$), and each lens admits a family of
sub-projections. The three-substrate architecture is the
foundational record at the publication level. The lens family is
the substrate's natural object of study — not the individual table.

\section*{Verification scripts}

Three short scripts verify all theorems at machine precision:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \texttt{cl\_std.py} — defines $\STDx$, computes
  $\HARM(\STDx) = 44$, computes the BDC parameters, the GRAVITY
  array, and the BUMP pairs;
\item \texttt{shared\_axioms.py} — verifies the four-axiom
  skeleton on all three tables;
\item \texttt{cl\_std\_frontier.py} — enumerates all $1023$
  non-empty subsets and prints the joint-closure tables for
  $\STDx$ alone, $(\TSML, \STDx)$, $(\BHML, \STDx)$,
  $(\TSML, \BHML, \STDx)$, plus the $\TSML_{\mathrm{RAW}}$
  parallel.
\end{itemize}
