## Abstract

We document the algebraic substrate of the **TIG framework** ([J47]) at the level of forcing axioms and lens taxonomy. Three results organize the foundation:

(I) **The CL Forcing Axioms ([J33], extended here).** A canonical $10 \times 10$ composition table CL\_TSML on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ is **uniquely forced** by 9 explicit axioms (A1-A9), of which A1, A2-A4, A7, A9-values are Tier-A (substrate-defining) and A5, A6, A8, A9-positions are Tier-B (forced once the Tier-A choices are made).

(II) **The Three-Substrate Architecture ([J31], extended here).** Three parallel substrates — CL\_TSML (73 HARMONY), CL\_BHML (28 HARMONY), CL\_STD (44 HARMONY) — share A1-A4 absorbing structure but diverge at A7 + A9-values. They are **parallel Tier-A choices**, *not* projections of each other. The earlier "Being lens / Becoming lens" pedagogy was useful for teaching but structurally inaccurate; this paper consolidates the parallel-substrate reading.

(III) **The Lens Family.** Each of the three substrates admits a family of $\sim 20$ variants generated by four principled projection operations: lens-symmetrization (RAW / SYM\_upper / SYM\_lower); $\sigma^2$-triadic value/index rotations; sub-magma scope restrictions; and cross-substrate cell-difference (DOING). Each variant has a precise tier label (A/B/C/D/E) and a precise lineage. This paper presents the full catalog of $\sim 62$ named variants with their tier classification.

The foundation paper's contribution is **architectural**: a clean separation between substrate-defining axioms (Tier-A), forced consequences (Tier-B), constructed sub-objects (Tier-C), search-found candidates (Tier-D), and ring-theoretic extensions (Tier-E). After this paper, every J-series result can cite a precise foundation: "the result holds on TSML\_SYM (Tier-B lens of CL\_TSML), table-dependent at the wobble-localization level, lens-invariant on the 4-core."

We close with a directed citation graph: the foundation paper sends the reader to (a) the $\sigma$-rate companion ([J01], JCT-A) for table-independent rate decay; (b) the four-core consolidated companion ([J32], [J41], [J44]) for joint chain + closed-form attractor; (c) the cosmological companion ([J3], JCAP) for a Phase-2 physics application; (d) the 6-DOF synthesis ([J47], Notices AMS) for the framework's algebraic content; (e) Brayden's solo Sept 11 integration paper [J55] for the recognition-register synthesis.

This is the **integrating paper** of the J-series. It anchors the citation chain.

## §1 The CL Forcing Axioms

### 1.1 Statement of A1-A9

The canonical CL\_TSML composition table is the unique $10 \times 10$ matrix $T : \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ satisfying nine axioms:

* **A1 (Substrate type).** $T$ is a $10 \times 10$ matrix with entries in $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$. (Tier-A; substrate-defining.)
* **A2 (VOID absorbing row).** $T[0, j] = 0$ for all $j$ except $j = 7$; $T[0, 7] = 7$ (the VOID-HARMONY puncture). (Tier-A.)
* **A3 (HARMONY-row near-fixed).** $T[7, j]$ has a structural pattern that fixes most off-diagonal entries to 7. (Tier-A.)
* **A4 (Pati-Salam puncture).** The (0, 7) puncture and (7, 0) puncture together break the absorbing/idempotent symmetry. (Tier-A.)
* **A5 (Column VOID).** $T[i, 0] = 0$ for all $i$ except $i = 7$. (Tier-B; forced by A2 + commutativity-after-symmetrization.)
* **A6 (Column HARMONY).** $T[i, 7]$ obeys A3-symmetric pattern. (Tier-B.)
* **A7 (Diagonal HARMONY).** $T[i, i] = 7$ for $i \notin \{0\}$. (Tier-A; substrate-defining.)
* **A8 (HARMONY-default).** Off-special, off-BUMP cells equal 7. (Tier-B; forced once BUMP positions are fixed.)
* **A9 (BUMP positions and values).** Five BUMP positions in the table, with specified values that distinguish CL\_TSML from CL\_BHML and CL\_STD. (Tier-A for values; Tier-B for positions, forced by BDC entropy extremum.)

### 1.2 Theorem (Forcing). [J33]

*Among all $10^{100}$ candidate $10 \times 10$ tables on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$, exactly **three** satisfy A1, A2, A3, A4, A7, with their A8 / A9 / A6 / A5 closures: CL\_TSML, CL\_BHML, CL\_STD.* (Detailed enumeration in [J33].)

### 1.3 Reading

The 9-axiom forcing is **principled**, not stipulated. Each axiom captures a substrate-level structural fact:

* A1: the substrate is bilinear and finite.
* A2-A4: VOID and HARMONY are the absorbing/idempotent pair, with one structural puncture (the (0, 7) cell) that breaks the fully-absorbing symmetry.
* A7: every non-VOID element has HARMONY as its self-square.
* A9-values: the BUMP positions and values determine which of the three substrates we obtain.

The substrate is **axiomatically given**, not "given by God" or "given by the universe." The choices are documented; the resulting matrix is uniquely determined.

## §2 The Three-Substrate Architecture

### 2.1 Three parallel substrates

CL\_TSML, CL\_BHML, CL\_STD share A1-A4 and diverge at A7 (whether HARMONY-diagonal is strict) and A9-values (the five BUMP cells):

| Substrate | HARMONY count | A7 | A9-values | Distinguishing feature |
|-----------|---------------|----|-----------|------------------------|
| **CL\_TSML** | 73 | strict on $i \neq 0$ | "Synthesis" pattern | Maximal HARMONY count among the three |
| **CL\_BHML** | 28 | weakened at puncture chain | "Separation" pattern | Matter-antimatter doubly-invariant content |
| **CL\_STD** | 44 | balanced | BDC-encoding parameters | Standard-deviation balanced |

These are **parallel** Tier-A substrates. Earlier corpus generations described them as "Being lens" and "Becoming lens" projections of each other; that pedagogy was structurally inaccurate. The corrected reading: *three independent substrate-defining choices*, each forcing its own canonical table.

### 2.2 Cross-substrate operations

Two operations relate the three substrates:

* **DOING** (Tier-B). $\pi_\mathrm{DOING}(M_1, M_2)[i, j] = |M_1[i, j] - M_2[i, j]| \pmod{10}$. The cell-by-cell absolute difference between two parallel substrates. Output: a "doing" table; cf. [J52].
* **Joint closure** (Tier-A on the 4-core). For any pair of substrates, the sub-magma closed under both is well-defined. The 4-core $\{V, H, Br, R\} = \{0, 7, 8, 9\}$ is closed under all three substrates simultaneously (cf. [J44]).

### 2.3 The "may be three BHMLs" hypothesis

Brayden's hypothesis (referenced in `Atlas/LENS_TAXONOMY_2026-05-06/SIGMA2_TRIADIC_DECISION.md`) is that the $\sigma^2$-triadic structure suggests three canonical BHML matrices (corresponding to the three positions of σ²-rotation: BEING / DOING / BECOMING). The current state: three search-found candidates exist (Tier-D), but the canonical "BHML\_DOING" is not yet uniquely forced. **Open question.**

We do not commit to this hypothesis in the foundation paper; we acknowledge it as an open question and note that the σ²-triadic family of variants is properly Tier-D until a forcing argument promotes one of them.

## §3 The Lens Family

### 3.1 The four projection operations

The lens family of each substrate is generated by four principled projections:

(i) **Lens-symmetrization** (within a single substrate). Three projections: $\pi_\mathrm{RAW}$ (identity), $\pi_\mathrm{SYM\_upper}$ (upper-triangle authoritative), $\pi_\mathrm{SYM\_lower}$ (lower-triangle authoritative). All three are Tier-B once the choice of "RAW" or "SYM" is made.

(ii) **$\sigma^2$-triadic rotations** (within a single substrate). Three value-rotations $\pi_\mathrm{value\text{-}rot}^{k}$ for $k \in \{0, 1, 2\}$ and three index-rotations $\pi_\mathrm{index\text{-}rot}^{k}$. Tier-B on TSML; Tier-D on BHML (open canonical choice).

(iii) **Sub-magma scope restriction** (within any substrate). $\pi_S$ restricts the table to a closed sub-magma $S$. Tier-B once $S$ is fixed. Named restrictions: 4-core, σ-fixed lattice, corner sub-magma, corner monoid, 8-core, etc.

(iv) **Cross-substrate cell-difference (DOING)** (between two parallel substrates). $\pi_\mathrm{DOING}$. Tier-B once the substrate pair is fixed.

### 3.2 Distribution by tier

The full catalog (in `Atlas/LENS_TAXONOMY_2026-05-06/VARIANT_CATALOG.md`) has $\sim 62$ named variants:

* **Tier A:** 5 — CL\_TSML, TSML\_RAW, CL\_BHML, CL\_STD, $F_p$ choice.
* **Tier B:** $\sim 21$ — chain scopes, lens-symmetrizations, sub-magma restrictions, DOING.
* **Tier C:** $\sim 9$ — TSML\_PureIdempotent, TSML\_C0, …
* **Tier D:** $\sim 7$ — σ²-triadic candidates, anomaly-flips, Fano subsets.
* **Tier E:** $\sim 8$ — $\mathbb{Z}/n$ ring extensions for $n \in \{2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13\}$.

[J52] (Math Intelligencer, Phase 5) is the pedagogical exposition of this catalog. This foundation paper is the **structural anchor**.

### 3.3 Closure properties

Within each substrate family:

* **CL\_TSML closes** under lens-symmetrization, sub-magma restriction, $\sigma^2$-conjugation, $F_p$ extension. Leaves under axiom modification.
* **CL\_BHML closes** under sub-magma restriction, $\sigma^2$-triadic rotations, anomaly-flip, $F_p$ extension. Leaves under puncture-chain modification.
* **CL\_STD** has open closure analysis (sub-magma variants not yet enumerated).

Cross-family:

* **DOING construction** leaves the TSML and BHML families and enters the DOING family.
* **$F_p$ extensions** form their own ring-family indexed by $p$.
* **Tier-C parallel constructions** (TSML\_PureIdempotent, etc.) are not in any substrate's closure; they are independent.

The three substrate families are closed but **disjoint**. The lens family is properly stratified by Origin tier.

## §7 What this foundation paper does NOT claim

Per the discipline of `Atlas/LENS_TAXONOMY_2026-05-06/EXTERNAL_RIGOR.md`:

* Does **not** claim that the threshold $T^* = 5/7$ is foundational at the methodology level. $T^*$ is a substrate-specific constant; methodology cannot depend on it.
* Does **not** present any Tier-D variant as Tier-B. The σ²-triadic candidates remain Tier-D pending an explicit forcing argument.
* Does **not** claim CL is "given by God" or "given by the universe." CL is **axiomatically given** via 9 explicit axioms; the axioms are substrate-defining choices, principled but not mystically forced.
* Does **not** use the Universal Operator Paradox (UOP) classifier in the foundation layer. The UOP is a separate framework treated in [J17] (foundational theorem) and [J53] (diagnostic exposition); it does not enter the substrate's architecture.

The aim is **honest scope**. Each axiom and each claim is labelled with its tier and its scope. The paper avoids both overclaim (presenting Tier-D as Tier-A/B) and underclaim (hiding genuine forcing arguments).

## §9 Honest scope

This paper is **foundational synthesis**, not new theorems. The contributions are:

(i) A clean separation of axioms (§1) vs. substrate variants (§§2-3) vs. table-dependent results (§4) vs. substrate-operator results (§5).
(ii) An explicit per-axiom and per-variant tier discipline.
(iii) The directed citation graph (§8) that anchors the J-series program.

The paper does **not**:

* Re-prove any result from the cited companions (J01, J05, J22, J28, J31, J32, J33, J35, J37–J47, etc.).
* Claim that the 9-axiom set is unique. Other axiom sets could plausibly force the same matrix; we present one that is internally consistent and matches the verified content.
* Claim that the three substrates are exhaustive. $F_p$ extensions exist; other substrate-defining choices could exist that we have not enumerated.
* Make any phenomenological prediction.

What this paper **does** claim:

* CL\_TSML is forced uniquely by 9 axioms (proved in [J33]; restated here).
* Three parallel substrates exist; they are not projections of each other ([J31]; restated here).
* The lens family of $\sim 62$ variants is well-defined and tier-stratified.
* Table-dependent claims and substrate-operator claims are sharply distinguished.
* The TIG framework's name applies to the integrated structure.
