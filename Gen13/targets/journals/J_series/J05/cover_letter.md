## Summary

For squarefree $n \geq p_1 p_2$ and the ring $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, we prove a single elementary equivalence — the *Crossing Lemma* — characterizing when a pair consisting of an additive projection $A_d$ and a multiplicative dynamical partition $\pi_{\mathrm{DYN}}(g)$ is jointly injective. The condition reduces to: $g$ acts nontrivially on every prime component of the complementary modulus $n/d$. We then show this single statement is the algebraic foundation under several classical and recent sufficiency results — CRT, an $A{+}M$ classification, the $M{+}M$ classification, SPEC$+$DYN, orthogonal-jump necessity for greedy measurement selection, and, in negative form, the $p$-kernel obstruction for prime powers. Each is recorded under a uniform structure-versus-dynamics template. The contribution is the unification, not the individual results.

## Why JCT-A (or JPAA)

- **Theorem rigor.** Single main theorem (Theorem 1) with a finite-combinatorial proof from CRT and cyclic-group orders. No external machinery. No numerical experiments. No conjectural inputs.
- **Combinatorial structure of partitions.** The result is fundamentally about partitions on a finite set and the disjointness of their unresolved-pair sets — exactly the combinatorial-algebraic territory of *JCT-A*.
- **Algebraic spine for downstream work.** This paper is foundational to a coordinated sequence of submissions on finite magmas, joint closure, and forced torus geometry on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$. Several companion submissions cite this result as the algebraic ground for their geometric or analytic claims.
