## Abstract

The **TIG framework** ([J47]) has, in its corpus, a proliferation of variants of the canonical $10 \times 10$ composition table on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ — TSML, TSML\_RAW, TSML\_SYM, BHML, CL\_STD, $F_p$ extensions, $\sigma^2$-triadic rotations, sub-magma restrictions, and more. To a reader encountering the framework for the first time, the proliferation can appear bewildering: which variant is the "real" one? What are the relationships between them? Why does each result cite a specific lens?

This pedagogical exposition addresses those questions directly. We organize the **lens family** of TSML — the 12+ variants generated by the canonical $10 \times 10$ bit pattern under three principled projection operations (lens-symmetrization, $\sigma^2$-triadic rotation, sub-magma scope restriction) — into a single coherent picture. The picture has three features:

(i) **Three parallel substrates.** TSML, BHML, and CL\_STD are *parallel* Tier-A substrates, not projections of each other. Each is defined by its own substrate-defining axioms (cf. [J31] Three-Substrate Architecture, [J33] CL Forcing Axioms).

(ii) **Lens-symmetrization within each substrate.** Each substrate admits three lens-projections: RAW (the literal bit pattern, non-commutative), SYM\_upper (commutative, upper-triangle authoritative), SYM\_lower (commutative, lower-triangle authoritative). The choice between RAW and SYM\_upper is *conventional*, and the corpus uses both depending on which structural feature is being analysed.

(iii) **Lens-dependent results.** Several published or pending results depend explicitly on which lens is used. The most striking example is the **joint TSML+BHML chain at size 7**, treated in [J32]: the size-7 chain element exists in some lens combinations and not in others. This is the "lens-dependence at size 7" phenomenon that [J32] reports.

The contribution of this paper is the **organizational picture**: one diagram, one walking-tour through the 12+ variants, one pedagogical reading of why the framework needs the lens family rather than a single canonical table. We illustrate each variant with its distinguishing structural fact and its tier classification; we organize the dependency graph with a single page; we close with three reader exercises.

This paper is **expository** in the *Math Intelligencer* register: aimed at the working mathematician who wants to read the TIG framework's papers without getting lost in lens-bookkeeping.

## §1 The substrate family — three parallel substrates

The TIG framework starts with the question: what $10 \times 10$ composition tables on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ satisfy a given collection of substrate-defining axioms?

[J33] (the CL Forcing Axioms) establishes that a single canonical table — call it CL\_TSML — is **uniquely forced** by 9 axioms (A1–A9). Three of those axioms (A7, A9-values) are substrate-defining choices: changing them gives a different parallel substrate. The corpus identifies three such substrates with full structural development:

| Substrate | HARMONY count | Defining feature | First reference |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **CL\_TSML** | 73 cells | Substrate-defining A7 + A9-values for "Synthesis" | [J33], [J9] |
| **CL\_BHML** | 28 cells | Substrate-defining variant for "Separation" / matter-antimatter | [J9], [J39] |
| **CL\_STD** | 44 cells | Standard-deviation balanced; BDC encoding parameters | [J9] |

These three substrates are **parallel** Tier-A choices, *not* projections of each other. The "Being lens / Becoming lens" pedagogy of earlier corpus generations was useful for teaching but structurally inaccurate; [J31] (Three-Substrate Architecture) corrects this. Each substrate has its own structural facts; results obtained on one substrate do not automatically transfer to the others.

For the rest of this paper, we focus on **CL\_TSML**. The other two substrates have parallel lens families with the same projection structure.

## §2 The lens-symmetrization projections

The literal CL\_TSML bit pattern (the unique $10 \times 10$ matrix forced by A1–A9) has 2 asymmetric upper/lower-triangle pairs at positions $(3, 9)$ and $(4, 9)$. Three symmetrization choices give three distinct lens projections of the same underlying bit pattern.

### 2.1 $\pi_\mathrm{RAW}$ — the identity projection

The literal bit pattern, with no symmetrization. Output: **TSML\_RAW** — non-commutative; 126 non-associative triples; characteristic polynomial coefficient $c_2 = 33 = 3 \cdot 11$ (the wobble localization, [J43]). Tier-A operation.

### 2.2 $\pi_\mathrm{SYM\_upper}$ — upper-triangle authoritative

For each cell $(i, j)$ with $i > j$, replace $M[i, j]$ with $M[j, i]$. Output: **TSML\_SYM** — commutative; 128 non-associative triples; no prime 11 in $c_2$. Tier-B operation (forced once "make commutative" + "upper-tri authoritative" are chosen).

### 2.3 $\pi_\mathrm{SYM\_lower}$ — lower-triangle authoritative

Same as SYM\_upper but with the lower triangle authoritative. Output: **TSML\_LOWERTRI** — commutative; 122 non-associative triples; $c_2 = 17$, $c_8 = 0$. Tier-B operation; tested in the corpus but not promoted to a primary lens.

### 2.4 Forced or chosen?

The choice between RAW, SYM\_upper, and SYM\_lower is a **Tier-A choice**: no deeper principle in the substrate forces upper-tri over lower-tri. SYM\_upper has the structural advantage of preserving the $\sigma^2$-cycle structure cleanly (the asymmetric cells $(3, 9)$ and $(4, 9)$ get values $3$ and $7$ — both substrate-canonical), but SYM\_lower would give $7$ and $3$ — also valid, just swapping roles.

The corpus uses **TSML\_RAW** for results where non-commutativity matters (e.g., [J43] wobble localization, the prime-11 in $c_2, c_8$). It uses **TSML\_SYM** for results where the symmetric closure regenerates the $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ structure ([J38]) and for the WP100s tower's Lie-Jordan duality. The choice is annotated explicitly in each paper.

## §3 The $\sigma^2$-triadic projections

The $\sigma$-permutation on $\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ fixes the Conservation Tetrad $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ and 6-cycles the Manifestation Hexad $\{1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7\}$. The square $\sigma^2$ acts as order 3 on the Hexad with two 3-cycles: **Cycle A** $\{1, 6, 4\}$ and **Cycle B** $\{7, 5, 2\}$.

The $\sigma^2$-triadic projection family rotates values or indices through these cycles, producing three "phase" lenses:

### 3.1 Value-rotation $\pi_\mathrm{value\text{-}rot}^{k}$

$\pi_\mathrm{value\text{-}rot}^{k}(M)[i, j] = \sigma^{2k}(M[i, j])$ for $k \in \{0, 1, 2\}$. Each cell's value is replaced by $\sigma^2$ applied $k$ times. The $\sigma^2$-fixed elements (anchors) are unchanged.

### 3.2 Index-rotation $\pi_\mathrm{index\text{-}rot}^{k}$

$\pi_\mathrm{index\text{-}rot}^{k}(M)[i, j] = M[\sigma^{2k}(i), \sigma^{2k}(j)]$. Indices are rotated.

### 3.3 Reading

The triadic projections were the source of the "BEING / DOING / BECOMING" terminology in earlier corpus generations. They are **Tier-D** when applied to BHML (search-found candidates not yet promoted to a canonical "DOING") and **Tier-B** when applied to TSML (where the σ² action's relation to the runtime processor's 4-core attractor is structural). Results that depend on a specific triadic phase ($k = 1$ or $k = 2$) are explicitly scoped.

## §4 Sub-magma scope restrictions

For any closed sub-magma $S \subset \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ under TSML, define $\pi_S(M)[i, j] = M[i, j]$ for $i, j \in S$ (and undefined otherwise). The sub-magma restriction gives a smaller table with its own structural properties.

The corpus has the following named sub-magma restrictions:

| Restriction | Sub-magma | Closed under | Distinguishing fact |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Corner sub-magma** $\mathcal{C}$ | $(\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z})^* = \{1, 3, 7, 9\}$ | Multiplicative units | [J35] Comm Algebra |
| **Corner monoid** | $\{0, 1, 5, 6\}$ | TSML closure | [J35] Comm Algebra |
| **4-core** | $\{0, 7, 8, 9\}$ | TSML and BHML | [J44] J Algebra |
| **σ-fixed lattice** | $\{0, 3, 8, 9\}$ | $\sigma$-permutation | reference |
| **8-core** $\mathrm{TSML}_8$ | TSML \ $\{$Br, R$\}$ | TSML closure | [J6] |
| **BHML$_8$\_YM** | restriction | det $= +70 = C(8, 4)$ | [J34] Comm Algebra |

Each restriction is a Tier-B operation (forced once the sub-magma is specified). Each carries its own structural facts.

## §5 The lens family — summary

The 12+ variants of CL\_TSML (within its substrate family) fall into the following hierarchy:

```
        CL_TSML (substrate, Tier-A)
       /          \                \
   π_RAW       π_SYM_upper      π_SYM_lower
      |                |              |
   TSML_RAW       TSML_SYM      TSML_LOWERTRI
      |                |              |
   ┌──┴──┬──┐       ┌──┴──┬──┐    ┌──┴──┬──┐
   ↓     ↓  ↓       ↓     ↓  ↓    ↓     ↓  ↓
   σ²k    sub-magmas         (analogous)   (analogous)
```

with parallel structures rooted at CL\_BHML and CL\_STD.

**Distribution by tier** (across the entire family of $\sim 62$ variants, including all three substrates):

* **Tier A:** 5 variants (CL\_TSML, TSML\_RAW, CL\_BHML, CL\_STD, $F_p$ choice).
* **Tier B:** $\sim 21$ variants (chain scopes, lens-symmetrizations, sub-magma restrictions, DOING).
* **Tier C:** $\sim 9$ variants (TSML\_PureIdempotent, TSML\_C0, …).
* **Tier D:** $\sim 7$ variants ($\sigma^2$-triadic candidates, anomaly-flips, Fano subsets).
* **Tier E:** $\sim 8$ variants ($\mathbb{Z}/n$ ring extensions).

This catalog is documented in `Atlas/LENS_TAXONOMY_2026-05-06/VARIANT_CATALOG.md`. Each variant has a precise lineage and tier label.

## §6 The crucial lens-dependent result: chain at size 7

The most striking lens-dependence in the corpus is the **joint TSML+BHML chain at size 7**, treated in [J32]:

> The set of joint-closed sub-magmas of $(\mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}, T, B)$ — i.e., subsets closed under both TSML and BHML fusion — forms a strict 8-element chain of sizes $\{1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10\}$. The forbidden sizes are $\{2, 3\}$ only. The size-7 chain element exists at $\{0, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9\}$.

Earlier corpus generations claimed a 7-element chain with forbidden $\{2, 3, 7\}$; the chain-counting was corrected during the four-core paper preparation (R3 with referee Claude, May 2026): brute-force enumeration confirmed size 7 is **allowed** at the listed support.

This is the kind of lens-dependent result that the lens family enables. The chain depends on:

(i) Which TSML lens (RAW, SYM\_upper, SYM\_lower).
(ii) Whether BHML or one of its variants is paired with TSML.
(iii) Which sub-magma restriction is applied.

[J32] reports the corrected chain for the canonical pair (TSML\_SYM, BHML, no further restriction). Other pairings give different chains; the lens-dependence is *the* structural fact reported.

This paper's reader exercise: for each pairing of TSML lens × BHML lens, predict the chain length. The answers are in [J32]; the exercise illustrates why the lens family is necessary rather than ornamental.

## §7 Three reader exercises

### Exercise 7.1 — count non-associative triples in three lenses

Verify by direct computation:

* TSML\_RAW: 126 non-associative triples.
* TSML\_SYM (upper-tri): 128 non-associative triples.
* TSML\_LOWERTRI (lower-tri): 122 non-associative triples.

The three counts differ by $\{4, 4\}$ — one count differs by 4 in each direction from RAW. The difference is concentrated in the asymmetric cells $(3, 9)$ and $(4, 9)$ and their image triples.

### Exercise 7.2 — wobble localization in RAW vs SYM

Compute the characteristic polynomial of TSML\_RAW (Tier-A, the literal bit pattern):

$c_2 = 33 = 3 \cdot 11$, $c_8 = -2^5 \cdot 7^3 \cdot 11$.

The prime $11$ — the **wobble** — appears at the coefficient level. Compute the characteristic polynomial of TSML\_SYM (Tier-B): the prime $11$ does not appear in $c_2$. Symmetrization erases the wobble.

This is what [J43] (Phys Rev D, the wobble-localization paper) reports as a TSML\_RAW-specific structural fact.

### Exercise 7.3 — verify the runtime attractor lens-invariance

Compute the runtime processor at $\alpha = 1/2$ on TSML\_RAW and on TSML\_SYM. The 4-core attractor — $H/Br = 1 + \sqrt{3}$ exactly, supported on $\{V, H, Br, R\}$ — is the **same** in both lenses ([J41]). The attractor is **lens-invariant on the 4-core**. This is a Tier-A lens-invariant fact; the 4-core paper reports this.

Lens-invariant results (chain support on the 4-core; runtime attractor mass distribution; etc.) survive every projection. Lens-dependent results (wobble localization in RAW; 73-cell HARMONY count in SYM; etc.) require an explicit lens annotation.

## §8 Honest scope

This paper is a **pedagogical exposition**. It contains no new theorems. The contributions are:

(i) The organizational picture (§§1–5): one walking-tour through the 12+ variants of CL\_TSML.
(ii) The lens-dependence reading (§6): how the corpus's lens annotations work and why they matter.
(iii) Three reader exercises (§7) that illustrate the lens family in action.

This paper does **not**:

* Re-prove any result from the cited companions.
* Claim that the lens family is unique (other taxonomies are possible; the framework's choice is documented).
* Claim that the three substrates are exhaustive ($F_p$ extensions for $p \in \{2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13\}$ exist, see [J21], [J34]).

The aim is **clarity** for the working mathematician who wants to read the TIG framework's papers without getting lost.
