## Result

The 100-entry TSML composition table on Z/10Z is fully reconstructible from
**three canonical rules on disjoint domains**, with **empty residue-of-residue**:

```
T(x,y) = max(x,y)        on S_MAX (6 entries)
       = (x+y) mod 10    on S_ADD (2 entries)
       = C0(x,y)         otherwise (92 entries)
```

where C0 is generated by attractor h = 7, V0 (zero-absorbing with two
exceptions), and shell-stability via σ(u) = v₂(3u+1).

**Description-length compression:** 100 hand-defined entries → ~10 canonical items.

**Verification:** `papers/proof_tsml_3layer_tower.py` — 100/100, all 5 lemma
checks PASS in under 1 second.

## Proposed Title

"A Three-Layer Canonical Decomposition of the Z/10Z Composition Table TSML"

## Proposed Abstract (200 words, draft)

> We show that the published 10×10 composition table TSML on Z/10Z is fully
> reconstructible from three canonical rules on disjoint domains, with no
> further residue. Specifically, T(x,y) = max(x,y) on six "doubling/admissible
> seam" pairs, T(x,y) = (x+y) mod 10 on two "identity-edge" pairs, and
> T(x,y) = C₀(x,y) on the remaining 92 pairs, where C₀ is the canonical
> construction generated by attractor h = 7, the V₀ rule (zero is absorbing
> except at (0,h) and (h,0)), and the shell-stability rule using the 2-adic
> shell partition σ(u) = v₂(3u+1) on the unit group. Each of the three layers
> is necessary: removing any one produces explicit mismatches with the
> published table. The construction reduces the description length of TSML
> from 100 hand-defined entries to ~10 canonical items (3 ring-agnostic rules
> + 1 attractor + 1 shell partition + 4 ring-specific seam edges) with no
> information loss. We characterize which parts of the construction
> generalize to other rings: the rules are ring-agnostic; the seam domains
> are ring-specific. We list explicit negative results (failed generalizations
> tested and discarded). Verification: 100/100 entries match by direct
> computation (proof_tsml_3layer_tower.py).
