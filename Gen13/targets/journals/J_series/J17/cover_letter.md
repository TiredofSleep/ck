## Summary

We submit a short note that records a single elementary equivalence — the Universal Orthogonality Principle (UOP) — characterizing when a pair of partitions of squarefree Z/nZ is sufficient (i.e., their meet is the discrete partition). The principle states: {pi_1, pi_2} is sufficient iff the joint map J = (f_pi_1, f_pi_2) is injective. The proof is three lines. The contribution is uniformity: every two-partition sufficiency theorem in the squarefree setting — multiplicative+multiplicative (M+M), additive+multiplicative (A+M), the corrected multiplicative+additive (M+A), additive+additive (CRT k-1), and the minimum-jump theorem (MVJN) — is a corollary of UOP, obtained by computing when J is injective for a specific algebraic choice of f and g. We also prove the coordinate-coverage characterization (a useful sufficient condition for joint injectivity that is not necessary in general) and the refinement trap (no partition family lying in a single refinement chain can be sufficient unless it already contains the discrete partition).

## Why JNT

- The setting (squarefree Z/nZ, partition pairs, CRT decomposition) is squarely in JNT's purview.
- The result unifies several classical and folklore sufficiency theorems under one clean criterion. The unification, not the individual theorems, is the contribution.
- The paper is short (under 12 pages amsart) and self-contained; the only external input is the CRT and basic facts about cyclic groups.

## Reproducibility

The proofs are finite-combinatorial and hand-checkable. No numerical script is required; the paper contains explicit examples (notably the n=15 counterexample to the prior M+A condition) that can be verified by direct computation in any computer algebra system in under one minute.
