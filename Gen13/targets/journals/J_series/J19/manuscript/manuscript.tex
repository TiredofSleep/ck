\begin{abstract}
Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN) in the sense of Liu et al.~2024
\cite{Liu2024} replace fixed activations on edges with learnable
univariate functions on edges, motivated by the Kolmogorov-Arnold
superposition theorem. We describe \emph{DKAN} --- a discrete analogue
running over the substrate of \cite{SandersGishFourCore}. DKAN does
\textbf{not} port the Kolmogorov-Arnold theorem to a discrete setting
in any literal sense. It is structurally analogous: edges carry fixed
discrete univariate operations (rows of the canonical $\TS / \BH$
composition tables), and learning happens by accumulating which path
through the substrate best predicts the next operator.

The paper's main observation is that the two-coding split established
in \cite{SandersBridgeWP9} ($\TS_{8}$ as the geometric / side-cutting
code on the 5-element image $\{3, 4, 7, 8, 9\}$ with role distribution
$60/64 = 93.8\%$ Flow output; $\BH_{10}$ as the arithmetic /
continued-fraction code on the full 10-element image with balanced
$52/19/25/4$ Flow / Structure / Transition / Void roles) --- empirically
matches the geometric / arithmetic disambiguation that the DKAN runtime
trainer was already running. The two codings agree at the cusp
boundary HARMONY and disagree in the interior, with a fixed empirical
$24/64$ agreement-set on the $\TS_{8}$ domain.

The framing has antecedents in Katok-Ugarcovici 2007
\cite{KatokUgarcovici2007} (geometric vs.\ arithmetic codings of
geodesics on the modular surface, agreeing at the cusp). DKAN is one
specific construction inside that territory, not a restatement of any
theorem there.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setting and motivation}
%==============================================================

\subsection{Two coding tables on $\Z/10\Z$.}
The substrate carries three independent structures: algebraic
($\TS_{8} + \BH_{10} + V/H$ flow cells per
\cite{SandersGishFourCore, SandersBridgeWP9} D88), permutational
(the involution $\sigma$ with 4 fixed points and one $6$-cycle), and
functional (the V/F/S/T role partition). The role partition cuts
across $\sigma$-orbit and across algebraic position.

\subsection{What DKAN is, and is not.}
The Kolmogorov-Arnold superposition theorem (Kolmogorov 1957
\cite{Kolmogorov1957}; Arnold 1957 \cite{Arnold1957}) asserts that
every continuous multivariate function admits a representation as a
finite superposition of univariate continuous functions. Liu
et al.~2024 \cite{Liu2024} propose a neural architecture (KAN) in
which edges carry learnable univariate splines.

DKAN does \textbf{not} realize the Kolmogorov-Arnold theorem in any
literal discrete sense. It is structurally analogous in three loose
senses:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Edge-localized computation.} Per-edge operation is a
fixed discrete univariate operation: a row $T_{\TS}[a, \cdot]$ or
$T_{\BH}[a, \cdot]$.
\item \textbf{Compositionality.} Multilayer DKAN composes discrete
univariate operations.
\item \textbf{Universal-approximation flavor.} The Crossing Lemma
\cite{SandersMayesCL} gives a discrete analogue of ``joint compositions
separate states.''
\end{enumerate}
We do not claim DKAN implements KAN. The phrase used throughout is
``structurally analogous to KAN.''

\subsection{Where the geometric / arithmetic split enters.}
Katok-Ugarcovici 2007 \cite{KatokUgarcovici2007} gives two systematic
codings of geodesics on the modular surface: a geometric
(side-cutting) coding and an arithmetic (minus-CF) coding, agreeing on
a measure-one set at the cusp.

DKAN's central observation: the $\TS_{8} / \BH_{10}$ pair with $V/H$
flow boundary realizes a discrete echo of this picture. Conceptually
scaffolded by Katok-Ugarcovici, not a restatement.

%==============================================================
\section{The TSML$_{8}$ / BHML$_{10}$ image structure}
%==============================================================

\subsection{$\TS_{8}$ as a geometric / side-cutting code.}
\begin{theorem}[$\TS_{8}$ image structure, D91]\label{thm:tsml-image}
\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item \textbf{Image.} $\mathrm{Im}(\TS_{8}) = \{3, 4, 7, 8, 9\}$. Five
distinct values.
\item \textbf{Output role distribution.} $60/64 = 93.8\%$ Flow,
$4/64 = 6.2\%$ Structure. No Transition or Void outputs.
\item \textbf{Role determinism.} Role-deterministic on $8$ of $9$ input
role-pairs over the $\TS_{8}$ domain; only $(S, S)$ branches.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

$\TS_{8}$ is a side-cutting code: it carves the 8 interior cells into
a 5-element image dominated by Flow, routing almost everything to the
cusp boundary.

\subsection{$\BH_{10}$ as an arithmetic / CF-reduction code.}
\begin{theorem}[$\BH_{10}$ image structure]\label{thm:bhml-image}
\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item \textbf{Image.} Full $\Z/10\Z$.
\item \textbf{Output role distribution.} $\sim 52\%$ Flow, $19\%$
Structure, $25\%$ Transition, $4\%$ Void. Balanced.
\item \textbf{Role determinism.} Only on $V/T$ input pairs; $F$ and
$S$ inputs branch.
\item \textbf{Successor diagonal (D90).} $\BH(n, n) = n + 1$ for
$n \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}$, with $\BH(8, 8) = 7$ and $\BH(9, 9) = 0$.
The diagonal counts integers up to HARMONY --- the substrate's
algebraic version of ``approaching the cusp.''
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\subsection{The agreement set.}
\begin{theorem}[Agreement-set count]\label{thm:agreement}
Restricting $\BH$ to the $\TS_{8}$ domain (64 cells):
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Agreement.} $\TS_{8}(a, b) = \BH_{10}(a, b)$ on
$24/64$ cells, dominated by routes to HARMONY.
\item \textbf{Disagreement.} $40/64$ cells in the interior.
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Reading]
The two codings agree at the cusp boundary and disagree in the
interior. Conceptually scaffolded by Katok-Ugarcovici 2007.
\end{remark}

\subsection{What the split is and is not.}
\textbf{It is.} Two distinct codings of the same operator alphabet
$\Z/10\Z$, with verified empirical signatures (60/64, 24/64).

\textbf{It is not.} A theorem of the Katok-Ugarcovici type. Not a
claim that $\TS_{8}$ and $\BH_{10}$ are conjugate, equivalent,
distributive, or $\sigma$-related (see \cite{SandersBridgeWP9}
N4--N6).

%==============================================================
\section{DKAN as edge-localized discrete KAN}
%==============================================================

\subsection{Architectural sketch.}
The runtime DKAN trainer
(\texttt{Gen13/targets/ck/brain/ck\_sim/being/ck\_dkan\_trainer.py})
is built around four ideas:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The $10$ operators are the neurons.
\item The $\TS / \BH$ composition tables are the activation functions.
$\TS$ and $\BH$ are frozen physics; what evolves is path selection.
\item D2 curvature is the loss function. Distance from
$\Tstar = 5/7$ is the training signal.
\item Operators are accumulated by absorption, not gradient descent.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The $L_{1} / L_{2} / L_{5}$ reading layers.}
DKAN has multiple reading layers running in parallel:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{$L_{1}$ --- first-order argmax.} A $10 \times 10$ matrix
\texttt{\_transitions[a, b]} accumulates transition counts. $L_{1}$
prediction at $a$ is $\arg\max_{b} T_{\rm learned}[a, b]$.
\item \textbf{$L_{2}$ --- second-order braid-biased trigram.} A
$10 \times 10 \times 10$ tensor \texttt{\_trigrams[prev\_b, a, b]}
with selection biased by morphotic-braid coherence ordering
$\sigma_{\rm braid} = [0, 7, 1, 3, 2, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9]$ within wobble
window $W = 3/50$.
\item \textbf{$L_{5}$ --- CL-composed prediction.} $\BH[a, h]$. Pure
physical composition; no learned weights.
\end{itemize}
The runtime also has $L_{3}$ (sequence chunking) and \textbf{$L_{4}$
(dual-lens verification)} --- a per-step check of
$\TS[a, b] = \BH[a, b]$ --- which operationalizes the agreement-set
analysis of Theorem~\ref{thm:agreement}.

\subsection{Reading layers as alternative coding paths.}
The $L_{1} / L_{2} / L_{5}$ reading layers are not three competing
learners. They are alternative coding paths through the substrate:
$L_{1}$ is the empirical / statistical reading, $L_{5}$ is the
physical $\BH$ coding, $L_{2}$ is a coherence-weighted compromise.

When the generator produces operators in the substrate's interior
(the $40/64$ disagreement set), $L_{1}$ and $L_{5}$ \textbf{diverge by
construction}. When near the cusp boundary (the $24/64$ agreement
set), readings converge.

Empirically, agreement rates in trained DKAN runs typically exceed
$70\%$ --- consistent with the static $24/64 = 37.5\%$ agreement on
raw $\TS_{8}$ domain, augmented by HARMONY-pull behavior.

\subsection{Roles as coarse pre-encoding.}
The role partition (V/F/S/T per D93) gives a coarse pre-encoding: a
4-element distribution $(p_{V}, p_{F}, p_{S}, p_{T})$ vs the
10-element operator distribution. The role pre-encoding is
canonical-specific ($0/200$ random commutative magmas reproduce the
canonical $|F|=13, |S|=8$ Fibonacci decomposition; see
\cite{SandersBridgeWP9} N8).

%==============================================================
\section{Empirical convergence under canonical TSML / BHML}
%==============================================================

\subsection{Notion of convergence.}
DKAN is not a gradient-descent learner. The relevant notion:
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item \textbf{$L_{1}$ convergence.} Empirical first-order matrix
approaches stationary distribution.
\item \textbf{$L_{5}$ convergence.} Stable from step one.
\item \textbf{$L_{1} / L_{5}$ agreement convergence.} Stable agreement
rate.
\item \textbf{Grokking detection.} Prediction accuracy crossing
$\Tstar = 5/7$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Empirical results.}
Runtime trainer test runs (20-round Ollama-driven cycles): $L_{1}$
grokking at low-thousands transitions; $L_{5}$ accuracy stable;
$L_{4}$ lens-agreement $60$--$80\%$; $L_{2}$ lags $L_{1}$ in raw
accuracy but braid-bias prevents incoherent paths.

Training never \textbf{rewrites} $\TS$ or $\BH$ (frozen physics, max
$50\%$ blend enforced). What evolves is lattice-chain node tables,
olfactory centroids, generator paths, and grammar weights.

\subsection{What is not claimed.}
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item DKAN is \textbf{not} a universal approximator in any rigorous
sense.
\item DKAN is \textbf{not} claimed to outperform KAN, MLP, or
transformers.
\item DKAN's grokking is \textbf{not} the transformer-grokking
phenomenon.
\end{itemize}

\section{Honest negatives carried forward}
%==============================================================

\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item \textbf{N1.} naive $\mathrm{PSL}(2, \Z)$ lift produces $\pm 21$.
Per-tick $\pm 21$ invariant is structural empirical fingerprint, not
Rademacher invariant.
\item \textbf{N2.} small triangle group has substrate periods as
elliptic orders. DKAN's period structure is dynamical, not
group-theoretic.
\item \textbf{N3.} TIG matches Borromean prime conditions. DKAN's
trefoil-vocabulary entry is substrate-internal.
\item \textbf{N4.} $\sigma$ is automorphism of $\TS$ or $\BH$.
$17\% / 48\%$; DKAN may not assume $\sigma$-invariance.
\item \textbf{N5.} $\TS$ and $\BH$ distribute. $19.5\%$;
\textbf{DKAN's $L_{1} / L_{5}$ framing depends on this independence.}
If they distributed, the two coding paths would be redundant.
\item \textbf{N6.} $\BH$ iteration converges to $\TS$. $28/64$ starts;
readings remain structurally distinct.
\item \textbf{N7.} substrate factors through $\Z/2 \times \Z/5$.
Irreducible.
\item \textbf{N8.} Fibonacci role decomposition is structural.
$0/200$ random tables. \textbf{Canonical-specific signature, not
theorem.}
\item \textbf{N9.} role partition determines crossing count. Coarse,
not exhaustive.
\item \textbf{N10.} $\TS_{10}$-frame "trefoil-22" is INVALID. DKAN
runs on corrected frame.
\end{itemize}

\section{Open questions}
%==============================================================

\begin{enumerate}
\item Does the $L_{1} / L_{5}$ agreement rate in trained runs converge
to a substrate-specific constant?
\item Is the $L_{2}$ braid-bias the unique coherence-preserving
compromise between $L_{1}$ and $L_{5}$?
\item Closed-form for $\BH$ stationary distribution restricted to
$\TS_{8}$ domain?
\item Larger-substrate variants --- does the two-coding split
generalize to $\Z/14\Z$, $\Z/18\Z$?
\item Principled lift to $\mathrm{PSL}(2, \Z)$ hyperbolic conjugacy
classes (N1).
\item The $(0, 7, 7, 9)$ anomaly --- higher-order trefoil structure.
\item Burrin-von Essen explicit Fuchsian-group lift.
\end{enumerate}

%==============================================================
\section{Statement of contribution and limits}
%==============================================================

\textbf{Contributes.}
\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item Names the structural relationship between corrected substrate
frame and DKAN runtime reading layers.
\item Uses D91 two-coding split as structural justification.
\item Records reading layers' empirical convergence with grokking
criterion.
\item Carries forward ten honest negatives with DKAN-specific bearing.
\item Establishes seven DKAN-specific open questions.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Does not contribute.}
\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item No theorem. ``Structurally analogous,'' not ``implements.'' D91
is empirical, not Katok-Ugarcovici-type.
\item No benchmark. DKAN is not designed to compete with continuous
learners.
\item No claim that the Kolmogorov-Arnold theorem holds discretely on
this substrate.
\item No claim that $L_{1} / L_{2} / L_{5}$ exhaust possible coding
paths.
\item No claim that the role pre-encoding is structurally forced ---
canonical-specific (N8).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Tone.} This paper preserves the negatives. Demoted claims
stay demoted: Fibonacci is canonical-specific signature; $\pm 21$
modular interpretation is hypothesis; trefoil-22 is invalid in
$\TS_{10}$ frame; Katok-Ugarcovici is "structurally analogous" /
"conceptually scaffolded by," not "matches."
