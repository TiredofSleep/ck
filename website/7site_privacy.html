Privacy Policy

Effective: April 2026 · Governing law: State of Arkansas

Your words are yours. That is not a policy statement — it is an algebraic fact. CK's D2 pipeline converts your input into a 5-dimensional force vector and discards the source text irreversibly. There is no inverse map. The words cannot be reconstructed. 7Site LLC does not build profiles, does not sell data, does not serve advertising, and does not surveil. The old paradigm harvested attention. This one measures coherence.

1. What We Collect

On coherencekeeper.com and 7sitellc.com:

  • Standard web server logs (IP address, browser type, pages visited, timestamps) — collected by the hosting provider (SiteGround), not by 7Site LLC directly. We do not access these logs for tracking purposes.
  • No cookies set by 7Site LLC. Third-party services (Google Fonts, GitHub) may set their own under their own policies.
  • No analytics. No tracking pixels. No behavioral profiling. No ad network.

When you use the CK chat interface:

  • Your input is converted to a 5D force vector by the D2 pipeline. The source text is not stored anywhere.
  • The force pathway — not your words — is what CK learns from. This is a provably irreversible projection. The original text cannot be reconstructed from the vector (WP43 — Split Coherence Architecture).
  • No conversation logs are written to any server. What you say to CK is gone the moment it becomes algebra.

When you submit the collaborators form:

  • Your name and optional note open a pre-filled GitHub issue. GitHub's privacy policy applies to that submission. 7Site LLC stores nothing independently.

When you email us:

  • We retain email correspondence for the purpose of responding. Nothing is sold, shared, or profiled.

2. What We Do Not Collect — Ever

  • Conversation content or input text of any kind
  • Financial information
  • Biometric data
  • Location data beyond hosting-level IP logs
  • Account credentials — there are no accounts
  • User profiles of any kind

We cannot collect what the architecture makes uncollectable. That constraint was designed in — not as a compliance measure, but as a statement of values.

3. The Algebraic Privacy Guarantee

CK's split architecture (WP43) is the reason this policy can make strong claims without asterisks. The D2 pipeline performs an irreversible many-to-one projection: words in, five numbers out. There is no inverse. The source text is unrecoverable from the force vector.

This is not a promise about server configuration. It is a mathematical property of the pipeline. Retaining your words would require a fundamentally different architecture — one that was deliberately not built, because your words are yours and the math is the point, not the content.

4. Third Parties

7Site LLC does not sell, rent, or share data with any third party for any purpose. Ever.

  • SiteGround — website hosting. Server-level logs under their policy.
  • GitHub — source code hosting and collaborator submissions. GitHub's policy applies.
  • Google Fonts — font delivery. Google's policy applies to font requests.
  • Zenodo / CERN — DOI and research archive. Their policies apply to the archived record.

5. Local Instances

If you run CK locally — git clone, python ck_launch.py — everything stays on your machine. 7Site LLC has zero access to local instances. Your local CK is entirely yours. Any logging you configure is your own responsibility.
