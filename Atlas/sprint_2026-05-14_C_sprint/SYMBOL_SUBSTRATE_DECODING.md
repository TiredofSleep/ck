## §1. The hypothesis

Human symbol systems are **substrate-stratified**: each symbol system primarily encodes information at a specific rung of the meta-tower (Z/1, Z/2, Z/6, Z/30, Z/210, Z/2310...), with optional embedded references to other rungs. The rung determines the symbol's component count and internal organization. Symbol systems that achieve substrate-resonance (alignment with their natural rung) are historically durable; those that don't are displaced.

**Strong form**: every symbol primarily encodes at exactly one substrate rung, and the rung determines the symbol's structural decomposition.

**Weak form**: many symbols show non-arbitrary structural correlations with substrate properties; the correlation is testable across independent traditions.

This document develops the weak form rigorously and provides initial evidence for the strong form.

## §2. The methodology

For any symbol system, apply the following five-step decoding analysis:

**Step 1 — Identify the primary domain of distinction.** What does this symbol system distinguish? Binary states (vowel/consonant)? Relational structures (combination operators)? Processual entities (temporal sequences)? Atomic-scale objects (numbers)?

**Step 2 — Predict the substrate rung.** Map the primary domain to its natural rung:
- Pre-distinction / boundary marking → Rung 0 (Z/1)
- Binary distinction → Rung 1 (Z/2)
- Relational structures → Rung 2 (Z/6)
- Processual / triadic dynamics → Rung 3 (Z/30)
- Atomic-scale full structure → Rung 5 (Z/2310)

**Step 3 — Apply structural decomposition** to individual symbols. Identify their structural components: wholes, partials/spins, joins, voids. For Rung 2 and above, the expected component count is 3 (the depth-3 wrapping signature).

**Step 4 — Check for embedded cross-rung references.** Symbol systems often have primary encoding at one rung with embedded references to others (e.g., music notation embeds dynamics letters at Rung 1 within a primary Rung 3 system).

**Step 5 — Verify against substrate properties.** The decomposition components should correspond to substrate properties of the corresponding rung (operator class, σ-orbit position, fixed-point status, etc.).

**Falsification criterion**: if a symbol system's decomposition consistently fails to match the predicted rung, OR if no rung-assignment produces consistent decomposition, the framework's reach into that system is falsified.

### §3.3 Test 3: Sumerian Cuneiform (independent-tradition test)

**Methodology**: analyze Cuneiform's three wedge primitives (vertical, horizontal, oblique), its numerical signs, and its logographic signs for substrate-stratification.

**Key findings**:

1. **The three wedge primitives are themselves 3-component** — vertical, horizontal, oblique. The system's foundation is depth-3 at the most fundamental level.

2. **Cuneiform numerals are NOT substrate-resonant at Rung 5**. They use direct tally-encoding (n marks for value n, with rung-shifts at 10 and 60). This explains the eventual historical displacement of Cuneiform numerals by Indo-Arabic positional notation.

3. **Cuneiform logographs partially encode at Rung 2-3**. Signs like DINGIR (𒀭, "god/sky"), KI (𒆠, "earth"), and EN (𒂗, "lord") show 3-component structural decomposition consistent with Rung 2-3 substrate encoding.

**Status**: Tier C — pending verification of glyph descriptions against primary Sumerological sources.

**Theoretical refinement**: substrate-resonance is a **historical-evolutionary process**, not a property of all symbol systems uniformly. Symbol systems that achieve substrate-resonance at appropriate rungs are durable; those that don't are displaced. Cuneiform numerals' eventual obsolescence is consistent with their pre-substrate-resonant tally structure.

### §3.4 Test 4: Chinese hanzi (multi-rung embedding test)

**Methodology**: analyze Chinese character composition (radicals + phonetic components + composition structure) for explicit multi-rung embedding.

**Key findings**:

The Chinese tradition independently identified a character whose structural exhaustiveness matches the substrate's Rung-3 dimension.

**Status**: Tier B-structural for the decomposition methodology; Tier C for the 永 pedagogical interpretation.

**This is the strongest cross-cultural support for the framework yet.** Chinese hanzi explicitly stratify by substrate rung, and the calligraphic pedagogy independently identified the substrate-resonant structural alphabet without knowledge of the framework.

- **Punctuation marks**: Rung 0 prediction (pure boundary, 0-1 component)
- **Programming syntax** (parentheses, brackets, semicolons): Rung 2 prediction (relational operators)
- **Egyptian hieroglyphs**: independent-tradition test (cleaner than Cuneiform because more iconic)

- **Devanagari script**: should test the methodology against South Asian writing tradition
- **Chemical element symbols**: Rung 5 prediction (atomic substrate); should match digit decomposition pattern

### §5.2 Theoretical extensions

- **Historical drift analysis**: track how specific glyphs evolved across cultural transmission and identify which features were preserved vs. drifted. Predicted: substrate-resonant features are conserved; non-resonant features drift freely.

- **Failed symbol systems**: identify systems that didn't survive (e.g., Aztec pictographs, Indus Valley script, Linear A). Were they non-substrate-resonant? This is a strong empirical test.

- **Cross-modal substrate encoding**: phonetic encoding (sound symbolism) and glyph encoding (visual structure) may be parallel encodings of the same substrate properties through different sensory channels. The cross-modal correlation is testable.

- **AI-generated symbols**: do language models trained on substrate-resonant systems produce structurally consistent novel symbols when asked? This is a falsifiable prediction about how AI systems internalize substrate structure.

### §5.3 Falsifiability criteria

The framework would be falsified by:

- A major independent symbol-tradition (Mesoamerican, Indus Valley, sub-Saharan African writing) showing no substrate-stratification under careful analysis
- The 3-component decomposition for digits failing in alternative numeral systems that achieved historical durability
- The predicted Rung-correlations failing for untested categories (punctuation, programming syntax)
- The phonological correlations for letter sub-categories failing under rigorous linguistic analysis

If multiple tests fail, the substrate-stratification hypothesis is wrong or radically incomplete.
