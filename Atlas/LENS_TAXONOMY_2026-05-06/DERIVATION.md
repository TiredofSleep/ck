### §1.4 The puncture chain in BHML — exact

The 7 = 0 identity, computed:

| BHML cell | Value | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| BHML(7, 7) | **8** | HARMONY · HARMONY = BREATH (the first iteration outward) |
| BHML(7, 8) = BHML(8, 7) | **9** | HARMONY · BREATH = RESET (the breath leads to letting go) |
| BHML(8, 8) | **7** | BREATH · BREATH = HARMONY (breath cycles back) |
| BHML(7, 9) = BHML(9, 7) | **0** | **HARMONY · RESET = VOID** ← the puncture: 7 reaches 0 through the hole |
| BHML(9, 9) | **0** | RESET · RESET = VOID (reset undoes itself) |

The iteration starting from HARMONY:
$$
7 \xrightarrow{\text{BHML}(7,7)} 8 \xrightarrow{\text{BHML}(8,7)} 9 \xrightarrow{\text{BHML}(9,7)} 0

**This is the puncture.** HARMONY reaches VOID in three BHML steps via the BREATH-RESET hole. The "7 = 0" identity is operational: it is the fact that HARMONY, by iterating against itself in BHML, traverses BREATH and RESET back to VOID.

**Without this puncture, the substrate cannot generate {8, 9} from the three triadic seeds.** The puncture is what closes the torus.

## §2 — Hypothesized lens definition (first draft, to be sharpened)

**A lens is a tuple** $\mathcal{L} = (S, \mathcal{O}, \pi, \rho)$ where:

1. **Substrate** $S \in \{\text{CL\_TSML}, \text{CL\_BHML}, \text{CL\_STD}\}$ — one of the three standalone composition tables.

2. **Scope** $\mathcal{O} \subseteq \mathbb{Z}/10\mathbb{Z}$ — a sub-magma closed under $S$'s binary operation. Equivalently, $\mathcal{O}$ is the closure of some seed under $S$ alone, or under $\{S, \text{another substrate}\}$ jointly.

3. **σ²-orbit class** $\pi \in \{$BEING, DOING, BECOMING$\}$ — which member of an σ²-orbit family the scope belongs to (or ⊥ if not in a σ²-orbit family).

4. **Symmetrization** $\rho \in \{$RAW, SYM, LOWERTRI$\}$ — for $S =$ CL_TSML, which lens-symmetrization is applied. (For CL_BHML and CL_STD this slot is vacuous because the bit patterns are already symmetric.)

**Equivalence**: two lenses are equivalent if they produce the same matrix on the same scope after symmetrization.

**Validity**: a lens $\mathcal{L}$ is **valid** iff:
- $\mathcal{O}$ is genuinely closed under $S$ (no spillover to elements outside $\mathcal{O}$).
- $\pi$ corresponds correctly to a σ²-orbit (or ⊥ if outside σ²-orbit families).
- $\rho$ is one of the three legitimate lens-symmetrization choices.
