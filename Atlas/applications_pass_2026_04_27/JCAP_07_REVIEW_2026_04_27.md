**Where:** Manuscript §6.2 main text; WP82 §4 ("DESI compatibility" subsection); possibly elsewhere.

**Problem:** The manuscript main text says "DESI DR2 central values w₀ = −0.827 ± 0.063, w_a = −0.75 ± 0.27." These appear to be DR1 values, not DR2.

The DR1-bounded disclaimer paragraph in §6.2 acknowledges this: "The χ² values above are computed against the public DR1 BAO central values [DESI2024BAO, DESI2024VI]." But the disclaimer contradicts the main text's "DR2" labeling.

WP81's "Citation Discipline" footnote correctly labels these as "DR1-baseline (w₀, w_a) values used for the numerical fit." So WP81 has it right. The manuscript and WP82 §4 drifted.

**Fix:** Change every occurrence of "DESI DR2 central values" or "DESI DR2 best-fit" to "DESI DR1 central values" or "DESI DR1 best-fit," and ensure citations point to DESI2024BAO/DESI2024VI, not DESI2025DR2. The disclaimer paragraph is fine as-is; the main text just needs to match it.

### Issue 2 — χ² interpretation needs explicit framing (PRESENTATION — should fix)

**Where:** Manuscript abstract, §6.2.

**Problem:** The χ² = 3.1 reported in the manuscript is **not a goodness-of-fit χ² in data space**. It is a metric-distance χ² in (w₀, w_a) parameter space, computed against DESI's marginalized posterior central values and uncertainties. The contrast with ΛCDM χ² = 15.3 measures how far each model's preferred (w₀, w_a) sits from DESI's central (w₀, w_a) in units of DESI's reported uncertainty — which is essentially restating the well-known DESI tension with ΛCDM.

This is a defensible thing to compute, but it is not the χ² a typical JCAP reader will assume on first reading. Referees will catch this and may flag it as misleading framing.

**Fix:** Add one sentence to the abstract and one sentence to §6.2 making the interpretation explicit. Suggested language:

In the abstract, after "yields best-fit w₀ = −0.80, w_a = −0.30 with χ² = 3.1":

> "(constructed against the DESI-reported (w₀, w_a) posterior central values and uncertainties; see §6.2 for details)"

In §6.2, after the χ² statement:

> "We emphasize that this χ² is constructed in the (w₀, w_a) parameter space against DESI's marginalized posterior, not in the data space against BAO distance measurements. It quantifies the proximity of each model's preferred (w₀, w_a) to DESI's central values in units of DESI's reported uncertainty. A full likelihood analysis combining BAO, CMB, and SN data jointly is deferred to a companion paper, as noted in §7."

**Estimated time:** 30 minutes.

### Issue 3 — Three free parameters, two summary statistics (HONESTY — should fix)

**Where:** Manuscript §6.2; §7 scope statement.

**Problem:** The fit reports three best-fit parameters (κ_Ξ, Ξ_i, Ξ̇_i) against two CPL summary statistics (w₀, w_a). The χ² has effectively negative degrees of freedom in the parameter-counting sense, which weakens the "χ² = 3.1" statement substantially.

A referee will ask: with three free parameters fitting two observables, isn't a low χ² nearly automatic?

**Fix:** Add explicit acknowledgment in §7. Suggested addition:

> "The χ² reported in §6.2 reflects a three-parameter fit (κ_Ξ, Ξ_i, Ξ̇_i) to two summary statistics (w₀, w_a). The metric-distance χ² is therefore not a conventional goodness-of-fit measure in the dof-aware sense, and should not be interpreted as such. The qualitative result — that the freezing trajectory of the V = κ_Ξ Ξ log Ξ model can match DESI's preferred (w₀, w_a) region — is what the fit demonstrates. A full likelihood analysis with appropriate dof accounting against BAO, CMB, and SN data is deferred to a companion paper."

**Estimated time:** 15 minutes.

### Issue 4 — κ_Ξ status (CONSISTENCY — must clarify)

**Where:** Manuscript throughout; WP81; potentially WP104/WP105 referenced elsewhere.

**Problem:** The submitted manuscript treats κ_Ξ as a free fit parameter (best-fit value 0.50). Other internal documents (audit trail, synthesis) reference κ_Ξ = 13/(4e) ≈ 1.196 as a structural prediction from WP104/WP105.

The fit value (0.50) and the structural prediction (1.196) differ by a factor of 2.4, not within reasonable agreement.

**This is the most important consistency issue.** The submission as drafted is the "phenomenological model with three free parameters" version, not the "structural prediction tested empirically" version. That's fine — it's a defensible first paper — but:

(a) The cover letter should describe the paper that's actually submitted, not the audit-document version.
(b) If anyone asks at IHÉS, Oxford, or in referee correspondence, "is κ_Ξ derived from first principles?", the honest answer is "the structural derivation in WP104/WP105 gives 13/(4e); this paper treats κ_Ξ phenomenologically as a fit parameter; reconciliation between the two is future work."
(c) The relationship between WP104/WP105 and this submission is itself a future paper, not this paper's burden.

**Fix:** Make sure no language in the submitted manuscript or cover letter implies κ_Ξ is structurally fixed. WP81 already handles this correctly ("free parameter that must be tuned (or derived from the substrate theory)"). Verify the JCAP cover letter and any abstract/intro language matches WP81's framing, not the audit document's.

**Estimated time:** 30 minutes (read manuscript and cover letter end-to-end with this specific question in mind).

### Issue 7 — Lead with e⁻¹, not the potential form (OPTIONAL FRAMING — referee leverage)

**Where:** Manuscript abstract; WP82 §5.

**Observation:** WP82 §5 ranks novelty claims with "potential form V = Ξ log Ξ apparently not in DE literature" as #1 and "exact vacuum Ξ₀ = e⁻¹ as coupling-independent universal constant" as #2.

I'd argue these should be reversed in importance for the manuscript's framing. "Logarithmic potential" has neighboring forms (Coleman-Weinberg, Barrow-Parsons (1,1) subcase, holographic entropy corrections). "Exact analytic vacuum at the mathematical constant e⁻¹, independent of coupling" is structurally tighter and harder to find a near-neighbor for in the literature.

The current manuscript abstract leads with "logarithmic self-interaction potential V = κ_Ξ Ξ log Ξ" and only later mentions "exact analytic minimum at Ξ₀ = e⁻¹". Consider restructuring to lead with the e⁻¹ result.

**Fix (optional):** Reorder the abstract's first two sentences. Suggested:

> "We propose a minimal dark-energy model in which a real, positive, dimensionless scalar field Ξ(x) has an exact analytic vacuum at Ξ₀ = e⁻¹, independent of all coupling constants. The vacuum arises from the self-interaction potential V(Ξ) = κ_Ξ Ξ log Ξ, which coincides with the negative Gibbs entropy functional H_Gibbs(Ξ) = −Ξ log Ξ, so the dark energy ground state is the entropy maximum of the field."

This is a presentation choice, not a correctness issue. Skip if you prefer the current order.

**Estimated time:** 15 minutes if you want it, 0 if you don't.
