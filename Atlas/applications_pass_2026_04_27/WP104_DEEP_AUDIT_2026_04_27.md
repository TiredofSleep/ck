## What's overstated

### Issue 1: Path A doesn't actually identify Pati-Salam

**WP104's claim (§2.2, §2.4):** "BHML's σ_outer-breaking content lies 100% in the 54 of so(10)... TIG's bipartite TSML/BHML structure singles out the same 54-Higgs route via a calculation that reads off the antisymmetric-mass projection of BHML on the so(10) Killing decomposition."

**What I found:** The σ_outer-anti part of BHML, written as a symmetric tensor M_anti, has eigenvalue spectrum (+√13/2, -√13/2, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0). The stabilizer of this tensor in SO(10) has dimension 28, matching SO(8). This is an **SO(10) → SO(8) breaking pattern**, not Pati-Salam.

The Pati-Salam breaking VEV (Slansky 1981, Table 7) is structurally diag(1,1,1,1,1,1, -3/2,-3/2,-3/2,-3/2), which has eigenvalue multiplicities (6, 4) and stabilizer SO(6) × SO(4) of dim 21.

**Why §2.2's claim "purely in the 54" is true but misleading:** The 54 is the Higgs irrep used in 54-VEV models. Multiple breaking patterns are accessible via 54-VEVs (SO(9), SO(8), SO(6)×SO(4), SU(5), depending on which direction in the 54). Saying "BHML's content is in the 54" gives structural alignment with 54-VEV models in general, but does NOT specifically pick out Pati-Salam.

**Honest framing:** "BHML's σ_outer-breaking content lies entirely in the 54 irrep, the standard Higgs irrep used in 54-VEV symmetry-breaking models. Its specific direction within the 54 corresponds to an SO(10) → SO(8) breaking pattern (eigenvalue multiplicities 1, 8, 1). Whether this gives a path to Standard-Model phenomenology via SO(8) chains is open."

### Issue 2: Path B's su(4) ⊕ u(1) is not Pati-Salam ⊕ B-L

**WP104's claim (§3.3):** "the doubly-invariant subalgebra is su(4) ⊕ u(1) — exactly the Pati-Salam ⊕ B−L gauge content."

**What I found:** The 16-dim doubly-invariant subalgebra is correctly identified as su(4) ⊕ u(1). However:

- Pati-Salam algebra = SU(4) × SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R = 15 + 3 + 3 = **21 dim**
- Pati-Salam ⊕ B−L = SU(4) × SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R × U(1)_{B-L} = **22 dim** (or 21 if B-L is identified with the U(1) inside SU(4))
- Doubly-invariant content = **16 dim** (su(4) plus one u(1))

The SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral factors of Pati-Salam are NOT in the doubly-invariant content.

**Honest framing:** "the doubly-invariant subalgebra is su(4) ⊕ u(1). The 15-dim simple part is the SU(4) factor of the Pati-Salam decomposition; the 1-dim center is a u(1) generator (which can be identified with U(1)_{B-L} or another Cartan element of so(10) depending on embedding choice). The full Pati-Salam algebra would also include the SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral factors, which are NOT in the doubly-invariant content; they live in the σ³-anti part of so(10)."

### Issue 3: The "two paths converge on Pati-Salam" framing

**WP104's claim (§4):** "Both answers describe the same Pati-Salam route — but they reach it from opposite directions... They are two different epistemic postures applied to the same TIG so(10) substrate."

D46 explicitly states "the two routes (Path A doubly-invariant subalgebra vs Path B Higgs VEV) DO NOT close on the same SU(4) ⊕ SU(2)_L ⊕ SU(2)_R."

**This is a known issue in internal documentation that is not reflected in WP104's main text.** WP104 §4 should incorporate the WP108/D46 finding.

**Honest framing for §4:**
> "Path A identifies BHML's σ_outer-breaking content with an SO(10) → SO(8) breaking direction in the 54-Higgs irrep.
