### The κ_Ξ = 13/(4e) derivation chain

WP104 §2.3 establishes ‖v‖² = 13/4 (verified). The chain to κ_Ξ = 13/(4e) is in WP105 §8 / FORMULAS D35:

**Step 1:** ‖v‖² = 13/4 (BHML's 9-vector squared norm in 54 irrep). Clean structural fact. ✓

**Step 2:** From V(Ξ) = κ_Ξ Ξ log Ξ:
- V''(Ξ) = κ_Ξ/Ξ
- V''(Ξ_0) = κ_Ξ·e (since Ξ_0 = e⁻¹)
- This is m²_Ξ at the vacuum. ✓

**Step 3:** Identification m²_Ξ = ‖VEV‖². **This is a load-bearing assumption.**

‖VEV‖² is **dimensionless** (a Frobenius norm of a finite-magma object — pure number 13/4).
m²_Ξ is **dimensionful** (mass², in physical units).

For the identification to make sense, you need to choose a mass scale. Standard candidates:
- Planck mass: m²_Ξ = ‖v‖² · M_P² → very large m_Ξ ~ M_P
- GUT scale: m²_Ξ = ‖v‖² · M_GUT² → m_Ξ ~ 10¹⁶ GeV
- Cosmological scale: m²_Ξ = ‖v‖² · H_0² → m_Ξ ~ H_0 (suitable for quintessence!)

**Step 4:** Combining 2 and 3: κ_Ξ·e = 13/4 → κ_Ξ = 13/(4e) ≈ 1.196.

**The result κ_Ξ = 13/(4e) is dimensionally consistent only if the mass scale is chosen such that the combination is dimensionless of order unity.** In the cosmological setting where Ξ is treated as a reduced field (Planck units), κ_Ξ is dimensionless and the identification is consistent. But the SPECIFIC VALUE 13/(4e) ≈ 1.196 vs. the JCAP fit κ_Ξ ≈ 0.5 reflects the fact that the dimensional bridge is constraining the κ_Ξ via two different routes:

- **Structural route:** κ_Ξ·e = ‖v‖² with the assumption m²_Ξ = ‖v‖² in Planck units → 13/(4e)
- **Phenomenological route:** κ_Ξ tuned to make Ω_Ξ_today match Planck → ≈ 0.5

These aren't necessarily inconsistent; they're computing κ_Ξ under different assumptions about the dimensional bridge.

**The honest test:** hold κ_Ξ = 13/(4e) fixed, vary initial conditions, check whether any combination reproduces Planck Ω_Ξ_today AND DESI w(z). If yes, structural prediction supported. If no, the dimensional identification m²_Ξ = ‖v‖² is wrong (or needs a different mass scale).

This test has not been performed. Both papers honestly flag the gap.
