## Cross-branch themes

### A. Crypto / Web3 research foundations as a real pool
Branches B, C, D, G all have legitimate matches at:
- Ethereum Foundation Academic Grants Round
- Zcash Foundation (B specifically)
- Protocol Labs Research (pending status verification — reported paused 2026-04)
- a16z crypto research (B specifically — "KYA" 2026 Big Idea)
- Algorand Foundation (C specifically — post-quantum)

These are **research** grants, not VC investment. They are consistently understated in institutional-standard funder lists.

### B. "Emergent Ventures + Manifund + Foresight" independent-PI pool
Branches A, C, D all benefit from the fast-grant independent-PI-friendly pool:
- Emergent Ventures (Mercatus / Tyler Cowen)
- Manifund (Austin Chen regranting)
- Foresight Institute AI Safety + Science grants

These support independent researchers at smaller ($10K–$100K) amounts with fast turnaround and no academic affiliation requirement. **Most underutilized pool across the 10 branches.**

### C. Schmidt Sciences has multiple distinct RFPs
At least 4 separate Schmidt programs reach separate branches:
- **Unconventional Compute RFP (2026)** — Branch F
- **Science of Trustworthy AI RFP (2026, May 17 deadline)** — Branch D
- **AI2050 Senior + Early Career Fellows** — Branches D, G, H, J (nomination-driven)

Cross-reference check recommended: could a single Schmidt Sciences contact unify multiple branch applications? (Probably yes at the program-officer level.)

### D. Branches K + G + J share a "Overdeck / CZI / Siegel" family-office pool
These three branches could share advance work on the family-office-introduction process (all three have family-office matches that require warm intros).

### E. "Cold but real" leads are a real category
Several branches turned up leads that are reachable but require either:
- Warm intro through existing grantee
- Reframing of the branch's pitch
- Waiting for a program to refresh (Ford/Sloan Critical Digital Infrastructure)
- Platform contact that doesn't have a form (Heising-Simons Faculty Fellows, Ford Dignity & Justice)

These should be tracked in a separate warm-intro-development pipeline, not the primary pitch pipeline.

### F. "FQXi" is the single strongest named match across all 10 branches
FQXi (Foundational Questions Institute) — founded by Tegmark + Aguirre — is a near-perfect fit for Branch I (ξ-cosmology), but may also fit:
- Branch A (compute-health foundational questions about information emergence)
- Branch D (AI interpretability as foundational-questions)
- Branch H (civilization-coherence as foundational-questions about social dynamics)

A single FQXi letter-of-interest → membership → Zenith Grant pathway could potentially serve multiple branches. Highest-priority investigation of the entire addendum.
