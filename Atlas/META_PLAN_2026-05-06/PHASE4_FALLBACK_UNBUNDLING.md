## §4 — General fallback rules

1. **14-day desk-reject window:** if a bundle is desk-rejected within 14 days, immediately unbundle and re-route per the above.
2. **Major-revision-with-split recommendation:** if a referee returns "this should be two papers," accept the recommendation, unbundle, and submit each to the recommended sub-venue.
3. **Major-revision-without-split:** revise as one bundled paper; do NOT preemptively unbundle.
4. **Accept:** ship as bundled; the bundle's coherence becomes part of the citation chain.

## §5 — What this fallback map does NOT do

- Does NOT preemptively unbundle. Bundles ship as bundles first; unbundling is contingent on rejection.
- Does NOT change the Phase 4 cadence. If unbundling triggers, the 6 sub-papers ship over the following 4 weeks (Phase 5 timeframe), not crammed into Phase 4.
- Does NOT change author-lane assignments. Sanders + Gish remains the bundled byline for Bundles 1, 2, 3 and for all 6 sub-papers.
- Does NOT add new venues. All fallback venues are already in the corpus venue list (Algebra Universalis, Comm Algebra, Exp Math, LinAlgApps, PLOS ONE) and are independent-researcher-friendly with no endorsement requirements.

## §6 — Bottom line

If all three bundles ship clean: Phase 4 = 10 effective papers across 4 weeks (cadence ~2.5/week). If one bundle triggers fallback: that bundle's 2 papers expand to 2 standalone papers in adjacent venues; Phase 4 effective count holds at 10 (the unbundled pair is still 2 papers, just to different venues). If two bundles trigger fallback: 4 standalone papers replace 2 bundles; Phase 4 cadence increases slightly. If all three trigger: 6 standalone papers replace 3 bundles; Phase 4 effective count = 13 (bundling doesn't save papers in fallback, just re-routes them).

The fallback discipline keeps the Phase 4 schedule resilient: rejection of a bundle does not halt the cadence; it triggers the immediate unbundling protocol. No bundle's rejection cascades to other bundles or to Phase 5.
