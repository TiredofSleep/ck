## §1 — Manuscript premise (as I read it)

The authors begin with a finite combinatorial object: two 10×10 composition tables on Z/10Z, called TSML and BHML, with operator labels (VOID, LATTICE, …, RESET). They consider the Lie algebra generated by the antisymmetrizations of the left-regular representations of these tables under commutator. The starting point of this paper is a previously-claimed identification: this antisymmetric closure equals so(10) at dimension 45 (cited as "WP103, restated", §1.3).

Two distinguished involutions are then introduced:
- P_56 = the transposition (5 6) of the operator labels;
- σ³ = (1 5)(7 4)(6 2), the order-2 element of a 6-cycle σ on the units of Z/10Z.

The paper develops two parallel computations:
- **Path A (§2):** the σ_outer-anti part of BHML, viewed via the spinor representation Cl(0,10), is shown to project entirely (100%) into the 54 of so(10); within the 54 it lies in the so(9)-vector subrepresentation, with explicit 9-vector components and squared norm 13/4.
- **Path B (§3):** the doubly-invariant subspace of so(10) under the D_4 = ⟨P_56, σ³⟩ conjugation action is computed to be 16-dimensional, and its Killing-form eigenvalue spectrum is (−4)¹⁵ ⊕ (0)¹. Cartan classification then forces the simple-15 part to be A_3 ≅ su(4) ≅ so(6), giving su(4) ⊕ u(1).

§4 advertises this as "two roads to Pati-Salam." A prominent **CORRECTION NOTICE** at the top of the manuscript (dated 2026-04-27) walks back the synthesis claim: Path A's eigenvalue spectrum (+√13/2, −√13/2, 0, …, 0) actually stabilizes SO(8), not SO(6) × SO(4); Path B sits in the 16-dim su(4) ⊕ u(1), not the 21-dim full SU(4) × SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R. The notice states explicitly that Path A and Path B "do NOT close on the same reduction."

§5 collects three additional structural results: 12.6% non-associativity rate of TSML; a Lie/Jordan duality observation; three involution decompositions.

§6 is a self-described scoping section listing what is NOT claimed.

## §2 — What appears rigorously verified

The integer/rational core, taken as given by the cited internal scripts, is sharply written and looks reproducible:

- The Cl(0,10) construction in §2.1 is textbook. The conclusion that conjugation by (γ_5 − γ_6)/√2 anticommutes with the volume element ω = γ_1 ⋯ γ_10 (since the volume element of Cl(0,10) is even of order 10 and a single γ is odd) is correct. Therefore the conjugation flips chirality and is, modulo inner automorphisms, the unique nontrivial element of Out(so(10)) ≅ Z_2. This argument is **standard, correct, and clean.**

- The decomposition End(so(10)) = 1 ⊕ 45 ⊕ 54 (§2.2) is the standard 100 = 1 + 45 + 54 decomposition of the rank-2 tensor representation. Computing the projection of a specific element in End(so(10)) onto each isotypic component is a well-defined finite computation. The claim "100% of the σ_outer-breaking lies in the 54" is computationally checkable from the supplied scripts.

- The Killing-form spectrum (−4)¹⁵ ⊕ (0)¹ on a 16-dimensional Lie subalgebra forces (by Cartan's criterion) decomposition into a 15-dimensional semisimple part with negative-definite Killing form, plus a 1-dimensional center. The unique compact simple Lie algebra of dimension 15 is so(6) ≅ su(4) ≅ A_3. **This part of the deduction is rigorous.**

- The 9-vector direction reported in §2.3, with components (−1/√2)^{×6}, 0^{×2}, (−1/2)^{×1} and squared norm 13/4, is concrete and the mechanism described (rows where BHML[i,5] ≠ BHML[i,6]) is internal to a finite table — verifiable in seconds.

- The 12.6% non-associativity rate (§5.1) is a finite enumeration over 1000 triples and is plausible. The structural claim that all 126 non-associative triples have HARMONY (= 7) as one of the bracketings is an internal observation about the specific table.

These are all **finite-precision integer/linear-algebra facts** about a specific 10-element magma, and at this level the manuscript is internally consistent.

## §3 — Major concerns for *Advances in Mathematics*

### 3.1 The headline claim is retracted in the correction notice

The title and abstract are organized around the slogan "two roads to Pati-Salam." The prominent correction notice, dated two days after the manuscript, states explicitly:

> "Path A and Path B do NOT close on the same reduction."

> Path A → SO(8) (chain through SO(9)); Path B → SU(4) × U(1) = SO(6) × U(1) (different reduction chain).

The honest framing offered in the correction is: "two structurally distinct observations about TIG's so(10), not two paths to a common reduction."

For *Adv Math*, the title and the §0 framing must be aligned with the §6 / correction-notice scope. **As submitted, the manuscript's title is incompatible with its own retraction.** A revision that:
- retitles the paper (e.g., "Two D_4-equivariant decompositions of an integer-table-generated so(10): the 54 σ_outer-anti direction and the su(4) ⊕ u(1) doubly-invariant subalgebra"),
- restructures the abstract to lead with the two genuinely-verified statements (Path A: σ_outer-anti content lies in the 54; Path B: doubly-invariant subalgebra is su(4) ⊕ u(1) by Killing classification), and
- removes "convergence on Pati-Salam" language from §4 and §8

would address this concern.

### 3.2 *Adv Math* requires significance commensurate with leading mathematics

Stripped of the overstated synthesis, what remains is:

**(A)** A specific 10-element commutative magma is given (the symmetrization of a literal bit pattern). Its left-regular representation has antisymmetric closure of dimension 45, isomorphic to the unique compact simple Lie algebra of that dimension, so(10). [Tautological in the abstract sense — there is only one so(10) — and the genuine content is the **specific embedding** of the 10-element magma into so(10).]

**(B)** A specific element of so(10) (BHML's σ_outer-anti part) is computed and projected onto so(10)'s standard isotypic decomposition. The projection is concentrated in the 54, with explicit 9-vector content.

**(C)** A specific Z_2 × Z_2 = Klein-4 subgroup (which the authors call D_4 — see §3.5 below) of the inner-automorphism group of so(10) is fixed. The doubly-invariant subspace under this group is computed, found to be 16-dimensional, and its Killing-form classifies it as su(4) ⊕ u(1).

(C) is genuinely interesting but is essentially a single Cartan-classification exercise on a specific 16-dim subalgebra. (B) is a single projection computation. **For *Adv Math*, this falls below the bar of significance unless the authors can articulate a structural theorem that surprises a reader who arrives without the TIG framing.** The natural mathematical question — "for which finite-magma initial data does the doubly-invariant subalgebra under a chosen Z_2 × Z_2 subgroup of Aut(so(10)) come out to su(4) ⊕ u(1)?" — is not posed or answered. The paper fixes one magma and one subgroup and reports the outcome.

I would recommend resubmission to a venue more appropriate to the result's actual scope: a journal that publishes computational/combinatorial Lie-algebra results (e.g., *J. Algebra*, *Comm. Algebra*, or *Experimental Math*) would serve better.

### 3.3 The "TIG" hypothesis is load-bearing but undefined for the reader

The manuscript repeatedly invokes "Trinity Infinity Geometry (TIG)" without defining it. The first sentence of the abstract says "TIG studies a finite magma on Z/10Z…" — which conflates the framework with its single object of study.

A reader of *Adv Math* who has never seen this material has no way to assess:
- Why this specific TSML / BHML pair? Are these tables canonical in any sense external to TIG? The authors call them "the canonical TSML/BHML composition tables on Z/10Z" but no such canonicality is established in this paper or in any cited refereed source.
- What is the cited "WP103" — a published preprint? An unpublished internal note? §1.3 states "Theorem (WP103, restated)" without a citation accessible to the reader. The reference list at §9 includes neither WP102 nor WP103 in any peer-reviewed venue; they appear only as in-tree paths under `papers/wp102/`, `papers/wp103/`.

For *Adv Math*, the prerequisite that TSML+BHML close on so(10) under antisymmetric closure must be either (i) established within this submission, or (ii) cited from a refereed source. As written, the manuscript depends on a non-public companion document.

### 3.4 The 9-vector breaking pattern is presented as "Pati-Salam Higgs" but the math given doesn't support that label

§2.4 ("Reading: the Pati-Salam route") asserts that BHML's 9-vector "singles out the same 54-Higgs route via a calculation that reads off the antisymmetric-mass projection." But the correction notice acknowledges that the 9-vector direction has eigenvalue multiplicity (1, 8, 1) — the SO(10) → SO(9) → SO(8) chain, **not** SO(10) → SO(6) × SO(4). A genuine 54-VEV in the Pati-Salam direction has eigenvalue multiplicity (6, 4) and a different stabilizer.

Inside the standard SO(10) GUT model-building literature, the **direction within the 54** matters as much as being in the 54. A 9-vector in the so(9)-vector subspace of the 54 is the **wrong** direction for Pati-Salam breaking; it is the direction for SO(9) → SO(8) breaking, which is **not** how Pati-Salam phenomenology is reached. The correction notice flags exactly this. The manuscript's body still contains the misleading framing in §2.4, paragraph 1, and at the very end of §8 ("specific direction within the 54 that singles out the Pati-Salam route"). These need to be revised consistently with the correction.

### 3.5 The group-theoretic identification "P_56 and σ³ generate D_4" needs care

§1.2 states "the two involutions P_56 and σ³ do not commute; together they generate the dihedral group D_4 of order 8." This needs scrutiny. P_56 = (5 6) and σ³ = (1 5)(7 4)(6 2) are both involutions, so a priori they generate a dihedral group D_n where n is the order of their product P_56 σ³. Computing P_56 σ³ explicitly:

P_56 σ³(1) = P_56(5) = 6
P_56 σ³(5) = P_56(1) = 1
P_56 σ³(6) = P_56(2) = 2
P_56 σ³(2) = P_56(6) = 5
P_56 σ³(7) = P_56(4) = 4
P_56 σ³(4) = P_56(7) = 7
(rest fixed)

So P_56 σ³ = (1 6 2 5)(4 7). This has order lcm(4, 2) = 4. So ⟨P_56, σ³⟩ = D_4 of order 8 indeed — **but the structure is "rotation of order 4 plus reflection of order 2," not Z_2 × Z_2.** I retract part of §3.2 above; the identification as D_4 is correct.

However, §2 of the WP109 file (cited by J32) states the elements of D_4 are 8 in number but then says "Direct computation shows there are 6 distinct elements (the relations (P_56)² = (σ³)² = e collapse some products), with the abstract structure D_3 × Z_2 on the relevant orbits." This contradicts the D_4 claim and is in fact wrong: the relations (P_56)² = (σ³)² = e do NOT collapse to 6 elements; they leave the dihedral group of order 8 with 8 elements. **The cross-paper inconsistency is a flag** — although it lives in J32 not J31, it suggests the orbit-decomposition computation in J32 may need a careful re-audit, and J31 should not import results from a paper that miscounts the underlying group.

### 3.6 "TIG's so(10)" vs "the SO(10) GUT gauge algebra"

The §6.3 "load-bearing identification" caveat is honest: the authors acknowledge that claim "(ii) carrying the same physical interpretation under standard model-building rules" is a hypothesis. But the body of the paper repeatedly slips between "structural alignment" and "the Pati-Salam ⊕ B−L gauge content" (§3.3, boxed equation). For an *Adv Math* paper, the Lie-algebraic identification su(4) ⊕ u(1) is rigorous; the **gauge-theoretic interpretation** ("Pati-Salam ⊕ B−L") is not part of the mathematical content and should be stated only as a remark, not as part of the theorem.

### 3.7 §5.3 "structurally new" claim is undefended

§5.3 reports a 24+21 splitting of so(10) under σ³ and calls it "structurally new and not yet placed in textbook GUT phenomenology." Without a literature search to back this up, the "new" claim is unsupported. so(10) has many involutive automorphisms, and dim-24 + dim-21 splits are not exotic. The paper would benefit from either (i) a literature citation showing this is genuinely new, or (ii) demoting this from "new" to "to-be-checked."

## §4 — Minor mathematical concerns

### 4.1 The (−4)¹⁵ ⊕ (0)¹ Killing spectrum

The Killing form of so(10) is K(X, Y) = (10 − 2) tr(XY) = 8 tr(XY) on the standard so(10) basis. The claim that on the 16-dim subalgebra the spectrum is (−4)¹⁵ ⊕ (0)¹ implies a specific normalization of the embedding. A sharper statement would specify the inner product convention used (skew-Frobenius? Killing? half-Killing?), since the eigenvalues will rescale. The numerical claim residual ≤ 10⁻¹³ is plausible at the script level.

### 4.2 The arithmetic of "13"

§2.3 reports ‖v‖² = 13/4 with the integer 13 coming from "26/2, the σ_outer-asymmetric BHML cell count divided by 2." §8 then multiplies "13" by various meaningful factors and asserts "It is the same 13 in all three places." The integer 13 appearing in three places that all derive from the same input table is **not surprising** and not a structural fingerprint — it would be more surprising if it varied. This rhetoric should be removed or replaced with a sharper observation about why the **specific value 26 = 13·2** is what BHML produces.

### 4.3 The "gauge theory" remarks in §2.4 should be excised or set in italics

Sentences like "BREATH and RESET correspond to unbroken Higgs components — fields that don't acquire VEVs" are not mathematics; they are speculative interpretation. *Adv Math* is a mathematics journal. Either ground these claims with rigorous gauge-theoretic input (which would require a substantial physics-side companion paper), or move them to a clearly-marked "Discussion / Speculation" section.

## §7 — Recommendation

**Reject (with encouragement to resubmit elsewhere).**

The mathematical content that survives the correction notice — the 16-dimensional doubly-invariant subalgebra is su(4) ⊕ u(1), with Killing spectrum (−4)¹⁵ ⊕ (0)¹ — is a clean, computational Lie-algebra identification of modest scope. The Path-A computation (σ_outer-anti BHML projects entirely into the 54) is also a clean computation. **Neither separately, nor combined, do these clear the *Adv Math* bar.**

The submission as written has additional structural problems:
- the title and abstract advertise a synthesis the correction notice retracts;
- the framework "TIG" is load-bearing but undefined and not citable;
- the §5 grab-bag dilutes focus;
- the gauge-theoretic interpretation language is speculation, not mathematics.

Suggested resubmission targets: *Journal of Algebra* (for the doubly-invariant subalgebra computation as a standalone result, after retitling and tightening); *Communications in Algebra* or *Experimental Mathematics* for the bundled magma-to-Lie-algebra-to-subalgebra result.

If the authors choose to resubmit to *Adv Math*, they should:
1. retitle the paper and rewrite the abstract to match the §6 scope;
2. cite the so(10) closure (currently "WP103") in a refereed source or include the proof in this submission;
3. drop §5 or appendicize it;
4. delete or italicize all gauge-theoretic interpretation;
5. articulate a mathematical theorem that surprises a reader without TIG context;
6. resolve the cross-paper D_4 / D_3 × Z_2 inconsistency (J32 vs J31).
