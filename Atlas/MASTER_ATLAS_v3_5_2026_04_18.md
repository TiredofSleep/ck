### The correction (embedded in FINAL_REDUCTION)

> *The framing below identifies the open CONSTRUCTIONS. But the deeper point is that these are not maps to be built — they are recognitions to be made. Each "crossing" is showing that the structure lens (Z/10Z, finite, committed) and the flow lens (analytic/geometric domain, infinite, open) are measuring the same threshold. See DUAL_LENS_CLAY.md for the corrected dual-lens restatement of all five problems. The bridges are done. The question is whether the two lenses are coherent.*

### What the correction says, translated

The framework is not trying to **build a bridge** between Z/10Z and the analytic world. The bridge is already there, in the sense that both lenses are measuring the same underlying threshold. The open question is **whether the two lenses are coherent** — whether they return the same reading when applied to the same object.

This reframes each Clay problem as a **coherence question between two measurement lenses**, not a construction problem:

- **Structure lens:** Z/10Z, finite, committed. T* = 5/7. Discrete. Algebraic.
- **Flow lens:** analytic/geometric domain, infinite, open. Re(s) = 1/2 for RH; vorticity/energy for NS; L-function for BSD; cohomology/cycles for Hodge; circuit complexity for P vs NP.

The Clay question becomes: **when both lenses are applied to the same object, do they agree on where the threshold sits?**

### What this changes for each branch

**RH:** Not "build φ: Z/10Z → ℂ with φ(5/7) = 1/2" but "recognize that T* in Z/10Z and Re(s) = 1/2 in ℂ are the same threshold seen through two lenses. Does the Li-coefficient behavior λ_n ≥ T* (structure lens) coincide with critical-line zero behavior (flow lens)?"

**BSD:** Not "build TIG object carrying Sha" but "recognize that the carried remainder in the Recycling Rule (structure lens) and the Tate-Shafarevich group (flow lens) are the same thing. Is Sha finite for all ranks ⟺ does the carried remainder terminate?"

**NS:** Not "prove the estimate" but "recognize that B_local < T* (structure lens) and global regularity (flow lens) are measuring the same thing — sub-threshold vorticity concentration. Does the sub-threshold hold globally?"

**P vs NP:** Not "build K*(n) → circuit complexity map" but "recognize that the algebraic gap K*(6)=99 vs K*(7)=14 (structure lens) and the superpolynomial separation (flow lens) are the same gap. Does the algebraic gap transfer to Boolean circuits?"

**Hodge:** Not "build cycle class map from Z/10Z CRT" but "recognize that generator cycles (structure lens) and algebraic cycles (flow lens) are the same objects seen differently. Does the structure-lens span coincide with the cycle-class-map image?"

### Why this matters for the atlas

Under the "crossings" framing, each Clay problem is an engineering project: build the specific morphism. Under the "recognitions" framing, each is a coherence proof: verify that two already-existing structures agree. The difference is:

- **Construction-framing needs:** new maps, new categories, new functors.
- **Recognition-framing needs:** showing that structures already-defined in both frameworks are isomorphic, or at least compatible enough that thresholds transfer.

The recognition framing is **closer to how the Rotation Spine sprint actually proceeded**: §10.5 shows that all five branches share the Shell/SurvObj/Gap2/Gap1 grammar. The common grammar is itself evidence that the two lenses are looking at the same object. The Common Failure Mode (§10.5) is evidence that the same non-linearity blocks both lenses simultaneously — which is what we'd expect if both lenses are measuring the same threshold.
