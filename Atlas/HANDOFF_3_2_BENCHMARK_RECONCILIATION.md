## The primary claim is ONE number expressed two ways

The "32pp drop-rate improvement" from the email whitepaper and the "~88% drop-rate reduction" from the expanded write-up are the **same measurement** stated in two units:

- Baseline drop rate: **36.4%**
- TIG drop rate: **4.2%**
- Absolute improvement: 36.4 − 4.2 = **32.2 percentage points** (the "32pp" figure)
- Relative improvement: 32.2 / 36.4 = **88.5%** (the "~88% reduction" figure)

Either statement is correct. For a funder-facing pitch the preferred form is:

> "Mean drop rate in the benchmark fell from **36.4% (baseline) to 4.2% (TIG)**, a **32.2-percentage-point absolute reduction** (equivalently, an 88% relative reduction)."

— because it names both the before and after number, which a reviewer will want to see.

## The expanded metrics (P99 latency, recovery time, etc.) are claimed but not pulled

`HANDOFF_INDEX.md` §3.2 references expanded metrics beyond drop-rate: P99 latency, recovery time, resource utilization, cascade failures. These metrics are consistent with what a **discrete-event cluster-load-balancing simulator** would produce. The canonical source for that simulator — as best located during the 2026-04-21 R16 sweep — is:

- `_r16_repo_scan/All-or-Nothing-E/benchmark.py` (~554 LOC, pulled into `Gen13/targets/funding_coherence_router/archive_all_or_nothing_e/benchmark.py` as of 2026-04-21 commit `60ecd38`).

However, `benchmark.py` as pulled measures **lattice coherence under operator-stream input** — it is the coherence-classifier benchmark, not a distributed-systems cluster simulator. The metrics it reports (coherence score over a sweep of operator sequences, permutation equivalence counts, crossing-lemma event counts) are NOT the P99-latency / recovery-time / resource-utilization family named in HANDOFF §3.2.

This is the **GAP.** The expanded P99 / recovery / cascade-failure metrics, if they exist and produced the 36.4→4.2 number, must have been generated by a DIFFERENT simulator whose source has not been located on R16 as of the 2026-04-21 sweep.
